The lower surface is nearly flat or slightly convex. There are no visible geometric discontinuities or self-intersections.

The u-velocity field shows acceleration over the upper surface. The lack of visible separation zones suggests the curvature is well-managed for the simulation conditions.
- **Parameter Mapping**:
    - `r0` (radius param ~0) results in a functional but perhaps slightly sharp leading edge.
    - `r1` (upper surface ~0.14) controls the max thickness/suction magnitude.
    - `r2` (trailing edge ~-0.73) creates a very thin tail; this negative value is critical for closing the shape.
    - `e2` (~0.9) successfully enforces the Kutta condition (sharp trailing edge).
- **Key Changes Needed**:

    - Increase **camber/aft-loading** (`a2` more negative, `r3` lower) to generate more lift, as the current design looks conservative.
    - Modify **upper surface curvature** (`r1` higher, `a1` shifted) to support the increased lift without inducing separation.
