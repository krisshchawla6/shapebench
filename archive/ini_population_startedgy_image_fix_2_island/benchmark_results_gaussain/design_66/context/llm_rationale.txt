**Strategy Focus: Underexplored Regions & Boundary Pushing**
*   **Exploration of Nose Angle (`a0`)**: Previous designs generally cluster `a0` between 0.0 and 0.13. There is a correlation where higher values (0.13 in Rank 0 vs 0.01 in Rank 43) perform better. I will **extrapolate this trend** and explore a significantly higher `a0` value (~0.25) to test if a more upturned/cambered leading edge entry improves the stagnation point alignment.
*   **Aft-Loading Thickness (`r1`, `a1`)**: To surpass Design 1, I will mimic the successful aft-loading strategy of Design 2 but with variation. I will set `a1` high (pushing max thickness back) but try a **thinner mid-section** (`r1` ~ 0.18 vs D1's 0.28) to reduce pressure drag, combined with the higher nose angle.
*   **Trailing Edge Reflex (`a2`)**: While Design 2 used `a2 = -0.22`, failure cases often had `a2 ~ 0`. I will maintain a negative angle but explore a slightly different camber distribution (`a2 = -0.28`) to balance the pitching moment caused by the modified nose.
*   **Geometric Goal**: Create a "High-Entry, Aft-Laminar" profile. This pushes the parameter space into a region of sharper, more cambered noses combined with thin, aft-loaded bodies, distinct from the blunt/thick failures (Design 4).
