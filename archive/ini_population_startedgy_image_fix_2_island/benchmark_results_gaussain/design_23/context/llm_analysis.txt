**Geometry Observations (Design 1):**
*   **Trailing Edge "Pinch":** The most distinct feature is likely a sharp, thin constriction in the aft section, driven by CP2's parameters (radius `-0.71` and edgy `1.0`). This creates a "kinked" or wedge-like trailing edge rather than a smooth taper.
*   **Conservative Camber:** The angular parameters are all very close to zero (`-0.01`, `0.13`, `-0.06`, `-0.07`), suggesting the airfoil has minimal curvature or camber variation from the baseline.
*   **Leading Edge:** The nose (CP0) has a slightly negative radius parameter (`-0.07`), indicating a relatively sharp or standard leading edge, not a blunt heavy-lifter profile.
*   **Smoothness:** Apart from the sharp CP2, the rest of the airfoil uses smooth parameters (`e < 0`), creating a hybrid jagged-smooth shape.

**Flow Observations:**
*   **Pressure Recovery:** The sharp CP2 likely forces a rapid pressure recovery at the trailing edge. While currently optimal (Rank 0), this feature often creates a localized pressure spike that can lead to separation if the angle of attack changes.
*   **Lift Generation:** The lack of significant camber (small `a` values) suggests the lift is primarily generated by thickness distribution and angle of attack, rather than geometric curvature.
*   **Stability:** The flow is likely stable but "stiff"—the sharp edge forces the wake to detach at a specific point.

**Key Design Changes Needed:**
1.  **Remove the Singularity:** Test if a *fully smooth* airfoil (all `e` near -1.0) can outperform the sharp-edged baseline by maintaining laminar flow further aft.
2.  **Increase Camber:** Explore the angular parameter space (`a`) significantly away from 0.0 to generate lift through curvature rather than just thickness.
3.  **Thicken the Aft Section:** Relax the extreme "pinch" at CP2 (currently `-0.71`) to a moderate value (e.g., `-0.45`) to allow for better structural volume and potentially gentler pressure recovery.
