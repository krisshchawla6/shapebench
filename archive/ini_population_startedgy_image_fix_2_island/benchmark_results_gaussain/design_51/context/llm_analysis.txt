- **Geometry**: The reference design (Design 1) exhibits a classic asymmetric cambered airfoil shape. The leading edge (LE) is smooth and well-rounded, transitioning into a convex suction side and a flatter pressure side. The trailing edge (TE) tapers to a singular point, indicating the sharpness constraint is working.
- **Flow Fields**: The simulation likely shows a high-velocity low-pressure region (suction peak) forming immediately aft of the leading edge on the upper surface, followed by a smooth pressure recovery. The absence of a large chaotic wake suggests the flow remains attached, confirming why this design ranks highly.
- **Flaws & Opportunities**: While efficient, the standard "peaky" pressure distribution (implied by the CP1 location) can be improved. The current thickness distribution might be slightly conservative (thick). To advance, we can reduce drag by thinning the profile and attempting to flatten the suction peak to delay turbulent transition (move towards a laminar flow profile).
