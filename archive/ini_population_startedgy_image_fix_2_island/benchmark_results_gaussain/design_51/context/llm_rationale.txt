- **Strategy Focus**: Instead of radical geometric shifts which led to failure in Designs 4-6, this design applies the **"Visual Intuition"** strategy to refine the successful topology of Design 1. The goal is to maximize Lift-to-Drag (L/D) by reducing form drag and improving pressure recovery.
- **Leading Edge (CP0)**: I have reduced the radius parameter `r0` slightly from 0.021 to `0.0185`. This creates a slightly sharper nose, reducing the stagnation region size and pressure drag, assuming the angle of attack remains within a moderate range.
- **Suction Side (CP1)**: The radius `r1` is reduced from 0.205 to `0.1950` to thin the airfoil, while `a1` is slightly increased to `0.2350`. This combination aims to maintain lift (via camber) while reducing the frontal area. The `e1` parameter is set to `-0.95` to ensure maximum surface smoothness.
- **Trailing Edge (CP2)**: The TE radius parameter `r2` is made more negative (`-0.88` vs `-0.84`) to pinch the trailing edge tighter, ensuring a sharper release point. The sharpness parameter `e2` is kept at `1.0`.
- **Pressure Side (CP3)**: `r3` is set to `-0.26` (more negative than Design 1's `-0.23`), which lifts the bottom surface slightly ("undercutting"). This effectively increases the aft camber and reduces the profile thickness, contributing to the high-efficiency goal.
