- **Visual Intuition Strategy**: The primary goal is to "fix" the visual defect of the pinched tail seen in Design 1. By moving the radius of the sharp corner (CP2) from -0.40 to a value closer to zero (e.g., -0.15 or -0.10), we effectively "inflate" or "extend" the trailing edge, reducing the severity of the concavity and allowing the flow to stay attached longer.
- **Aerodynamic Refinement**: Airfoils require a smooth thickness distribution. I will increase the radii of the leading edge (CP0) and the "shoulders" (CP1, CP3) slightly and symmetrically to ensure the airfoil has sufficient internal volume and a smoother curvature, preventing premature separation on the forward section.
- **Geometric "Cambering"**: To generate lift, I will introduce slight asymmetry in the angle parameters. I will tilt the upper surface point (CP1) positively and the lower surface point (CP3) negatively, effectively increasing the camber of the airfoil.
- **Diversity**: This design moves away from the "random blob" space towards a structured, recognizable airfoil shape (Smooth LE, Smooth Top/Bottom, Sharp TE). It explores the region of the parameter space where `radius` is consistent and `edgy` is highly polarized (very smooth vs very sharp).
