*   **Strategy Focus (Exploration):** The current population clusters around moderate thickness and camber. To identify novel high-performance regions, I will explore a **High Camber, Aft-Loaded** archetype. This moves away from the "teardrop" shape towards a more classic under-cambered lifting foil.
*   **High Camber Geometry:** I will significantly increase `r1` (Upper surface) to `0.35` and decrease `r3` (Lower surface) to `-0.45`. This divergence (`r1` >> `r3`) should create a strong arch, potentially increasing lift, which is a major component of the reward function (likely L/D or similar). This area of the parameter space (`r1` > 0.3, `r3` < -0.3) is unexplored in the top ranks.
*   **Aft-Loading (Angle Shift):** I will push the control points P1 and P3 further back by increasing their angle magnitudes (`a1` to `0.35`, `a3` to `-0.35`). This shifts the point of maximum thickness/camber rearward, exploring a laminar-flow-style thickness distribution rather than the forward-biased thickness of Design 1.
*   **Chord Extension:** I will push `r2` (Trailing Edge) to `-0.85`. Since `r2` ~ -0.7 yielded good results and `r2` > 0 yielded poor results, pushing further into the negative range should effectively increase the chord length, reducing the relative thickness ratio and potentially lowering drag.
*   **Leading Edge Refinement:** I will set `r0` to `-0.15`. This is slightly "tighter" than the baseline `0.0` but avoids the catastrophic bluntness of `r0 > 0.5`. A sharper LE is better suited for the thinner, more cambered profile being generated.
