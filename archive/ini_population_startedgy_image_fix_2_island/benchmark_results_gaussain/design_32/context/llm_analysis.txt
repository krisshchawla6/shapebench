*   **Geometry:** Design 1 (representative image) displays a relatively thick, teardrop-shaped airfoil. The leading edge is quite bulbous and round. The trailing edge tapers to a sharp point, but the overall thickness-to-chord ratio appears quite high. The surface curvature is smooth without jagged interruptions.
*   **Flow Field:** The pressure contours show a distinct stagnation region at the leading edge and a suction region (low pressure) on the upper surface. The flow appears attached with a clean wake, though the thickness likely generates significant pressure drag. There are no signs of massive early separation, but the pressure recovery gradient at the rear looks steep due to the thickness.
*   **Parameter Mapping:** The "bulbous" nose corresponds to `r0` near 0.0 (or slightly negative in Design 1, but relative to the very large `r0` of failed designs, it's moderate). The "thick" body corresponds to `r1` and `r3` being relatively close to neutral or positive sum. The sharp trailing edge is driven by `e2` being close to 1.0. The successful designs (1 & 2) rely on a large negative `r2` (~ -0.7) to extend the chord and thin out the shape; flaws in bad designs (3-6) correlate with positive `r0` (too blunt) or insufficient `r2` extension.
