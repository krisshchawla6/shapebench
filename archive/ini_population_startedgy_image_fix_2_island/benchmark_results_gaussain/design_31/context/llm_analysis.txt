**Geometry Observations (Design 1)**
*   **Asymmetric Cambered Profile**: The design exhibits a classic cambered airfoil shape, driven by the positive angle on CP1 ($0.26$) and negative on CP3 ($-0.22$).
*   **Sharp Trailing Edge**: The geometry narrows to a distinct sharp point at the rear, corresponding to the high "edgy" parameter at CP2 ($0.97$).
*   **Smooth Leading Edge**: The front of the airfoil is rounded and continuous, consistent with the very low "edgy" parameter at CP0 ($-0.93$).
*   **Thickness Distribution**: The airfoil has moderate thickness (r0 $\approx$ 0.42), but the upper surface peak (CP1) is relatively constrained (r1 $\approx$ 0.10).

**Flow Field Observations (Inferred from Reward)**
*   **Attached Flow**: The high reward ($1.60$) indicates the flow remains largely attached over the suction side, generating lift without excessive drag.
*   **Pressure Recovery**: The pressure gradient towards the trailing edge is likely gradual enough to prevent massive separation bubbles.
*   **Effective Lift**: The asymmetry suggests good lift generation at $\alpha=0$, though there may be room to improve L/D ratio by refining the thickness-to-camber relationship.

**Flaws & Mappings**
*   **Potential Thickness Limits**: Design 6 failed significantly ($0.55$ reward) with larger radii ($r0=0.65$, $r1=0.30$). This suggests the solver penalizes excessive bluntness or thickness, likely due to drag or separation.
*   **Parameter Correlation**: High performance is strongly correlated with a sharp CP2 ($e2 \approx 1.0$) and smooth CP0 ($e0 \approx -1.0$). Intermediate parameters (CP1, CP3) are currently clustered in a narrow range.
