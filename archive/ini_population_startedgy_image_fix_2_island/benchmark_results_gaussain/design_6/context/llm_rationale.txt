*   **Exploration Strategy - Aft-Loading**: The design database shows `angle_param` values clustered near `0.0` (max `0.2` in Design 1, `0.14` in Design 2). This results in standard thickness distributions centered near the front/middle. I will explore **Aft-Loading** by setting high positive `angle_param` values (> 0.6) for the middle control points. This pushes the maximum thickness and camber further back, mimicking laminar flow airfoil concepts (like NACA 6-series).
*   **Geometry - Smoothness**: Learning from Design 1's failure (`edgy=1.0`) and Design 2's success (`edgy=-0.96`), I will enforce `edgy_param` near `-1.0` for the leading edge and main body to ensure attached flow.
*   **Thickness Variation**: Design 1 and 2 had top-surface radii (`r1`) around `0.42-0.44`. I will explore a **thicker** section (`r1 ~ 0.68`) combined with the aft-shift to see if a robust, high-volume airfoil can perform well.
*   **Camber Exploration**: Design 2 found success with a negative bottom radius (`r2 ~ -0.4`). I will adopt a moderate negative value (`r2 ~ -0.2`) to maintain camber but shift its location aft (`a2 > 0.6`) to match the top surface, creating a cohesive aft-loaded shape.
*   **Nose Refinement**: I will significantly reduce the leading edge radius (`r0`) from the failing `0.94` to `0.35`. This provides a distinct nose without the bluff-body penalty of Design 1.
