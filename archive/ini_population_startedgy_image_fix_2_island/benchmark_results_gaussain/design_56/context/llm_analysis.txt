*   **Geometry Observations**: The representative design (Design 1) exhibits a classic, asymmetric airfoil shape with a relatively round leading edge and a sharp trailing edge. The upper surface is smooth with maximum thickness occurring around 30-40% of the chord. The lower surface appears slightly convex, contributing to volume but potentially adding pressure drag.
*   **Flow Field Observations**: The high reward suggests the flow is fully attached. There is likely a distinct low-pressure suction peak near the leading edge on the upper surface, followed by a smooth pressure recovery. The wake is thin and symmetric, indicating minimal separation and low form drag.
*   **Parameter Mappings**: The high performance correlates with `edgy` parameters near -1.0 for the front/mid points (ensuring smoothness) and +1.0 for the trailing edge (ensuring a clean release point). The `radius` parameters at CP1 and CP2 control the thickness distribution, while the `angle` at CP3 controls the trailing edge geometric angle of attack.
*   **Key Design Changes Needed**: To advance the population, we need to move away from this "standard" NACA-like distribution. The current design might be lift-limited by its conventional camber line. A key improvement would be to shift the lift generation further aft (aft-loading) and flatten the upper surface pressure distribution to delay transition or separation.
