- **Flow Observations:**
    - The flow field likely exhibits a clean stagnation point at the leading edge and a suction peak on the upper surface.

    - The wake is thin and stable, characteristic of the high reward (4.85) achieved.

- **Mapping & Improvements:**
    - **Flaws:** While Design 1 is efficient, it appears conservative (low thickness, moderate camber). It may be leaving maximum lift capability on the table.
    - **Parameter Mapping:** High rewards strongly correlate with `r2` (Trailing Edge) being near `-0.9` and `e2` being `1.0` (sharp). Positive `r2` values (Designs 4 & 6) lead to broken geometries and failure. `r1` controls upper surface height/lift potential.
    - **Key Changes:** 
        1. Significantly increase `r1` (Upper Surface) to test a high-lift, thicker profile.
        2. Increase `r0` (Leading Edge) slightly to support the flow turning around a thicker nose.
        3. Adjust `a2` (Trailing Edge Angle) to add rear camber (aft-loading) without inducing separation.
