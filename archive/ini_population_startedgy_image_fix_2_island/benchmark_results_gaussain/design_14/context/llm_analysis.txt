**Geometry and Flow Observations:**
- **Geometry**: The design shown in the images (Design 1) appears as a stubby, irregular "blob" rather than an airfoil. It lacks a defined leading or trailing edge, resembling a distorted polygon. The thickness distribution is chaotic, with no clear taper.
- **Flow Field**: The flow visualization confirms significant separation. There are likely large low-pressure zones in the wake due to the blunt shape (bluff body aerodynamics), resulting in high pressure drag. The lack of a streamlined tail prevents pressure recovery.
- **Parameter Mapping**: Design 1 has a highly negative radius parameter for the third control point (`r2 = -0.720489`). Assuming standard ordering where indices map around the shape (0=LE, 1=Top, 2=TE, 3=Bot), this negative value likely "pulls in" the trailing edge, creating the blunt, stubby shape observed. The sharpness parameter `e2 = 0.763` is high (good for a TE), but the geometry is sabotaged by the radius pulling it inward.
