**Image Analysis (based on Design 1/Design 19):**
*   **Geometry:** The airfoil features a classic asymmetric cambered shape with a rounded leading edge and a sharp trailing edge. The upper surface curvature is convex and smooth, while the lower surface appears relatively flat or slightly concave near the rear.
*   **Flow Fields:** The pressure coefficient ($C_p$) plot shows a strong suction peak (low pressure) on the upper surface near the leading edge (roughly 15-20% chord), followed by a gradual pressure recovery. The velocity magnitude plot confirms high-speed flow over the forward upper surface. The wake region (visible in u-velocity) is narrow, indicating attached flow and relatively low form drag.
*   **Visible Flaws:** The suction peak is located quite far forward (front-loaded), which might induce an early transition from laminar to turbulent flow, increasing skin friction. The thickness tapers off somewhat quickly after the peak.
*   **Parameter Mapping:** The forward suction peak corresponds to `a1` ~ 0.21 (upper control point angle). The sharp trailing edge is correctly driven by `e2` = 1.0. The Leading Edge radius is controlled by `r0`.
