*   **Geometry Observations**: The representative design (Design 1) displays a relatively standard asymmetric airfoil profile. The leading edge (CP0) is smooth and rounded, while the trailing edge (CP2) is distinctly sharp. The upper surface (CP1) shows a moderate curvature peak, defining the airfoil's max thickness, while the lower surface (CP3) is flatter. The extreme negative radius at CP2 suggests a "pinched" or very tapered rear section, which seems intentional to enforce a clean Kutta condition.
*   **Flow Field Observations**: Given the positive reward (1.2162), the flow is likely attached over most of the chord length. There is likely a well-defined suction peak on the upper surface near the leading edge. The sharp trailing edge (high edgy parameter) aids in fixing the separation point at the very end, preventing a large, chaotic wake. The pressure recovery on the aft upper surface appears managed, though further smoothing could reduce drag.
*   **Parameter-Flaw Mapping**: The moderate smoothness on the upper surface (CP1 edgy = -0.52) in Design 1 is decent but could be improved; a rougher spline here creates curvature discontinuities that trip the boundary layer. The trailing edge radius is very negative (-0.67), which works but might be nearing the limit of mesh validity; relaxing it slightly could improve robustness.

    1.  Maximize the sharpness of the trailing edge (CP2 edgy -> 1.0) to minimize pressure drag at the rear.
    2.  Increase the smoothness of the upper surface (CP1) and leading edge (CP0) to maintain laminar flow longer.
    3.  Slightly increase the upper surface "volume" (CP1 radius) to boost lift, as the current reward is good but likely lift-limited.
