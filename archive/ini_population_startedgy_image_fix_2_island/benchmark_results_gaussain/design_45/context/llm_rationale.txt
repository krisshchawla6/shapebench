*   **Strategy - Aft-Loaded Thickness:** To advance the population, I will explore a "Laminar Flow" inspired geometry. Current top designs have their upper crest (CP0) angle near 0.0 or slightly negative. I will push the max thickness further aft by setting CP0's angle positive (`a0 > 0.1`). This can delay the pressure recovery point, reducing skin friction drag.
*   **Nose Refinement:** Observations show the top-ranked design has a blunter nose (`r1=0.22`) than the second rank. I will aim for a "Goldilocks" zone for the nose radius—sharper than a blunt stall-proof wing but rounder than a razor—around `r1 ≈ 0.25` to `0.30`, combined with a higher angle `a1` to smooth the entry to the aft-loaded crest.
*   **Trailing Edge Extension:** I will maximize the effective chord length by setting the TE radius (CP2) larger (`r2 > 0.9`). A longer effective chord generally improves Reynolds number effects and lift efficiency. I will keep the angle slightly negative to maintain camber but not so aggressive as to induce separation.
*   **Lower Surface Scoop:** To counteract the potential lift loss from the laminar upper surface, I will introduce slightly more under-camber in the lower surface by adjusting CP3. I will keep the radius small/negative but increase the magnitude of the negative angle.
