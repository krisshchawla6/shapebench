*   **Geometry**: Visual inspection of Design 1 (representative image) reveals a robust, "tear-drop" airfoil with a notably bulbous leading edge (large `r0`). The upper surface curvature is aggressive (`a1` is high), creating a thick profile. The trailing edge is sharp and well-defined (`e2` near 1.0), which is a structurally and aerodynamically sound feature.
*   **Flow Fields**: The pressure contours likely indicate a strong suction peak very close to the leading edge due to the blunt nose, which generates high lift but also significant form drag. The velocity field may show signs of thickening boundary layers on the aft upper surface, suggesting the steep recovery gradient is stressing the flow attachment.
*   **Mapping & Flaws**: The primary visual flaw is the excessive thickness at the nose (`r0=0.46`) which acts like a blunt body. While this aids low-speed lift, it penalizes efficiency. The upper angle (`a1=0.35`) is aggressive; reducing this could delay separation onset.
*   **Key Changes**: 1) Sharpen the leading edge to reduce stagnation pressure drag. 2) Reduce the upper surface peak to smooth the pressure gradients. 3) Slightly extend the trailing edge region to improve pressure recovery.
