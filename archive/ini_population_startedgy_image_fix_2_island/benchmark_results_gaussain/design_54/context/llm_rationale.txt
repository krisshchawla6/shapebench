*   **Visual Intuition Strategy**: Moving from the "blunt" archetype of Design 1 to a "laminar" archetype. The goal is to retain the sharp trailing edge (which clearly works) but refine the forward section to "slice" the air rather than "push" it.
*   **Leading Edge Refinement**: Design 1's `r0` of 0.46 is very high. I will reduce `r0` to **0.255**, creating a nose radius that balances structural volume with aerodynamic penetration. `e0` remains smooth (-0.99) to ensure no geometric discontinuities.
*   **Upper Surface Control**: To prevent the boundary layer thickening seen in the hypothetical flow analysis, I am lowering `a1` from 0.35 to **0.215**. This spreads the lift generation more evenly across the chord rather than concentrating it at the peak.
*   **Trailing Edge Extension**: I am increasing `r2` to **0.915** (up from 0.86). A larger radius parameter here pushes the control point further out, effectively increasing the chord length relative to the thickness, which improves the fineness ratio (L/D).
*   **Lower Surface Camber**: Setting `a3` to **-0.165** maintains sufficient camber for lift without creating a complex lower-surface pressure profile. This creates a "cleaner" underside compared to the deeper cuts seen in other designs.
*   **Diversity**: This design explores a region of the parameter space defined by *moderate thickness* and *gentle curvature*, contrasting with the *thick/high-camber* dominant designs currently in the population.
