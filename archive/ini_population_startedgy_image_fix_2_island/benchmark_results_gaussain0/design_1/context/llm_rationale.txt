*   **Baseline Aerodynamic Archetype**: Applying the Visual Intuition Strategy by constructing a classic "cambered wing" profile. I am prioritizing a shape that looks "right" to an engineer: a thick, rounded front and a tapering, sharp rear.
*   **Camber Generation**: I've set the radius parameter for the upper control point (`r1`) significantly higher than the lower control point (`r3`). This creates an asymmetric thickness distribution designed to generate a pressure differential between the surfaces.
*   **Flow Attachment**: By setting the leading edge `edgy_param` to a highly negative value (-0.88), I am ensuring a smooth, curvature-continuous nose. This is critical to prevent the pressure spikes and early separation common in randomly generated shapes.
*   **Kutta Condition Simulation**: The trailing edge (`r2`) is assigned a high `edgy_param` (0.92). Visually, this creates a sharp point, which is essential for forcing the flow to leave the airfoil tangentially, reducing wake turbulence.
*   **Chordwise Distribution**: Using the `angle_param` to bias the upper thickness slightly forward and the lower thickness slightly aft. This mimics the geometry of high-lift, low-drag profiles (like the NACA 4-digit series) which typically place maximum thickness at 30-40% of the chord.
