- **Exploration Strategy Focus**: The current population is heavily clustered around a specific "thin-plate" strategy where `r0` and `r3` are negative and `r1` is positive. To find new local optima, I am intentionally exploring the opposite radial configuration for the leading edge and lower surface.
- **Leading Edge Exploration**: I am setting `radius_param` for the first control point (`r0`) to a high positive value (0.7258). This is a direct inversion of the top three designs and will test if a different leading-edge curvature can produce beneficial stagnation characteristics.
- **Thickness Distribution**: By moving `angle_param` for the upper surface (`a1`) to a negative extreme (-0.8823), I am shifting the peak thickness of the airfoil to a completely different chord-wise position compared to Design 1, which had a very high positive `a1`.
- **Trailing Edge Variation**: While keeping the `edgy_param` high for the third point (`e2 = 0.9521`) to maintain a sharp trailing edge (a known requirement for efficiency), I am pushing its angular offset (`a2`) to a positive extreme (0.8542) to explore a significantly different "exit angle" for the flow.
- **Lower Surface Convexity**: I am using a positive `radius_param` for the fourth point (`r3 = 0.4231`), whereas all top-performing designs used negative values. This will explore a convex lower-surface profile, which may change the pressure distribution and pitching moment characteristics.
- **Evolutionary Advancement**: This design serves as a "jump" in the search space to prevent the optimizer from getting stuck in the current thin-cambered local maximum, testing whether a thicker or differently cambered profile can yield comparable or superior rewards.
