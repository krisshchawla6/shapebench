*   **Strategy Focus (Visual Intuition):** I am designing a shape that conforms to the visual characteristics of a classical subsonic airfoil: a well-rounded leading edge to handle various angles of attack and a sharp trailing edge to enforce the Kutta condition and minimize wake turbulence.
*   **Leading Edge Definition:** I have assigned a high positive radius and a significantly negative edginess to the forward-facing control point (CP2). This creates the "blunt" look necessary to prevent early flow separation and high-pressure gradients at the nose.
*   **Trailing Edge Definition:** I have set the rear-facing control point (CP0) to a negative radius (shortening the distance from center) and high edginess. This creates the characteristic sharp "V" shape at the back, which is visually and physically required for efficient pressure recovery.
*   **Upper Surface Camber:** The top control point (CP1) is given a positive radius and a positive angular offset to shift the maximum thickness forward. This encourages a low-pressure zone (suction) on the upper surface, which is the primary driver for lift.
*   **Lower Surface Management:** The bottom point (CP3) is designed with a slight inward radius and smooth curvature to ensure the lower surface is relatively flat or slightly concave, maintaining a pressure differential between the two surfaces.
*   **Evolutionary Goal:** By establishing this "intuitive" aerodynamic shape as the first design, I am providing the optimizer with a robust starting point from which it can later explore more radical, non-obvious geometric variations.
