- **Geometry**: The design shown (Design 1) is a high-camber, thin airfoil with a distinct geometric inflection point or "kink" on the upper surface approximately two-thirds of the way back. The leading edge is relatively sharp, and the lower surface exhibits a slight concave curvature towards the trailing edge. The overall profile is highly asymmetric, designed for high lift at the cost of potential flow separation.
- **Flow Field / Simulation Results**: The pressure field shows a strong suction peak on the upper leading edge, which is desirable for lift. However, there is a visible pressure disturbance at the geometric kink on the upper surface, likely causing local flow instability. The velocity field (U and V) shows significant acceleration over the top, but the wake is wider than ideal, suggesting that the geometric discontinuity is contributing to pressure drag.
- **Mapping & Flaws**: The "kink" on the upper surface is directly attributable to the `edgy_param` `e2` (0.3576) of the third control point and its proximity to the second point. The extremely negative `r0` (-0.9240) creates a very thin leading edge that may be prone to stall at higher angles of attack.
- **Key Changes Needed**:
  - Reduce the edginess of the upper aft control point (`e2`) to create a smoother curvature and eliminate the pressure spike.
  - Slightly increase the `radius_param` for the upper surface (`r2`) to further exploit the lift-generating camber while maintaining smoothness.
  - Moderate the extreme `radius_param` of the leading edge (`r0`) to create a more robust nose profile.
