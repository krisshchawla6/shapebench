Design 1 exhibits a very slender, almost membrane-like profile with a significant geometric "pinch" near the mid-chord, evidenced by the low radius parameter in the second control point (r=0.2332). While this design achieved the highest reward (5.3926), the pressure field analysis shows a highly concentrated suction peak at the very beginning of the leading edge followed by a rapid pressure recovery, which likely makes the design sensitive to stall at varying angles of attack. The velocity field indicates a relatively clean wake, but the thinness of the profile limits the structural volume and potentially the maximum lift coefficient. The trailing edge (CP3) is sharp (e=0.93), which is aerodynamically sound, but the transition from the "pinched" mid-section to the tail is somewhat abrupt. Key changes needed include: 1) Increasing the radial parameters of internal control points to create a fuller, more conventional thickness distribution; 2) Smoothing the leading edge curvature to spread the suction peak; 3) Stabilizing the pressure gradient by ensuring a more gradual transition towards the trailing edge; 4) Moving the maximum thickness further aft to explore laminar flow benefits.
