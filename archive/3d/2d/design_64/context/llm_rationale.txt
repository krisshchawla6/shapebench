*   **Novel Design Strategy**: I am abandoning the "pinch-point" approach (radius < 0.3) seen in Designs 1-5. Instead, I will use an "envelope" strategy where all four control points are positioned at larger radii to define the outer boundary of a more conventional, high-performance airfoil.
*   **Leading Edge Optimization**: By setting CP0 at a moderate radius with high smoothness (edgy = -0.98) and near-zero angular offset, I aim to create a robust, rounded leading edge that handles a wider range of angles of attack without separation.
*   **Upper Surface Camber**: CP1 will be placed at a high radius and a positive angular offset (upper surface) to define the peak thickness and camber. This should maximize lift while maintaining a smooth transition (edgy = -0.96) to the trailing edge.
*   **Sharp Trailing Edge**: To minimize pressure drag and ensure clean flow shedding, CP2 is designated as the trailing edge with maximum sharpness (edgy = 0.99) and maximum radial distance.
*   **Lower Surface Control**: CP3 will define the lower surface with a slightly lower radius than the upper surface to create an asymmetric, lift-generating profile, ensuring the bottom is smooth to prevent drag-inducing vortices.
*   **Exploration**: This design moves the search toward the "outer rim" of the parameter space, testing if a more traditional aerodynamic teardrop shape outperforms the irregular, pinched geometries found in the previous top designs.
