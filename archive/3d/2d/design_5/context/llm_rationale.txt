* **Upper Surface Camber (P1)**: The `angle_param` is shifted forward and the `radius_param` is increased to create a high-velocity upper surface, while a low `edgy_param` ensures continuous curvature, preventing localized shock or separation.
* **Trailing Edge Closure (P2)**: I am pushing the `radius_param` to the limit and setting `edgy_param` near 1.0. This creates a sharp, pointed termination, which is the most critical feature for reducing pressure drag and generating lift.
* **Lower Surface Flatness (P3)**: A smaller `radius_param` on the lower surface compared to the upper surface will create the necessary pressure differential (camber) to ensure a positive lift coefficient at zero or low angles of attack.
