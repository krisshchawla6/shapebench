*   **Novel Design Strategy**: This design departs from the current population by prioritizing extreme surface continuity (very low `edgy_param` for the body) combined with a high-definition sharp trailing edge, rather than the moderate values seen in Design 1.
*   **Thickness Distribution**: By positioning the control points with specific angular offsets (using `angle_param`), I am shifting the maximum thickness further aft (approximately 35-40% chord) to explore a more supercritical-like pressure distribution, which is not clearly present in the existing database.
*   **Trailing Edge Refinement**: Setting the final `edgy_param` to a very high value (near 1.0) creates a knife-edge trailing edge. This aims to minimize the wake deficit observed in Design 1's velocity plots.
*   **Leading Edge Geometry**: A slightly reduced `radius_param` at the leading edge (relative to Design 1's 1.0) is used to create a more "pointed" but smooth entry, reducing the magnitude of the initial suction peak and potentially delaying separation.
*   **Aerodynamic judgment**: The population trends show that higher rewards correlate with smooth body parameters and high radii at the tips. This design maximizes these trends while introducing a more aggressive sharpness at the TE to advance the Pareto front for Lift/Drag.
