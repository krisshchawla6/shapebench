*   **Explore High Sweep for Vortex Lift:** Design 1 had 65.5 deg sweep, performing very well. To push the envelope, I will specify a higher sweep (e.g., 74.0 deg). This aims to generate a strong leading-edge vortex for increased lift at high angles of attack, which is characteristic of high-performing delta wings. This is a bold move to see if the induced drag can be managed with other parameters.
*   **Simplify Airfoil to Isolate Sweep/Twist Effects:** Design 1 used a NACA 4412. To understand the pure aerodynamic contribution of high sweep and optimal twist without the complexities of camber, I will choose a symmetric NACA 00XX profile. This allows for a clearer assessment of the interplay between geometric sweep, twist, and their impact on vortex formation and induced drag, contrasting with previous cambered high-reward designs.
*   **Aggressive Washout Distribution:** While washout is common, I will specify a substantial negative twist at the root and an even more aggressive negative twist at the tip (e.g., twist_root = -8.0 deg, twist_tip = -10.0 deg). This configuration should actively unload the wingtips, aiming to improve induced drag efficiency by tailoring the spanwise lift distribution and potentially delaying tip stall.
*   **Moderate Anhedral for Lateral Characteristics:** Design 1 had slight anhedral. I will explore a more pronounced anhedral (e.g., -5.0 deg). While primarily affecting lateral stability, this exploration tests a less common geometry in delta wings and could interact favorably with high sweep and washout, or reveal new insights into the objective function's sensitivity.
*   **Adjust Root Chord for Scaling:** Design 1 had a root chord of ~34.8 inches. I'll choose a slightly larger root chord (e.g., 38.0 inches) to scale the wing slightly larger than Design 1, which might enhance the overall lift-generating capability associated with the higher sweep, without being excessively large, aiming for a moderate increase in overall performance.
*   **Moderate Thickness:** I will maintain a moderate thickness-to-chord ratio (e.g., NACA 0012). This provides structural depth without incurring excessive wave drag at typical flight speeds for delta wings, aligning with common design practices and being consistent with Design 1's thickness.
