*   **Exploiting Successful Washout**: The most prominent feature of Design 1 is its progressive washout, with the root twist being more negative than the tip. I will replicate and slightly intensify this successful pattern, setting `twist_root` to -8.0 degrees and `twist_tip` to -5.0 degrees. This maintains a strong washout at the root, which can help distribute load away from the root, while allowing for some lift near the tip, further optimizing spanwise lift distribution.
*   **Slightly Increased Sweep for Enhanced Vortex Lift**: Design 1's 65.5-degree sweep proved highly effective. I will incrementally increase `le_sweep` to 67.0 degrees. This slight increase aims to enhance the strength of leading-edge vortices, potentially boosting lift and CL/CDi, while remaining within the demonstrated high-performance sweep range.
*   **Maintain Optimal Camber Profile**: The NACA 44xx airfoil of Design 1 clearly contributed to its high reward. I will retain `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4` to ensure the benefits of cambered lift are preserved in the new design, as this has shown to be a critical performance driver.
*   **Reduced Thickness for Drag Reduction**: Design 1 utilized `naca_t=12`. To further optimize the CL/CDi ratio, I will reduce the airfoil thickness to `naca_t=10`. A thinner airfoil generally results in lower profile drag, which could lead to a significant improvement in the objective function if other parameters are well-chosen.
*   **Modest Increase in Root Chord**: The `root_chord_in` of Design 1 (34.78 inches) was effective. I will slightly increase the `root_chord_in` to 36.0 inches. This subtle increase in overall wing scale could lead to a higher overall lift coefficient, which, when combined with optimized drag, can improve CL/CDi.
*   **Consistent Slight Negative Dihedral**: Design 1 had a slightly negative dihedral. I will maintain this by setting `dihedral` to -2.5 degrees. This value is close to the previous successful design and likely contributes to desirable lateral stability characteristics without negatively impacting the primary objective.
