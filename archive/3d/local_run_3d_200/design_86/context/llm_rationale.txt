*   **Optimal Sweep Exploration**: Design 1's success with a 65.5° sweep and the poor performance of designs with sweeps above 70° strongly indicates an optimal region in the mid-to-high 60s. I will select a sweep of **67.0 degrees**, slightly higher than Design 1, to explore the upper boundary of this promising range for potentially stronger, yet stable, vortex formation.
*   **Targeted Washout for Vortex Control**: Design 1 demonstrated the effectiveness of washout (root more negative than tip) for efficient load distribution. I will employ a `twist_root = -8.0` and `twist_tip = -5.0`. This maintains a significant washout differential (3.0 degrees, slightly more than D1's ~2.5 degrees) to promote outboard lift, manage vortex initiation, and potentially further reduce induced drag.
*   **Balanced Camber and Reduced Thickness**: The best designs utilized cambered airfoils (NACA 44xx, 24xx). To explore a slightly different aerodynamic characteristic from Design 1's NACA 4412, I will choose `naca_m=2` and `naca_p=4`, providing less aggressive camber. Coupled with `naca_t=10`, a thinner profile, this aims to investigate the trade-off between camber-induced lift and reduced profile drag, leading to a **NACA 2410** airfoil.
*   **Strategic Root Chord Adjustment**: Design 1 has a root chord of ~34.8 inches. To explore the effect of a larger lifting surface while retaining favorable sweep and twist characteristics, I will set `root_chord_in = 40.0 inches`. This offers increased wing area for potentially higher overall lift, but is still moderate compared to the maximum allowable, avoiding excessively large wings which might introduce other issues.
*   **Dihedral for Diversity and Stability**: Design 1 had a small negative dihedral. To promote diversity in the population and understand the sensitivity of this parameter, I will select a small positive `dihedral` of **3.0 degrees**. This explores a region less represented by high-reward designs, investigating if minor positive dihedral can offer stability benefits without significantly impacting CL/CDi.
*   **Novel Design Principle**: This design integrates the observed optimal sweep and effective washout strategy with new combinations of root chord, camber, thickness, and dihedral to uncover novel high-performing configurations rather than incremental adjustments.
