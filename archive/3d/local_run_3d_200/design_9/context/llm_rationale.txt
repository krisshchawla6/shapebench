*   **Explore a moderately high sweep angle (68.5 degrees):** While 58.36 degrees yielded the best result so far, many efficient delta wings operate at higher sweeps. The previously poor-performing higher sweep designs likely lacked optimized twist. This design aims to find an optimal balance, providing robust leading-edge vortices while managing their effects on drag, by pushing beyond the current best's sweep.
*   **Implement significant overall negative twist with strong washout (twist_root = -5.0, twist_tip = -10.0):** The strong washout in Design 1 was crucial. This design takes an even more aggressive approach with an overall negative wing angle and maximum washout at the tip to further unload the outer wing, improve lift distribution, and delay vortex breakdown, aiming to maximize CL while minimizing CDi.
*   **Introduce camber with a NACA 2412 airfoil:** This is a key novel exploration. All top designs used NACA 00xx. Using a NACA 24xx profile (2% max camber at 40% chord) will generate lift at zero angle of attack, potentially increasing CL/CDi for specific flight regimes compared to symmetric airfoils by tailoring the pressure distribution.
*   **Reduce airfoil thickness to 12% (NACA 2412):** To complement the introduction of camber and potentially mitigate any drag penalty from higher sweep, a thinner airfoil (12% thickness compared to 15% in Design 1) is selected to reduce profile drag and improve the overall aerodynamic efficiency.
*   **Select a mid-range root chord (30.0 inches):** The previous successful designs clustered around 32-36 inches. A slightly smaller root chord will explore the effect of overall wing scale and wetted area, potentially finding an efficiency sweet spot for a more compact design, without drastically changing the overall planform.
*   **Maintain near-zero dihedral (1.0 degree):** Given that near-zero dihedral performed best in previous designs, a small positive dihedral is chosen to maintain good aerodynamic efficiency without introducing adverse stability or drag penalties, focusing the optimization on the more impactful parameters of sweep, twist, and airfoil.
