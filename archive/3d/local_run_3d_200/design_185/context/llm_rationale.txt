*   The current population's high-performing designs largely cluster around a `le_sweep` of approximately 65 degrees. To implement the **Exploration Strategy**, we will push towards the **lower boundary** of the `le_sweep` parameter, setting it to 45.0 degrees. This will allow us to investigate the performance of a truly low-sweep delta wing, a significant departure from existing successful designs.
*   All previous designs utilize cambered airfoils (`naca_m=2` or `4`). As part of our **Exploration Strategy** for underexplored regions, we will use a **symmetric airfoil** by setting `naca_m=0` (and `naca_p=0` as required). This explores if camber is essential for high CL/CDi or if a simpler profile can perform well in different geometric contexts.
*   The highest reward designs exhibit a moderate washout, while aggressive washout leads to poor performance. To further explore twist distributions beyond the current population, we will set `twist_root=0.0` (no twist at the root) and `twist_tip=-2.0` (minimal washout at the tip). This creates a very different load distribution compared to previous designs, especially when combined with the low sweep and symmetric airfoil.
*   Dihedral values near zero or slightly negative show promise. To maintain diversity and test for interactions with the new low-sweep, symmetric profile, we will explore a moderately positive `dihedral` of 10.0 degrees. This pushes towards the positive boundary of dihedral for this novel configuration.
*   The `root_chord_in` values in the database are mostly in the mid-to-high range. To **explore the lower end** of the `root_chord_in` parameter and its effects on overall wing scale and aerodynamic characteristics, especially with a low-sweep design, we will set it to 15.0 inches.
*   For the airfoil thickness, given the symmetric profile and low sweep, we will opt for a moderately thin airfoil, `naca_t=10`. This is a slight deviation from the typical 12-16 seen in higher-performing cambered designs and balances structural needs with potential drag reduction.
