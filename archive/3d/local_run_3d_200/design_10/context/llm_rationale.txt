*   **High Sweep for Enhanced Vortex Lift**: The best design utilized a moderate-high sweep. I will push towards an even higher sweep angle (e.g., 75-78 degrees) to maximize the leading-edge vortex strength, aiming for a significant increase in lift. This is a bold exploration beyond the current top design.
*   **Aggressive Tip Washout for Load Distribution and Vortex Control**: The high reward of Design 1 underscores the importance of washout. I will implement strong washout at the root and an even more aggressive negative twist at the tip. This strategy aims to ensure an optimal spanwise load distribution, delay vortex breakdown, and maintain strong, stable vortices across a wider range of angles of attack.
*   **Novel Front-Loaded Camber (NACA 40xx)**: Design 1 uses NACA 4413. To diversify and explore a different aerodynamic strategy, I will use a NACA 40xx profile (m=4, p=0). This places the maximum camber at the leading edge, a novel approach compared to previous designs. This could potentially generate lift more efficiently at higher angles of attack where vortex lift dominates, or manage pressure distribution uniquely.
*   **Reduced Thickness for Drag Reduction**: Design 1 has 13% thickness. To complement the high sweep and potentially offset any drag increase, I will select a thinner airfoil (e.g., 9% thickness). This should reduce skin friction and potentially improve overall aerodynamic efficiency, pushing the design towards a leaner configuration.
*   **Moderate Root Chord for Balanced Size**: The best designs tend to have a moderate root chord. I will select a root chord slightly smaller than Design 1 (e.g., 28 inches) to keep the overall wing size manageable, balancing lift area with reduced wetted area and potential induced drag.
*   **Slight Negative Dihedral Exploration**: Design 1 has a slight positive dihedral, and extreme values performed poorly. I will experiment with a small negative dihedral (e.g., -3.5 degrees). This explores a different lateral stability characteristic in conjunction with high sweep and aggressive washout, seeking novel high-performance regions.
