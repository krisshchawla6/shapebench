*   **Strategy Focus: Exploration of Underexplored Regions.** My primary goal is to push the boundaries of previously successful parameter ranges to discover new, potentially higher-performing design spaces rather than incremental improvements around known optima.
*   **Lower Sweep Exploration:** The current highest reward designs are clustered around 60-65 degrees sweep. I will push towards the lower end of the `le_sweep` range (e.g., 50-55 degrees). This is significantly different from previous successful designs and will drastically change the wing's planform and potentially its vortex lift characteristics, offering a novel design point.
*   **Increased Root Chord:** To complement the lower sweep, I will opt for a larger `root_chord_in` (e.g., 40-45 inches). This increases wing area and potentially aspect ratio for a given span, which might be beneficial for lower sweep wings by increasing overall lift and reducing induced drag if the load distribution is well managed. It also explores the upper range of the root chord parameter.
*   **Positive Dihedral Exploration:** All successful designs exhibited negative or near-zero `dihedral`. I will explore a significant positive `dihedral` (e.g., 8-12 degrees). This is a completely unexplored region for high-reward designs and could reveal new insights into lateral stability and how it interacts with the lower sweep and twist for lift efficiency.
*   **Retained Best Airfoil & Adjusted Twist:** I will retain the `NACA 44xx` profile (specifically `naca_m=4`, `naca_p=4`) which yielded the highest reward, as it seems to be a robust performer. For `naca_t`, I'll use a slightly thinner profile (e.g., 10-12) to potentially reduce profile drag. For twist, I will keep `twist_root` with strong washout to manage root loading, and `twist_tip` with moderate washout, aiming for efficient spanwise load distribution with the new sweep and dihedral.
