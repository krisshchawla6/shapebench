*   **Exploitation of Optimal Sweep**: Design 1 (65.5 deg) and Design 4 (65.6 deg) both have high sweep angles and are the top performers. This strongly indicates that a sweep in the mid-60s is highly effective for maximizing CL/CDi for these delta wings. The new design will exploit this by maintaining the sweep within this successful range.
*   **Refinement of Washout Twist**: Design 1's `twist_root = -7.1` and `twist_tip = -4.6` provided the highest reward. Other designs using maximal `twist_root = -10.0` performed significantly worse. This suggests that while washout is critical, there's an optimal degree. We will slightly increase the magnitude of washout from Design 1's values (`twist_root` to -8.0, `twist_tip` to -5.0) to explore if a slightly stronger, but still balanced, washout improves efficiency.
*   **Retention of Cambered Airfoil**: Design 1's NACA 4412 was a key factor in its high performance. Designs 2 and 3 also used cambered airfoils (2411, 4411). This strongly suggests that a cambered airfoil is essential for achieving high CL/CDi. We will continue to use `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4` to leverage this benefit.
*   **Targeted Thickness Exploration**: Design 1 used NACA 4412. Designs 2, 3 used 11% thickness, while Design 4 used 16%. We will explore a slightly thicker airfoil than Design 1 but still within a moderate range, using `naca_t=14`, to see its impact on efficiency in combination with the proven camber.
*   **Moderate Scale Adjustment**: Design 1 had a root chord of 34.8 inches. Design 4 had 40.9 inches. We will try a moderate increase in `root_chord_in` to 38.0 inches, aiming to increase overall lift potential without incurring excessive drag penalties, while still maintaining the core aerodynamic efficiency.
*   **Slight Dihedral Adjustment**: Design 1 had a slight anhedral (-1.7 deg). We will slightly increase this anhedral to -2.5 degrees, exploring its nuanced effect on stability and lift distribution in conjunction with the other optimized parameters.
