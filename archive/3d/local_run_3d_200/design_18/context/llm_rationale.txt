*   **Novelty & Exploration (Strategy Focus):** Instead of incremental changes to Design 1, this design will explore a new region of the parameter space, pushing towards higher sweep and aggressive washout combined with a thinner airfoil, based on general aerodynamic principles for delta wings.
*   **High Sweep for Vortex Lift:** High sweep angles are characteristic of delta wings and are essential for generating strong leading-edge vortices, which contribute significantly to lift at higher angles of attack. Previous designs show good performance up to 74 degrees; we will push this further to 78 degrees to explore the maximum benefits.
*   **Aggressive Washout:** To complement the high sweep and manage the load distribution, an aggressive washout profile is selected. Setting `twist_tip` to a very negative value (-9.5 degrees) relative to `twist_root` (-6.0 degrees) aims to reduce induced drag and prevent premature tip stall, which is critical for maintaining efficiency with high sweep.
*   **Cambered and Thinner Airfoil:** Maintaining a cambered airfoil (`naca_m=4, naca_p=4`) is crucial for overall lift. By selecting a thinner airfoil (`naca_t=8`), we aim to reduce profile drag while still benefiting from the camber, potentially improving the CL/CDi ratio, especially at higher speeds or specific flight regimes.
*   **Moderate Root Chord:** A moderate root chord (35.0 inches) provides sufficient wing area to generate lift effectively without making the wing excessively large, balancing overall scale with the high sweep.
*   **Negative Dihedral Exploration:** A moderate negative dihedral (-7.5 degrees) is chosen. While its direct impact on CL/CDi is not strongly correlated in the previous designs, exploring this value in combination with other high-performing characteristics helps cover the design space and might reveal beneficial interactions for stability or performance.
