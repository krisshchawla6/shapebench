1.  **Explore very high leading-edge sweep:** Design 1 has a moderate sweep (~63 deg). Designs 3 and 5, with higher sweeps (~74 deg), performed poorly. This indicates an unexplored region where other parameters might not have been optimized. I will push towards the maximum sweep (e.g., 78 deg) to investigate its true potential for very strong vortex lift, aiming for a different aerodynamic regime.
2.  **Utilize a strongly negative tip twist with moderate root twist:** Design 1 had max root washout (-10 deg) and mild tip washout (-2.83 deg). For a very highly swept wing, I want to explore maximizing washout at the tip (e.g., -9 to -10 deg) to shed vortices cleanly and prevent premature tip stall, while having a more moderate washout at the root (e.g., -5 deg) to ensure sufficient lift generation near the fuselage.
3.  **Incorporate higher camber with a thinner airfoil:** Design 1 used NACA 2411. I will use a NACA 44xx series (4% camber) and a thinner profile (e.g., 9% thickness) to enhance lift coefficient from camber while potentially reducing form drag, which could be critical for high-sweep designs.
4.  **Explore significantly negative dihedral:** Design 1 had slight negative dihedral. I will push this parameter to a more extreme negative value (e.g., -12 deg). While primarily affecting lateral stability, it's worth exploring if strong negative dihedral can interact favorably with the strong vortex flow and high sweep to improve CL/CDi.
5.  **Use a larger root chord for increased wing area:** With a high sweep and potentially thinner airfoil, a larger root chord (e.g., 45 inches) will help maintain a substantial wing area, ensuring sufficient overall lift is generated, especially as the planform becomes narrower with high sweep.
