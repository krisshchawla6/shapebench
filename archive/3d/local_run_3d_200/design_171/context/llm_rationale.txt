*   **Boldly Explore Higher Sweep:** The best designs are clustered around 63-68 degrees of sweep. To ensure diversity and avoid local optima, I will push the `le_sweep` parameter significantly higher, to 72.0 degrees. This aims to maximize vortex lift potential, which is characteristic of high-performance delta wings, and see if a new high-reward region can be found with complementary parameter adjustments.
*   **Adjust Twist for Higher Sweep:** With increased sweep, the wing's inherent stability and flow characteristics change. Instead of the aggressive washout of many prior designs, I will explore a moderate washout at the root (`twist_root=-5.0`) and a mild washout at the tip (`twist_tip=-2.0`). This creates a gentler twist gradient than Design 1, which might be more harmonious with the higher sweep to maintain an efficient lift distribution and prevent premature flow separation.
*   **Leverage Proven Camber/Thickness:** The NACA 4412 airfoil in Design 1 proved very successful. I will retain the `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4` to benefit from the proven lift-generating capability of a cambered airfoil. However, I will slightly increase the thickness to `naca_t=14`. This modest increase could offer structural benefits and potentially broaden the operating envelope without drastically penalizing CL/CDi, especially given the higher sweep.
*   **Explore Positive Dihedral:** Design 1 utilized a slight anhedral. To promote diversity and understand the broader design space, this new design will incorporate a moderate positive dihedral (`dihedral=5.0`). This will investigate how lateral stability characteristics might affect the overall aerodynamic efficiency in combination with the high sweep and new twist profile.
*   **Maintain Moderate Root Chord:** A `root_chord_in` of 40.0 inches keeps the wing in a substantial size category, similar to some higher-ranked designs, ensuring adequate wing area for lift generation while allowing the other parameters to define its unique aerodynamic characteristics.
