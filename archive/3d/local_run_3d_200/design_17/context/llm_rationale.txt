*   **Exploring Moderate Sweep for Efficiency:** The highest reward (Design 2, 20.2761) was achieved with a moderate sweep angle of ~63.8 degrees, significantly lower than Design 1 (~74.4 degrees). This suggests an optimal balance point where sufficient vortex lift is generated without excessive induced drag. I will explore a sweep angle slightly below this to probe the lower end of the effective range for good CL/CDi, aiming for `60.5` degrees.
*   **Targeted Washout for Load Control:** High performing designs consistently show negative twist (washout) at both root and tip. Design 2 had (-8.7, -7.0) and Design 1 had (-5.3, -10.0). I will implement significant tip washout (`-9.5` degrees) to maintain desirable stall characteristics and load distribution, but couple it with a less aggressive root twist (`-6.5` degrees) to ensure sufficient lift generation over the inner wing sections.
*   **Enhanced Lift with Cambered Airfoil:** The poor performance of Design 5 (NACA 0011, reward -1.1039) clearly indicates the need for camber. The NACA 44xx series has consistently appeared in top designs (Design 2, 3). I will utilize a `NACA 44xx` profile for its proven lift-generating capabilities, specifying `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4`.
*   **Optimizing Airfoil Thickness:** Successful designs show a range of thicknesses (9, 13, 15). To balance structural integrity and minimize form drag, I will select a moderate thickness of `12%` (`naca_t=12`), which is a good compromise and centrally located within the successful designs.
*   **Increased Scale for Potential Lift:** Designs 2 and 3, which performed well, had larger root chords (31.8 and 39.9 inches, respectively). A larger wing generally allows for higher overall lift. I will experiment with a larger `root_chord_in` of `42.0` inches to explore if scaling up with optimized aerodynamics leads to higher CL/CDi.
*   **Neutral Dihedral for Stability/Drag Balance:** The top design (Design 2) had a small positive dihedral (1.06 degrees). Extreme positive or negative dihedral can negatively impact performance or stability. I will choose a near-zero, slightly positive dihedral (`0.5` degrees) to maintain a neutral-to-slightly-stable lateral characteristic without introducing significant induced drag from an overly aggressive setting.
