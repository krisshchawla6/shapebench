*   **Aggressive Twist Profile**: High-reward designs consistently show significant negative `twist_root` and `twist_tip`, with `twist_root` being more negative than `twist_tip`. This implies a specific angle of attack distribution favoring the tip or managing root stall. I will push this trend further by setting `twist_root` to its maximum negative value (-10.0 deg) and `twist_tip` to a less negative value (-2.0 deg) to create a pronounced twist differential.
*   **Optimized Sweep for Vortex Lift**: Design 1 (reward 4016) has moderate sweep (~65 deg), while Design 4 (reward 48) has very high sweep (~79 deg). I will explore a sweep in the upper-moderate range (~72.0 deg). This is higher than Design 1, aiming to potentially enhance vortex lift, but not as extreme as Design 4, attempting to avoid excessive induced drag.
*   **Exploration with Symmetric Airfoil (NACA 00xx)**: Most top designs use cambered NACA profiles (`44xx`, `24xx`). I want to explore a novel strategy by using a symmetric airfoil (`NACA 00xx`). This will isolate the effect of the geometric twist on lift distribution and induced drag, removing the contribution of aerodynamic camber. This is a bold exploration to see if a combination of high sweep and aggressive twist can compensate for the lack of camber.
*   **Increased Thickness with Symmetric Airfoil**: To provide some structural depth and potentially reduce profile drag (if lift is generated effectively by twist), I'll choose a thicker symmetric airfoil (NACA 0018). This provides structural volume and allows comparison against the thinner cambered airfoils used previously.
*   **Larger Root Chord**: To explore scale effects and potential for higher overall lift (and thus potentially higher CL/CDi if drag scales less severely), I will choose a root chord in the upper range (48.0 inches). This ensures a substantial wing while allowing the new sweep and twist parameters to operate.
*   **Moderate Positive Dihedral**: Previous high-reward designs show slight negative dihedral. I will explore a moderate positive dihedral (7.5 degrees) to investigate its impact on lateral stability while maintaining high aerodynamic performance, exploring a different aspect of the design space.
