*   **Hybridize with Design 1 as the Core:** Design 1 is the most successful design. We will use its general structure and parameter ranges as a baseline, blending in exploratory aspects inspired by other designs or general aerodynamic principles.
*   **Explore Slightly Higher Sweep for Enhanced Vortex Lift:** Design 1 had a sweep of 65.5 degrees. To enhance vortex lift further, we will slightly increase the leading-edge sweep to 68.0 degrees, staying within the optimal range observed for delta wings.
*   **Maintain Balanced Washout but Fine-Tune Magnitude:** Design 1's twist distribution (-7.1 root, -4.6 tip) yielded excellent results. We will maintain a similar differential washout (root more negative than tip) but slightly reduce the overall magnitude of washout, aiming for a twist_root of -6.5 degrees and twist_tip of -3.8 degrees. This explores if a slightly less aggressive, but still balanced, twist distribution can improve the lift-to-drag ratio further.
*   **Increase Root Chord for Higher Aspect Ratio Effect:** Design 1 used 34.8 inches. To slightly increase the effective wing area and potentially improve the aspect ratio effect without changing the taper ratio (which is fixed), we will increase the root chord to 38.0 inches. This aims for more lift potential.
*   **Investigate Thinner Airfoil for Drag Reduction:** Design 1 used NACA 4412. Maintaining the successful 4% camber at 40% chord (NACA 44), we will reduce the thickness to 10% (NACA 4410). This aims to reduce parasite drag, which could improve CL/CDi if the lift generation isn't significantly compromised.
*   **Explore Slight Positive Dihedral:** Design 1 and 2 both used slight anhedral. To diversify and explore the stability landscape, we will set a small positive dihedral of 1.5 degrees. This could influence roll stability characteristics and potentially slightly alter the induced drag, testing the boundaries of beneficial dihedral.
