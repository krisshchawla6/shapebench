*   **Explore a lower sweep angle (55.0 deg)**: The top design (Design 1) is at 63 degrees. Many lower-reward designs are at higher sweeps (73-74 degrees). This suggests an optimum around 60-65 degrees, but I want to push towards the lower end of the range (45-80 deg) to explore a different aerodynamic regime where vortex lift might be less dominant but induced drag could be proportionally lower or generated differently, potentially finding a new optimum for CL/CDi.
*   **Introduce a novel twist distribution (twist_root = -6.0, twist_tip = -8.0)**: Design 1 had a very aggressive root washout. This new design maintains significant washout, but applies *more* washout at the tip than the root. This is a novel approach compared to the existing designs, aiming to load the inner wing less aggressively while potentially ensuring the tip is well-behaved and doesn't stall prematurely, which could improve CL/CDi.
*   **Utilize a higher camber (NACA 40XX)**: Design 1 (NACA 2411) and other designs with camber showed better rewards than designs without (NACA 00XX). I will use NACA 40XX (4% max camber at 0% chord, i.e., leading edge, which is novel) and a moderate thickness (14). This combination should contribute significantly to lift generation and explore a different camber distribution.
*   **Increase root chord (45.0 in) for scaling exploration**: Design 1 has a root chord of 33.2 inches. A larger root chord, while keeping other parameters in proportion, allows for exploration of increased wing area and overall scale, potentially leading to higher absolute lift values without necessarily sacrificing efficiency if the chosen aerodynamic parameters are effective.
*   **Implement a moderate positive dihedral (8.0 deg)**: Design 1 has slight anhedral. I want to test a distinctly positive dihedral to investigate its impact on lateral stability and induced drag characteristics in combination with the chosen sweep and twist, offering a different stability profile.
