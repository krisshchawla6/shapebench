*   **Explore Moderate-High Sweep**: Design 1's `le_sweep=65.5` was excellent. I will explore a slightly higher sweep, `68.5` degrees, which is still within the moderate range but slightly increases the leading-edge contribution to vortex lift, potentially leading to higher lift coefficients, while not going to extreme values like `75+` that might introduce excessive induced drag.
*   **Aggressive Washout for Load Control**: Design 1 effectively used washout (`twist_root=-7.1`, `twist_tip=-4.6`). I will explore a more aggressive washout, setting `twist_root` to `-9.5` and `twist_tip` to `-6.0`. This aims to further improve the lift distribution, ensuring the root doesn't stall prematurely and reducing induced drag by minimizing tip loading.
*   **Optimized Camber Profile**: The `NACA 4412` profile of Design 1 was highly successful. I will retain `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4` to leverage this proven camber effectiveness, providing good camber lift.

I will select a thinner airfoil, `naca_t=10`, to potentially lower profile drag, assuming the structural and lift generation benefits of the thicker airfoil are not overwhelmingly critical here.

This increases the overall wing area slightly, potentially boosting total lift without drastically altering the aspect ratio given the chosen sweep.
