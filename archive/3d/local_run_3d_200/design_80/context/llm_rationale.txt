Past high-sweep designs (e.g., Design 4) failed due to symmetric airfoils, suggesting the interaction is key.
*   **Utilize significant camber (NACA m=2, p=4)**: The database clearly shows that cambered airfoils (NACA 44xx, 24xx) are essential for high CL/CDi in delta wings. I will use NACA 24xx to provide active lift generation, aiming for efficient cruise performance by reducing the required angle of attack.
*   **Implement strong root washout and moderate tip washout**: Both high-reward designs feature substantial negative twist. I will apply a strong negative twist at the root (closer to -10 degrees) to unload the inboard section, and a moderate negative twist at the tip (around -5 degrees) to maintain good flow attachment, manage vortex formation, and distribute lift efficiently, thus minimizing induced drag.

The aim is to reduce profile drag, assuming the reduced thickness doesn't negatively impact vortex stability or flow separation too much, especially at higher sweep where the effective chord is longer.
*   **Maintain a mid-range root chord and slight anhedral**: The root chord of 30-35 inches for top performers suggests this range is effective for the current scale. I'll pick a value within this. A small negative dihedral is also consistent with the top-performing designs, and I'll keep it consistent to isolate the effects of other primary parameters.
