*   **Prioritize Cambered Airfoils:** The stark difference in rewards between designs with `naca_m=0` (symmetric) and `naca_m>0` (cambered) unequivocally demonstrates that camber is essential for high CL/CDi. Therefore, this new design will use `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4` to maximize inherent lift.
*   **Refine Washout for Lift Distribution:** Design 1's success with moderate, differential washout (more at root) indicates a good strategy for managing vortex lift and induced drag. This design will explore a similar washout pattern, with significant negative twist at the root and slightly less at the tip, aiming for an efficient load distribution.
*   **Explore Higher Sweep for Vortex Lift:** Design 1 had a sweep of 65.5 degrees. To explore the boundaries of effective vortex lift generation, this new design will try a slightly higher sweep angle (e.g., around 70 degrees), hypothesizing that a stronger, more stable vortex may form, potentially boosting lift-to-drag.
*   **Optimize Airfoil Thickness:** Design 1's NACA 4412 was successful. Design 5 with NACA 2418 was not. A thinner airfoil (`naca_t=10`) combined with the `NACA 44` profile will be explored, aiming to reduce profile drag while maintaining the benefits of camber, potentially improving the CL/CDi ratio further.
*   **Moderate Root Chord:** A moderately increased root chord compared to Design 1 will be used (e.g., 38 inches) to slightly increase the wing area, which could translate to more lift without a disproportionate increase in induced drag, given the other optimized parameters.
