*   **Embrace Lower Sweep (Exploration Strategy)**: The current optimal designs reside in the 63-65 degree sweep range. To diversify the population and explore underrepresented regions, I will push `le_sweep` significantly lower, towards the minimum boundary at **52.0 degrees**. This will challenge the existing understanding of vortex lift characteristics for very acute delta wings and may uncover a different balance of vortex strength and induced drag.
*   **Maximize `root_chord_in`**: While small root chords led to poor performance, the maximum `root_chord_in` (up to 50 inches) has not been fully explored with favorable aerodynamic parameters. I will set `root_chord_in` to **48.0 inches** to investigate the effect of a large wing area in combination with the lower sweep and novel twist distribution.
*   **Explore Positive `twist_root`**: All previous high-reward designs utilized negative `twist_root`. To explore an untried configuration, I will set `twist_root` to **5.0 degrees** (wash-in). This radical change aims to redistribute lift towards the root, which might be beneficial with a lower sweep, and test the limits of what constitutes an effective twist profile.
*   **Moderate `twist_tip` and `dihedral`**: Keeping `twist_tip` at a moderate negative value of **-5.0 degrees** ensures some washout at the tip, which is generally beneficial for tip stall characteristics and stability. For `dihedral`, I will use a moderately positive value of **8.0 degrees**. This is an unexplored region for successful designs, as the best designs had slightly negative dihedral, and high positive dihedral correlated with poor performance. This allows exploration of lateral stability implications with the new sweep and twist.
*   **Cambered, Thinner Airfoil**: Symmetric airfoils (NACA 00xx) performed poorly in previous designs. I will use a cambered airfoil with **`naca_m=2`** (less camber than the top design's `m=4`) and `naca_p=4` to provide some lift generation from the airfoil shape. For thickness, I will opt for a thinner profile, **`naca_t=8`**, which is towards the lower end of the range, aiming to minimize profile drag given the potentially increased wetted area from the larger root chord and lower sweep.
