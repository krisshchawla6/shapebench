Our strategy is **Exploration**, focusing on under-explored regions of the parameter space to find novel, high-performing designs.

*   **Explore lower sweep angles (e.g., 45-60 degrees):** The current top designs are clustered in the mid-60s sweep. Pushing to a significantly lower sweep (e.g., 50 degrees) drastically changes the aspect ratio and vortex lift characteristics. This region is largely unexplored by high-reward designs. We need to understand how other parameters interact with this different sweep regime.
*   **Test a truly symmetrical airfoil (NACA 00XX):** Most high-performing designs leverage camber (NACA 44XX, 24XX). By setting `naca_m=0` and `naca_p=0`, we remove airfoil camber as a lift-generating mechanism, forcing the design to rely entirely on sweep-induced vortex lift and twist for lift and load distribution. This is a critical unexplored region for high reward and will provide insights into the pure aerodynamic shape contribution.
*   **Combine extreme washout with symmetrical airfoil:** To compensate for the lack of camber and potentially weaker vortex lift at lower sweep, we will maintain aggressive washout (negative twist) at both root and tip, similar to what was effective in Design 1. This ensures good load distribution and potentially delays flow separation. Specifically, a large negative `twist_root` and a significant `twist_tip` are chosen.
*   **Investigate strong positive dihedral:** All high-reward designs in the provided list have negative or near-zero dihedral. Exploring a significant positive dihedral (e.g., +12 degrees) is a departure from current trends and could uncover novel stability or aerodynamic interactions, particularly with lower sweep and a symmetrical airfoil.
*   **Utilize a thicker profile for robustness/strength:** With no camber and lower sweep, exploring a higher thickness (e.g., 18% chord) allows for a robust structural design and might interact differently with vortex formation at lower sweep angles compared to thinner profiles. This is also an under-explored region for high-reward designs.
