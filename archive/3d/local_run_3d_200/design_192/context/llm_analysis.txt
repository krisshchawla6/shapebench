Assuming a typical Cp distribution for such a design, we'd expect:
*   **Good Vortex Lift:** The 65.5-degree sweep is well within the range for generating stable leading-edge vortices, contributing significantly to lift. The Cp distribution would likely show strong suction peaks near the leading edge, especially towards the root, indicative of these vortices.
*   **Effective Load Distribution:** The washout (negative twist at root and tip) helps to distribute the lift more evenly across the span, preventing premature tip stall and reducing induced drag. This is crucial for delta wings operating at higher angles of attack. The Cp distribution would likely show a more benign pressure recovery towards the trailing edge compared to a wing without twist, and a relatively uniform spanwise loading.
*   **Moderate Camber:** The NACA 4412 profile adds inherent lift, allowing the wing to achieve a target lift coefficient at a lower angle of attack, which can improve efficiency.

**Key Issues/Opportunities (for the population's exploration):**
1.  **Sweep Angle Extremes:** Most high-reward designs cluster around moderate sweep (63-68 degrees). The lower (45-60) and higher (70-80) ends of the sweep range are under-explored for high rewards. 2.  **Camber Profile Diversity:** NACA 44XX profiles are dominant among the listed designs. Symmetrical airfoils (NACA 00XX, i.e., m=0, p=0) and profiles with different camber distributions (e.g., m=2, p=4) are not well-represented in top-performing designs. Exploring these could reveal new lift generation strategies.
3.  **Dihedral Impact:** Dihedral values are mostly negative or near zero. The full range of positive dihedral (+10 to +15) has not yielded a high-rewarding design yet and could interact uniquely with other parameters.
