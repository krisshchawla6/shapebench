The representative design (Design 1) features a highly swept delta wing (74.26 degrees) with a relatively small root chord (23.68 inches). It utilizes a NACA 2007 airfoil (2% max camber at 0% chord, 7% thickness) and exhibits significant anhedral (-8.90 degrees). Both the root and tip are "washed in" (negative twist angles), with the root more pronounced (-10.0 degrees) than the tip (-4.14 degrees). The Cp distribution image shows strong leading-edge suction, indicative of significant vortex lift. However, despite the vortex lift, Design 1's reward (25.88) is very low compared to the top-performing design (Design 2, reward 338.50), suggesting it's not efficient.

Issues observed:
*   The very high sweep might lead to increased induced drag, overshadowing the benefits of vortex lift.
*   The NACA 2007 profile with `p=0` (camber at the leading edge) is an aggressive design that might contribute to separation or specific high-drag characteristics not conducive to overall efficiency.
*   The combination of strong wash-in at the root and less at the tip creates a peculiar spanwise load distribution that might be suboptimal for CL/CDi.

Key design changes needed for population improvement:
1.  **Moderate Sweep Exploration:** Investigate the performance around the 60-65 degree sweep range, as seen in the top-performing Design 2.
2.  **Optimized Twist Distribution:** Experiment with positive twist at the tip (washout) or a more balanced wash-in/wash-out distribution to improve load sharing and reduce induced drag.
3.  **Camber and Thickness Variation:** Explore NACA profiles with `p=4` (camber aft of leading edge) as seen in high-ranking designs, potentially with higher `m` (camber) and `t` (thickness) values.
4.  **Root Chord Scaling:** Systematically vary the root chord to understand its impact on total lift and drag at different wing scales.
