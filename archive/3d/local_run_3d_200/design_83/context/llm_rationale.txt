*   **Higher `le_sweep` for enhanced vortex lift:** The top design (D1) had `le_sweep=65.5`. To explore a bolder region for potentially higher vortex lift without reaching the severely underperforming extreme of 80 degrees, we will set `le_sweep = 72.0`. This tests if a higher sweep can yield better results when combined with optimized parameters.
*   **Optimized washout for high sweep:** For a higher sweep wing, precise twist management is crucial to control vortex formation and reduce induced drag. We will use a significant but not maximal root washout (`twist_root = -8.5`) and a moderately strong tip washout (`twist_tip = -5.0`), maintaining the critical differential washout observed in top designs.
*   **Thinner, moderately cambered airfoil (`NACA 2409`):** Design 2 (`NACA 2411`) had a decent reward. We will adopt `naca_m = 2` and `naca_p = 4` to ensure camber-generated lift, but pair it with a thinner `naca_t = 9`. This aims to reduce profile drag, which becomes more critical at higher sweeps, while still providing effective lift.
*   **Neutral dihedral for stability exploration:** The top designs showed slight anhedral. To explore a different stability characteristic and simplify the geometry, we will set `dihedral = 0.0`. This will test if a perfectly flat wing can achieve high performance when other parameters are well-optimized.
*   **Moderate `root_chord_in`:** The sweet spot for `root_chord_in` appears to be around 33-35 inches. We will choose `root_chord_in = 32.0` inches, slightly smaller than D1, to align with the higher sweep and thinner airfoil, potentially optimizing overall wing area and wetted surface for improved CL/CDi.
