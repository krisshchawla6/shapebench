*   **Embrace High Sweep:** The top two designs (1 and 3) feature high sweep angles (63.8 and 74.4 degrees). This new design will explore a high sweep of `70.0` degrees, balancing the successful `le_sweep` of Design 1 with the higher sweep of Design 3 to investigate its potential with a larger root chord.
*   **Optimize Twist Washout:** All successful designs utilize significant negative twist (washout). We will maintain a strong washout, setting `twist_root` to `-7.5` and `twist_tip` to `-9.0`. This maintains a beneficial load distribution, preventing premature tip stall, and exploring a slightly stronger tip washout than Design 1 but less extreme than Design 3.
*   **Utilize Proven Camber Profile:** Designs with `NACA 44xx` (like Design 1) or `NACA 40xx` (like Design 3) performed well. We will stick with `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4` (NACA 44xx profile) as seen in the best performing design, capitalizing on its demonstrated ability to generate lift efficiently.
*   **Moderate Root Chord for High Sweep:** Design 1 had a `root_chord_in` of ~31.8, and Design 3 (higher sweep) had ~23.7. To complement the `70.0` degree sweep, we will try a larger `root_chord_in` of `38.0`. This explores if increased overall planform area at high sweep can further boost performance by providing more surface for vortex interaction.
*   **Maintain Neutral Dihedral:** The top performing design had near-zero dihedral (`1.0`). We will set `dihedral` to `0.5`, reinforcing the observation that near-neutral dihedral is preferable for maximizing CL/CDi.
*   **Moderate Thickness:** The top designs had `naca_t` between 9 and 13. We'll set `naca_t` to `11`, a moderate value that balances structural requirements with minimizing form drag.
