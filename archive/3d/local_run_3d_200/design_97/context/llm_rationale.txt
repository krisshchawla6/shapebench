The goal is to generate a diverse, novel design. Design 1 has a clear winning strategy. I will explore parameters that are significantly different from Design 1, but with a logical aerodynamic hypothesis, to push the boundaries of the search space.

*   **Explore Higher Sweep with Camber**: Design 1 has moderate sweep (65.5). Design 5 at 74.2 performed poorly, possibly due to lack of camber and aggressive root twist. I will choose a high, but not extreme, sweep angle (e.g., 70.0 degrees) to enhance vortex lift, combined with a cambered airfoil to ensure adequate lift generation.
*   **Novel Twist Distribution (Positive Root, Washout Tip)**: Many lower-reward designs use aggressive negative root twist. Design 1's twist is moderate. I will try a novel twist configuration with a *slight positive twist* at the root (`twist_root=1.0`) and a *moderate washout* at the tip (`twist_tip=-5.0`). This aims to potentially shift the lift distribution more inboard, modify the vortex initiation, and explore a different approach to spanwise load management, contrasting sharply with previous designs.
*   **Less Aggressive Camber but Still Cambered (`NACA 2410`)**: Design 1 used `NACA 4412`. To achieve diversity while still ensuring lift with higher sweep, I will use `NACA 2410`. This means `m=2` (2% max camber), `p=4` (max camber at 40% chord), and `t=10` (10% thickness). This offers less camber than Design 1, potentially reducing pitching moments, but still provides a beneficial airfoil shape, and is slightly thinner, which might reduce profile drag at higher speeds or angles of attack associated with higher sweep.
*   **Smaller Root Chord**: Design 1 has a root chord of 34.7 inches. To explore the "slender" wing regime that might interact well with higher sweep, I will opt for a significantly smaller root chord (e.g., 22.0 inches). This will result in a wing with a higher effective aspect ratio for its given planform area (compared to a larger chord with same sweep), which might offer induced drag benefits if the lift distribution is well-managed.
*   **Significant Positive Dihedral**: Design 1 had a slight anhedral. I will explore a significant positive dihedral (`10.0` degrees). This is a bold exploration to see its impact on lateral stability and how it might influence the overall lift and induced drag, as dihedral can affect spanwise load distribution and vortex structures, especially on a delta wing.
