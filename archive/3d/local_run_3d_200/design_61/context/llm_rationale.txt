*   The current top design (Design 1) leveraged moderate sweep and a cambered NACA 4412 airfoil with significant washout. My new design will push the sweep further to explore if higher vortex lift can be harnessed effectively.
*   Previous high-sweep designs (5, 6) performed poorly, likely due to symmetric airfoils (Design 5) or suboptimal twist/thickness (Design 6). I will pair a higher sweep with strong camber (`NACA 44xx`) and aggressive washout to attempt to make high sweep work.
*   Cambered airfoils, specifically `NACA 44xx`, have shown the best results. I will stick with the `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4` combination to maximize lift benefit.
*   To complement higher sweep and strong camber, I will use a thinner airfoil (`naca_t=10`) than Design 1 (`naca_t=12`) to reduce drag and potentially improve the CL/CDi ratio, pushing into the lower end of the thickness range that has not been explored with top-performing camber types.
*   Aggressive washout is a consistent feature of high-reward designs. I will maximize `twist_root` to -10.0 and use a less negative `twist_tip` to ensure a strong twist differential and effective load management across the span.
*   Dihedral has generally been negative or neutral in successful designs. I will explore a modestly positive `dihedral` (e.g., 3.0 degrees) as a novel variation, to see its impact on overall performance and explore the design space.
