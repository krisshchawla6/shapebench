*   **Explore Cambered Airfoil (NACA 24xx):** The most significant unexplored design space is the use of camber. Both successful and unsuccessful previous designs were uncambered (NACA 00xx). Introducing a moderate camber (naca_m=2, naca_p=4) will allow us to assess how camber affects lift generation and induced drag, potentially leading to higher CL/CDi.
*   **High Leading Edge Sweep (72.0 deg):** The previous designs clustered around 58-61 degrees. Pushing the sweep angle to the higher end of the range (70-80 degrees) will explore a regime where vortex lift generation is typically more pronounced. This, combined with camber, might unlock high-performance configurations for high-aspect-ratio delta wings.
*   **Moderate Root Chord (38.0 inches):** This value is chosen to be distinct from the previous designs (34.89 and 31.47 inches) but still within a practical range. It allows for exploration of geometric scaling without drastically altering the fundamental aerodynamic behavior we're trying to investigate with sweep and camber.
*   **Controlled Twist Distribution (twist_root=-1.0, twist_tip=-5.0):** Design 1's success with significant washout suggests this is generally good. Design 2's failure with positive root twist is noted. This design proposes a slight negative twist at the root, coupled with a moderate washout at the tip, which is less aggressive than Design 1 but aims to manage tip loading effectively while allowing the new high-sweep and cambered profile to dominate.
*   **Explore Positive Dihedral (7.5 deg):** Previous designs either had near-zero or negative dihedral. Exploring a moderate positive dihedral is essential to understand its impact on lateral stability characteristics and overall aerodynamic efficiency in combination with the new high-sweep and cambered wing.
*   **Increased Thickness with Camber (NACA 2418):** To complement the introduction of camber and high sweep, a slightly thicker airfoil (NACA 2418) compared to the previous 11% and 15% designs will be used. This explores the robustness and potential benefits of a thicker, more robust wing structure, especially when combined with camber which might allow for higher lift coefficients.
