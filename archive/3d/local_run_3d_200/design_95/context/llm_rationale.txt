The "Novel Design Strategy" dictates focusing on general trends and aerodynamic first principles, rather than incremental modifications. I will aim for a design that explores a less common combination of parameters to push the boundaries of performance.

*   **Aggressively High Sweep (76.0 deg):** The current top design has 65.5 degrees of sweep. I will push the `le_sweep` significantly higher, towards the upper limits of the range. This is a bold move to maximize the potential for stable vortex lift at higher angles of attack, which is a defining characteristic of high-performance delta wings, accepting that this might also increase induced drag if not managed properly.
*   **Maximized Washout Distribution (twist_root=-10.0, twist_tip=-8.0):** To counteract the increased induced drag and manage the lift distribution effectively for such a high sweep, I will implement severe negative twist (washout). Maxing out `twist_root` and setting `twist_tip` to a strong negative value aims to reduce root loading and prevent premature tip stall, which is crucial for maintaining efficient lift.
*   **Forward-Biased Camber (NACA 4010):** Instead of the typical `naca_p=4` seen in Design 1, I will select `naca_m=4` but set `naca_p=0`. This is an unusual NACA configuration (max camber at 0% chord) but allowed by the parameter space. It aims to explore the effect of a highly curved leading edge on vortex generation and attachment at extremely high sweep, potentially enhancing leading-edge suction and vortex stability.
*   **Reduced Thickness (NACA t=10):** To minimize profile drag, which becomes more critical at higher speeds, I will opt for a thinner airfoil section (`naca_t=10`) compared to Design 1's 12. This is a trade-off that should improve overall efficiency, especially if the high sweep effectively manages lift.
*   **Mid-Range Root Chord (35.0 inches):** I will keep the `root_chord_in` at a moderate value to ensure the wing maintains a practical scale. This parameter generally scales the design without drastically changing its fundamental aerodynamic character compared to sweep, twist, or airfoil shape.
*   **Neutral Dihedral (0.0 deg):** Setting `dihedral` to zero ensures that any observed performance changes are primarily attributable to the aggressive changes in sweep, twist, and airfoil, allowing for a clearer understanding of their isolated effects on CL/CDi.
