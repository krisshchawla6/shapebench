*   **Explore very high sweep (e.g., ~75.0 degrees)**: The existing designs, including the highest-reward one, largely cluster in the 63-68 degree sweep range. Pushing to a much higher sweep angle will explore a region where leading-edge vortex formation is even more dominant and stable, potentially leading to higher lift coefficients at higher angles of attack with a different balance of induced drag. This tests the hypothesis that higher sweep can further enhance vortex lift benefits.
*   **Introduce a completely symmetric airfoil (NACA 00xx)**: No previous design has explored `naca_m = 0`. This is a significant unexplored region of the design space. For a highly swept delta wing, where vortex lift contributes significantly, a symmetric airfoil might reduce profile drag and pitching moments compared to cambered airfoils, potentially improving overall efficiency, especially if the high sweep effectively generates the necessary lift without inherent camber.
*   **Implement a less aggressive twist distribution (e.g., slightly positive root, moderate negative tip)**: Many low-reward designs utilized extreme negative twist at both root and tip. Design 1, the top performer, had moderate negative twists. This new design will explore a less severe washout, with a slightly positive twist at the root and a moderate negative twist at the tip, aiming to optimize load distribution in conjunction with high sweep and a symmetric airfoil without excessively penalizing lift.
*   **Utilize a thinner airfoil (NACA 008)**: The thickness range of 6-10% has been largely unexplored. In combination with a highly swept, symmetric wing, a thinner airfoil could significantly reduce overall profile drag, leading to a higher CL/CDi. This design will test the efficacy of combining minimal thickness with other aggressive exploration parameters.
*   **Explore higher positive dihedral (~10.0 degrees)**: While dihedral's impact on CL/CDi is secondary to stability, the range of highly positive dihedral values has not been thoroughly explored. Applying a significant positive dihedral to this novel, highly swept, symmetric design allows us to observe its interaction with the new aerodynamic characteristics and uncover any unforeseen benefits or penalties.
