*   **Novel Sweep Exploration (le_sweep):** The top design (D1) had 63.8 degrees. I will push slightly higher into the moderate-to-high sweep range, targeting `le_sweep=68.0`. This aims to enhance vortex lift without reaching the likely drag-increasing extremes of 74+ degrees seen in lower-performing designs (D2, D3, D4).
*   **Consistent Washout (twist_root, twist_tip):** Washout is a clear indicator of successful designs. I will implement a significant, but not maxed-out, washout at both root and tip, ensuring the tip is slightly more washed out than the root. This is `twist_root=-7.5` and `twist_tip=-9.0`. This strategy aims for controlled load distribution and vortex shedding.
*   **Slight Anhedral (dihedral):** Based on the strong negative correlation between high dihedral values and reward, I will maintain a slight anhedral. Setting `dihedral=-2.5` aims for lateral stability characteristics favorable to delta wings without hindering performance.
*   **Novel Airfoil Profile (naca_m, naca_p, naca_t):** To foster diversity and explore a new region, I will move away from the `NACA 4xxx` series prevalent in the top designs. I will explore `naca_m=2` (2% max camber), `naca_p=4` (max camber at 40% chord), and `naca_t=12` (12% thickness). This `NACA 2412` airfoil represents a less cambered profile than seen in high-reward designs, potentially reducing profile drag while still offering some aerodynamic shaping, relying more heavily on vortex lift.
*   **Medium-Large Root Chord (root_chord_in):** A larger root chord generally means a larger wing area, which can translate to more lift. I will choose `root_chord_in=40.0` to ensure a substantial lifting surface, slightly larger than the best performer D1 (31.8in) but within a reasonable range, to see if increased scale can improve performance with the other optimized parameters.
