*   **Effective Lift Distribution**: The negative twist (washout) at both the root (-7.1 deg) and tip (-4.6 deg) likely leads to a well-distributed lift profile across the span, effectively mitigating tip stall and reducing induced drag compared to designs with poor twist.
*   **Beneficial Vortex Lift**: The moderate leading-edge sweep (65.5 deg) is indicative of strong, stable leading-edge vortices contributing significantly to lift, especially at higher angles of attack, a key characteristic for delta wings.
*   **Lift from Camber**: The NACA 4412 airfoil (4% max camber at 40% chord) provides inherent aerodynamic lift at zero angle of attack and improves lift at operational angles, contributing directly to a higher CL.
*   **Controlled Flow**: The high CL/CDi suggests minimal flow separation or highly controlled vortex flow, with no large areas of high drag-inducing turbulence shown by the Cp distribution.
