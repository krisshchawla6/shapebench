Our strategy is **Exploration Strategy**: to review the design database to identify UNDEREXPLORED regions of the parameter space and push towards boundary regions or distinct parameter combinations.

*   **Radical Airfoil Change (Symmetric):** The most consistently present and least varied parameter has been the NACA airfoil type, always showing camber (m=2 or 4, p=4). To boldly explore, I will select a symmetric airfoil (NACA m=0, p=0), which has not been tested in any previous design. This eliminates the inherent lift from camber and forces the design to rely purely on angle of attack and vortex lift, which could lead to novel aerodynamic characteristics or reveal if camber is always necessary for high CL/CDi.
*   **Aggressive High Sweep:** To complement the lack of camber and potentially maximize vortex lift, I will push the leading-edge sweep to a much higher value, 75.0 degrees. This is significantly higher than the mid-60s range seen in all previous designs, including the high-reward ones, exploring the upper bound of the sweep parameter space.
*   **Extreme Thinness:** To minimize profile drag, which might become more critical with a symmetric airfoil relying on higher angles of attack, I will select the thinnest possible airfoil (NACA t=6). This explores the extreme lower end of the thickness range, far from the current designs (10-16%).
*   **Near-Neutral Tip Twist with Moderate Root Washout:** With a symmetric airfoil and high sweep, the ideal twist distribution might differ. I will explore a moderate root washout (-5.0 deg) to manage root loading, but with minimal tip washout (-0.5 deg), essentially making the tip near-neutral. This allows for more aggressive tip loading and tests a twist configuration less aggressive than previous designs.
*   **Large Root Chord Exploration:** To provide substantial wing area with a high sweep and thin airfoil, and to explore the upper limit of the size parameter, I will set the root chord to 48.0 inches. Most previous designs were in the 30-41 inch range, so this pushes towards a larger overall scale.
*   **Slight Anhedral:** Dihedral has shown variation, but mostly negative or small positive. I will stick with a slight anhedral (-2.5 deg) as it has been common in successful designs, avoiding extremes while focusing exploration on other parameters.
