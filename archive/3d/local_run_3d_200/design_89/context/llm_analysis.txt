The Cp distribution from the image clearly shows intense low-pressure regions along the leading edges of the wing's upper surface, indicative of strong and well-formed leading-edge vortices. These vortices are crucial for generating high lift efficiently on delta wings. The pressure recovery towards the trailing edge appears smooth, suggesting good flow attachment and minimal separation.

The deeper washout at the root, progressively reducing towards the tip, helps to distribute lift more evenly, delay tip stall, and effectively manage induced drag. The NACA 4412 airfoil provides beneficial camber and a suitable thickness for structural integrity without excessive drag. The small anhedral (-1.7 degrees) contributes to stable roll characteristics.
