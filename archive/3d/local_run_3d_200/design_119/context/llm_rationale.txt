The goal is to explore the design space creatively and boldly, not just incrementally tweak the top design. The previous top design (Design 1) shows outstanding performance with moderate sweep, specific washout, and a cambered airfoil. My reasoning for the new design is:

*   **Bold Exploration of Symmetric Airfoil**: A significant departure from the top designs is to explore a `NACA 00xx` profile (`naca_m=0, naca_p=0`). This removes any inherent airfoil camber, forcing the wing to rely purely on vortex lift, sweep, and twist for performance. This is a bold test of the underlying aerodynamic principles for delta wings.
*   **Moderate Sweep for Vortex Lift**: Set `le_sweep` to 60.0 degrees. This is slightly lower than the current top performer (65.5 degrees) but still well within the range for strong vortex lift. It explores a different part of the sweep-performance curve, potentially reducing induced drag associated with higher sweep while still maintaining good vortex generation.
*   **Inverted Washout Gradient**: Design 1 had `twist_root` more negative than `twist_tip`. This new design will explore the opposite: `twist_tip` more negative than `twist_root` (e.g., `twist_root=-6.0`, `twist_tip=-9.0`). This tests how shifting more washout to the tip affects spanwise load distribution and vortex behavior when combined with a symmetric airfoil.
*   **Exploring Positive Dihedral**: All current top designs have negative dihedral. To promote diversity and understand its impact, I will specify a moderate positive dihedral (`dihedral=5.0`). This parameter influences lateral stability and may have subtle effects on flow structure over the wing.
*   **Moderate Root Chord**: A `root_chord_in` of 38.0 inches is chosen to be robust, slightly larger than Design 1, providing a solid baseline size for this new geometric and aerodynamic strategy.
*   **Moderate Thickness**: A `naca_t` of 10% is chosen. This is a common and efficient thickness for a symmetric airfoil, not overly thick to incur excessive wave drag, nor too thin to be structurally challenging or suffer low Reynolds number effects.
