1.  **Diversify Sweep Exploration:** The top-performing designs (Design 1 & 2) lean towards medium-to-high sweep (63-74 deg). To encourage diversity and find potentially new optimal regions, this design will explore the lower end of the effective sweep range at `le_sweep = 55.0`. This challenges the notion that higher sweep is always better by aiming for reduced induced drag.
2.  **Aggressive Camber and Thinness for Lift Efficiency:** To compensate for potentially reduced vortex lift at a lower sweep angle, this design will maximize the airfoil's inherent lift capability by using `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4`. Simultaneously, to maintain efficiency, a significantly thinner airfoil (`naca_t=9`) will be used compared to Design 1 (`naca_t=13`) to reduce profile drag.
3.  **Novel Twist Distribution for Load Management:** Design 1's twist is uniquely effective, with negative twist at both root and tip. This new design will explore a contrasting strategy: a slight positive `twist_root` (1.0 deg) combined with the maximum possible washout at the `twist_tip` (-10.0 deg). This creates a strong positive-to-negative twist gradient, aiming to aggressively offload the tips and encourage lift generation towards the root, which could be beneficial for stability and efficient vortex formation at a lower sweep.
4.  **Maintain Moderate Root Chord and Neutral Dihedral:** The `root_chord_in` will be set to a moderate value of `28.0` inches, similar to previous successful designs, providing a good balance of wing area without excessively scaling the design. `Dihedral` will be set to a slightly negative value of `-2.0`, maintaining near-neutral lateral stability while allowing the primary aerodynamic shaping to come from sweep, camber, and twist.
5.  **Advance Population with a Distinct Design Strategy:** This design intentionally combines parameters in a way not seen in the top-ranked designs: lower sweep, max camber, thin airfoil, and a unique positive-to-negative twist. This bold exploration is aimed at discovering new optima or expanding the known boundaries of high-performance delta wing configurations.
