*   **Aggressive Camber for High Lift:** Building on Design 1's success with `NACA 4412`, this design will utilize the highest available camber (`naca_m=4`) with standard camber position (`naca_p=4`). This aims to maximize the lift coefficient and potentially improve the CL/CDi ratio, assuming induced drag can be managed.
*   **Optimized Washout Distribution for Spanload Control:** To improve upon Design 1's twist, I will explore a more pronounced differential washout. `twist_root` will be set very negatively (e.g., -9.0 deg) to aggressively unload the root and promote a strong leading-edge vortex, while `twist_tip` will be moderately negative (e.g., -2.5 deg). This large difference is intended to create an ideal spanwise lift distribution, delaying tip stall and minimizing induced drag.
*   **Slightly Lower Sweep for Vortex Efficiency and Reduced Induced Drag:** While 65.5 degrees worked well, I'll explore a slightly lower sweep angle (e.g., 62.0 deg). This provides a different balance between vortex lift contribution (which can be strong even at lower sweeps for delta wings) and induced drag, potentially finding an alternative optimal point for CL/CDi that hasn't been thoroughly explored with camber and specific twist.
*   **Moderate Chord and Thickness:** A `root_chord_in` of 37.5 inches is chosen to be slightly larger than Design 1, exploring a larger wing area for lift generation. The `naca_t` will be set to 10 to provide a good balance between reducing profile drag (compared to thicker airfoils) and maintaining structural viability, especially with higher camber.
*   **Consistent Slight Anhedral:** Maintain a slight anhedral (`dihedral=-2.5`) to promote favorable lateral stability characteristics, consistent with successful designs and good practice for highly swept wings.
