*   **Exploitation of Top Performer**: The new design heavily exploits the parameter patterns of Design 1, which achieved by far the highest reward (4016.0090). This includes maintaining a moderate sweep, cambered airfoil, and a specific twist distribution.
*   **Refined Differential Twist**: Design 1's `twist_root` of -7.13 and `twist_tip` of -4.61 suggests that having the tip less washed out than the root is critical for optimal lift distribution. The new design pushes `twist_root` to -9.0 (more washout) and `twist_tip` to -6.0. This maintains the differential (tip is less washed out by 3 degrees relative to root) but shifts the overall washout levels to be more pronounced, potentially further optimizing spanwise lift.
*   **Moderate Sweep Adjustment**: The `le_sweep` is set to 67.0 degrees, a slight increase from Design 1's 65.5 degrees. This aims to slightly enhance vortex lift typical of delta wings, while staying well within the moderate range to avoid the low performance observed in the highly swept Design 2.
*   **Slightly Reduced Airfoil Thickness**: The NACA 4410 airfoil (cambered, 10% thickness) is chosen. This retains the proven camber of Design 1 (NACA 4412) but slightly reduces thickness from 12% to 10%. This minor reduction aims to decrease profile drag without significantly compromising lift or overall aerodynamic efficiency, as very thin airfoils (D6, 4407) performed poorly.
*   **Subtle Anhedral**: The `dihedral` is set to -2.5 degrees, a slight increase in anhedral compared to Design 1's -1.72 degrees. Modest anhedral was present in multiple higher-performing designs (D1, D3, D4), suggesting a slight anhedral could contribute positively to stability or induced drag reduction.
*   **Consistent Root Chord**: The `root_chord_in` is set to 36.0 inches, maintaining it in a similar mid-range to Design 1. This parameter has a scaling effect, and maintaining a proven size helps focus optimization on other aerodynamic features.
