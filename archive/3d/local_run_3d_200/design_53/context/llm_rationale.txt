*   **Apply Exploration Strategy: Focus on low sweep:** The current high-reward designs cluster around mid-to-high sweep angles (63-65 degrees). The parameter space for `le_sweep` below 60 degrees, particularly closer to the 45-degree minimum, is significantly under-explored with proven aerodynamic features. This represents a distinct aerodynamic regime potentially favoring higher effective aspect ratios and lower induced drag, which could lead to novel high-performance designs.
*   **Leverage proven NACA profile:** Designs using symmetric airfoils or improperly configured camber (`naca_p=0` with `naca_m>0`) consistently perform poorly. To give this low-sweep exploration the best chance of success, we will combine it with the highly successful `NACA 44xx` airfoil (camber `naca_m=4`, max camber position `naca_p=4`). This hypothesis needs testing in a new sweep regime.
*   **Maintain effective washout:** The top-performing designs utilize significant negative twist at the root combined with less negative twist at the tip (washout). This helps manage lift distribution and prevent premature tip stall, which is beneficial across different sweep angles. We will apply a similar washout strategy to this low-sweep design.
*   **Explore slightly larger root chord:** With a lower sweep, a slightly larger root chord could compensate for a reduced projected span in some contexts and ensure adequate wing area for lift, without venturing into extreme sizes that have shown mixed results.
*   **Conservative dihedral and thickness:** Slight anhedral (-1 to -3 deg) has been observed in higher-reward designs, so we will maintain a similar slight anhedral for stability. For thickness, we'll try a moderate increase from the D1's 12% to 14% to see if slightly thicker airfoils can retain good performance, exploring a small step in the `naca_t` range.
