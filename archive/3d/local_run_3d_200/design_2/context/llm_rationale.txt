*   **Acknowledge "No previous designs"**: Since no prior data is available, the primary goal for this first design is to establish a diverse initial point in the design space by combining various parameters, rather than refining existing ones. This will provide a crucial starting point for the evolutionary process.
*   **Focus on fundamental delta wing characteristics**: A moderately high leading edge sweep of 68.0 degrees is chosen to ensure the design generates significant vortex lift, a defining characteristic of high-sweep delta wings, without pushing to the extreme high-drag regions of the parameter space immediately.
*   **Simplify initial airfoil complexity**: Starting with a symmetric airfoil (`naca_m=0, naca_p=0`) helps isolate the aerodynamic effects primarily driven by the planform and twist. This provides a clearer baseline for understanding the impact of sweep and twist before introducing the added complexity of camber.
*   **Explore moderate twist distribution**: A slight wash-in at the root (`twist_root=2.0`) and moderate washout at the tip (`twist_tip=-4.0`) is applied. This distribution is a common strategy to improve spanwise load distribution, potentially increasing lift efficiency and delaying tip stall, which will be valuable to evaluate in early designs.
*   **Neutral dihedral and practical sizing**: Dihedral is set to 0.0 degrees to eliminate its influence on lateral stability for this initial exploration, simplifying the analysis of other aerodynamic effects. A mid-range root chord of 30.0 inches and a moderate thickness of 12% are chosen to represent a practical wing size, avoiding extreme Reynolds number effects or excessive profile drag.
