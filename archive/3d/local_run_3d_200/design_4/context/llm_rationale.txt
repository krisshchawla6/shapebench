Since there is no existing design database to "exploit," this initial design leverages general aerodynamic principles for high-performance delta wings to establish a strong baseline. The goal is to maximize CL/CDi through a balanced approach.

*   **Moderate-High Sweep (68.0 deg)**: This sweep angle is chosen to effectively generate stable leading-edge vortices, a primary source of lift for delta wings, without incurring excessive wetted area or induced drag penalties associated with extremely high sweep angles. It represents a common and efficient compromise.
*   **Moderate Root Chord (35.0 inches)**: A mid-range root chord ensures a reasonably sized wing, avoiding potential low Reynolds number effects of very small wings and computational expense of very large ones, establishing a practical and representative scale.
*   **Mild Washout Twist (twist_root=1.0, twist_tip=-3.0)**: Implementing a mild washout (net -4.0 degrees from root to tip) aims to improve the spanwise lift distribution. By slightly unloading the wingtips, this configuration seeks to reduce induced drag and potentially delay tip stall, which are critical for maximizing CL/CDi.
*   **Zero Dihedral (0.0 deg)**: For this initial performance-focused design, zero dihedral simplifies the lateral stability characteristics, allowing the optimization to focus purely on lift and drag efficiency without introducing additional stability-related complexities.
*   **Moderate Camber (naca_m=2, naca_p=4)**: A NACA 24XX profile provides a proven moderate camber distribution (2% max camber at 40% chord). This choice aims to enhance lift generation at typical operating angles of attack, offering a performance advantage over purely symmetric airfoils without incurring excessive drag penalties.
*   **Relatively Thin Airfoil (naca_t=12)**: A 12% thickness-to-chord ratio is selected to minimize profile drag (both friction and form drag). Thinner airfoils generally result in higher CL/CDi, and 12% strikes a good balance between aerodynamic efficiency and practical considerations (like potential structural depth, though not directly evaluated here).
