*   **Exploration of Low Sweep (Boundary Push):** The current best designs are in the mid-high sweep range (~63-75 degrees). This new design will aggressively explore the lower end of the sweep spectrum (e.g., ~45-55 degrees) to understand its impact on CL/CDi, potentially reducing induced drag or shifting vortex characteristics for a novel solution.
*   **Maximizing Washout at Tip (Boundary Push):** Design 1 demonstrated the benefit of significant washout. This design will push the tip twist to its maximum negative limit (-10.0 degrees) to investigate the effects of extreme load alleviation at the wingtip, hoping to further improve efficiency. The root twist will be milder to allow for a significant twist gradient.
*   **Investigating Moderate Camber with Low Sweep:** The NACA 44xx series proved effective with mid-range sweep. This design will explore the NACA 24xx series (2% max camber at 40% chord) which was poorly explored or paired with poor parameter combinations previously. This tests if a lower camber can perform well when combined with an unexplored low sweep and extreme washout.
*   **Exploring Extreme Negative Dihedral (Boundary Push):** Dihedral's impact is not fully clear from the existing population. Designs with high positive dihedral performed poorly. This design will push towards the negative dihedral boundary (e.g., -12.0 degrees) to explore its influence on lateral stability and induced drag in concert with low sweep and strong washout.
*   **Mid-to-Low Root Chord:** Designs with very large root chords performed poorly. Design 1 had a mid-range root chord. This design will use a slightly smaller root chord than Design 1, aiming for a balance that doesn't become too unwieldy or too small, to facilitate the low sweep and extreme twist effects.
*   **Thicker Airfoil Exploration:** Design 1 used 13% thickness. This design will explore a thicker airfoil (e.g., 20%) to see if the increased structural depth or different pressure distribution interactions with low sweep and strong washout can yield a higher reward, as thickness above 14% is less common in the high-reward designs.
