1.  **Suboptimal Vortex Formation/Breakdown**: Lower sweep angles or improper twist can lead to weaker vortices or early breakdown, reducing vortex lift and increasing drag.
2.  **Inefficient Load Distribution**: Poor twist distribution can result in highly localized high-pressure areas (or insufficient low pressure), leading to high induced drag or flow separation.
3.  **Excessive Induced Drag**: While vortex lift is desired, if sweep is too high without proper twist, or if the overall wing planform isn't optimized, induced drag can become dominant.
4.  **Profile Drag**: Thicker airfoils or inappropriate camber can increase profile drag, negatively impacting CL/CDi.
