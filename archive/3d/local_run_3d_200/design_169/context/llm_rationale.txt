*   **Explore lower moderate sweep:** Design 1 succeeded with 65.5 degrees. To ensure diversity and explore the design space effectively, we will try a slightly lower sweep angle of 60.0 degrees. This is still within the "moderate" range observed to be successful, but sufficiently different from the top performer to provide new data on how vortex lift and induced drag scale with sweep.
*   **Increase root chord slightly:** Design 1 used 34.8 inches. Designs with very large or very small chords performed poorly. We'll increase the root chord to 40.0 inches, testing if a slightly larger planform in conjunction with other optimized parameters can yield higher lift.
*   **Push "Inverse Washout" further:** The most striking feature of Design 1's twist is that `twist_tip` (-4.6) is less negative (or relatively higher AoA) than `twist_root` (-7.1). This "inverse washout" might be crucial for its high performance by influencing vortex breakdown and spanwise load distribution. We will push this extreme by setting `twist_root` to its maximum negative (-10.0) and `twist_tip` to a much less negative value (-2.0), maximizing the difference and relative tip AoA. This is a bold exploration of a potentially dominant feature.
*   **Maintain optimal camber, reduce thickness:** The NACA 44xx series proved superior. We'll keep `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4`. To potentially reduce profile drag and explore this aspect further, we'll decrease the thickness from 12 to 9, testing if a thinner profile combined with optimal planform and twist can boost performance.
*   **Moderate positive dihedral:** Design 1 has a slightly negative dihedral. While strong positive dihedral designs performed poorly, this might have been due to other suboptimal parameters. We will explore a moderate positive dihedral (5.0 degrees) to see if it can offer benefits in lateral stability without detrimental aerodynamic costs when other parameters are set more favorably, providing valuable data for this parameter's interaction.
