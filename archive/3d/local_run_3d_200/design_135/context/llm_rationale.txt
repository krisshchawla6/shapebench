*   **Build on Camber Success**: The NACA 44xx airfoil has demonstrated strong performance in Design 1. To maintain a high baseline for lift while exploring other parameters, I will retain `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4`. This ensures a cambered profile with max camber at 40% chord, which seems effective.
*   **Moderate and Differentiated Washout**: The highest-performing design (Design 1) used a moderate washout where `twist_root` was more negative than `twist_tip` (-7.1 vs -4.6). Poor designs used aggressive, large negative twist values for both. For this new design, I will experiment with a slightly less aggressive root washout (`twist_root=-5.0`) than Design 1, but a slightly more negative tip washout (`twist_tip=-6.0`). This creates a different, but still moderate, load distribution strategy where the tip sheds slightly more load compared to Design 1, potentially improving tip stall characteristics or induced drag.
*   **Explore Higher Sweep with Refined Twist**: Design 1's sweep of 65.5 degrees worked well. Designs with higher sweep (e.g., 67.7 deg in Design 6) performed poorly, but were confounded by extreme washout. By combining a slightly higher sweep (`le_sweep=69.0`) with the refined, moderate twist strategy, I aim to see if the benefits of increased vortex lift at higher sweep can be realized without incurring excessive induced drag or adverse stall characteristics.
*   **Optimize for Thickness**: Design 1 used 12% thickness. To further reduce drag, I will explore a slightly thinner airfoil by choosing `naca_t=10`. This change is a step towards reducing form drag, which could improve CL/CDi if lift generation remains strong due to the chosen sweep and twist.
*   **Maintain Moderate Root Chord and Dihedral**: A root chord of 34.8 inches was effective in Design 1. I will slightly increase the `root_chord_in` to `38.0` inches to explore a slightly larger wing area and potentially increased lift, while remaining within a moderate range. For `dihedral`, I will opt for a small positive value (`2.0 deg`), moving slightly away from the small negative of Design 1, to explore its impact on lateral stability without expecting a significant degradation in CL/CDi.
