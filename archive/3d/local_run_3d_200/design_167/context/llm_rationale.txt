*   **Deep Exploration of Low Sweep:** The existing population heavily favors higher sweeps (63-76 deg), with the best design around 65.5 deg. To understand the full sweep design space and its interaction with other parameters, I will significantly lower the `le_sweep` to 52.5 degrees, pushing towards the lower boundary (45.0 deg). This will test if a less swept delta wing, potentially with less reliance on strong vortex lift, can still achieve high efficiency.
*   **Novel Zero-Camber Airfoil:** All previous designs incorporate camber (NACA 24xx or 44xx). I will introduce a completely symmetric, zero-camber airfoil (`naca_m=0`, `naca_p=0`). This radical change will determine if the aerodynamic benefits of camber can be forgone for potential reductions in profile drag, especially in combination with a lower sweep. This is a high-risk, high-reward strategy.
*   **Unexplored Positive Root Twist:** All existing designs utilize negative `twist_root`. I will set `twist_root` to a positive value (5.0 deg). This creates an "inverted" twist distribution compared to previous designs, where the root has positive incidence relative to the tip (which still has washout via negative `twist_tip`). This aims to explore its effects on load distribution and flow separation characteristics with the low sweep and zero-camber profile.
*   **Increased Root Chord:** To potentially compensate for the reduced lift from lower sweep and zero camber, and to investigate scaling effects with these novel parameters, I will use a larger `root_chord_in` of 45.0 inches, pushing towards the upper end of the range.
*   **Moderate Positive Dihedral:** The current population mostly explores negative or near-zero dihedral. I will set `dihedral` to a moderately positive value (7.5 deg). This explores an under-represented region of the dihedral parameter space and its interaction with the other novel design choices, potentially impacting lateral stability and induced drag.
*   **Moderate Thickness:** To isolate the effects of these significant changes in sweep, camber, and twist, I will maintain a moderate `naca_t` of 12. This value is consistent with the best-performing design (Design 2), allowing for a fair comparison of the novel geometric and aerodynamic strategies without introducing an additional unknown from extreme thickness.
