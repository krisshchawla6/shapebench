*   **Embrace Higher Sweep with Intent:** The best designs are around 65 degrees. While Design 6 (72.9 deg) performed poorly, it used a symmetric airfoil and positive dihedral. I hypothesize that a higher sweep (e.g., ~70 degrees) could offer greater vortex lift potential, and this potential might be unlocked by pairing it with a carefully optimized cambered airfoil and twist distribution, rather than simply attributing low performance solely to high sweep.
*   **Aggressive Wash-in Twist Distribution:** Design 1's success implies that less washout at the tip than the root is beneficial. To push this further and actively manage spanwise load distribution for the higher sweep, I will implement a deep washout at the root (e.g., -9.0 deg) and a very minimal washout at the tip (e.g., -1.0 deg). This creates a strong wash-in from root to tip, aiming to distribute lift more effectively and potentially delay tip stall or reduce induced drag for the chosen higher sweep.
*   **Optimal Camber and Thinner Profile:** Cambered airfoils consistently show better rewards than symmetric ones. I will select NACA 4410. This maintains a strong 4% camber for lift generation but utilizes a thinner profile (10% thickness compared to Design 1's 12% or Design 3's 16%) to explore potential reductions in form drag, hoping to improve the CL/CDi ratio.
*   **Enhanced Anhedral:** High-performing designs tend to have slight anhedral. I will explore a moderately more negative dihedral (~-7.5 degrees). This could interact favorably with the higher sweep and twist to enhance lateral stability or effective dihedral, especially at higher angles of attack.
*   **Moderate Root Chord for Scale:** I will select a root chord in the upper-mid range (e.g., 38 inches). This provides a substantial wing area, balancing the overall scale and lift potential with the other design parameters without being excessively large (like Design 3's 40.8 inches, which had a lower reward).
*   **Novelty through Parameter Combination:** This design combines a moderately high sweep with an aggressive wash-in twist, a thinner cambered airfoil, and a more pronounced anhedral, a configuration not explicitly seen or highly rewarded among the previous designs, pushing the boundaries of exploration.
