*   **Hybrid Strategy - Building on Success:** The new design adopts a hybrid strategy, primarily building upon the highly successful Design 2 (Reward: 4016.0090) which featured `le_sweep=65.5`, `root_chord_in=34.7`, `twist_root=-7.1`, `twist_tip=-4.6`, `dihedral=-1.7`, `NACA 4412`. We aim to refine these winning parameters with targeted variations.
*   **Increased Sweep for Enhanced Vortex Lift:** We will slightly increase the `le_sweep` to 69.5 degrees from Design 2's 65.5 degrees. This aims to generate stronger and potentially more stable leading-edge vortices, leading to a higher proportion of vortex lift without venturing into the overly high sweep (75.6 deg) that led to low rewards in other designs.
*   **Balanced Washout Distribution:** To further optimize the spanwise lift distribution and control vortex behavior, we will apply a slightly more aggressive `twist_root` of -8.0 degrees (more negative than Design 2's -7.1, but less extreme than Design 1's -10.0) and a `twist_tip` of -5.5 degrees (more negative washout than Design 2's -4.6, but less than Design 3's -9.0). This should promote a more efficient load distribution towards the outer wing, reducing induced drag.
*   **Slightly Larger Root Chord with Mild Anhedral:** The `root_chord_in` will be increased slightly to 37.0 inches (from Design 2's 34.7) to explore the effects of a moderately larger wing area, potentially allowing for higher lift coefficients. The `dihedral` will be set to -2.5 degrees, maintaining the beneficial slight anhedral observed in Design 2 while exploring a minor variation.
*   **Optimized Cambered Airfoil with Reduced Thickness:** We will retain the `naca_m=4` (4% max camber) and `naca_p=4` (camber at 40% chord) from Design 2, as this camber profile proved highly effective for lift generation. However, we will reduce `naca_t` (thickness) from 12% to 9%. This thinner airfoil aims to decrease profile drag, further improving the CL/CDi ratio without significantly compromising the lift benefits of the camber.
