*   **Apply Exploration Strategy: High Sweep Angle**: The current population primarily explores sweep angles between 60 and 70 degrees. To explore an underrepresented region and push the boundaries of vortex lift, we will set `le_sweep` to a very high value, near the upper limit (78.0 degrees). This aims to maximize vortex strength and understand its interaction with other parameters.
*   **Explore Milder Washout**: All top designs utilize strong negative twist (washout). We want to understand if a less aggressive twist distribution can still achieve high performance, especially when paired with very high sweep. We will set `twist_root` to -1.0 and `twist_tip` to -3.0, representing a much milder washout than previously explored.
*   **Investigate Significant Positive Dihedral**: Dihedral has primarily been negative or close to zero in the high-performing designs. Exploring the positive range (e.g., +10.0 degrees) is a significant departure, allowing us to assess its impact on lateral stability and induced drag in combination with high sweep.
*   **Combine with Moderate Root Chord and Thin Cambered Airfoil**: To complement the very high sweep and explore its interplay with other dimensions, we'll use a moderate `root_chord_in` (30.0 inches) to manage overall wing area. For the airfoil, we'll select a cambered but relatively thin NACA profile (NACA 2408: `naca_m=2`, `naca_p=4`, `naca_t=8`). This explores if a thinner airfoil can maintain performance, potentially reducing parasitic drag, with the expectation that the high sweep and vortex lift will compensate for reduced inherent airfoil lift.
