*   **Embrace higher sweep for enhanced vortex lift:** Design 1 has a sweep of 65.5 degrees. To explore a distinct regime, we will increase `le_sweep` significantly towards the upper end of the range (e.g., 75.0 degrees). This pushes the design into an area where leading-edge vortices are expected to be stronger and more stable, potentially leading to higher lift coefficients.
*   **Prioritize cambered airfoil for inherent lift:** The top-performing design and other higher-reward designs utilize cambered airfoils (NACA 24xx, 44xx). Designs with NACA 00xx profiles consistently show much lower rewards. We will stick with `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4` to maintain a strong, forward-biased camber, providing a good baseline lift coefficient and potentially reducing reliance on high angles of attack for lift.
*   **Implement conventional tip washout:** Design 1 has more washout at the root than the tip. For this new design, we will explore a more conventional washout strategy where `twist_tip` is significantly more negative (more washout) than `twist_root`. This aims to unload the wingtips, ensuring a more elliptical-like spanwise load distribution and delaying tip stall, which could improve efficiency.
*   **Explore a different scale with root chord:** Design 1 uses a moderate `root_chord_in`. For this new, higher-sweep design, a slightly smaller `root_chord_in` (e.g., 28.0 inches) will be chosen to explore how the wing's overall scale interacts with the increased sweep and modified twist distribution. This could also affect the effective aspect ratio.
*   **Neutral dihedral for isolated aerodynamic study:** Design 1 has a slight negative dihedral. To simplify the analysis of our other significant changes, we will choose a `dihedral` of 0.0 (neutral). This allows us to observe the primary effects of the high sweep, camber, and twist distribution without the added complexity of lateral stability considerations.
*   **Maintain moderate thickness:** Design 1 used `naca_t=12`. We will keep `naca_t=12` for this new design to hold thickness constant while we explore the impact of the other, more drastic changes (sweep, twist, chord). This helps isolate the effect of these primary parameters.
