*   **Exploit Camber and Camber Position**: Design 1, the top performer, uses a significant 4% camber (NACA 4412). Uncambered airfoils (NACA 00xx) consistently yield low rewards. Therefore, a highly cambered airfoil (NACA 44xx) will be selected to maximize lift, using `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4`.
*   **Amplify "Washin-like" Twist Distribution**: The most successful designs (D1, D2) exhibit a pattern where `twist_root` is more negative than `twist_tip` (e.g., `twist_root`=-7.1, `twist_tip`=-4.6 for D1), effectively increasing the angle of attack towards the wing tip. This counter-intuitive "washin-like" distribution will be emphasized by setting `twist_root` to its maximum negative value (-10.0) and `twist_tip` to a less negative value (-3.0) to explore its upper bounds.
*   **Explore Increased Sweep**: Design 1 has a moderate sweep of 65.5 degrees. To probe for higher vortex lift contributions, the sweep will be increased to 70.0 degrees, placing it in the upper-moderate range, to test its interaction with the optimized twist and camber configuration.
*   **Scale Up Root Chord**: Design 1 has a root chord of 34.8 inches. A larger root chord of 45.0 inches will be investigated to scale up the wing area, potentially increasing overall lift and providing diversity in wing sizing.
*   **Test More Negative Dihedral**: The top designs generally feature negative dihedral. To explore its impact on stability and performance, a moderately negative dihedral (-8.0 degrees) will be chosen, differing from Design 1's -1.7 degrees, to see if increased negative dihedral is beneficial.
*   **Moderate Thickness for Diversity**: Design 1 uses 12% thickness. To provide a different point in the design space and ensure diversity, a thickness of 14% will be chosen, which is slightly higher than D1 but still within a reasonable range for performance.
