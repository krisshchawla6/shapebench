*   **Explore higher sweep for enhanced vortex lift:** Design 1 achieved high reward with a moderate-high sweep (63.8 deg). Design 2 (reward 13.3) used an even higher sweep (74.4 deg). To push the boundaries of vortex lift, this new design will explore a very high sweep angle (e.g., near 78 degrees) to assess the maximum benefit of this phenomenon.
*   **Maximize tip washout for induced drag reduction:** Design 1 had significant root washout (-8.77 deg) and moderate tip washout (-7.02 deg). Design 2 implemented maximal tip washout (-10.0 deg). Combining very high sweep with maximal tip washout is a bold exploration to minimize induced drag by unloading the tips, potentially leading to a more efficient lift distribution despite high sweep.
*   **Introduce moderate camber with different position of max camber:** Design 1 used `NACA 4413`. To provide diversity in airfoil choice while still benefiting from positive lift and tailored pressure distribution, a `NACA 24xx` airfoil (2% max camber at 40% chord) will be used. This explores a milder camber compared to `NACA 44xx`, but with the same max camber position.
*   **Maintain moderate root chord:** The highest performing designs (1, 2, 6) consistently used moderate `root_chord_in` values (23-35 inches). Sticking within this range ensures the wing has a reasonable scale without introducing extreme scaling effects that could mask the impact of sweep and twist changes.
*   **Investigate moderate anhedral for stability diversity:** Previous high-reward designs had near-zero or positive dihedral. A moderate negative dihedral (anhedral) will be explored to investigate its effect on lateral stability and handling qualities, diversifying the population's stability characteristics.
