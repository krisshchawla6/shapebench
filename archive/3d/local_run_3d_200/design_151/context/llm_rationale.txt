The current top design exhibits moderate sweep, significant washout, and a cambered airfoil. To advance the population through exploration, the next design will venture into significantly underexplored regions and parameter combinations, adopting a distinct aerodynamic strategy.

*   **Explore lower sweep region**: Most successful designs cluster around 63-75 degrees of sweep. We will select a much lower `le_sweep` (e.g., 50-55 degrees) to investigate delta wings where vortex lift might be less dominant, potentially leading to lower induced drag if coupled with an efficient planform and airfoil. This pushes a significant boundary.
*   **Introduce symmetric airfoil (`NACA m=0`)**: The entire population has used cambered airfoils (`NACA m=2` or `m=4`). Introducing a completely symmetric airfoil (`naca_m=0, naca_p=0`) is a crucial exploratory step. This will provide insights into the fundamental performance of a delta wing reliant solely on shape, sweep, and twist for lift, removing camber-induced pitching moments from the equation.
*   **Investigate extreme thinness (`naca_t=6`)**: Airfoil thickness has been mostly in the 7-16 range. Pushing to the absolute minimum `naca_t=6` will test if the drag reduction from a very thin profile can offset potential structural or lift generation challenges, especially with a lower sweep and symmetric airfoil.
*   **Moderate, uniform washout**: While many designs feature extreme root washout, we will apply a moderate and uniform negative twist at both root and tip (e.g., -5 degrees). This offers a distinct twist profile to explore lift distribution, aiming to balance tip loading and induced drag for a low-sweep, symmetric wing, without resorting to the extreme values seen previously.
*   **Explore positive dihedral**: The top design has mild negative dihedral, and others vary. Exploring a moderately positive dihedral (e.g., 7-10 degrees) in conjunction with a low-sweep, symmetric design is an unexplored combination. This could influence roll stability and effective angle of attack across the span in novel ways.
*   **Increase root chord to compensate**: To potentially offset any reduction in lift from the lower sweep and symmetric airfoil, we will increase the `root_chord_in` towards the upper end of the allowed range (e.g., 45-50 inches). This also explores a region of larger physical scale that is currently underexplored.
