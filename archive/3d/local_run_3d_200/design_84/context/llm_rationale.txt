*   **Embrace Aggressive Camber (Novel Exploration):** The top-performing Design 2 (NACA 4412) and Design 3 (NACA 2411) clearly show the benefit of cambered airfoils over the symmetric NACA 0011 of Design 1. I will push this further by selecting NACA 44xx, aiming for maximum camber to generate significantly more lift and improve the CL/CDi, especially at lower angles of attack. This is a direct response to the poor performance of the uncambered Design 1.
*   **Explore Higher Sweep for Enhanced Vortex Lift:** Designs with sweep around 63-65 degrees have shown good performance. To diversify and explore the upper bounds of vortex lift potential, I will significantly increase the leading edge sweep to around 72 degrees. This aims to generate a stronger and more stable leading-edge vortex, pushing the boundaries of the design space while addressing the potentially weak vortex observed in Design 1.
*   **Optimized Differential Twist for Load Management:** Design 3, with its large difference between root and tip twist (-10.0 vs -2.8), achieved a respectable reward. I will adopt a similar strategy: maximum washout at the root (`twist_root=-10.0`) and a less aggressive washout at the tip (`twist_tip=-2.0`). This creates a spanwise variation in angle of attack that should help unload the root while maintaining effective lift generation at the tips, contributing to a more efficient and stable vortex system for the highly swept wing.
*   **Reduced Root Chord for Efficiency at High Sweep:** The high-performing designs have root chords in the 33-35 inch range. With a significantly increased sweep and aggressive camber, a slightly smaller root chord (e.g., 30 inches) might be beneficial. This could reduce wetted area and induced drag for a given wing planform, potentially improving efficiency when combined with robust vortex lift, leading to a bolder exploration of the efficiency frontier.
*   **Consistent Anhedral and Moderate Thickness:** High-performing designs exhibit slight anhedral (negative dihedral, -1.7 to -3.4 degrees). I will maintain this trend with a value of -2.5 degrees, as it typically contributes positively to lateral stability in highly swept configurations. For thickness, I will choose a moderate value (NACA 4410), slightly thinner than the NACA 4412 of Design 2, seeking to reduce profile drag while maintaining structural integrity.
