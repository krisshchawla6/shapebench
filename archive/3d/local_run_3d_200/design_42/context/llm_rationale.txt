*   **Novel Design Strategy:** The current best design (Design 1) shows that a particular combination of moderate-high sweep, specific twist, and camber can yield exceptional results. However, its twist distribution is not a conventional "washout" where the tip is more negative than the root. I will explore a design with aggressive *conventional washout* (tip significantly more negative than root) to assess its impact on CL/CDi.
*   **Exploring High Sweep for Vortex Lift:** Design 1's sweep of 65.5 degrees appears to be a good starting point for strong vortex generation. I will slightly increase the sweep to 72.0 degrees to potentially enhance vortex strength further, while staying within the range where such vortices are stable and dominant.
*   **Aggressive Washout for Spanwise Loading:** I hypothesize that an aggressive conventional washout (e.g., root at -4.0, tip at -9.0) will lead to a more favorable spanwise lift distribution, reducing induced drag even with a high sweep. This directly contrasts with Design 1's twist pattern, offering a novel exploration of the twist space.
*   **Milder Camber with Moderate Thickness:** Design 1 used NACA 4412. I will try NACA 2410 (milder 2% camber, 40% position, and thinner 10% thickness). This allows for a fresh investigation into how a less aggressively cambered, thinner airfoil combines with strong vortex lift and aggressive washout to achieve high CL/CDi, potentially reducing profile drag while relying more on vortex lift.
*   **Moderate Root Chord:** I will set the root chord to a moderate value of 35.0 inches, similar to Design 1, to ensure a comparable scale and allow the aerodynamic shape (sweep, twist, airfoil) to be the primary drivers of performance differences.
*   **Slight Positive Dihedral:** I will explore a small positive dihedral (3.0 degrees) to observe its effect on overall stability and performance, as most previous designs have been near neutral or slightly negative.
