*   **Reinforce Cambered Airfoil**: The top-performing designs (1, 2, 3, 4, 6) all utilize cambered NACA airfoils (`naca_m=4` or `naca_m=4`, `naca_p=4`). The symmetric NACA 0016 (Design 5) yielded an extremely low reward. Therefore, I will maintain a cambered airfoil, specifically `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4`, to ensure a strong baseline for lift generation.
*   **Target Higher Sweep for Vortex Lift**: Designs 2 and 6, while not as good as Design 1, achieved respectable rewards with higher sweep angles (~74 degrees). Design 1, with a moderate sweep (~64 degrees), was the best. I will explore a sweep in the upper-middle range of the successful designs, around 70-72 degrees, to capitalize on strong vortex lift characteristics while potentially mitigating some induced drag from extremely high sweeps.
*   **Exploit "Washout" Twist Strategy**: Design 1 featured `twist_root` more negative than `twist_tip` (washout in absolute terms, but tip relatively less negative than root). Designs 2 had `twist_tip` more negative than `twist_root`. I will emulate the successful washout pattern of Design 1 (more negative at root) and push the overall magnitude of negative twist slightly to enhance lift distribution and reduce induced drag. Specifically, I'll aim for a strong negative `twist_root` and a less negative `twist_tip`.
*   **Optimize Thickness for Drag Reduction**: Design 1 and 6 used NACA 4413. Design 2 used NACA 4009. A slightly thinner airfoil can reduce drag. I will explore `naca_t=12`, a value slightly thinner than Design 1 but thicker than Design 2, to seek a balance between drag reduction and maintaining structural/aerodynamic integrity.
*   **Maintain Moderate Root Chord**: The best designs show a range of root chords. A moderate root chord provides sufficient planform area for lift without excessive wetted area or structural demands. I will choose a value close to Design 1 but slightly larger, similar to some other good performers.
*   **Conservative Dihedral**: Design 1 had slight positive dihedral. Designs 2 and 6 had slight negative. I will opt for a slight positive dihedral, similar to the top design, as it seems to contribute positively or at least neutrally to the overall CL/CDi.
