*   **Strategy Focus: Exploration of Underexplored Regions.** The current population primarily explores sweep values between 58 and 68 degrees. A significant portion of the higher sweep range (above 70 degrees, up to 80 degrees) remains largely untested. My new design will aggressively push into this high-sweep region to explore its potential for strong vortex lift.
*   **Leverage Camber:** The highest-reward design (Design 1) utilized a NACA 4412 airfoil. This suggests that a cambered profile with the maximum available camber (`naca_m=4, naca_p=4`) is generally beneficial for generating lift efficiently. I will retain this proven airfoil type to isolate the effects of other parameter changes.
*   **Aggressive Washout Strategy:** Most higher-performing designs incorporate significant washout. I will implement a strong, distinct washout by setting both `twist_root` and `twist_tip` to negative values close to the boundary (e.g., -9.5 at the root and -8.0 at the tip), maintaining a similar distribution to the successful Design 1 but with greater magnitude to see its interaction with high sweep.
*   **Explore Thinner Airfoils:** Existing designs mainly use NACA thicknesses of 11-16%. To complement the high sweep and potentially reduce profile drag (or wave drag if relevant at higher speeds), I will select a very thin airfoil (e.g., `naca_t=8`), pushing towards the lower bound of the available thickness range (6%). This combination of high sweep and thin airfoil is largely unexplored.
*   **Investigate Extreme Anhedral:** The dihedral range is -15 to 15, but most existing designs are near zero or slightly negative. I will explore a significantly negative dihedral (e.g., -14.0) to see its effect on lateral stability and induced drag in conjunction with high sweep. This is an underexplored boundary condition.
*   **Moderate Root Chord:** To allow for focus on the aerodynamic shape changes (sweep, twist, airfoil), I will keep the root chord in a well-performing mid-range (e.g., 30-35 inches), similar to the successful Design 1, avoiding interaction with extreme scaling effects for this exploratory step.
