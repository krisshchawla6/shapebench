*   **Leverage proven camber profile:** The `NACA 44xx` airfoil (m=4, p=4) is strongly correlated with the highest reward. This indicates it provides an excellent lift-to-induced-drag ratio. We will maintain `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4` to build upon this successful aerodynamic foundation.
*   **Bold exploration of higher sweep:** Designs 5 and 6 explored higher sweeps (68-69 deg) but suffered from non-optimal airfoil or chord choices. This design will push the `le_sweep` further towards the upper range (e.g., 72.0 deg) to investigate the potential of stronger vortex lift in combination with the proven `NACA 44xx` profile. This represents a more diverse exploration.
*   **Aggressive washout for induced drag reduction:** With higher sweep potentially increasing induced drag, a more aggressive washout (larger difference between `twist_root` and `twist_tip`) will be implemented to better distribute lift span-wise and unload the wing tips, aiming to mitigate induced drag. We will set `twist_root` to a more negative value and `twist_tip` to a less negative (but still negative) value.
*   **Moderate chord and slight negative dihedral:** The top design utilizes a moderate root chord and slight negative dihedral. We will maintain these general trends but slightly vary them (e.g., slightly larger chord, slightly more negative dihedral) to explore adjacent parameter spaces while remaining in a region that seems aerodynamically favorable.
*   **Thinner airfoil for efficiency:** Design 1 used `naca_t=12`. A slightly thinner `naca_t=10` will be used to reduce profile drag, which may contribute positively to the CL/CDi ratio, especially when combined with a larger chord and higher sweep.
