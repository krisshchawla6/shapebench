The highest performing design (Design 1) featured a moderate sweep (65.5 deg), mid-range root chord (34.8 inches), moderate negative twist at both root and tip (washout), slight anhedral, and a NACA 4412 airfoil (cambered, moderate thickness). Its high reward suggests a good balance of vortex lift and reduced induced/profile drag.

Key observations from the current population:
- **Sweep:** All designs fall within 63.12 to 72.92 degrees. The lower sweep range (45-60 deg) and very high sweep range (75-80 deg) are entirely unexplored.
- **Twist:** All designs exhibit washout (negative twist at root and tip), with `twist_root` often at the maximum negative (-10.0 deg). The possibility of positive twist (washin) or milder washout (closer to 0) has not been explored significantly, especially for the root. Design 1 had less aggressive washout than many others, which might be a factor in its success.
- **NACA Thickness:** Thickness values are clustered between 11 and 18. The thinner (6-10) and thicker (19-24) ends of the spectrum are unexplored.
- **Root Chord:** Designs are mostly in the 30-40 inch range. Very small (10-25 inches) and very large (45-50 inches) root chords are not thoroughly tested.
- **Dihedral:** A good range of dihedral values has been tested, but combinations with other extreme parameters are missing.

Key changes needed to improve exploration:
1.  Explore the lower end of the sweep parameter space.
2.  Investigate twist distributions with positive root twist or very mild washout at the tip.
3.  Test significantly thinner airfoils (NACA t = 6-10).
4.  Explore larger root chord values in combination with other novel parameters.
