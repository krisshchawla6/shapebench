*   **Embrace higher sweep for increased vortex lift**: The current best designs operate around 60-65 degrees sweep. To push the boundaries and leverage potentially stronger leading-edge vortices, I will select a significantly higher sweep angle, near the upper end of the range (e.g., 76.0 degrees). This is a bold departure from the current top performers.
*   **Maintain beneficial camber**: Designs with cambered airfoils (NACA 44XX and 24XX) consistently show higher rewards than symmetric airfoils. I will stick with a proven camber profile, NACA 44xx (specifically `naca_m=4`, `naca_p=4`), as seen in Design 1, to ensure a baseline of effective lift generation.
*   **Explore extreme differential twist**: Design 1 has washout at both root and tip. To complement the high sweep and explore load redistribution, I will apply maximum washout at the root (`twist_root = -10.0`) to offload the inner wing and promote strong vortex formation, combined with a slight wash-in at the tip (`twist_tip = 2.0`). This extreme combination aims to manage the vortex breakdown and potentially improve outer wing lift, offering a novel load distribution.
*   **Reduce thickness for drag reduction**: Design 1 uses a 12% thick airfoil. With a higher sweep and the goal of maximizing CL/CDi, reducing profile drag is important. I will opt for a thinner airfoil, `naca_t=9`, which is towards the lower end of the range, to potentially improve aerodynamic efficiency.
*   **Adjust root chord and dihedral for diversity**: I will slightly reduce the `root_chord_in` compared to previous designs (e.g., 28.0 inches) to explore scaling effects while aiming for high performance. For `dihedral`, I will choose a moderately positive value (e.g., 4.0) to explore its impact on stability, as previous successful designs had small positive or negative values.
