*   **Hybridizing High Performers**: This design blends successful attributes from Design 1 (highest reward, NACA 4412, effective sweep and twist) and Design 2 (high reward, aggressive root twist, NACA 2411). The goal is to combine their complementary strengths to find a new optimum.
*   **Aggressive Root Twist for Lift**: Design 2's `twist_root` of -10.0 deg suggests that a very negative angle at the root can significantly contribute to lift generation. We adopt a strong negative `twist_root` of -9.5 deg, taking inspiration from this successful trait.
*   **Balanced Tip Washout**: Design 1 has a `twist_tip` of -4.6 deg, and Design 4 has an even stronger washout at -7.1 deg, which also yielded a decent reward despite being a symmetric airfoil. We choose a `twist_tip` of -5.5 deg to maintain a significant washout, ensuring good tip behavior and mitigating tip stall, which is a blend between D1 and D4's effective values.
*   **Increased Sweep for Stronger Vortices**: The best performing designs are in the mid-60s sweep range. To explore further improvements in vortex lift, we increase `le_sweep` to 67.5 degrees, pushing slightly beyond Design 1's sweep, aiming for enhanced vortex generation without excessive drag penalty.
*   **Consistent Camber Profile**: High-reward designs (1, 2, 3) consistently utilize cambered airfoils (NACA m=2 or 4). We retain `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4` (like Design 1 and 3) as this profile has demonstrated the highest potential for CL/CDi.
*   **Moderate Chord and Thickness**: A `root_chord_in` of 36.0 inches is chosen as a moderate value, between the top performers, to provide a good scale for the wing. A `naca_t` of 11, consistent with several good performing designs, aims to minimize profile drag while maintaining a practical thickness. A small negative `dihedral` of -2.5 is also chosen, consistent with the trend in top designs.
