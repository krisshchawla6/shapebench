*   **Emphasize High Sweep for Vortex Lift:** Design 1 has a sweep of 65.5 degrees, which is good. To explore more aggressive vortex lift generation, I will increase the leading-edge sweep towards the upper end of the effective range for delta wings (e.g., 70-75 degrees). This will create stronger leading-edge vortices.
*   **Maintain Aggressive Washout for Efficiency:** The top performers universally exhibit significant negative twist (washout). This is crucial for achieving an efficient spanwise load distribution, reducing induced drag, and preventing premature tip stall. I will implement strong washout at both the root and the tip to maximize this benefit.
*   **Leverage Cambered Airfoil for Intrinsic Lift:** Designs using symmetric airfoils (NACA m=0) consistently yielded lower rewards, even with good twist. Camber (NACA m=2 or 4) provides inherent lift, allowing for lower angles of attack for a given lift and improving CL/CDi. I will select a cambered airfoil type.
*   **Moderate Root Chord & Thickness:** Extremes in root chord (like Design 5's high value) have shown poor performance. I will keep the root chord in the moderate range (similar to top performers) to balance wing area with overall drag. For thickness, a slightly thinner airfoil (e.g., NACA 2410) might offer reduced profile drag, especially with increased sweep.
*   **Slight Anhedral/Near-Zero Dihedral:** The top performers generally have slightly negative or near-zero dihedral. Extreme positive dihedral (as seen in Design 5) correlated with very poor performance. A slight negative dihedral (anhedral) can sometimes improve roll stability characteristics in swept-wing aircraft.
