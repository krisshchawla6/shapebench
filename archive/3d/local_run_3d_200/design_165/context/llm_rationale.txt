*   **Strategy Focus: Exploration of Untried Airfoil Type**: The `naca_m=0` (symmetric airfoil) configuration has not been explored at all. This is a significant discrete parameter variation that could fundamentally change aerodynamic behavior, particularly with high sweep where vortex lift is dominant. Setting `naca_m=0` necessitates `naca_p=0`.
*   **Explore Extreme High Sweep**: The current top designs cluster around 65 degrees leading edge sweep. By pushing `le_sweep` to its upper limit (80.0 degrees), we aim to explore the very high sweep regime, where vortex lift becomes even more pronounced. This combination with a symmetric airfoil is a novel test.
*   **Test Positive/Zero Twist for Different Load Distribution**: All prior designs featured negative twist (washout). To explore a new load distribution strategy, this design will incorporate a positive `twist_root` and a zero `twist_tip`. This creates a wash-in condition (tip stall tendency) or a very subtle load distribution, which could interact uniquely with extreme sweep and a symmetric airfoil.
*   **Explore Extreme Positive Dihedral**: Most designs had negative or near-zero dihedral. To explore a completely different aspect of lateral stability and wing interaction, setting `dihedral` to its maximum positive value (15.0 degrees) will push the design to an unexplored boundary.
*   **Investigate a Smaller Root Chord**: The current population favors root chords in the 30-40 inch range. By selecting a `root_chord_in` value towards the lower end (e.g., 20.0 inches), we explore a smaller overall wing, which could have implications for overall drag and lift-to-weight ratio (though weight isn't explicitly optimized here, size matters).
*   **Explore a Thicker Airfoil at the Upper Range**: Current designs generally use moderate thicknesses. By specifying a `naca_t` in the upper range (e.g., 20), we explore the impact of a thicker profile, which could affect structural rigidity and potentially high-angle-of-attack performance, completing a highly exploratory design.
