*   **Prioritize higher sweep for enhanced vortex strength**: Design 1 performed exceptionally well with a moderate-high sweep (65.5 degrees). To explore a novel region and potentially increase vortex lift, this design will target a higher sweep angle (e.g., 72.5 degrees), pushing into a zone where leading-edge vortices are typically stronger and more stable at higher angles of attack.
*   **Implement aggressive washout at both root and tip**: The top designs consistently employ significant washout. To further optimize lift distribution and reduce induced drag, this design will apply maximum washout at the root (-10.0 degrees) and a substantial but slightly less aggressive washout at the tip (-8.0 degrees). This aims to shed vorticity more gradually and improve spanwise loading compared to uniform twist or less pronounced washout.
*   **Utilize a moderately cambered airfoil for foundational lift**: The database clearly shows that cambered airfoils (NACA 44xx, 24xx) yield much higher rewards than symmetric profiles. This design will use a NACA 24xx series, which provides moderate camber with the maximum camber at 40% chord, offering a good balance of lift augmentation without excessive pitching moment.
*   **Explore a moderately thick profile for robust design**: While Design 1 uses 12% thickness, this design will explore a slightly thicker profile (e.g., 18%). This allows for a different pressure distribution, potentially improving low-speed handling or providing more internal volume, without significantly increasing form drag compared to the dominant induced drag for a delta wing.
*   **Increase root chord for overall scale**: To explore the effect of a larger wing area, the root chord will be set towards the higher end of the range (e.g., 45.0 inches). This increases the overall lift potential, assuming the aerodynamic efficiency (CL/CDi) can be maintained or improved through the other parameter choices.
*   **Investigate positive dihedral**: Most top designs have neutral or negative dihedral. To diversify the design space and check if moderate positive dihedral could contribute to improved CL/CDi (perhaps through subtle changes in vortex interaction or spanwise flow not yet observed), a moderate positive dihedral (e.g., 7.5 degrees) will be specified.
