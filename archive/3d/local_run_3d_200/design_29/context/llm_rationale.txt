To advance the population and explore novelty while learning from the top performer, I will focus on integrating the most successful design principles from Design 2 with explorations into the higher sweep regime and thinner airfoils.

*   **Adopt Extreme Root Washout:** The most striking feature of Design 2's success was `twist_root = -10.0` combined with `twist_tip = -2.83`. This creates a strong unloading effect at the root, likely optimizing the spanwise lift distribution to reduce induced drag. I will replicate this `twist_root` value and maintain a similar positive gradient towards the tip.
*   **Explore High Sweep:** While Design 2 had moderate sweep, higher sweep designs (e.g., Design 5 at 73.47 deg) still performed relatively well. I will push the `le_sweep` even higher (closer to the upper limit) to investigate the effectiveness of the extreme root washout in a region where vortex lift is typically more dominant. This is a bold exploration to see if benefits from vortex lift can be maximized with optimal twist.
*   **Utilize a Thinner, Moderately Cambered Airfoil:** Design 2 used NACA 2411 (t=11), and Design 5 (symmetric) used NACA 0009 (t=9). Thinness generally reduces drag. I will choose NACA 2409, providing moderate camber (`naca_m=2`, `naca_p=4`) but with a thinner profile (`naca_t=9`) to potentially improve aerodynamic efficiency at higher speeds associated with higher sweep.
*   **Adjust Root Chord for High Sweep:** Higher sweep wings often have lower aspect ratios. To complement the higher sweep and potentially reduce overall drag, I will select a `root_chord_in` slightly smaller than the best performer, aiming for a more compact yet efficient design.
*   **Maintain Stable Dihedral:** Extreme dihedral values were detrimental to performance. I will select a small negative dihedral, consistent with the trend observed in better-performing designs, to maintain lateral stability without incurring significant performance penalties.
