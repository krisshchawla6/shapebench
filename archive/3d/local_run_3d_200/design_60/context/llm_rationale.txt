*   **Explore lower sweep range**: Designs with high sweep (70+ degrees) consistently yielded low rewards. Design 1's moderate sweep of ~65 degrees performed best. To diversify and creatively explore, I will significantly lower the `le_sweep` to the lower end of the range, around 52 degrees. This will test a regime where vortex lift is less dominant but induced drag might be significantly reduced, potentially leading to a better CL/CDi ratio.
*   **Maintain Cambered Airfoil**: The top-performing designs utilize cambered airfoils (NACA 4412, 2411), while symmetric airfoils performed poorly. To ensure adequate lift and efficiency, I will use a cambered airfoil profile (NACA 2410), which is slightly milder in camber than Design 1's 44xx, and thinner, to explore its interaction with lower sweep.
*   **Targeted Washout Distribution**: All successful designs employ washout. Design 1 had more root washout than tip (-7.1 vs -4.6). To explore differently, I will set a moderate `twist_root` (-5.5) and a more pronounced `twist_tip` washout (-8.5). This strategy aims to shed lift aggressively at the tip, potentially reducing tip vortices and induced drag, while maintaining sufficient lift generation from the root and mid-span.
*   **Moderate Root Chord with Low Sweep**: Design 1 had a moderate root chord (34.7 inches). With a significantly lower `le_sweep`, the overall planform area might increase. I will use a slightly smaller `root_chord_in` (28.0 inches) to manage the overall size and aspect ratio, ensuring it's not excessively large given the reduced sweep, which could otherwise lead to higher wetted area and skin friction drag.
*   **Positive Dihedral**: Dihedral values in the top designs were mostly negative or near zero. To explore a new configuration and its effect on stability and potentially induced drag, I will select a moderately positive `dihedral` (5.0 degrees), which is an untested variation for high-performing designs in the current population.
