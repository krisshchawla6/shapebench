*   **Exploration Strategy: Push to Positive Twist:** The most striking characteristic of the current population is the pervasive use of negative twist (washout), with the best design having moderate washout. This suggests that positive twist (washin) is a completely unexplored and high-leverage area. We will introduce significant washin to evaluate its impact on lift distribution and vortex strength.
*   **Explore Lower Sweep Boundary:** All current designs have `le_sweep` above 63 degrees. We will push towards the lower end of the sweep range (e.g., 50 degrees). This might reduce induced drag at lower angles of attack and interact uniquely with the washin.
*   **New Camber Distribution:** While `naca_m=4` worked well, the `naca_p=4` (max camber at 40% chord) has been dominant. We will combine `naca_m=4` with `naca_p=0` (max camber at leading edge). This aggressive leading-edge camber might complement the lower sweep and positive twist.
*   **Explore Higher Thickness:** `naca_t` has mostly been in the 8-18 range. We will explore a thicker airfoil (`naca_t=20`) which is on the higher end, to see its performance implications with lower sweep and washin.
*   **Moderate Root Chord:** We will select a moderate `root_chord_in` (e.g., 38 inches) to ensure the design scales reasonably without being overly small (like Design 6) or excessively large, allowing the other parameter changes to dominate the aerodynamic response.
*   **Moderate Positive Dihedral:** To complement the positive twist, we will explore a moderate positive dihedral (e.g., 5-8 degrees) which is also less explored than negative dihedral.
