*   **Exploration of High Sweep:** Most successful designs are in the mid-60s sweep. To explore new aerodynamic regimes, I will push the leading edge sweep significantly higher, towards 75.0 degrees, to investigate the effects of stronger vortex lift and its interaction with other parameters.
*   **Exploration of Small Root Chord:** All previous designs have root chords in the mid-to-high range (33-41 inches). To diversify the population and explore scaling effects, I will choose a much smaller root chord of 15.0 inches, pushing towards the lower boundary.
*   **Exploration of Extreme Positive Dihedral:** While most high-reward designs utilized anhedral, Design 6 showed moderate success with positive dihedral. To fully explore its impact on stability and performance, I will set a high positive dihedral of 13.0 degrees, near the upper boundary of the range.
*   **Exploration of Very Thin, Uncambered Airfoil:** The `naca_t` parameter has been mostly in the 10-16 range. To explore the lower boundary of thickness, I will specify `naca_t=7`. Combined with an uncambered profile (`naca_m=0`, `naca_p=0`), this pushes the airfoil choice into an underexplored, potentially high-speed efficient, region of the design space.
*   **Washout Strategy:** I will retain a clear washout pattern, similar to successful designs. I will set `twist_root = -8.0` and `twist_tip = -2.0`, providing a 6-degree washout differential (tip less negative than root), which has been consistently associated with better performance in the population.
