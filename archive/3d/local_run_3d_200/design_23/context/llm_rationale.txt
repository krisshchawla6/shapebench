The primary strategy is **Exploration**, focusing on underexplored regions and parameter combinations distinct from the existing population, particularly the high-reward Design 1.

*   **Underexplored Sweep**: Design 1 (63.12 deg) is the only high-reward design, with others performing poorly at higher sweeps (>70 deg). This indicates that the 45-60 degree sweep range is critically underexplored and might unlock new aerodynamic regimes for the delta wing. I will push towards a significantly lower sweep to explore this boundary.
*   **Novel Twist Distribution**: Design 1 features strong root washout and moderate tip washout. I want to test a radical departure: a moderate root washout but a *positive* twist (wash-in) at the tip. This counter-intuitive approach could create a highly non-linear load distribution, potentially improving vortex formation or reducing induced drag differently for a low-sweep wing.
*   **Extreme Dihedral**: The population has mostly explored slight anhedral or dihedral. To drastically explore the lateral stability characteristics and their interaction with lift, I will specify a high positive dihedral, which is currently unrepresented at significant magnitudes.
*   **Aggressive Camber for Lift**: With a lower sweep angle (potentially less reliance on vortex lift), maximizing direct lift from the airfoil itself becomes more critical. Therefore, using the most aggressive camber available (`naca_m=4`, `naca_p=4`) is a strategic choice to compensate and ensure sufficient CL.
*   **Moderate Root Chord and Thickness**: To allow the other exploratory parameters to dominate, I'll select a moderate root chord and a balanced thickness. This prevents confounding effects from extreme size or profile drag, allowing us to better isolate the impact of the other novel parameter choices.
