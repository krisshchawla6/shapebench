*   **Embrace Moderate-High Sweep for Vortex Lift:** The highest reward came from a moderate sweep (65.5 degrees). To explore enhanced vortex lift characteristics without going to the detrimental extremes observed in Design 5 (75.65 degrees), I will choose a sweep angle slightly higher, around 71.5 degrees. This aims to leverage strong leading-edge vortices while still being manageable.
*   **Maintain Moderate Root Chord:** The successful designs had root chords in the 33-40 inch range. I will select a value in the middle of this range (e.g., 37 inches) to ensure a reasonable wing size that is neither excessively large (leading to high wetted area/weight) nor too small.
*   **Implement Optimized Washout Twist:** The best design had `twist_root` significantly more negative than `twist_tip`. I will apply a similar strategy by setting `twist_root` to -9.0 degrees and `twist_tip` to -5.0 degrees. This promotes load relief at the tip, mitigating stall, and shifting lift distribution inboard, which can contribute to reducing induced drag.
*   **Select Cambered Airfoil and Moderate Thickness:** Symmetric airfoils performed poorly. NACA 44xx was successful. I will choose NACA 44xx to provide inherent lift characteristics. For thickness, I will select 14%, a moderate value, providing a good balance between structural integrity/internal volume and minimizing profile drag.
*   **Keep Dihedral Near Neutral:** Extreme dihedral values led to lower rewards. A slightly negative dihedral (-3.0 degrees) is chosen to maintain good lateral stability characteristics, similar to the top-ranked design, without introducing significant adverse aerodynamic effects.
*   **Novel Exploration:** This design combines a slightly increased sweep angle (compared to the best performing design) with a refined twist distribution and a proven cambered airfoil, aiming to discover if the benefits of higher sweep can be realized with careful aerodynamic parameter tuning.
