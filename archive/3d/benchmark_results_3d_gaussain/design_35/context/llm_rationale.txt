*   The current high-reward designs are tightly clustered around a moderate sweep (51-53 deg) and NACA 240X airfoils. To effectively explore the design space and find potentially new optima, I will venture into an **under-explored region of higher sweep angles**, specifically around 65-70 degrees. This will significantly alter vortex formation and lift characteristics.
*   All previous high-reward designs utilized a NACA 240X airfoil. To test the impact of increased camber, I will specify a **NACA 440X airfoil (m=4, p=4)**, meaning 4% max camber at 40% chord. This could potentially generate more lift, but its interaction with a higher sweep delta wing needs to be evaluated.
*   The highest reward design (Design 2) featured a less aggressive washout (-3.82 deg twist_tip) compared to others. Building on this insight, and combined with the new high-sweep configuration, I will set a **moderate positive twist at the root (around 6 degrees)** and a **mild negative twist at the tip (around -3 degrees)**. This attempts to balance load distribution across the wing while avoiding excessive drag from extreme washout.
*   All top designs have root chords between 29-31 inches. I will explore a **larger root chord (around 38 inches)** to increase the overall scale of the wing, which, when combined with higher sweep, could lead to different aerodynamic efficiencies or structural considerations.
*   Dihedral has been consistently high and positive (12.5-15 deg) in successful designs. To explore its impact in conjunction with higher sweep, I will opt for a **moderately positive dihedral (around 8 degrees)**, moving away from the extreme high values while still maintaining a positive stance.
*   NACA thickness has ranged from 6 to 9. I will choose a **NACA thickness of 10** to provide a slightly thicker wing, which is still aerodynamically reasonable and might offer structural advantages at a larger scale.
