*   **Explore higher sweep angles for diversity**: All current high-reward designs are clustered in the 48-51 degree sweep range. To explore a different aerodynamic regime and promote diversity as per the task instructions, this design will feature a much higher leading-edge sweep, set to `70.0` degrees. This will heavily rely on strong leading-edge vortices for lift and challenge the optimization's ability to balance vortex lift with increased induced drag at higher sweeps.
*   **Investigate symmetric airfoil (NACA 00xx)**: The current top designs exclusively use NACA 24xx airfoils. To explore an alternative and simplify the camber component, this design will use a symmetric NACA 00xx airfoil (`naca_m=0`, `naca_p=0`). This removes the inherent pitch-down moment from camber and allows for a cleaner assessment of the high-sweep and twist interaction.
*   **Optimize twist for high sweep and symmetric profile**: With a high sweep and symmetric airfoil, the optimal twist distribution will likely differ from the previous designs. I will explore a more aggressive positive root twist (`twist_root=7.0`) to generate lift closer to the root, combined with a moderate washout (`twist_tip=-7.0`) to maintain an efficient lift distribution across the highly swept wing and manage vortex behavior.
*   **Explore moderate negative dihedral**: All top designs use maximum positive dihedral. For diversity and to see if a different lateral stability characteristic might be beneficial for a highly swept wing, this design will use a moderate *negative* dihedral (`dihedral=-5.0`). This "anhedral" configuration might reduce effective spanwise flow effects or interact differently with the strong vortices.
*   **Adjust thickness and root chord for new regime**: For a highly swept wing, a slightly thinner airfoil might be beneficial to reduce profile drag, so I'll set `naca_t=12`. The `root_chord_in` will be set to `35.0` to provide a reasonable wing area for the high sweep, allowing for potentially higher lift generation without making the wing excessively large.
