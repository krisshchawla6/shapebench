The current top designs cluster around a specific set of parameters, particularly low sweep and NACA 24xx airfoils. To achieve "diversity and bold exploration," I will challenge one of these core assumptions while retaining elements that seem universally beneficial.

*   **Bold Exploration: Transition to a Symmetric Airfoil (NACA 00xx).** All previous top designs utilize a NACA 24xx airfoil. Introducing a NACA 00xx (symmetric, no camber) is a significant departure. This will test if high CL/CDi is achievable without inherent camber, relying more on angle of attack and twist for lift, potentially offering advantages in drag at certain conditions or simplifying manufacturing.
*   **Re-evaluate Sweep for Symmetric Profile:** Since a symmetric airfoil typically generates less lift at zero angle of attack compared to a cambered one, a higher effective aspect ratio could be beneficial to maintain lift-to-drag. Therefore, setting `le_sweep` to its absolute minimum of **45.0 degrees** will maximize the effective span and potentially reduce induced drag, compensating for the lack of camber.
*   **Reinforce Proven Twist Strategy:** The strong washout (`twist_root` positive, `twist_tip` negative) has been highly effective in previous designs for managing lift distribution and preventing tip stall. With a symmetric airfoil, precise load distribution through twist becomes even more critical. Thus, I will use the maximum proven values: `twist_root = 10.0` and `twist_tip = -10.0`.
*   **Explore Dihedral Maximum:** While positive dihedral is observed, setting `dihedral` to its maximum of **15.0 degrees** explores the upper boundary of this parameter. This could enhance lateral stability, and its interaction with a low-sweep, symmetric, highly-twisted wing is unknown, offering an opportunity for novel performance.
*   **Moderate Root Chord and Thickness:** A `root_chord_in` of **38.0 inches** is chosen as a moderate-to-large size, providing sufficient wing area while staying within the range of successful designs. A `naca_t` of **12** offers a balance between structural integrity and aerodynamic efficiency, being within the range of previous successful thickness values.
