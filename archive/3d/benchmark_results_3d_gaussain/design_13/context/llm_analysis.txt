The current design uses a moderate sweep (64.7 deg), moderate root chord (31.76 in), slight positive twist at the root (1.13 deg), and slight washout at the tip (-1.89 deg), with positive dihedral (6.22 deg) and a NACA 2411 airfoil (cambered, moderate thickness).

Key observations and potential issues for improvement:
*   **Vortex strength/stability**: While good, the vortex lift might be further enhanced by increasing sweep without reaching diminishing returns or breakdown.
*   **Induced Drag**: The washout at the tip is beneficial, but a more aggressive or carefully tuned twist distribution could lead to a more elliptical or optimal spanwise lift, further reducing induced drag.
*   **Camber/Thickness interaction**: The NACA 24xx series provides inherent lift, but exploring more aggressive camber (e.g., NACA 44xx) might offer higher CL at the cost of potential pitching moment shifts or drag increases, which needs to be balanced.
*   **Dihedral effects**: The predominantly positive dihedral in top designs contributes to stability, but exploring neutral or slightly negative dihedral could offer different stability characteristics or subtle aerodynamic benefits for pure performance, especially if combined with other changes.

1.  Explore the higher end of the sweep range (e.g., 70-75 degrees) to maximize vortex lift without incurring excessive induced drag.
2.  Implement a more pronounced washout at the tip (-3 to -6 degrees) combined with a neutral or slightly negative root twist to actively tailor the spanwise lift distribution.
3.  Investigate higher camber (e.g., NACA 44xx) to see if increased inherent lift can synergize with enhanced vortex lift from higher sweep.
4.  Test designs with neutral or slightly negative dihedral to broaden the understanding of its impact on CL/CDi beyond just stability.
