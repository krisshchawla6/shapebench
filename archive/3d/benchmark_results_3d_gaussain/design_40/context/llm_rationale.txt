*   **Exploration Strategy: Lower Sweep Boundary**: The current top designs cluster around 51-53 degrees sweep. To explore new aerodynamic regimes, I will push `le_sweep` to a much lower value (e.g., 48 degrees), closer to the minimum boundary of the parameter space. This tests a more "conventional" delta wing design, which might rely less on strong vortex lift and more on attached flow, potentially leading to different CL/CDi characteristics.
*   **Exploration Strategy: Symmetric Airfoil**: All previous designs used `NACA 24xx`. I will introduce a `NACA 00xx` airfoil (`naca_m=0`, `naca_p=0`). This eliminates airfoil camber as a lift-generating mechanism, forcing the design to rely solely on angle of attack, sweep, and twist for lift, providing a critical baseline comparison and understanding the contribution of camber.
*   **Exploration Strategy: Negative Dihedral**: All previous designs used positive dihedral. I will explore a significant negative `dihedral` (e.g., -10 degrees). This dramatically alters lateral stability characteristics and might influence vortex behavior or effective aspect ratio in an unexpected way, pushing the boundaries of the design space.
*   **Twist Strategy (Neutral Root, Moderate Washout)**: Given a symmetric airfoil, I will set `twist_root` to 0.0 degrees to evaluate the baseline performance without initial twist camber. I will retain a moderate negative `twist_tip` (washout, e.g., -5.0 degrees) to still help manage tip loading and reduce induced drag, which has been consistently beneficial in previous designs.
*   **Exploring Larger Scale & Thickness**: The current designs are relatively consistent in `root_chord_in` (around 30 inches) and `naca_t` (6-8%). I will increase `root_chord_in` to the upper mid-range (e.g., 40 inches) and `naca_t` to a thicker value (e.g., 12) to assess the impact of larger physical scale and structural volume, potentially aiding in better Reynolds number performance or structural integrity for a different flight regime, while staying within reasonable limits.
