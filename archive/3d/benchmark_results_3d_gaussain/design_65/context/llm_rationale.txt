*   **Exploration of Symmetric Airfoil (`NACA 00xx`):** All high-reward designs utilize cambered NACA 24xx airfoils. This new design will use a symmetric NACA 00xx profile (`naca_m=0, naca_p=0`), representing a significant, untried region in the parameter space. This aims to assess the potential for high CL/CDi purely through vortex lift and optimized twist, potentially reducing profile drag associated with camber.
*   **Intermediate Leading Edge Sweep:** The optimal sweep for current designs is 46-48 degrees, while a very high sweep (65.88 degrees) resulted in poor performance. This design explores an intermediate sweep of 58.0 degrees. This pushes into a region beyond the current optimum but avoids the extreme negative performance observed at 65 degrees, aiming to find new sweet spots for vortex generation with a symmetric airfoil.
*   **Aggressive Twist for Load Management:** To compensate for the lack of camber from the symmetric airfoil, an aggressive twist distribution will be used: maximum positive `twist_root` (10.0 deg) and maximum negative `twist_tip` (-10.0 deg). This configuration maximizes washout, intended to optimize spanwise lift distribution, control vortex formation, and potentially mitigate tip stall on a highly swept symmetric wing.
*   **Negative Dihedral Exploration:** All previous high-reward designs utilized high positive dihedral. This design will explore a moderate negative dihedral of -5.0 degrees. This tests an entirely different stability characteristic and its aerodynamic interactions with a symmetric, highly swept wing. This is a bold exploration into an underexplored region of the dihedral parameter.
*   **Moderate Root Chord and Thickness:** A `root_chord_in` of 30.0 inches maintains a practical wing size, similar to existing successful designs. A `naca_t` of 12 (forming a NACA 0012 profile) provides a reasonable thickness for a symmetric airfoil, balancing structural considerations with aerodynamic performance.
