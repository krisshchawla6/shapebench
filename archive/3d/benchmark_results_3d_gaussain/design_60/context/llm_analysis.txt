The provided designs consistently show high rewards (CL/CDi) for delta wings with relatively low leading edge sweep (46-51 degrees), positive root twist (4-10 degrees), strong negative tip twist (washout, -3 to -10 degrees), and generally positive dihedral (10-15 degrees). All successful designs leverage a NACA 24xx airfoil, indicating moderate camber (2%) at 40% chord, with thickness ranging from 7% to 14%.

Key observations:
*   **Low Sweep Dominance**: All top designs are clustered at the lower end of the `le_sweep` range (45-51 degrees). This suggests that moderate sweep might be effective for the given objective, but higher sweep angles (up to 80 degrees) are completely unexplored.
*   **Camber Preference**: The consistent use of NACA 24xx profiles indicates that a specific amount of camber (m=2, p=4) is found beneficial. Symmetric airfoils (m=0, p=0) and higher camber (m=4) are entirely unexplored.
*   **Washout**: Strong tip washout (`twist_tip` between -8 to -10 degrees) seems to be a consistent feature of high-performing designs, likely helping to distribute lift and prevent tip stall.
*   **Dihedral**: High positive dihedral is favored (often at the boundary of 15 degrees), which suggests that dihedral might be coupling positively with stability or induced drag characteristics for these designs.

Key design changes needed to improve diversity and exploration:
1.  **Explore higher sweep angles**: Test designs significantly beyond the 51-degree sweep seen in current top performers.
2.  **Investigate different NACA profiles**: Crucially, test symmetric airfoils (NACA 00xx) and potentially higher camber (NACA 4xXX) to understand their impact.
3.  **Vary dihedral more broadly**: Explore neutral or negative dihedral to understand its role.
4.  **Test thicker airfoils**: The current designs are mostly in the 7-14% thickness range; higher thicknesses (up to 24%) are untried.
