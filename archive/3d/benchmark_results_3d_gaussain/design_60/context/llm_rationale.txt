*   **Apply Exploration Strategy: High Sweep**: The current population is heavily clustered in the lower sweep range (45-51 degrees). To aggressively explore the design space, I will set `le_sweep` to a much higher value, around 68 degrees. This will allow the optimizer to assess the performance of strong vortex lift dominant designs, which inherently have different aerodynamic characteristics than lower sweep designs.
*   **Apply Exploration Strategy: Symmetric Airfoil**: All previous high-reward designs used NACA 24xx. A significant unexplored region is the use of symmetric airfoils (`naca_m=0`, which requires `naca_p=0`). This design will test a purely symmetric airfoil, removing the explicit camber component to see if vortex lift alone, combined with twist, can generate high CL/CDi, or if camber is critical for the current objective.
*   **Maintain Beneficial Twist Characteristics**: The population data consistently shows that strong washout (`twist_tip` negative, close to -10 deg) and positive root twist (`twist_root` positive) are effective. I will maintain these trends (e.g., `twist_tip` = -9.5, `twist_root` = 6.0) to provide a beneficial load distribution to the new high-sweep, symmetric design.
*   **Explore Neutral Dihedral**: The population strongly favors high positive dihedral. I will set `dihedral` to 0.0 to explore a neutral setting. This will isolate the effects of the high sweep and symmetric airfoil without the influence of dihedral, helping to understand its contribution or if it's primarily for stability (not directly rewarded here).
*   **Explore Higher Thickness**: The `naca_t` parameter has mostly been explored in the lower-to-mid range (7-14). I will increase the thickness to 18%, pushing towards the upper range boundary to understand the impact of thicker airfoils on induced drag versus potential structural benefits (not directly evaluated, but a common design trade-off).
*   **Moderate Root Chord**: I will select a `root_chord_in` value of 35.0 inches, which is slightly larger than the average of previous designs. This allows for a sufficiently large wing area with the increased sweep, providing a good baseline for evaluating the other exploratory parameters without pushing the chord to an extreme.
