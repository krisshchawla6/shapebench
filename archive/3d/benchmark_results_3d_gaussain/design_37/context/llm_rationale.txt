This extreme twist distribution aims to aggressively manage the spanwise load and delay tip stall, potentially compensating for the higher sweep.

This challenges the assumption that camber is always necessary for high CL/CDi in a delta wing, forcing the lift generation to rely purely on angle of attack, vortex lift, and the aggressive twist.
*   **Explore lower dihedral:** The current top designs use high positive dihedral. I will set the dihedral to -5.0 degrees. This explores the negative range, testing the robustness of the high sweep and aggressive twist combination to maintain performance even with parameters that typically reduce roll stability.

This will investigate the effect of a slightly thicker, symmetric wing on drag and lift efficiency, as a thicker section might offer structural benefits if performance remains high.
