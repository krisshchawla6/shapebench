*   **Novel Design Strategy**: The goal is to generate a diverse, novel design. The previous designs are clustered in a specific region (low sweep, strong washout, cambered). This new design will intentionally target a completely different region of the design space to explore new aerodynamic regimes.
*   **High Leading-Edge Sweep**: To explore designs fundamentally different from the current population, I will dramatically increase the `le_sweep` to 70.0 degrees. This explores the very slender, high-speed delta wing characteristics, where stable leading-edge vortices become critical for lift.
*   **Symmetric Airfoil Exploration**: Departing from the consistent use of NACA 24xx profiles, I will specify a `NACA 0015` airfoil (`naca_m=0`, `naca_p=0`, `naca_t=15`). This eliminates inherent camber, allowing assessment of performance primarily through angle of attack and vortex lift for this high-sweep configuration, offering a clean baseline for this new design philosophy.
*   **Moderate Twist Distribution**: Instead of the aggressive `twist_root=10.0` and `twist_tip=-10.0` seen in top designs, I will implement a more moderate washout: `twist_root=0.0` and `twist_tip=-5.0`. This aims for a more uniform load distribution across the span or a different interaction with the strong leading-edge vortices characteristic of high sweep, moving away from tip unloading purely for induced drag.
*   **Slight Anhedral**: All current high-reward designs feature positive dihedral. To further diversify and explore, a slight `dihedral` of `-7.0` (anhedral) will be chosen. This can influence roll stability and might have interesting interactions with the strong vortex systems of a high-sweep delta, potentially reducing tip-drag or altering stability characteristics.
*   **Larger Root Chord**: To ensure sufficient wing area for lift generation with the high sweep, I will specify a `root_chord_in` of 42.0 inches, which is on the larger side of the parameter space, allowing this more slender wing to generate meaningful lift.
