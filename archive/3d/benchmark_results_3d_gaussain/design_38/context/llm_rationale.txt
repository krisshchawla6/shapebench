This aims to combine the best elements for overall efficiency.
*   **Increased Sweep Exploration**: The top designs cluster around 51-53 degrees sweep. I will incrementally increase the sweep to 55.0 degrees. This explores the mid-range of higher sweep angles, aiming to enhance leading-edge vortex lift while not excessively penalizing induced drag, which might happen at much higher sweeps.
*   **Aggressive Twist Distribution**: Building on Design 4's effectiveness with extreme twists and Design 2's good washout, I will specify a high positive root twist (9.0 deg) and a significantly aggressive tip washout (-8.5 deg). This aims to achieve a highly optimized spanwise load distribution, working in conjunction with the increased sweep to control flow separation and enhance vortex stability, thus maximizing CL/CDi.
*   **Sustained High Dihedral**: Design 1 and 4 both achieved high rewards with maximum dihedral (15.0 deg). I will maintain a high positive dihedral of 14.5 degrees, assuming this continues to provide beneficial lateral stability characteristics or favorable aerodynamic interactions for high CL/CDi.
*   **Optimized Root Chord**: The highest performing designs have root chords in the 29-32 inch range. I will select a root chord of 30.0 inches to keep the overall wing scale consistent with successful designs.
*   **Proven Airfoil Profile**: The NACA 2407 airfoil used in Design 1 (and 4) performed exceptionally well. I will stick with the NACA 2407 profile, as its camber and moderate thickness have proven effective for lift generation with low profile drag in this delta wing context.
