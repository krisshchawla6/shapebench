1.  **Initial Exploration (Novel Design Strategy):** As this is the very first design, the strategy is to establish a strong initial candidate by leveraging well-known delta wing aerodynamic principles, rather than attempting to fix non-existent issues from previous designs. This approach ensures a diverse starting point for the evolutionary process.
2.  **Moderate-High Sweep for Vortex Lift:** Delta wings primarily generate lift through stable leading-edge vortices. A leading-edge sweep in the upper-middle range (e.g., 60-70 degrees) is generally optimal for promoting strong, stable vortex generation, leading to high lift coefficients without excessive induced drag penalties at typical flight conditions.
3.  **Washout for Improved Load Distribution:** Implementing washout (twist_tip significantly more negative than twist_root) helps to unload the wingtips. This reduces induced drag, improves stall characteristics by delaying tip stall, and creates a more elliptical-like lift distribution across the span, which is beneficial for CL/CDi.
4.  **Mild Camber for Lift Enhancement:** Introducing a small amount of positive camber (NACA m=2, p=4) can provide a noticeable increase in lift coefficient at a given angle of attack compared to a symmetric airfoil, without significantly increasing profile drag. This directly contributes to a higher CL.
5.  **Moderate Thickness for Efficiency:** A moderate airfoil thickness (e.g., 10-14%) strikes a good balance between structural viability and aerodynamic performance. It avoids the excessive drag of very thick airfoils while still allowing for internal structure, and it is less prone to adverse pressure gradients than extremely thin sections.
6.  **Balanced Root Chord and Dihedral:** A mid-range root chord (e.g., 30 inches) provides a reasonable overall scale for the wing. A small positive dihedral (tips up) contributes to positive lateral stability, which is generally desirable for controllable flight, without being extreme enough to introduce significant design challenges or performance penalties in this initial exploration phase.
