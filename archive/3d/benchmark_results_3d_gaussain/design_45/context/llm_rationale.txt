*   The top-performing designs (D1, D2, D3, D4) consistently utilize a combination of moderate leading-edge sweep (48-52 degrees), high positive twist at the root, significant washout at the tip, and high dihedral. This suggests a strong correlation between these parameters and high CL/CDi.
*   Applying the Hybrid Strategy, we will blend characteristics from the highest-reward designs (D1, D2, D4). We will target a sweep angle slightly higher than D1 but within the successful range of D2 and D4, aiming for an optimal vortex generation point.
*   We will maintain a high positive twist at the root (interpolated from D1 and D2) to encourage strong vortex formation near the fuselage, and a significant washout at the tip (interpolated from D1, D2, D4) to control spanwise load distribution and reduce induced drag.
*   The dihedral will be kept very high, close to the maximum observed in the top designs (D1, D2, D4), as this parameter consistently appears in the high-reward configurations.
*   To explore a novel aspect while staying within successful airfoil characteristics, we will maintain the NACA 240 camber profile (m=2, p=4) but will increase the thickness to NACA 2410. This is a step up from the 2406-2409 range seen in previous designs and might offer better structural performance or different aerodynamic benefits without sacrificing the proven camber.
*   The root chord will be set as an intermediate value from the top designs, balancing the overall wing size for the chosen sweep and airfoil.
