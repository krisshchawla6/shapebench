*   **Bold Exploration of High Sweep:** Previous designs are in the mid-60s degrees of sweep. To promote diversity and discover potentially higher-performing regions, I will push the leading-edge sweep (`le_sweep`) significantly higher, towards the upper limit. This maximizes the potential for strong leading-edge vortices, which are a primary source of lift for delta wings at higher angles of attack.
*   **Aggressive Washout for Drag Reduction:** With a high sweep, vortex lift is strong. To ensure this lift is generated efficiently and induced drag is minimized, an aggressive washout is critical. I will set a significantly positive `twist_root` and a significantly negative `twist_tip` to create a large wash-out effect, aiming for an optimal spanwise load distribution.
*   **Thinner Airfoil for Profile Drag Reduction:** To complement the high lift generated by strong vortices and aggressive washout, minimizing profile drag is crucial for CL/CDi. I will select a thinner airfoil (`naca_t`) than seen in previous designs to reduce skin friction and potentially improve high-speed characteristics (though not directly modeled here, it's a first principle).
*   **Maintain Moderate Camber:** While reducing thickness, I will keep a moderate camber (NACA 24xx) to provide some inherent lift at lower angles of attack, which can be beneficial before vortex lift fully develops, or to contribute to overall lift without excessive drag penalty.
*   **Mid-Range Root Chord:** A mid-range `root_chord_in` value will ensure the wing is of a reasonable scale, avoiding potential issues with very small (Reynolds effects) or very large designs, providing a stable baseline for the other parameter explorations.
*   **Explore Negative Dihedral:** While not directly affecting CL/CDi significantly, exploring negative `dihedral` allows for sampling a different region of the design space and investigating potential secondary effects or interactions with other aerodynamic features, contributing to overall diversity.
