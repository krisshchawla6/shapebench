1.  **Prioritize Significant Washout:** The highest performing design (Design 2) had negative `twist_tip` (washout), while Design 1 had positive. Washout is aerodynamically sound for delta wings as it unloads the tips, delays separation, and improves spanwise lift distribution, directly enhancing CL/CDi. I will select a distinctly negative `twist_tip` value to strongly enforce this characteristic.
2.  **Optimize for Strong Vortex Lift with Moderate-High Sweep:** Designs in the mid-60s sweep range showed the best performance. To continue exploring this productive region and potentially enhance vortex strength, I will choose a `le_sweep` value slightly higher than previous top performers but still within the effective range, ensuring robust vortex generation.
3.  **Leverage Cambered Airfoil and Balanced Thickness:** Cambered airfoils (NACA 24XX) have consistently yielded better rewards than symmetric ones. I will retain a cambered airfoil (`naca_m=2`, `naca_p=4`) and explore a moderate thickness (`naca_t=12`) to balance efficient lift generation with reasonable profile drag.
4.  **Explore Increased Scale and Moderate Dihedral:** Design 2 (highest reward) had a larger `root_chord_in` than Design 1. To see if a larger overall wing area, combined with optimized twist and sweep, can further boost performance, I will select a somewhat larger `root_chord_in`. A moderate positive `dihedral` will be maintained to ensure stability, similar to the better-performing designs.
5.  **Novel Combination for Exploration:** This design combines a slightly higher sweep with a definitively strong washout, a larger root chord, and a proven cambered airfoil. This particular combination aims to explore a novel region of the design space that adheres to robust aerodynamic principles for delta wings, moving beyond incremental tweaks of existing designs.
