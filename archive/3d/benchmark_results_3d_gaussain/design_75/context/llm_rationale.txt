*   **Exploit minimal sweep**: The highest reward (Design 1) came from `le_sweep = 45.0`. We will maintain a very low sweep value, slightly perturbed from the absolute minimum, to confirm its benefit and ensure robustness.
*   **Maximize twist aggressiveness**: All top designs use strong positive root twist and maximal tip washout. Design 1 had `twist_root=10.0` and `twist_tip=-10.0`. This aggressive twist profile (leading to an unloaded tip) is crucial for an efficient delta wing, effectively reducing induced drag and improving lift distribution. We will replicate this strong twist.
*   **Maintain moderate dihedral**: The best performing designs have positive dihedral between 7-12 degrees. Design 1 had 7.33 degrees. We will select a value in this proven moderate range to provide stability without excessive drag.
*   **Utilize proven camber profile**: All top performers (Design 1-6) used `naca_m=2` and `naca_p=4`. This NACA 24xx profile is clearly effective for the objective, providing camber for lift while maintaining efficiency. We will retain this configuration.
*   **Target lower thickness**: While `naca_t` varied, lower values (like 9 in Design 1 and 2) are generally associated with lower profile drag. We will select a value at the lower end of the observed effective range for better aerodynamic efficiency.
*   **Adjust root chord**: The `root_chord_in` varied across high performers. Design 1 had 29.25, while Design 6 (also high reward) had 33.72. We will select a value slightly higher than Design 1 to explore a larger wing area within the optimal sweep range, potentially increasing overall lift while maintaining the optimal span loading.
