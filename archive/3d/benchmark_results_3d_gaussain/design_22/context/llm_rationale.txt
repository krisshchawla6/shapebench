This new design employs a Hybrid Strategy, blending successful traits from Design 1 (highest reward), Design 3, and Design 4 to explore a promising region of the design space.

*   **Blending Sweep:** Design 1 had the best reward with a moderate sweep (55.64), while Designs 3 (64.75) and 4 (66.21) showed reasonable performance at higher sweeps. The new design targets a sweep of 61.5 degrees, aiming to leverage increased vortex lift potential found in D3/D4 while staying within a range that has shown good performance, distinct from the leading design.
*   **Intermediate Root Chord:** Design 1 had a root chord of 28.09 inches, and Designs 3/4 had slightly larger chords (31.76, 34.10). The new design adopts a root chord of 31.0 inches, interpolating between these successful sizes to slightly increase wing area for potential lift generation without excessively increasing induced drag, especially with the higher sweep.
*   **Moderate Washout:** Design 1 had a significant washout (3.15/-3.15), and D3 had a milder washout (1.13/-1.89). The new design uses a balanced moderate washout (twist_root=2.0, twist_tip=-2.5) to ensure effective spanwise load distribution and prevent tip stall, a critical feature for delta wings, adapting the successful washout concept to the slightly higher sweep.
*   **Reduced Dihedral:** Design 1 had a high dihedral (10.89 degrees), while D3 had 6.22 degrees. To explore the impact of reduced lateral stability on CL/CDi and potentially improve effective lift, this design uses a dihedral of 5.5 degrees, significantly lower than D1 but still positive, allowing for a different balance of stability and performance.
*   **Fine-tuned Airfoil Thickness:** All top designs utilize a NACA 24xx profile. Design 1 used NACA 2408. Designs 3 and 4 used 2411 and 2414, respectively, which were associated with slightly lower rewards. To explore the optimal thickness for the chosen sweep and other parameters, this design utilizes NACA 2409, a slight increment from the best performing D1, maintaining the effective camber while testing a marginally thicker section to see if it can improve performance in the explored sweep range.
