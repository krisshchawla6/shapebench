*   **Explore high sweep angles (Underexplored Region)**: The existing top designs are clustered at the absolute minimum `le_sweep` (45 degrees). This indicates a significant unexplored region between 50 and 80 degrees. I will push towards a high sweep angle (e.g., 72.5 degrees) to investigate the potential for strong vortex lift to contribute to CL/CDi, a fundamentally different aerodynamic approach than that explored so far.
*   **Test symmetric airfoil (NACA 00xx) (Underexplored Region)**: All previous top designs use a cambered NACA 24xx airfoil. To diversify and understand how a high-sweep, vortex-dominated wing performs without explicit airfoil camber, I will specify a symmetric airfoil (`naca_m=0`, `naca_p=0`). This will help disentangle the contributions of vortex lift and geometric camber.
*   **Moderate root chord for high sweep**: While existing designs show good performance with moderate to high `root_chord_in`, a very high sweep might benefit from a slightly more compact root chord for structural efficiency and reduced wetted area, so I'll select a value in the mid-to-high range that is not maximal.
*   **Adjust twist for high sweep & no camber**: With a high sweep and symmetric airfoil, the lift distribution will be different. I will use a moderate positive `twist_root` and a strong, but not maximum, `twist_tip` washout to maintain a good load distribution and mitigate induced drag, while allowing the high sweep to dictate much of the lift generation.
*   **Maintain positive dihedral and moderate thickness**: Keeping `dihedral` in the positive range maintains lateral stability, which has been consistently favored. `naca_t` will be set to a moderate value to ensure structural feasibility and reduce profile drag without being overly thin for a delta wing.
