The Hybrid Strategy will focus on blending the characteristics of the top-performing designs (Design 1, 2, and 3) to create a novel configuration that integrates their successful traits while exploring slightly varied parameters.

*   **Combine successful sweep and chord trends:** Designs 1, 2, and 3 all have sweep angles in the ~65-66 degree range and root chords between 27 and 32 inches. I will choose a sweep of `66.5` degrees, slightly above the average of the top three, and a root chord of `33.0` inches, slightly larger than Design 1 but well within a reasonable range, to potentially increase overall lift without excessive drag penalties.
*   **Integrate effective twist strategies:** Design 1 and 3 feature positive root twist, while Design 1 and 2 employ washout at the tip. I will combine a positive `twist_root` of `1.5` degrees (an interpolation between Design 1 and 3) with a moderate `twist_tip` of `-3.0` degrees (a value between Design 1 and Design 2's strong washout) to achieve a balanced lift distribution and delay tip stall.
*   **Leverage consistent dihedral success:** Designs 1 and 2 had higher dihedral values (6.22 and 8.70 degrees) and performed well. I will select a `dihedral` of `7.5` degrees, an intermediate-high value, to maintain good lateral stability.
*   **Maintain proven camber and explore thickness:** All top designs utilize NACA `24xx` profiles. I will stick with `naca_m=2` and `naca_p=4` due to their consistent success. For `naca_t`, the successful designs range from 7 to 14. I will choose `12`, a moderate thickness, slightly higher than Design 1, aiming for a balance between aerodynamic efficiency and potential structural benefits, without incurring excessive drag.
