*   **Boldly explore higher sweep angles**: The top performing designs are all clustered in a narrow, lower sweep range (51-53 degrees). Design 5 (59.9 degree sweep) performed poorly, but it also had lower root twist and dihedral. To achieve diversity and test a different aerodynamic strategy, I will significantly increase the leading edge sweep to the upper-mid range (e.g., 65-70 degrees). This aims to leverage potentially stronger vortex lift characteristics of higher sweep delta wings.
*   **Maintain and maximize beneficial twist and washout**: The pattern of high positive root twist (near 10 degrees) and strong negative tip twist (washout, near -10 degrees) is strongly correlated with high rewards. This suggests a crucial role in optimizing lift distribution and mitigating induced drag. To synergize with the higher sweep, I will maximize both parameters: `twist_root = 10.0` and `twist_tip = -10.0`.
*   **Maximize dihedral for stability/performance correlation**: High positive dihedral (near 15 degrees) is also a consistent feature of the top-performing designs. While primarily a stability parameter, it appears to have a positive correlation with CL/CDi in this design space. I will set `dihedral = 15.0` to maintain this successful characteristic.
*   **Slightly increase root chord**: High-reward designs feature a root chord around 30-31 inches. To explore a slightly larger overall scale and its interaction with the much higher sweep, I will set `root_chord_in` to a moderately larger value (e.g., 38 inches). This increases wing area and could influence how the increased sweep affects total lift and drag.
*   **Refine airfoil thickness**: The highest reward came from NACA 2408. To explore a slight variation while maintaining the successful camber characteristics, I will choose NACA 2407. This slightly thinner airfoil might offer reduced profile drag, which could be beneficial, especially if the higher sweep and larger root chord generate sufficient lift.
