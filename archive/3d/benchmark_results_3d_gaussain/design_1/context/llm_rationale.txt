Given the absence of a design database, the "Hybrid Strategy" will be interpreted as combining fundamental aerodynamic principles to generate a diverse and theoretically sound initial exploration. I will aim to blend characteristics known to contribute to both high lift and reasonable drag efficiency in delta wings.

*   **Combine moderate-to-high sweep with effective twist**: High sweep is excellent for vortex lift. To maximize its efficiency and reduce induced drag, I will pair a relatively high sweep (e.g., ~70 degrees) with a significant washout (positive root twist, negative tip twist). This combines the "high vortex lift" potential with an "optimized load distribution" strategy.
*   **Integrate moderate camber for lift enhancement**: To improve overall lift coefficient without excessive pitching moments, I will introduce a moderate NACA camber profile (e.g., NACA 24xx). This represents blending the "pure delta (vortex lift)" approach with "airfoil-enhanced lift."
*   **Select balanced thickness and root chord**: A mid-range thickness (e.g., 12-16%) will balance structural robustness with aerodynamic drag. A moderate root chord ensures a reasonable wing area and aspect ratio, allowing for a good representation of the design space without being too small or too large.
*   **Explore slight positive dihedral**: While delta wings primarily rely on sweep for stability, a small positive dihedral can contribute to lateral stability without significant penalty, providing a diverse stability characteristic.
