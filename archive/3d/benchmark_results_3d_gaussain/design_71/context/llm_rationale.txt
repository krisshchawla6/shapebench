The exploitation strategy demands refining the successful patterns observed in the high-reward designs. My new design will build on the strengths of Design 1, 2, 3, and 4, which exhibit a clear synergy of parameters leading to high CL/CDi.

*   The top performers overwhelmingly feature `le_sweep` at the lowest end of the range (45-48 degrees). I will maintain this crucial characteristic, setting `le_sweep` to a value very close to the minimum to maximize benefits from reduced induced drag.
*   The extreme twist distribution (`twist_root` near 10.0, `twist_tip` near -10.0) is a hallmark of the highest-ranked designs. This suggests excellent load distribution. I will slightly adjust these values from the maximums, exploring a minor refinement to the aggressive twist, aiming for a potentially more optimized spanwise lift curve.
*   All high-reward designs utilize a cambered NACA 24xx airfoil. I will keep `naca_m=2` and `naca_p=4` to leverage this proven lift-enhancing profile. I will select a `naca_t` of 12 (NACA 2412), exploring a slightly thicker section than Design 1 (2409) but thinner than Design 3 (2414) to see if this intermediate thickness provides a better balance.
*   `root_chord_in` values in the 29-34 inch range have shown consistent success. I will choose a value in the middle of this range (e.g., 32.5 inches) to ensure an appropriate wing scale, distinct from previous high performers but within their successful scope.
*   Dihedral values between 7 and 12 degrees are common among the top designs. While Design 1 had 7.33, Design 4 (rank 3) had 11.81. I will set the dihedral to a value around 11.0 degrees, higher than Design 1 but not at the maximum, to explore a slightly different lateral stability configuration without venturing into less successful max-dihedral territory.
