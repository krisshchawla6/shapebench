*   **Boldly explore a symmetric airfoil:** All top designs use NACA 24xx (cambered). Shifting to a NACA 00xx (symmetric airfoil by setting `naca_m=0, naca_p=0`) is a significant departure. This forces the design to rely purely on angle of attack and geometry for lift, potentially revealing novel efficient configurations when combined with aggressive twist.
*   **Combine moderate-high sweep with aggressive twist:** While the best design had lower sweep, many other designs had moderate-high sweep but relatively less aggressive twist or less optimized twist distributions. I will try `le_sweep=65.0` (still within typical delta range, but higher than Design 1) and pair it with a strong wash-in `twist_root=7.0` and a strong washout `twist_tip=-7.0`. This is pushing the twist magnitudes beyond what was seen in the top designs (e.g., Design 1: +/- 3.15, Design 3: +/- 5.5).
*   **Investigate anhedral (`dihedral` < 0):** All top designs have positive dihedral. Introducing anhedral (`dihedral=-5.0`) is a bold exploration. While it might reduce inherent stability, it could potentially offer a slight induced drag benefit or simply reveal a region of the design space previously unvisited for CL/CDi optimization, adhering to the "diversity and bold exploration" mandate.
*   **Maintain a moderate `root_chord_in` and `naca_t`:** Given that the best designs had moderate chord lengths (28-33 inches) and thinner airfoils (8-11% thickness), I will select `root_chord_in=32.0` and `naca_t=10`. This leverages previous successes in scaling and profile drag, while keeping the primary exploration focus on airfoil type, twist, sweep, and dihedral.
