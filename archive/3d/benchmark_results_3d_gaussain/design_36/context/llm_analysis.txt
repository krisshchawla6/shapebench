The top-performing designs (Rank 0-3, 5) consistently exhibit a moderate leading edge sweep (around 51-53 degrees), a strong positive twist at the root (7.5-10 degrees), significant washout at the tip (-3.8 to -10 degrees), high positive dihedral (12.5-15 degrees), and a NACA 24XX airfoil with low thickness (6-7%). This combination appears to maximize the CL/CDi ratio, likely through optimized lift distribution, effective vortex management, and reduced induced drag. The NACA 24XX series provides a modest amount of camber (2% max camber at 40% chord), contributing to lift. Designs with higher sweep (64-66 degrees, e.g., Designs 4 and 6) showed significantly lower rewards, suggesting that the current twist and camber strategy is not effective at these higher sweep angles, or that high sweep itself imposes a significant drag penalty.

**Key design changes needed to improve designs in the population or explore new high-performance regions:**
1.  **Explore the extremes of sweep**: Systematically test both very low (45-50 deg) and very high (70-80 deg) sweep angles, rather than just the mid-range.
2.  **Vary twist distribution radically**: Investigate combinations beyond the "positive root, negative tip" pattern, such as negative root twist, or less aggressive washout/washin.
3.  **Test symmetric airfoils (NACA 00XX)**: Evaluate performance without inherent camber to understand the isolated effects of sweep and twist on vortex lift.
4.  **Explore zero or negative dihedral**: Assess the impact of different lateral stability configurations on overall performance.
