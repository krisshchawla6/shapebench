*   **Bold Exploration of High Sweep**: The current top designs are clustered at moderate sweep (50-53 deg). To significantly diversify the population and explore new aerodynamic regimes, this design pushes the `le_sweep` to a very high value (75.0 deg), approaching the upper limit. This will test the wing's performance in a regime where vortex lift is expected to be more dominant, but potentially at the cost of higher induced drag if not managed effectively.
*   **Symmetric Airfoil (NACA 0012)**: To isolate the aerodynamic effects of this extreme sweep and twist combination, a symmetric airfoil (`naca_m=0`, `naca_p=0`) will be used. This removes the inherent camber of the NACA 24XX series found in top designs, forcing the design to rely purely on geometric twist and vortex generation for lift distribution, providing a clearer understanding of these parameters' roles.
*   **Inverted Twist Gradient**: Instead of the high positive `twist_root` and strong washout `twist_tip` of the top designs, this design adopts a slightly negative `twist_root` (-2.5 deg) and a very mild washout `twist_tip` (-0.5 deg). This explores a fundamentally different spanwise load distribution strategy for high sweep, aiming to prevent root stall or manage vortex behavior differently.
*   **Negative Dihedral for Diversity**: All top designs feature high positive `dihedral`. This design incorporates a negative `dihedral` (-7.5 deg) to explore its impact on roll stability and overall CL/CDi. This is a significant deviation and provides critical data on a less explored region of the design space.
*   **Moderate Root Chord and Thickness**: The `root_chord_in` is set to a slightly larger but still moderate value (38.0 inches) compared to the top designs to maintain scale, while `naca_t` is set to a moderate 12%. This avoids extreme geometric scaling or overly thin airfoils, focusing the novelty on sweep, twist, and airfoil camber.
