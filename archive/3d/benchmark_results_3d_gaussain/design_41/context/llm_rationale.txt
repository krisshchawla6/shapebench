*   **Explore significantly higher sweep (e.g., 70 degrees)**: The current top designs are clustered around 51-55 degrees sweep. To achieve novel designs and explore new aerodynamic regimes, I will push the sweep much higher towards the upper limit. High sweep can enhance vortex lift, which is characteristic of delta wings and can maintain attached flow at higher angles of attack, potentially improving CL/CDi in a different way than moderate sweep designs.
*   **Implement maximum washout at the tip (`twist_tip` = -10.0)**: To complement the high sweep and ensure optimal load distribution, I will combine a strong positive root twist with the maximum allowable washout at the tip. This aggressive twist gradient should work to unload the wingtips, reduce induced drag, and delay tip stall, which is critical for highly swept wings.
*   **Utilize a higher camber profile (NACA 4xxx series)**: All previous top designs used NACA 24xx airfoils. To explore a different lift generation mechanism, I will specify a NACA 44xx airfoil. This higher max camber percentage should provide increased inherent lift, which, when combined with high sweep and tailored twist, could unlock new performance potential for CL/CDi.
*   **Maintain a moderate root chord and thickness**: While exploring extreme sweep and twist, I will keep the `root_chord_in` around the values seen in successful previous designs (e.g., 30 inches) to maintain a comparable wing size and avoid introducing an additional, unrelated variable's extreme effects. A moderate `naca_t` (e.g., 10) will balance structural considerations with potential drag penalties for a highly cambered and swept wing.
*   **Moderate positive dihedral**: Positive dihedral has been a consistent feature in the higher-ranking designs. While not a primary driver for CL/CDi, maintaining a moderate positive dihedral will ensure adequate lateral stability, allowing the search to focus on the lift-to-drag characteristics without compromising overall stability.
