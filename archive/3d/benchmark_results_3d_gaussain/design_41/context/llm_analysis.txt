The representative design (Design 1) with `le_sweep=51.6`, `root_chord_in=29.1`, `twist_root=7.5`, `twist_tip=-3.8`, `dihedral=15.0`, and NACA 2407 achieved a reward of 35.5280. The Cp distribution shows a well-developed low-pressure region over the upper surface, indicative of good lift generation, likely benefiting from both airfoil camber and leading-edge vortex lift. The pressure recovery towards the trailing edge appears generally smooth. The high positive dihedral suggests good lateral stability, which might indirectly contribute to the objective by ensuring stable flight conditions for performance evaluation.

However, the existing designs cluster around a moderate sweep angle (51-55 degrees). While effective, this might not fully exploit the potential of vortex lift at higher sweep angles or for different flight envelopes. The twist distribution is also relatively conservative compared to the full range available for washout at the tip.

Key design changes needed to improve designs in the population and explore novel areas:
1.  **Increase leading edge sweep**: Explore significantly higher sweep angles to emphasize vortex lift generation and potentially reduce induced drag at higher CL.
2.  **More aggressive twist distribution**: Investigate the effect of maximum possible washout at the tip, potentially combined with a strong positive root twist, to optimize spanwise load distribution more effectively for different sweep angles.
3.  **Vary camber profile**: Explore a higher maximum camber percentage (e.g., NACA 4xxx series) to enhance lift, which could interact differently with increased sweep and aggressive twist.
4.  **Optimize thickness for higher sweep/camber**: Adjust thickness to complement the potentially higher lift and sweep, aiming for a balance between structural needs and aerodynamic efficiency.
