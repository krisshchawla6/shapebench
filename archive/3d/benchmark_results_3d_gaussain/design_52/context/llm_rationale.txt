*   **Explore High Sweep Regime**: The current population excels in the lower sweep range. To advance the population and uncover new optima, we must rigorously explore the higher sweep angles (e.g., 60-80 degrees). High sweep can enhance vortex lift at higher angles of attack, which might offer a new performance frontier if induced drag is manageable. This design will feature a substantially higher sweep (e.g., 70 degrees).
*   **Investigate Different Twist for High Sweep**: The dominant strategy of strong positive root twist and extreme washout might not be optimal for very high sweep wings. For a highly swept wing, the leading edge vortex itself generates significant lift. A more neutral or less aggressive twist might allow the vortex system to develop more cleanly or distribute load differently. Let's try a neutral `twist_root` (0.0 deg) and a moderate `twist_tip` washout (-5.0 deg).
*   **Test Higher Camber Airfoil (NACA 44xx)**: The top designs exclusively use NACA 24xx. Introducing higher camber (NACA 44xx, i.e., `naca_m=4`, `naca_p=4`) could provide additional lift. This needs to be tested in combination with high sweep to see if the benefits of camber outweigh potential drag penalties or adverse interactions with the vortex system.
*   **Examine Anhedral/Neutral Dihedral**: All high-performing designs exhibit significant positive dihedral. We will explore anhedral (negative dihedral, e.g., -5.0 degrees) to understand its aerodynamic impact, especially in combination with high sweep, as it affects spanwise flow and vortex stability, potentially trading some stability for slight induced drag reduction or a different lift distribution.
*   **Moderate Root Chord and Thickness**: To avoid extreme aspect ratios for the highly swept wing and ensure reasonable structural depth, a moderate `root_chord_in` (35.0 inches) and `naca_t` (14) will be selected. This is slightly larger/thicker than average, allowing for a robust exploration of the high sweep, high camber, and anhedral combination.
