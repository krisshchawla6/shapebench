This new design will employ a **Hybrid Strategy**, blending successful attributes from the top-performing designs (Design 1, 3, and 5) to create a novel configuration with a high likelihood of good performance.

*   **Blend Successful Sweep:** The top three designs (1, 3, 5) all feature leading edge sweeps between 64.75 and 65.95 degrees. This indicates a sweet spot for balancing vortex lift and induced drag. The new design will target an intermediate sweep of **65.5 degrees** to stay within this proven high-reward region.
*   **Combine Twist Strategies:** Design 1 had a slight washout (-1.89 deg), Design 5 had a more significant washout (-5.32 deg), while Design 3 had a positive tip twist (1.88 deg) but still performed well. The new design will explore a **moderate washout of -3.0 degrees** to blend the benefits of controlled load distribution from Design 1 and the slightly stronger washout of Design 5, while not going to the extreme washout of lower-ranked designs. Root twist will be kept slightly positive at **0.5 degrees**, similar to successful designs.
*   **Reinforce Positive Dihedral:** All high-reward designs show positive dihedral, with Design 5 having the highest at 8.7 degrees. Negative dihedral was associated with poor performance. The new design will target a strong positive dihedral of **7.0 degrees** to enhance stability, drawing from the higher values seen in top performers.
*   **Optimize Root Chord and NACA Profile:** Root chord values in top designs range from ~27 to ~31 inches. Designs with very large chords (40.95 in for Design 6) performed worse. The new design will use a moderate root chord of **35.0 inches**, slightly larger than the top designs but well below the poor performers, to explore potential benefits of increased area without excessive induced drag. For the airfoil, the NACA 24xx series (2% camber, 40% max camber position) has consistently led to high rewards. We will select NACA **2412**, varying the thickness (12% chord) from the previous top designs (10, 11, 14) to explore this parameter within a proven camber family.
