*   **Exploration of High Sweep:** The current successful designs are clustered in the 50-55 degree sweep range. Design 3, at ~60 degrees, performed poorly, but this doesn't invalidate higher sweeps. I will explore a significantly higher `le_sweep` (e.g., 68.0 degrees) to push into an under-explored region of the design space, investigating the potential benefits of stronger vortex lift at more aggressive sweep angles.
*   **Introduction of Symmetric Airfoil:** All existing high-reward designs use a NACA 24xx profile. This is a major unexplored characteristic. I will introduce a symmetric airfoil (`naca_m=0, naca_p=0`) to determine if inherent camber is strictly necessary for high CL/CDi, especially in conjunction with higher sweep. This tests a fundamentally different aerodynamic strategy.
*   **Adjusted `root_chord_in` for High Sweep:** With a higher sweep, the wing's effective aspect ratio changes. I will specify a slightly smaller `root_chord_in` (e.g., 25.0 inches) to explore its interaction with the high sweep and symmetric airfoil. This explores the lower end of the chord range compared to previous successful designs.
*   **Moderate Twist for Exploration:** With a high sweep and symmetric airfoil, the optimal twist distribution may differ from previous designs. I'll maintain a positive `twist_root` (5.0 degrees) for general lift and a moderate washout `twist_tip` (-2.0 degrees) to prevent tip stall while exploring a less aggressive twist difference than some top performers, seeing its effect with the new sweep/airfoil combination.
*   **Mid-range `dihedral`:** While max dihedral appeared beneficial in some previous designs, I'll set `dihedral` to a moderate positive value (8.0 degrees). This maintains a positive effect on stability while allowing the impact of the new sweep and airfoil to be more pronounced without being overshadowed by an extreme dihedral.
*   **Increased `naca_t` with Symmetric Airfoil:** Current designs use 6-8% thickness. With a symmetric airfoil, I'll try a slightly higher thickness (e.g., 10%) to see how it affects drag characteristics and structural volume potential in this new design configuration.
