*   **Boldly explore a significantly higher leading edge sweep (e.g., ~65 degrees):** The current population's best designs are clustered in the 46-48 degree sweep range. To diversify the search and explore a different aerodynamic regime where vortex lift becomes more dominant, I will choose a substantially higher sweep. This aims to discover new optima that might leverage high-sweep characteristics more effectively.
*   **Introduce a symmetric airfoil (NACA 00xx):** All current high-reward designs utilize the NACA 24xx cambered airfoil. By switching to a NACA 00xx symmetric profile, I aim to understand the pure aerodynamic contribution of the delta wing's planform geometry (sweep, twist) and its associated vortex lift, isolating it from the effects of airfoil camber. This is a creative exploration to uncover fundamental relationships.
*   **Balance twist distribution for a high-sweep, symmetric configuration:** With a higher sweep and no airfoil camber, the twist distribution becomes even more critical for managing lift and drag. I will use a moderate positive root twist (~5 degrees) to maintain some load at the root, combined with a significant, but not maximum, tip washout (~-7 degrees) to offload the tips, prevent premature stall, and reduce induced drag, adapting to the new planform.
*   **Maintain a mid-range root chord and moderate thickness:** To ensure a reasonable scale and structural viability for the new configuration, the root chord will be set in the mid-range of previous successful designs. A moderate thickness (e.g., 12%) will be chosen for the symmetric airfoil to offer a good balance between structural requirements and minimizing profile drag.
*   **Explore a moderately positive dihedral:** While previous top designs favored high positive dihedral, I will select a moderately positive dihedral (~7 degrees). This choice provides some lateral stability while exploring its interaction with the high-sweep, symmetric airfoil configuration, testing if a less extreme dihedral can still contribute positively to performance in this new design space.
