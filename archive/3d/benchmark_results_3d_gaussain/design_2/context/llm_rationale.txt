Given that this is the first design iteration and no previous designs or performance data are available, the strategy is to conduct a bold exploration of the parameter space. The goal is to generate a diverse and novel design that can serve as a strong initial data point for the evolutionary process, testing a combination of features that could lead to high performance.

*   **Aggressive Sweep Exploration**: To leverage the characteristic vortex lift of delta wings, I will set `le_sweep` to a high value (e.g., 75.0 degrees). This explores the upper end of the sweep range, aiming for strong leading-edge vortices to generate lift.
*   **Effective Twist Distribution**: I will combine a small positive `twist_root` (e.g., 2.0 degrees) with a significant negative `twist_tip` (e.g., -5.0 degrees). This creates wash-out, which is crucial for controlling spanwise load distribution, preventing tip stall, and potentially improving induced drag by shedding load more efficiently. This explores both positive and negative twist values.
*   **Moderate Camber Introduction**: To explore the effect of camber on lift augmentation, I will select `naca_m = 2` (2% max camber) and `naca_p = 4` (camber at 40% chord). This investigates how a moderately cambered airfoil section contributes to overall lift beyond what the planform alone provides.
*   **Thin Airfoil for Efficiency**: A `naca_t` of 8 (8% thickness) is chosen to keep the airfoil relatively thin. This generally leads to lower profile drag, which is beneficial for maximizing the CL/CDi ratio, while still allowing for some structural depth. This explores the lower end of the thickness range.
*   **Neutral Dihedral and Mid Root Chord**: `dihedral` will be set to 0.0 to isolate the aerodynamic effects of other parameters without introducing lateral stability complexities in this initial exploration. A `root_chord_in` of 30.0 inches provides a reasonable wing scale for simulation, neither too small nor too large.
