As this is the initial design generation, the "Hybrid Strategy" (which relies on examining existing designs) cannot be directly applied. Instead, the focus is on creating a robust, reasonably performing baseline design that will allow subsequent evolutionary steps to build upon. My reasoning for this first design is as follows:

*   **Establish a Baseline**: Generate a design that is likely to have decent performance without being at the extreme ends of the parameter space. This provides a central point for the optimizer to explore from.
*   **Moderate Sweep**: A leading-edge sweep of 65.0 degrees is chosen. This is in the middle-to-high range, balancing the benefits of vortex lift (which increases with sweep) against potential increases in induced drag, providing a good starting point for typical delta wing performance.
*   **Balanced Size**: A `root_chord_in` of 30.0 inches provides a moderate wing size, allowing for reasonable aerodynamic forces without being excessively large or small, which could lead to very low or very high aspect ratios.
*   **Controlled Twist for Load Distribution**: A neutral `twist_root` (0.0) combined with a slight `twist_tip` of -2.0 degrees (washout) is introduced. This minor washout at the tip helps to reduce tip loading, improve lift distribution, and potentially mitigate early tip stall, which is a common issue for highly swept wings.
*   **Symmetric Airfoil, Moderate Thickness**: Starting with a symmetric NACA airfoil (`naca_m=0`, `naca_p=0`) allows us to assess the performance primarily driven by planform and twist before introducing camber. A `naca_t` of 12 ensures a reasonable thickness for structural integrity and typical aerodynamic efficiency.
*   **Neutral Dihedral**: A `dihedral` of 0.0 degrees is selected as a neutral starting point for lateral stability. Extremes in dihedral are often used for specific stability requirements but are not ideal for an initial general-purpose baseline.
