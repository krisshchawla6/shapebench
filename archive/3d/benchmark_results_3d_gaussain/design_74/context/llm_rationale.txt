*   The top-performing designs, especially Design 2, consistently show that a leading-edge sweep at or very close to the minimum (45.0 degrees) is highly effective for maximizing CL/CDi. This suggests minimizing the induced drag associated with higher sweep, while still benefiting from some vortex lift effects inherent to delta wings.
*   An aggressive twist distribution, with maximum positive root twist (10.0 deg) and maximum negative tip twist (-10.0 deg), is a signature of the highest-reward designs (Design 2 and 3). This setup is crucial for achieving an optimal lift distribution across the span, reducing induced drag by minimizing tip-loading and potentially delaying stall.
*   The NACA 24xx series (NACA m=2, p=4) has proven to be successful across all high-reward designs. Furthermore, designs with lower thickness (NACA 2409 in Design 2 and 3) slightly outperform those with higher thickness (NACA 2414 in Design 1). Therefore, a thinner NACA 24xx airfoil, specifically `naca_t=8`, should be explored to further reduce profile drag.
*   Dihedral values for the top designs are consistently positive, in the range of ~7-11 degrees. Design 2 achieved the highest reward with `dihedral=7.338`. We will target a value close to this, slightly lower than previous attempts, to further refine lateral stability effects in conjunction with other parameters.
*   Root chord in the 25-34 inch range has yielded good results. We will select a value within this range to maintain overall wing size and ensure reasonable Reynolds numbers for efficient operation.
