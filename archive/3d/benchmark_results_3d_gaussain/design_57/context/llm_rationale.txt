*   **Hybridize Twist for Optimal Load:** The top designs (Design 1, 4, 3) all feature significant wash-out at the tip and positive wash-in at the root. We will blend these successful characteristics by adopting a high positive root twist (8.8 deg, taking inspiration from Design 1 & 4) and a very strong tip washout (-9.8 deg, leaning towards the maximum washout seen in Design 1 & 3) to ensure an efficient spanwise lift distribution and minimize induced drag.
*   **Explore Slightly Higher Sweep:** While the current top designs are at the lower end of the sweep range (46-48 deg), we hypothesize that a slightly increased sweep might leverage more vortex lift without incurring excessive drag if combined with the effective twist. We choose `le_sweep = 53.5` degrees, exploring a mid-range value beyond the existing high-performers but not extremely high, to test this hypothesis.
*   **Increase Root Chord:** Design 4 demonstrated strong performance with a larger root chord (33.72 inches) than Design 1. We will adopt a similarly large `root_chord_in = 34.5` inches to investigate the benefits of increased wing area, aiming for higher overall lift potential while relying on the optimized twist and sweep to manage drag.
*   **Investigate Max Camber Airfoil:** All top designs utilized NACA m=2. To explore a different aerodynamic strategy, this design will boldly explore the maximum available camber `naca_m = 4` (with `naca_p = 4`). This could lead to a significant increase in CL, and combined with an intermediate `naca_t = 11`, we aim for a robust, high-lift airfoil section.
*   **High Dihedral:** Design 3 achieved high performance with maximum dihedral (15.0 deg). We will adopt a high positive dihedral of `14.5` degrees to maintain good lateral stability, a common trait among high-performing designs.
