*   **Exploitation of Low Sweep:** The top five designs, and especially the highest-ranking ones, exhibit `le_sweep` values at the absolute minimum (45.0 degrees) or very close to it. This strongly suggests that lower sweep angles are critical for maximizing CL/CDi in this operational context, likely by reducing induced drag penalties or optimizing effective aspect ratio. We will maintain a low sweep angle.
*   **Refinement of Twist Strategy:** High positive `twist_root` (approaching 10.0 degrees) combined with significant `twist_tip` washout (approaching -10.0 degrees) is a dominant pattern among high-reward designs. This twist distribution is highly effective at tailoring the spanwise lift distribution for minimal induced drag. We will maintain these aggressive twist values.
*   **Consistent Dihedral:** All top designs feature positive `dihedral` (7 to 11 degrees). While not a primary aerodynamic driver for CL/CDi, it appears to be a consistent feature without significant penalty, contributing to stability. We will maintain a positive dihedral value within this range.
*   **Targeted NACA Airfoil Selection:** The NACA 24XX series (2% max camber at 40% chord) is prevalent among high-reward designs. This indicates that moderate camber is beneficial. `naca_t` (thickness) shows some variation (9-14). We will stick to the 24XX camber profile and explore an intermediate thickness.
*   **Optimizing Root Chord:** `root_chord_in` shows variation (25-40 inches) within the top designs. Design 1 has a high `root_chord_in`. We will explore a value in the upper-middle range, combining it with the identified optimal sweep and twist.
