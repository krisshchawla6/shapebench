*   **Leverage washout for induced drag reduction:** The consistent high performance of designs with significant washout strongly suggests its importance for optimizing spanwise lift distribution and minimizing induced drag. My new design will feature an aggressive washout.
*   **Cautiously increase sweep for vortex lift:** The current top designs cluster around 51-57 degrees. I will explore a slightly higher sweep (e.g., around 60 degrees) to potentially enhance stable leading-edge vortex lift, while avoiding the extremely high sweep that proved detrimental in Design 6.
*   **Explore higher camber airfoil:** All top designs used NACA 24xx. I will introduce a NACA 44xx airfoil, which provides more camber, to investigate if this can further improve lift-to-drag ratio by generating more lift at lower angles of attack, or by interacting favorably with the twist distribution.
*   **Maintain strong positive dihedral:** Positive dihedral is a consistent feature of high-reward designs, likely contributing to stability and favorable flow. I will select a value towards the upper end of the successful range.
*   **Optimize root chord for scaling:** The root chord defines the scale. I will choose a value in the mid-range of successful designs to maintain a reasonable overall wing size, ensuring it's not too small or excessively large.
