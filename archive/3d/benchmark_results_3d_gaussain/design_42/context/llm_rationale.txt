*   **Maintain Optimal Sweep Range**: The top-performing designs consistently demonstrate that a leading-edge sweep in the low-mid 50s (51-53 degrees) is highly effective for maximizing CL/CDi. This sweep range likely balances vortex lift benefits with manageable induced drag. My new design will maintain this successful characteristic by selecting a value within this proven range.
*   **Refine Twist Distribution for Drag Reduction**: The previous high-reward designs utilized significant positive root twist and strong washout at the tip. This strategy is key to tailoring the spanwise load for minimum induced drag. I will push the washout further towards the maximum negative limit, while keeping the root twist high, to try and achieve an even more ideal (potentially more uniform) lift distribution, thus enhancing efficiency.
*   **Leverage High Dihedral**: High positive dihedral (near max 15.0 deg) has been a consistent feature of the top designs. This parameter likely contributes to stability and may interact favorably with the wing's vortex system, improving overall performance. I will maintain maximum positive dihedral to exploit this identified advantage.
*   **Optimize Airfoil Thickness with Proven Camber**: The NACA 24xx series, providing 2% max camber at 40% chord, has proven effective. Combining this with a moderate thickness (e.g., 7%) as seen in the top design (NACA 2407) offers a good balance of lift generation and reduced profile drag. I will stick to these characteristics.
*   **Slightly Increase Root Chord**: To explore the impact of overall scale on the aerodynamic performance, I will slightly increase the root chord compared to the top designs (e.g., from 29-31 to 32.5 inches). This allows for a larger lifting surface which, if efficiently utilized with the optimized twist and sweep, could yield higher CL without a proportional increase in CDi.
