This indicates a potential sweet spot where vortex lift is sufficient, but induced drag penalties are less severe than at very high sweeps. I will push this further to 55 degrees to explore this boundary.
*   **Maintain strong twist for optimal load distribution:** The significant positive twist at the root and negative twist at the tip in successful designs (e.g., `twist_root=5.49`, `twist_tip=-5.76` in Design 1) is critical for efficient lift distribution and reducing induced drag. I will maintain this design principle by using a positive root twist and a substantial negative tip twist.
*   **Utilize the thinnest possible airfoil for lower profile drag:** Design 1 uses 8% thickness (NACA 2408). To aggressively reduce profile drag, I will select the minimum allowed thickness of 6% (NACA 2406), assuming the associated camber can still provide efficient lift.
*   **Consistent camber for efficient lift:** All top-performing designs utilize NACA 24xx airfoils. This implies that 2% camber positioned at 40% chord is an effective configuration for the target aerodynamic performance, so I will retain `naca_m=2` and `naca_p=4`.
*   **Moderate root chord for balanced performance:** While Design 1 has a moderate root chord (~33 inches), some other decent designs have slightly lower chords (~27-31 inches). To complement the lower sweep and thinner airfoil, I will opt for a slightly smaller root chord (~25 inches) to potentially reduce overall wetted area and associated drag.
