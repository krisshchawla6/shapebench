*   **Strategy Focus: Explore Symmetric Airfoil (NACA 00xx)**: The current top designs all rely on cambered airfoils (NACA 24xx). A significant unexplored region is the use of symmetric airfoils (NACA 00xx, i.e., `naca_m=0`, `naca_p=0`). This design will test whether airfoil camber is critical for high CL/CDi, or if the delta planform's vortex lift mechanism can achieve high efficiency independently. This is a bold exploration to diversify the design space.
*   **Strategy Focus: Introduce Anhedral (Negative Dihedral)**: All previous designs have utilized positive dihedral. Exploring negative dihedral (anhedral) at a moderate value (e.g., -10.0 degrees) is a completely novel parameter choice. Anhedral can affect lateral stability and may alter the ground effect or vortex interaction, offering a new avenue for CL/CDi optimization, despite potentially reducing stability.
*   **Explore Low Leading-Edge Sweep (45.0 deg)**: While the best designs are around 50-60 degrees, and higher sweeps (65+ deg) performed poorly, the lowest end of the spectrum (45.0 deg) remains largely unexplored by high-reward designs. This design will push to the lower boundary of `le_sweep` to investigate the impact on vortex lift and induced drag with a less swept planform, potentially finding a different sweet spot for CL/CDi.
*   **Pair Symmetric Airfoil with Thinner Section (NACA 0008)**: To complement the symmetric airfoil, a relatively thin profile (`naca_t=8`) will be chosen. This minimizes profile drag, which is crucial for CL/CDi, especially when the lift contribution from camber is removed. This thickness is comparable to some of the best-performing previous designs.
*   **Moderate Root Chord (40.0 inches)**: The best previous designs range from 28-34 inches. A slightly larger `root_chord_in` of 40.0 inches is chosen to scale the wing and provide ample area, especially with the low sweep. This is also a less explored region of the `root_chord_in` parameter space for higher performing designs.
*   **Subdued Twist Distribution (2.0 deg root, -5.0 deg tip)**: Given the exploratory nature with a symmetric airfoil and low sweep, an aggressive twist might be counterproductive or hard to interpret. A small positive root twist (wash-in) of `2.0` degrees is maintained for some load distribution control, coupled with a moderate washout of `-5.0` degrees at the tip to ensure favorable tip behavior without excessive twist.
