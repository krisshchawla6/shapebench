The strong positive root twist (`8.26 deg`) combined with maximum negative tip twist (`-10.0 deg` washout) is crucial for an optimized spanwise lift distribution, which effectively increases the aerodynamic aspect ratio and significantly reduces induced drag. This twist strategy likely delays tip stall and ensures a more uniform load across the wing. The NACA 2409 airfoil (2% camber at 40% chord, 9% thickness) provides efficient lift generation for the specified conditions. Positive dihedral (9.79 deg) contributes to lateral stability.
*   **Potential Issues (minor, given high reward)**: While highly efficient, there might be subtle optimizations possible. The very strong root twist could lead to slightly higher peak pressures at the root at certain angles of attack. The maximum washout might slightly limit the overall lift coefficient achievable if it over-unloads the tips excessively, though it's clearly beneficial for CL/CDi.
