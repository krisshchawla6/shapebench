*   **Explore High Sweep Boundary**: The best-performing designs have moderate sweep (51-60 degrees). Designs with higher sweep (64-66 degrees) have lower rewards. I want to push the `le_sweep` parameter towards its upper limit (e.g., 75-80 degrees) to rigorously explore this underexplored region. This will test if vortex lift benefits can outweigh the induced drag penalty at these extreme angles, especially when combined with optimized twist.
*   **Maximize Twist for Load Control**: Design 1's high positive `twist_root` (7.5 deg) was highly beneficial. I will push `twist_root` to its absolute maximum (10.0 deg) and combine it with a strong `twist_tip` washout (e.g., -8.0 deg). This extreme twist distribution aims to aggressively tailor the spanwise lift distribution, which could be crucial for performance at very high sweep angles by mitigating tip stall and optimizing induced drag.
*   **Investigate Symmetric Airfoil**: All current high-reward designs utilize cambered NACA 24xx airfoils. To understand the pure geometric effects of sweep and twist without the inherent lift from camber, I will select a symmetric airfoil (`naca_m=0, naca_p=0`). This represents a significant unexplored region of the airfoil parameter space.
*   **Explore Negative Dihedral**: The top-ranked Design 1 features maximum positive dihedral (15.0 degrees). I will explore the opposite end by selecting a significantly negative `dihedral` (e.g., -10.0 degrees). This will test how extreme lateral stability characteristics affect performance, particularly in combination with a high sweep and extreme twist, providing valuable data on an underexplored boundary.
