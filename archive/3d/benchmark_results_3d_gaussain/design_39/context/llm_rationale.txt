*   The highest-reward designs (2, 3, 4, 5) consistently feature a relatively low sweep angle (around 51-54 degrees). This parameter range appears to strike an optimal balance between vortex lift generation and induced drag, therefore, the new design will select a sweep angle within this well-performing region.
*   A dominant pattern for high reward is a combination of very high positive `twist_root` (approaching 10.0 degrees) and very high negative `twist_tip` (approaching -10.0 degrees). This strong washout distribution is crucial for optimizing spanwise lift, reducing induced drag, and will be pushed to its practical limits in the new design.
*   Maximum positive dihedral (15.0 degrees) is frequently observed in the top-performing designs, indicating its significant contribution to aerodynamic efficiency or stability characteristics that enhance CL/CDi. The new design will leverage this by setting dihedral to its maximum.
*   All top designs use a NACA 24XX profile, with `naca_m=2`, `naca_p=4`, and low thickness (`naca_t=6` or `naca_t=7`). This suggests that a moderately cambered, very thin airfoil is optimal for minimizing profile drag while contributing to lift. The new design will adopt NACA 2406.
*   The root chord consistently falls in the 29-31 inch range for the best designs. This narrow window suggests an optimal overall wing scale for the given conditions, and the new design will maintain a root chord within this established successful range.
