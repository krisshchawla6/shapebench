The goal is to generate a novel and diverse design, focusing on bold exploration rather than incremental tweaks, while still learning from the top-performing designs.

*   **Boldly explore a lower sweep angle:** The top designs feature moderate sweep. I will push towards the lower end of the sweep range (e.g., 45-50 degrees). This dramatically alters the wing's aspect ratio and vortex characteristics, potentially revealing a different optimal aerodynamic regime for CL/CDi.
*   **Maintain strong washout based on top performers:** The success of Designs 1 and 2 is strongly linked to significant washout. This indicates that optimized spanwise lift distribution to reduce induced drag is crucial for high CL/CDi, regardless of other parameters. I will ensure a substantial negative twist at the tip relative to the root.
*   **Introduce a symmetric airfoil (NACA 00xx):** All current top designs use cambered NACA 24xx airfoils. To explore a different aerodynamic strategy, I will use a symmetric airfoil (NACA m=0, p=0). This will test if camber is always necessary for high CL/CDi, or if a symmetric section can perform well with optimal planform and twist. I'll pair it with a moderate thickness.
*   **Explore anhedral (negative dihedral):** All previous designs have positive dihedral or near-zero. Introducing a moderate anhedral (negative dihedral) will investigate its impact on CL/CDi and lateral stability characteristics in this new, low-sweep, symmetric airfoil configuration. This is a less explored region of the design space.
*   **Utilize a larger root chord:** Most previous designs were in the 28-35 inch range. I will choose a significantly larger root chord to explore the effects of increased wing area and scale on the overall CL/CDi, especially with the chosen low sweep and symmetric airfoil.
