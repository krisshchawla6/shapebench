*   **Shift to Higher `le_sweep`**: The current top designs are clustered around 48-52 degrees. To explore a novel region and potentially enhance leading-edge vortex lift, I will target a moderately higher sweep of `58.0` degrees. This tests if a more dominant vortex can yield better CL/CDi without excessively penalizing induced drag by going to extreme sweeps.
*   **Increase `root_chord_in`**: To support potentially higher lift generation from the increased sweep and to provide a larger planform area, a slightly larger root chord of `35.0` inches will be used. This scales the overall wing for potentially greater performance.
*   **Aggressive `twist_root` with Moderate `twist_tip`**: To complement the higher sweep and promote strong vortex formation early on while maintaining good spanwise load distribution and delaying tip stall, I'll set a high positive `twist_root` of `8.0` degrees and a moderate negative `twist_tip` of `-7.0` degrees. This aims for an optimal balance of lift distribution and controlled vortex behavior.
*   **Explore `NACA 44xx` airfoil with Reduced `naca_t`**: Current designs use `NACA 24xx`. I will experiment with `NACA 44xx` (higher maximum camber) by setting `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4`, to investigate its impact on lift at a higher sweep. Combined with a thinner profile (`naca_t=8`), this seeks to maximize lift while minimizing profile drag for the chosen higher sweep.
*   **Maintain Positive `dihedral` (but slightly less than max)**: Previous top designs consistently show high positive dihedral to be beneficial. I will maintain a positive dihedral (`12.0` degrees) but slightly reduced from the maximum (15 deg), to allow other parameter variations to express their effect more fully, while still retaining some lateral stability benefits.
