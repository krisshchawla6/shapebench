*   **Novel Design Strategy Implementation**: I am moving away from the "pinched" design of the current top-performing individuals. Instead of placing a control point very close to the center ($r \approx 0.2$), I will use a more balanced radius distribution ($r \approx 0.5-0.6$) for the mid-body control points to create a conventional, higher-performance airfoil shape.
*   **Aerodynamic Camber**: I will introduce significant asymmetry by positioning $CP_1$ (upper) and $CP_2$ (lower) with different radial and angular offsets. This aims to maximize lift-to-drag by creating a smooth upper surface camber while maintaining a flatter lower surface.
*   **Leading Edge Smoothing**: To prevent early separation and manage the stagnation point, I will set $CP_0$ with a high radius and minimum edginess ($e = -0.98$), ensuring a perfectly rounded nose.
*   **Trailing Edge Sharpness**: Following the trend of successful designs but pushing it further, I will maximize the edginess of $CP_3$ ($e = 0.995$) to define a precise Kutta condition at the trailing edge.
*   **Geometric Exploration**: By spreading the angular parameters ($a_n$), I will explore a design that shifts the maximum thickness further back (around 35-40% chord), which is generally more efficient for subsonic flow than the front-heavy designs seen in the current population.
