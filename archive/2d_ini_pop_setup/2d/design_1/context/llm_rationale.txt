* **Exploration Strategy Application**: Since this is the first design, I am pushing towards the boundaries of the parameter space to define the "envelope" of possible shapes. I will use extreme positive radial values for the upper surface and extreme positive edgy values for the trailing edge.
* **Geometric Diversity**: By setting the `angle_param` to divergent values across control points, I aim to stretch the airfoil along a primary axis, moving away from a circular primitive toward a high-aspect-ratio lifting body.
* **Camber Generation**: I will explore an asymmetrical radial distribution (Point 1 high positive, Point 3 moderate negative) to create a distinct pressure side and suction side, which is essential for lift generation.
* **Sharpness Gradient**: I am testing the `edgy_param` limits by setting a very low value (-0.9) at the intended leading edge for maximum smoothness and a very high value (+0.95) at the trailing edge to force a sharp termination.
* **Parameter-Geometry Mapping**: This design tests if high `radius_param` values (near 1.0) coupled with specific `angle_param` offsets can effectively simulate a classical NACA-like thickness distribution while remaining within the defined bounds.
