*   **Novel Design Strategy Implementation**: Instead of incremental testing, this design establishes a "Cambered Teardrop" baseline, utilizing extreme `edgy_param` values to differentiate the leading edge (smooth) from the trailing edge (sharp).
*   **Kutta Condition Enforcement**: I am setting the first control point (P0, usually the trailing edge) to a maximum `edgy_param` (1.0) and high `radius_param` to create a defined, sharp termination point, which is critical for reducing wake turbulence and drag.
*   **Asymmetric Pressure Distribution**: By setting a high positive `radius_param` for the upper surface (P1) and a negative or low `radius_param` for the lower surface (P3), I aim to create a pressure differential (lower pressure on top, higher on bottom) to generate immediate lift.
*   **Leading Edge Robustness**: Control Point 2 (the leading edge) will use a very low `edgy_param` (-0.95) to ensure a smooth, circular profile, which helps maintain attached flow across a wider range of inflow conditions.
*   **Angular Optimization**: I will use subtle `angle_param` offsets to shift the maximum thickness of the airfoil toward the front (around 30% chord), mimicking high-performance laminar flow airfoils which reduce skin friction drag.
