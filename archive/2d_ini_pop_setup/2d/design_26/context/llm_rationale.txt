*   **Novel Design Strategy Application**: I am moving away from the highly cambered, "thin-crust" approach of the top designs to explore a thicker, aft-loaded supercritical-style profile. This deviates from the current population's trend of extreme lower-surface concavity.
*   **Aft-Loading**: By positioning CP1 (upper surface) further aft (a1 ≈ 0.7) and increasing the radius, I aim to shift the maximum thickness point rearward, potentially extending the laminar region and smoothing the pressure gradient.
*   **Trailing Edge Sharpness**: Existing high-reward designs have moderate TE sharpness. I will aggressively increase the `edgy_param` for CP3 to near-maximum (0.88+) to test if a true "knife-edge" TE significantly boosts the L/D ratio by minimizing the wake.
*   **Lower Surface Normalization**: Instead of the high `r` value seen in Design 1's CP2 (which creates a deep "scoop"), I will use a moderate `r` value to create a flatter lower surface, which is often more efficient for subsonic/transonic cruise.
*   **Search Advancement**: This design tests whether a more "conventional" high-performance airfoil shape can outperform the current population's "extreme-camber" local optima, providing a new direction for the evolutionary search.
