*   **Strategy Focus - First Principles**: Instead of mutating the chaotic Design 1, I am generating a fresh design based on the fundamental aerodynamic principle of a "cambered teardrop." This ensures a baseline of attached flow before optimization refines it.
*   **Thickness Distribution**: To minimize the form drag seen in Design 1, I am implementing a parabolic radius distribution. CP1 and CP2 (upper surface) get high positive radii (`0.6`-`0.7`) to carry lift, while CP4 (trailing edge) gets a negative radius (`-0.9`) to ensure closure.
*   **Kutta Condition Enforcement**: CP4 is explicitly set to `edgy = 0.95` (sharp) and low radius. This forces the flow to leave the trailing edge cleanly, preventing the recirculation likely present in the previous design.
*   **Camber Introduction**: Unlike a symmetric shape, I am biasing the upper surface points (CP1, CP2) to higher radii than the lower surface points (CP6, CP7). This introduces camber to generate lift efficiently.
*   **Leading Edge Smoothing**: CP0 is set to a moderate radius (`0.2`) with maximum smoothness (`-0.95`). This replaces the "wall" of Design 1 with a functional stagnation point that guides flow over the suction side.
