### Key Learnings

**1. The "Pebble Trap" Threshold is Lower than Predicted**
The designer predicted a collapse at $>1.90$. However, this result shows that a combined $r_0 + r_2$ of **~1.82** is already deep within the "Pebble Trap." The airfoil has lost its slender profile and morphed into a low-aspect-ratio "slug." This suggests the "Radius-Distance Law" has a much narrower margin for error than previously assumed.

**2. Loss of the Kutta Condition**
By pushing $r_2$ (rear radius) and $r_0$ (front radius) to their limits simultaneously, the geometry engine appears to have prioritized a "closed loop" shape over a sharp trailing edge. The result is a rounded posterior which cannot effectively fix the stagnation point at the trailing edge, leading to a massive drop in lift and a surge in pressure drag.

**3. The Risk of "Over-Smoothing"**
While the goal of changing $e_3$ from -0.92 to -0.32 was to avoid the "Fang" (concavity) trap, it also removed the high-pressure "pocket" on the lower surface that contributed to Design 1's high reward. In the absence of a long chord, this smoothing turned the airfoil into a simple ellipse.

**4. Exploitation vs. Fragility**
This design demonstrates that in high-performance regions of the parameter space, the "reward landscape" is extremely steep. A small increase in chord (from 1.79 to 1.82) resulted in a total collapse rather than a marginal gain. Future iterations must treat the 1.80 limit for $r_0+r_2$ as a hard "Red Line."
