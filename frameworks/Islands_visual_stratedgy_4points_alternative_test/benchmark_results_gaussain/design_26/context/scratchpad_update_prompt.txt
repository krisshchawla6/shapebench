## Global Heuristics
*   **The Radius-Distance Law:** Radius acts as polar distance from centroid. Maximize LE ($r_0$) and TE ($r_2$) to increase chord, but avoid simultaneous saturation [iter 7, 24, 25].
*   **The Stretching Paradox (The Pebble Trap):** Sum of $r_0 + r_2 \ge 1.90$ triggers geometric collapse. Splines implode into a "pebble" instead of extending [iter 25, 26].
*   **The Volume Budget:** High radii on CP1/CP3 (>0.45) "steal" length. Simultaneous high values across all four radii force "Pebble" morphology [iter 21, 25, 26].
*   **The Waisted Trap:** Side radii < 0.40 under high chord tension creates "boomerang" profiles. CP1/CP3 must be $\ge$ 0.40 to maintain convexity [iter 11, 13, 17].
*   **The Hook/Fang Boundary:** Negative lower edginess ($e_3 < 0$) is the primary "Fang" driver. Values as high as -0.25 still fail to ensure a convex belly [iter 24, 25, 26].
*   **The Closure Imbalance:** Reducing $r_1$ while maintaining high $r_3$ shifts area downward, preventing TE closure and creating blunt vertical faces [iter 22, 25].
*   **The Beaking Threshold:** $r_0 > 0.90$ creates a "wedge" nose. $a_1 < -0.40$ combined with high $r_0$ concentrates volume into a forward "beak" [iter 13, 25].
*   **The Stagnation Alignment:** $a_0 \approx 0.0$ minimizes frontal area and stagnation drag [iter 24].
*   **The Kutta Singularity:** TE Edgy ($e_2 = 1.0$) is mandatory. High $e_2$ is nullified if the geometry collapses into a pebble [iter 2, 21, 26].

## Degree of Freedom Mapping

### CP0: Leading Edge (LE)
*   **Radius [0][0]:** Chord driver. Limit to 0.90 if $r_2$ is high; >0.92 risks collapse [iter 21, 26].
*   **Angle [0][1]:** Vertical incidence. $a_0 \approx 0.0$ for minimum frontal drag [iter 4, 24].
*   **Edgy [0][2]:** -1.0 for C-infinity continuity [iter 2].

### CP1: Upper Surface / Peak
*   **Radius [1][0]:** Thickness control. 0.35-0.40 is the "slender limit." Above 0.41 contributes to "Pebble Trap" when chord is stretched [iter 8, 26].
*   **Angle [1][1]:** Peak positioning. $a_1 \approx -0.45$ shifts thickness to ~25% chord [iter 1, 25].
*   **Edgy [1][2]:** Low values (-0.9) ensure smooth apex curvature [iter 4, 24].

### CP2: Trailing Edge (TE)
*   **Radius [2][0]:** Rearward extension. 1.0 is max; must be traded against $r_0$ to stay below the 1.90 combined limit [iter 7, 26].
*   **Angle [2][1]:** Horizontal alignment; keep near 0.0 to prevent lift-induced tail drag [iter 4, 11].
*   **Edgy [2][2]:** 1.0 enforces Kutta condition; critical for flow closure [iter 2, 25].

### CP3: Lower Surface
*   **Radius [3][0]:** Stability Anchor. 0.42-0.45 is the "Concavity Floor." Values >0.50 risk geometric collapse [iter 17, 26].
*   **Angle [3][1]:** Tuck control. $a_3 \approx 0.12$ is the limit for convexity when $e_3$ is negative [iter 8, 25].
*   **Edgy [3][2]:** **The Fang Driver.** $e_3 < 0$ creates concave notches. $e_3 = -0.25$ is still insufficient for convexity; requires values $\ge 0.0$ [iter 24, 26].

### Key Learnings

**1. The "Pebble Trap" Threshold is Lower than Predicted**
The designer predicted a collapse at $>1.90$. However, this result shows that a combined $r_0 + r_2$ of **~1.82** is already deep within the "Pebble Trap." The airfoil has lost its slender profile and morphed into a low-aspect-ratio "slug." This suggests the "Radius-Distance Law" has a much narrower margin for error than previously assumed.

**2. Loss of the Kutta Condition**
By pushing $r_2$ (rear radius) and $r_0$ (front radius) to their limits simultaneously, the geometry engine appears to have prioritized a "closed loop" shape over a sharp trailing edge. The result is a rounded posterior which cannot effectively fix the stagnation point at the trailing edge, leading to a massive drop in lift and a surge in pressure drag.

**3. The Risk of "Over-Smoothing"**
While the goal of changing $e_3$ from -0.92 to -0.32 was to avoid the "Fang" (concavity) trap, it also removed the high-pressure "pocket" on the lower surface that contributed to Design 1's high reward. In the absence of a long chord, this smoothing turned the airfoil into a simple ellipse.

**4. Exploitation vs. Fragility**
This design demonstrates that in high-performance regions of the parameter space, the "reward landscape" is extremely steep. A small increase in chord (from 1.79 to 1.82) resulted in a total collapse rather than a marginal gain. Future iterations must treat the 1.80 limit for $r_0+r_2$ as a hard "Red Line."
