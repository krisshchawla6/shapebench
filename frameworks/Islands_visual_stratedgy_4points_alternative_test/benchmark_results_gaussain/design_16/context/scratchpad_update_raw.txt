# Airfoil Optimization Reference Card

## Global Heuristics
*   **The Radius-Distance Law:** Radius functions as polar distance from centroid. Maximize LE (CP0) and TE (CP2) radii while minimizing Upper (CP1) and Lower (CP3) radii to maximize chord and fineness ratio [iter 7, 8].
*   **The Waisted Trap:** Side radii < 0.40 under high chord tension creates "boomerang" or "waisted" morphology. CP1/CP3 must be $\ge$ 0.45 to maintain convex profiles [iter 11, 13, 17].
*   **The Beaking Threshold:** CP0 Radius > 0.90 overrides smoothness settings, creating a sharp "wedge" nose. Truly rounded subsonic stagnation points likely require Radius < 0.80 [iter 13, 17].
*   **The Concavity Floor:** CP3 Radius ~0.50 is the threshold to eliminate "scoops" and establish a convex "pressure face," essential for efficient lift and flow attachment [iter 8, 11, 17].
*   **The Slender Transition:** To exit "stalled pebble" regime, use extreme radii for CP0/CP2 (>0.9) and low radii for CP1/CP3 (<0.4), though this risks structural collapse [iter 8, 13].
*   **Chord Truncation:** Low TE Radius (CP2) "shortens" the chord, creating "Thimble" morphology [iter 7].
*   **The Kutta Singularity:** TE Edgy [2][2] = 1.0 is mandatory for flow shedding. Orientation must align with mean flow to avoid "hooked" tail drag [iter 2, 11].
*   **Flow Attachment:** Steep recovery slopes (high CP1 Radius) trigger separation. Flattening via lower CP1 Radius (~0.35-0.40) promotes laminar streamlines [iter 1, 8, 13, 17].

## Degree of Freedom Mapping

### CP0: Leading Edge (LE)
*   **Radius [0][0]:** Primary chord driver. > 0.95 creates a sharp "beak." Lowering toward 0.80 is necessary for nose rounding [iter 7, 13, 17].
*   **Angle [0][1]:** Controls vertical centering/incidence of the nose [iter 4].
*   **Edgy [0][2]:** -1.0 targets C-infinity continuity; neutralized by high Radius tension [iter 2, 13].

### CP1: Upper Surface / Peak
*   **Radius [1][0]:** Controls "hump" thickness. ~0.40 prevents mid-body collapse while maintaining a slender high-speed profile [iter 8, 17].
*   **Angle [1][1]:** Negative values (~ -0.4) shift peak forward to ~30% chord, lengthening the recovery ramp [iter 1, 13, 17].
*   **Edgy [1][2]:** Low values ensure smooth curvature over the apex [iter 4].

### CP2: Trailing Edge (TE)
*   **Radius [2][0]:** Rearward chord extension. 1.0 maximizes length and aspect ratio [iter 7, 11, 17].
*   **Angle [2][1]:** Aligns tail point; must be near 0.0 for horizontal chord alignment [iter 4, 11].
*   **Edgy [2][2]:** 1.0 enforces sharp geometric singularity for Kutta condition [iter 2, 8, 11].

### CP3: Lower Surface
*   **Radius [3][0]:** Camber/Flatness control. ~0.50 validates the "Concavity Floor," producing a gently convex, high-lift belly [iter 11, 17].
*   **Angle [3][1]:** Positive values (~0.19) tuck the lower-rear inward for a smooth, non-hooked transition to the TE [iter 8, 17].
*   **Edgy [3][2]:** High negative values (-0.9) maintain lower surface continuity [iter 4].
