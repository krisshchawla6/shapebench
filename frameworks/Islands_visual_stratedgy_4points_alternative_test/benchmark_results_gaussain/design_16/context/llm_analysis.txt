- **Geometry of Design 1**:
    - The airfoil is extremely slender with a very high chord length (long and thin).
    - There is a noticeable "beaking" effect at the leading edge, where the nose is sharply pointed rather than rounded, likely due to extreme radial tension.
    - The upper surface is nearly flat, while the lower surface exhibits a significant "waisted" or concave "scoop" near the mid-section.
    - The trailing edge is sharp and well-defined, aligned horizontally with the flow.

- **Flow Field of Design 1**:
    - **Pressure (p)**: There is a strong stagnation point at the sharp nose. The concave "bite" on the lower surface creates a localized region of higher pressure that may contribute to drag rather than efficient lift.
    - **Velocity (u, v)**: The flow appears mostly attached on the upper surface due to the thin profile, but the sharp leading edge likely triggers a small separation bubble or pressure spike at non-zero angles of attack.
    - **Wake**: The wake is relatively thin, indicating low profile drag, but the lower surface geometry is suboptimal for pressure distribution.

- **Parameter-Geometry Mapping**:
    - `r0 = 1.0` (CP0 Radius) created the extreme chord length but also the "beaking" flaw.
    - `r1 = 0.186` (CP1 Radius) resulted in an extremely thin upper profile, bordering on a "waisted" look when combined with low CP3 values.
    - `r3 = 0.215` (CP3 Radius) is clearly below the "Concavity Floor" (~0.40), causing the visible lower-surface scoop/bite.
    - `e2 = 1.0` (CP2 Edgy) successfully enforced the sharp trailing edge Kutta condition.

- **Key Changes Needed**:
    - Increase `r3` (Lower Surface Radius) to > 0.45 to eliminate the lower-surface concavity and create a flat/convex belly.
    - Slightly reduce `r0` from 1.0 to ~0.96 to mitigate the "beaking" effect and round the leading edge for better stagnation.
    - Increase `r1` (Upper Surface Radius) to ~0.38 to provide more volume for lift while maintaining a high-speed slender profile.
