# Airfoil Optimization Reference Card

## Global Heuristics
*   **The Radius-Distance Law:** Radius acts as polar distance from centroid. Maximize LE ($r_0$) and TE ($r_2$) to increase chord, but avoid simultaneous saturation [iter 7, 24, 25].
*   **The Stretching Paradox:** Maximizing both $r_0$ (>0.95) and $r_2$ (1.0) simultaneously triggers geometric collapse, resulting in a truncated "pebble" instead of a long chord [iter 25].
*   **The Volume Budget (Chord Collapse):** High radii on CP1/CP3 (>0.45) "steal" length. High mid-body radii force "Pebble" morphology [iter 21, 25].
*   **The Waisted Trap:** Side radii < 0.40 under high chord tension creates "boomerang" profiles. CP1/CP3 must be $\ge$ 0.40 to maintain convexity [iter 11, 13, 17].
*   **The Hook/Fang Boundary:** Negative lower edginess ($e_3 < 0$) is the primary "Fang" driver. Even $e_3 = -0.45$ creates a deep concave notch/stagnation trap [iter 24, 25].
*   **The Closure Imbalance:** Reducing $r_1$ while maintaining high $r_3$ shifts area downward, preventing TE closure and creating blunt vertical faces [iter 22, 25].
*   **The Beaking Threshold:** $r_0 > 0.90$ creates a "wedge" nose. $a_1 < -0.40$ combined with high $r_0$ concentrates volume into a forward "beak," sacrificing the recovery ramp [iter 13, 25].
*   **The Stagnation Alignment:** $a_0 \approx 0.0$ minimizes frontal area and stagnation drag [iter 24].
*   **The Kutta Singularity:** TE Edgy ($e_2 = 1.0$) is mandatory. $r_2$ must be balanced against $r_0$ to prevent the solver from rounding the tail [iter 2, 21, 25].

## Degree of Freedom Mapping

### CP0: Leading Edge (LE)
*   **Radius [0][0]:** Chord driver. 0.82 for stability; >0.95 risks collapse if $r_2$ is also high [iter 21, 25].
*   **Angle [0][1]:** Vertical incidence. $a_0 \approx 0.0$ for minimum frontal drag [iter 4, 24].
*   **Edgy [0][2]:** -1.0 for C-infinity continuity [iter 2].

### CP1: Upper Surface / Peak
*   **Radius [1][0]:** Thickness control. 0.35-0.40 is the "slender limit." Low values help TE convergence [iter 8, 24].
*   **Angle [1][1]:** Peak positioning. $a_1 \approx -0.45$ shifts thickness to ~25% chord; requires stable $r_0$ to avoid "beaking" [iter 1, 25].
*   **Edgy [1][2]:** Low values (-0.9) ensure smooth apex curvature [iter 4, 24].

### CP2: Trailing Edge (TE)
*   **Radius [2][0]:** Rearward extension. 1.0 is theoretical max; triggers truncation if $r_0$ is over-extended [iter 7, 25].
*   **Angle [2][1]:** Horizontal alignment; keep near 0.0 to prevent lift-induced tail drag [iter 4, 11].
*   **Edgy [2][2]:** 1.0 enforces Kutta condition; critical for flow closure [iter 2, 25].

### CP3: Lower Surface
*   **Radius [3][0]:** Stability Anchor. 0.42-0.45 is the "Concavity Floor" [iter 17, 25].
*   **Angle [3][1]:** Tuck control. $a_3 \approx 0.12$ is the limit for convexity when $e_3$ is negative [iter 8, 25].
*   **Edgy [3][2]:** **The Fang Driver.** Negative values ($e_3 < 0$) create concave notches. $e_3 \approx -0.45$ is still insufficient for a convex belly; requires values near 0.0 or positive [iter 24, 25].
