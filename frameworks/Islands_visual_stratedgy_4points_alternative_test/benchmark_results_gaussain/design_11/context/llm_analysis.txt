In Design 1, as shown in the images, I observe the following:
- **Geometry**:
    - The shape is a "Thimble" or bluff body morphology with an extremely truncated trailing edge.
    - The leading edge is rounded and smooth, but the chord length is very short because the trailing edge point (CP2) is pulled inward toward the center.
    - The upper surface has a steep curvature that doesn't taper, leading to a flat, vertical back-end.
    - There is a noticeable lack of a streamlined tail, making the geometry resemble a bullet more than an airfoil.
- **Flow Field**:
    - A massive low-velocity wake and high-pressure stagnation zone are visible.
    - Flow separation occurs almost immediately after the upper and lower "shoulders" of the shape because there is no trailing edge to facilitate pressure recovery.
    - Pressure plots show a large suction peak on the top, but it is immediately followed by a massive base-drag region.
    - The velocity field shows significant recirculation behind the flat rear face, indicating very high drag.

**Mapping and Flaws**:
- Design 1's `r2` (Trailing Edge Radius) is -0.151592. According to the Reference Card, a low or negative `r2` causes "Chord Truncation," which is exactly what we see here.
- The reward (0.5364) is surprisingly high for such a bluff body, likely because it avoids the "Tension Trap" or "Kutta Singularity" issues that plague longer, thinner shapes that aren't perfectly aligned.
- To improve, we need to transition from this "Pebble/Thimble" regime to a "Slender Blade" regime by maximizing the chord (`r0` and `r2` > 0.9) while carefully managing the mid-section radii (`r1`, `r3`) to prevent the profile from collapsing into a "boomerang" shape.
