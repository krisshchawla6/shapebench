# Airfoil Optimization Reference Card

## Global Heuristics
*   **The Radius-Distance Law:** Radius functions as a polar distance from the centroid. To maximize chord and fineness ratio: Maximize LE (CP0) and TE (CP2) radii while minimizing Upper (CP1) and Lower (CP3) radii [iter 7].
*   **Chord Truncation:** Reducing TE Radius to "taper" the tail instead "shortens" the chord. Low TE Radius = "Thimble" morphology [iter 7].
*   **The Scale Trap:** Low radii across all CPs create "miniature" or "seed" profiles. These act as bluff bodies with low Reynolds numbers, regardless of sharpness [iter 4, 7].
*   **Chord-Thickness Ratio:** High-performance targets ~10:1 ratio. Small horizontal distance between CP0 and CP2 relative to CP1/CP3 height results in "stubby/pebble" shapes [iter 1, 4].
*   **The Kutta Singularity:** TE Edgy [2][2] = 1.0 is mandatory to enforce a sharp vertex for flow shedding; however, without chord stretch (high CP2 Radius), it merely creates a "sharp wedge" [iter 2, 5, 7].
*   **Flow Attachment:** Steep recovery slopes (high CP1 Radius + forward CP1 Angle) trigger massive flow separation [iter 1].
*   **Thinning Strategy:** To achieve aerodynamic aspect ratios, *reduce* CP1 and CP3 radii to flatten the profile against the chord line [iter 2, 7].
*   **Singularity Stability:** Extreme Edgy values (-1.0 to 1.0) are computationally stable; the generator handles the transition from perfectly smooth to perfectly sharp without artifacts [iter 5].

## Degree of Freedom Mapping

### CP0: Leading Edge (LE)
*   **Radius [0][0]:** Primary driver of forward chord extension. High values (>0.9) stretch the nose forward; low values (<0.4) contribute to chord-length deficiency [iter 4, 7].
*   **Angle [0][1]:** Controls vertical centering of the nose [iter 4].
*   **Edgy [0][2]:** -1.0 ensures a perfectly circular, C-infinity continuous stagnation point [iter 2, 5, 7].

### CP1: Upper Surface / Peak
*   **Radius [1][0]:** Controls "hump" fullness. High values create steep forward ramps; low values (~0.4) thin the profile and move toward laminar streamlines [iter 2, 7].
*   **Angle [1][1]:** Lower values (~ -0.35) shift peak forward to ~30% chord [iter 1, 5].
*   **Edgy [1][2]:** Low values ensure smooth transition over the peak [iter 4].

### CP2: Trailing Edge (TE)
*   **Radius [2][0]:** Primary driver of rearward chord extension. Must be high (>0.7) to prevent "Thimble" truncation. Low values pull the TE toward the LE [iter 5, 7].
*   **Angle [2][1]:** Aligns tail point; must be near 0.0 for horizontal chord alignment [iter 4].
*   **Edgy [2][2]:** 1.0 effectively enforces a sharp geometric singularity for the Kutta condition [iter 2, 5, 7].

### CP3: Lower Surface
*   **Radius [3][0]:** Low values (~0.25) flatten the lower surface relative to the top, creating necessary camber for lift [iter 5, 7].
*   **Angle [3][1]:** Positive values (~0.2) tuck the lower-rear inward [iter 2, 4].
*   **Edgy [3][2]:** High negative values (-0.9) maintain smooth lower surface continuity [iter 4].
