### Key Learnings

1.  **The "Camber Trap"**: By trying to fix the "waisting" (CP1) and the "beak" (CP0) simultaneously, the design transitioned from a slender, high-speed shape into a high-camber, "thick" shape. The aerodynamic performance will likely drop significantly due to pressure drag from the blunt profile.
2.  **Angle Sensitivity**: The lower surface (CP3) is extremely sensitive to the Angle parameter. A move from a small positive angle to a small negative angle transformed a "slight hook" into a deep cavity. This suggests the "Concavity Floor" is much higher than 0.0 for this parameter set.
3.  **Chord Length is Relative**: High radii for CP0 and CP2 do not guarantee a "slender" look if the mid-body (CP1/CP3) is expanded. The fineness ratio is a result of the *ratio* between chord length and thickness; here, the thickness increased so much that the "slender" goal was lost.
4.  **The "Shrimp" Geometry**: The resulting shape resembles a high-lift, low-speed device rather than an optimized airfoil. While it might generate high lift at very low speeds, the drag penalty at any significant Reynolds number would be prohibitive. The "waisted" look of Design 1, while visually "fragile," was likely much more aerodynamically efficient.
