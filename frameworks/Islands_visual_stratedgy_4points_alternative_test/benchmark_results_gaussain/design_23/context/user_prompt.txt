# Airfoil Design Context

## Parameter Space
Each airfoil is defined by N control points, where each point has 3 parameters:
- radius_param: Controls radial distance from center. Range: [-1.0, 1.0]
- angle_param: Controls angular offset from default position. Range: [-1.0, 1.0]  
- edgy_param: Controls corner sharpness (0=smooth, 1=sharp). Range: [-1.0, 1.0]

Action vector format: [r0, a0, e0, r1, a1, e1, ..., rN, aN, eN] (flat array of 3*N values)

# Airfoil Optimization Reference Card

## Global Heuristics
*   **The Radius-Distance Law:** Radius functions as polar distance from centroid. Maximize LE (CP0) and TE (CP2) while minimizing Upper (CP1) and Lower (CP3) to maximize chord and fineness [iter 7, 8].
*   **The Volume Budget (Chord Collapse):** High radii on CP1/CP3 (>0.45) "steal" length from the chord. High mid-body radii force "Pebble" morphology [iter 21].
*   **The Waisted Trap:** Side radii < 0.40 under high chord tension creates "boomerang" profiles. CP1/CP3 must be $\ge$ 0.40 to maintain convexity [iter 11, 13, 17].
*   **The Hook/Fang Boundary:** High lower radius ($r_3 \ge 0.50$) combined with positive lower angle ($a_3 \ge 0.30$) triggers self-intersecting "hooks" or "fangs," destroying the manifold [iter 22].
*   **The Closure Imbalance:** Aggressively reducing $r_1$ (upper) while maintaining high $r_3$ (lower) shifts the center of area downward, preventing TE closure and creating blunt, high-drag vertical faces [iter 22].
*   **The Beaking Threshold:** CP0 Radius > 0.90 creates a sharp "wedge" nose. Rounded subsonic stagnation requires Radius < 0.85 [iter 13, 17, 21].
*   **The Kutta Singularity:** TE Edgy [2][2] = 1.0 is mandatory. Orientation [2][1] must be ~0.0 to avoid "hooked" tail drag [iter 2, 11, 21, 22].

## Degree of Freedom Mapping

### CP0: Leading Edge (LE)
*   **Radius [0][0]:** Chord driver. 0.80-0.85 for rounding. Values ~0.82 are stable but appear truncated if the spline fails downstream [iter 13, 21, 22].
*   **Angle [0][1]:** Controls vertical incidence/centering [iter 4].
*   **Edgy [0][2]:** -1.0 for C-infinity continuity [iter 2].

### CP1: Upper Surface / Peak
*   **Radius [1][0]:** Thickness control. 0.35-0.40 is the "slender limit." Must be balanced against $r_3$ to ensure trailing edge convergence [iter 8, 21, 22].
*   **Angle [1][1]:** Negative values (~ -0.4) shift peak forward to ~30% chord [iter 1, 17, 21].
*   **Edgy [1][2]:** Low values ensure smooth apex curvature [iter 4, 22].

### CP2: Trailing Edge (TE)
*   **Radius [2][0]:** Rearward extension. 1.0 maximizes aspect ratio. This is neutralized if CP3 geometry forces a blunt vertical face [iter 7, 11, 22].
*   **Angle [2][1]:** Horizontal alignment; keep near 0.0 [iter 4, 11, 22].
*   **Edgy [2][2]:** 1.0 enforces Kutta condition; critical for flow closure [iter 2, 21, 22].

### CP3: Lower Surface
*   **Radius [3][0]:** Stability Anchor. ~0.45 is the "Concavity Floor." Exceeding 0.50 with positive angles causes catastrophic "hook" deformities [iter 17, 21, 22]. For slender airfoils, $r_3$ must stay $< 0.40$ if $a_3$ is positive [iter 22].
*   **Angle [3][1]:** Tuck control. $a_3 \approx 0.2$ is smooth; $a_3 \ge 0.3$ risks spline looping and "fang" formation [iter 8, 17, 22].
*   **Edgy [3][2]:** High negative values (-0.9) maintain lower surface continuity [iter 4, 21].
