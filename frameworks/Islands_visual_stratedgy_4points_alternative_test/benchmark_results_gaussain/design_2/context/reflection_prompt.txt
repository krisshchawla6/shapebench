## Designer's Predictions

- **Leading Edge Continuity:** I will use a moderate radius and a highly negative Edgy parameter for CP0 to ensure a smooth, C-infinity continuous nose, which is critical for preventing early transition to turbulence or separation at the stagnation point.
- **Forward-Shifted Thickness:** I will shift the CP1 (upper peak) angle negatively (~ -0.28). This moves the maximum thickness forward, allowing for a gentler pressure recovery toward the trailing edge, which helps maintain flow attachment.
- **Trailing Edge Sharpening:** To address the "Base Drag Trap," I will set the CP2 radius to a near-minimum value while maximizing the Edgy parameter. This should create a clean, sharp termination point rather than a blunt "cliff."
- **Camber Generation:** By keeping CP3 (lower) radius smaller than CP1 (upper) and slightly shifting its angle, I will introduce a subtle camber intended to maximize lift while maintaining a thin, low-drag profile.
