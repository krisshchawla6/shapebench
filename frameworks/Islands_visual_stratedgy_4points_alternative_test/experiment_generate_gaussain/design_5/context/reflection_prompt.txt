## Designer's Predictions
- **Exploitation Strategy**: I am refining the circle's successful "smooth and thick" characteristic while introducing essential airfoil features (leading edge bluntness and trailing edge sharpness).
- **Radial Delta Management**: To avoid the -5.0 penalty seen in Design 2 and 5, I will limit the change between adjacent radii to $\leq 0.3$. This ensures the spline remains convex and avoids the "notches" that cause radial collapse.
- **Chord Extension**: By maintaining $r_0$ and $r_4$ near 1.0, I define a clear horizontal chord line, shifting the geometry from a bluff body to a streamlined one.
- **TE Sharpening**: I will set $e_0$ to a high negative value (-0.95) to create a Kutta-condition-compliant trailing edge, which is essential for reducing the massive wake seen in the circle's flow field.
- **Camber Introduction**: By setting $r_2$ (upper) slightly higher than $r_6$ (lower), I create a positive camber to generate lift while maintaining enough thickness ($>0.4$) to prevent flow separation.

The images show a perfectly circular geometry (Design 1), which is the baseline population member. 
- **Geometry**: 
    - The shape is a perfect unit circle, characterized by equal radial parameters (1.0) and zero angular/edgy offsets.
    - It is perfectly symmetric and lacks any streamlining or chord-wise extension.
    - The trailing edge is as blunt as the leading edge, which is the primary geometric flaw for aerodynamic efficiency.
- **Flow Field**:
    - The velocity plot shows a massive, stagnant wake (dark purple) extending far behind the circle, indicating total flow separation.
    - The pressure plot shows a high-pressure stagnation point at the leading edge (yellow) and a broad low-pressure zone on the sides, but the lack of a tapered trailing edge prevents pressure recovery.
    - There is significant wake asymmetry/instability visible in the field plots, typical of high-drag bluff bodies.

Design 1's reward of -0.0036 is the current benchmark. Designs 2 and 5 received a penalty reward of -5.0000. In Design 5, the parameter `r6` (lower surface) was 0.1335 and `r2` (upper surface) was 0.4184. The extreme drop from `r0=1.0` to these low values likely triggered the "Radial Collapse" mentioned in the reference card, causing a non-physical geometry. To improve, we must maintain radial support (keep $r_n > 0.35$) while stretching the circle into a chord.
