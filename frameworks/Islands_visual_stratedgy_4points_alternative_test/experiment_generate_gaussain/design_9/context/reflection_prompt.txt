## Designer's Predictions
- **Strategy Focus: Exploitation**: The circle (Design 2-5) is the only successful geometry so far. I will use it as a template and apply a "stretching" transformation to move from a bluff body to a streamlined one.
- **Applying the Scale Rule**: The total chord ($r_0 + r_4$) will be set to 2.35, exceeding the 2.0 threshold to avoid the "Nugget" trap while reducing the thickness-to-chord ratio.
- **Camber Introduction**: I will set $r_2$ (upper apex) slightly higher than $r_6$ (lower apex) to create a subtle positive camber for lift generation.
- **TE/LE Specialization**: Following the Reference Card, $e_0$ is set to -0.8 for a sharp Kutta condition at the tail, and $e_4$ is set to 0.5 to round the nose for a smoother stagnation zone.
- **Curvature Control**: To avoid the "Peanut" (concave waist), I will maintain all radii near 1.0, ensuring that the "valleys" at $r_2$ and $r_6$ are very shallow and supported by strictly intermediate $r_1, r_3, r_5, r_7$ values.

- **r1, r3, r5, r7**: I am setting these to values between the LE/TE and the apexes to ensure a smooth, convex transition (monotonicity).

The provided images show a representative design from the population, which in this case is a near-perfect circle (as seen in the white shape).
- **Geometry**:
    - The shape is a highly symmetric, smooth bluff body (circle/cylinder).
    - All control points are positioned at a constant radius, creating a "Nugget" geometry.
    - There are no sharp features, discontinuities, or taper; the trailing and leading edges are identical.
- **Flow Field**:
    - The pressure field shows a clear stagnation point at the leading edge (high pressure) and significant low-pressure zones on the upper and lower shoulders where the flow accelerates.
    - The velocity field reveals a massive, wide wake extending far downstream, indicating high pressure drag.
    - The wake is symmetric, suggesting zero lift, and the flow remains attached until the "shoulders," after which large-scale separation occurs.

Design 1's action vector (Reward -5.0000) shows irregular radius values ($r_2=0.21, r_6=-0.03$) and inconsistent angles. This confirms that deviating too wildly from the circle without maintaining convexity leads to the "Peanut" trap or geometric invalidity. Designs 2-5 (Reward -0.0036) represent the circle baseline, which is the current performance peak. To improve, we must transition from a circle to a streamlined airfoil while maintaining the stability of the circle's scale.

**Key changes needed**:
1.  **Chord Extension**: Increase $r_0$ (TE) and $r_4$ (LE) to create a longitudinal axis.
2.  **Trailing Edge Definition**: Apply a negative `edgy_param` to $e_0$ to sharpen the aft section.
3.  **Monotonic Tapering**: Ensure radial transitions between points follow the $\Delta r < 0.1$ rule to prevent the "Peanut" collapse.
