## Objective
Optimize an airfoil shape to maximize aerodynamic performance (reward).

## Designer's Predictions
- **Strategy Focus (Novel Design)**: I am abandoning the circular baseline entirely. Instead of incremental changes to the radius of 1.0, I am implementing a classical "NACA-inspired" teardrop profile using first principles of streamlining and airfoil geometry.
- **Pressure Drag Reduction**: By reducing the thickness (lowering $r_2$ and $r_6$), I will move the separation point toward the trailing edge, narrowing the wake significantly.
- **Lift Generation**: I will set $r_2 > r_6$ to create a larger suction surface on the top, encouraging lower pressure and positive lift.
- **Leading Edge Management**: I am using a negative $e_4$ value to maintain a blunt, rounded leading edge, which is more robust to changes in angle of attack than a sharp leading edge.
- **Longitudinal Balance**: I will use $a_2$ and $a_6$ to shift the maximum thickness forward (approx. 30% chord), which helps maintain attached flow over the rear of the airfoil.

## Per-Parameter Predictions

- I am setting `param[0][0]` (r0) to **0.9850** because I predict it will extend the chord rearward to form a distinct trailing edge.
- I am setting `param[0][2]` (e0) to **0.9500** because I predict it will create a sharp point at the trailing edge to satisfy the Kutta condition.
- I am setting `param[4][0]` (r4) to **0.8500** because I predict it will provide a substantial forward chord extension for streamlining.
- I am setting `param[4][2]` (e4) to **-0.8000** because I predict it will create a blunt, circular leading edge to prevent early flow separation.
- I am setting `param[2][0]` (r2) to **0.3200** because I predict it will define the upper suction surface height, significantly thinning the profile compared to the circle.
- I am setting `param[6][0]` (r6) to **0.2200** because I predict it will create a thinner lower surface than the top, establishing the necessary camber for lift.
- I am setting `param[2][1]` (a2) to **-0.3000** because I predict it will shift the maximum thickness of the upper surface forward.
- I am setting `param[1][0]` and `param[7][0]` to intermediate values (**0.6500**) to ensure a smooth, tapered transition toward the sharp trailing edge.

The images reveal a design that is fundamentally non-aerodynamic for lift generation:

- **Geometry**:
    - The shape is a perfect circle (cylinder), created by uniform radial parameters ($r_n = 1.0$).
    - It is perfectly symmetric and smooth, with a thickness-to-chord ratio of 1.0.
    - There is no distinct leading or trailing edge; the geometry is a bluff body.
- **Flow Field / Simulation**:
    - The pressure plot shows high-pressure stagnation at the front and low-pressure acceleration at the top and bottom, but it is entirely symmetric, resulting in zero lift.
    - The velocity field shows massive flow separation immediately after the mid-point, creating a wide, turbulent wake.
    - The third plot indicates the onset of periodic vortex shedding (Von Karman vortex street), which causes high pressure drag and structural vibration.

**Mapping to Parameters**:
Design 1's action vector [1.0, 0.0, 0.0...] confirms that setting all radii to 1.0 and all edgy/angle params to 0.0 creates a unit circle. This shape is the worst-case scenario for lift-to-drag ratio. 

**Key Changes Needed**:
1.  **Extreme Aspect Ratio Increase**: Stretch the chord by maximizing $r_0$ and $r_4$ while significantly reducing $r_2$ and $r_6$ to transition from a bluff body to a streamlined body.
2.  **Kutta Condition Enforcement**: Set a high $e_0$ value to sharpen the trailing edge, forcing the flow to leave smoothly and establishing circulation.
3.  **Camber Introduction**: Differentiate $r_2$ (suction side) and $r_6$ (pressure side) to create an asymmetric pressure distribution.
