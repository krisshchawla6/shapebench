## Objective
Optimize an airfoil shape to maximize aerodynamic performance (reward).

## Designer's Predictions
- **Visual Intuition Strategy**: The primary goal is to transform the high-drag cylinder into a low-drag teardrop shape. By looking at the massive wake in the velocity plot, it is clear that the "shoulders" of the shape ($P_2, P_6$) are too wide, and the rear is too blunt.
- **Chord Elongation**: I will maximize the distance between the leading edge ($P_4$) and trailing edge ($P_0$) to increase the chord length, which naturally reduces the thickness-to-chord ratio.
- **Wake Reduction**: By sharpening the trailing edge ($e_0 \rightarrow 1.0$), I am forcing the flow to leave the surface at a single point, significantly narrowing the wake and reducing pressure drag.
- **Transition Smoothing**: To avoid the "waisting" or "peanut" failure seen in Design 4, I will set transition radii ($P_1, P_7, P_3, P_5$) to intermediate values that ensure a strictly convex hull.
- **Camber Introduction**: I will make the upper surface ($r_2$) slightly more extended than the lower surface ($r_6$) and shift the upper points forward using $a_2$ to mimic high-performance NACA profiles.

## Per-Parameter Predictions
- I am setting **params[0][0]** to **0.9850** because I predict it will extend the trailing edge to the maximum allowable chord length.
- I am setting **params[0][2]** to **0.9900** because I predict it will create a knife-like trailing edge, fixing the Kutta condition.
- I am setting **params[4][0]** to **0.8800** because I predict it will create a distinct, forward-reaching leading edge.
- I am setting **params[4][2]** to **-0.9000** because I predict it will result in a smooth, blunt nose capable of handling varying flow incidence.
- I am setting **params[2][0]** to **0.3200** because I predict it will significantly thin the profile, reducing the frontal area and pressure drag.
- I am setting **params[2][1]** to **0.1800** because I predict it will shift the maximum thickness forward to approximately 30% of the chord.
- I am setting **params[6][0]** to **0.2400** because I predict that making the bottom surface thinner than the top will create positive camber and lift.
- I am setting **params[1][0] and params[7][0]** to **0.6800** because I predict these bridge values will maintain a smooth taper from the midsection to the sharp tail.

- **Geometry**:
    - The observed shape is a perfect circle, representing a non-streamlined bluff body.
    - It is perfectly symmetric both horizontally and vertically, with a 1:1 aspect ratio.
    - The control points are placed at equal radial distances, creating a smooth but aerodynamically inefficient boundary.
    - There is no distinct trailing edge to facilitate flow recombination, which is a fundamental geometric flaw for lift generation.

- **Flow Field / Simulation Results**:
    - The pressure field shows a classic stagnation point at the leading face and symmetric low-pressure regions at the zenith and nadir.
    - Significant flow separation is visible; the flow cannot follow the steep curvature of the rear hemisphere, leading to a massive wake.
    - The velocity plot reveals a broad, low-energy wake region extending downstream, which is the primary source of the high pressure drag.
    - The lack of asymmetry in the pressure distribution confirms that the design produces zero net lift.

- **Parameter Mapping & Necessary Changes**:
    - Design 1 uses `radius_param = 1.0` for all points, resulting in the observed circle.
    - The reward is poor because the "airfoil" is actually a cylinder, maximizing drag.
    - Key Change 1: Increase $r_0$ and $r_4$ while decreasing $r_2$ and $r_6$ to create a streamlined chord.
    - Key Change 2: Set $e_0$ to a high positive value to create a sharp trailing edge, which is essential for reducing the wake size.
    - Key Change 3: Apply subtle asymmetry in $r_2$ vs $r_6$ and $a_2$ vs $a_6$ to introduce camber for lift generation.
