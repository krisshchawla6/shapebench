- **Visual Intuition Strategy**: The primary goal is to transform the high-drag cylinder into a low-drag teardrop shape. By looking at the massive wake in the velocity plot, it is clear that the "shoulders" of the shape ($P_2, P_6$) are too wide, and the rear is too blunt.
- **Chord Elongation**: I will maximize the distance between the leading edge ($P_4$) and trailing edge ($P_0$) to increase the chord length, which naturally reduces the thickness-to-chord ratio.
- **Wake Reduction**: By sharpening the trailing edge ($e_0 \rightarrow 1.0$), I am forcing the flow to leave the surface at a single point, significantly narrowing the wake and reducing pressure drag.
- **Transition Smoothing**: To avoid the "waisting" or "peanut" failure seen in Design 4, I will set transition radii ($P_1, P_7, P_3, P_5$) to intermediate values that ensure a strictly convex hull.
- **Camber Introduction**: I will make the upper surface ($r_2$) slightly more extended than the lower surface ($r_6$) and shift the upper points forward using $a_2$ to mimic high-performance NACA profiles.
