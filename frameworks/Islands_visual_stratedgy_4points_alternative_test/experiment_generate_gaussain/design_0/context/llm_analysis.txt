*   **Geometry**:
    *   The shape is a perfect circle, representing a highly non-aerodynamic baseline.
    *   The surface is perfectly smooth with no discontinuities or sharp features.
    *   The design is perfectly symmetric across both the horizontal and vertical axes.
    *   The "thickness" is equal to the "chord," making it extremely bluff and prone to high pressure drag.

*   **Flow Field / Simulation Results**:
    *   The pressure plot shows a clear stagnation point at the front (high pressure) and significant low-pressure zones on the upper and lower shoulders where flow accelerates.
    *   The velocity field reveals a massive, symmetric wake extending far downstream, indicating total flow separation.
    *   There is zero lift generated because the flow field is top-bottom symmetric.
    *   The drag is clearly dominated by "form drag" rather than skin friction, caused by the lack of a tapered trailing edge.

**Relentless Assessment**:
*   The blunt rear of the design is the primary failure; it prevents flow recovery and forces a massive separation zone.
*   The excessive thickness causes unnecessary flow acceleration and low-pressure regions that contribute to drag without providing lift.
*   The mapping shows that setting all `radius_param` values to 1.0 creates this circle. To improve, we must vary these radii to create an elongated chord and a much thinner profile.
*   Key changes needed: (1) Elongate the chord by keeping leading/trailing radii high while collapsing the "shoulder" radii, (2) Implement a sharp trailing edge using the `edgy_param`, (3) Introduce camber to break symmetry and generate lift.
