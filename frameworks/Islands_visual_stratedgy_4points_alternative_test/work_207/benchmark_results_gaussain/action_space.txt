# Airfoil Optimization Reference Card

## General Design Rules
*   **Flow Direction**: Right-to-Left. CP0=LE (Nose); CP4=TE (Tail) [iter 2].
*   **TE Sharpness**: CP4 must be $r, e = 1.0$ for Kutta condition/drag reduction [iter 5].
*   **Monotonicity Gradient**: Radii must generally decrease from Max Thickness toward the TE. $r_{CP3} > r_{CP2}$ creates a "molar tooth" bulb that triggers massive separation [iter 9].
*   **Nose Parabola**: $r_{CP0}$ should be $\leq r_{CP1/7}$. If $r_{CP0}$ is significantly larger than neighbors, negative edginess creates a "hammerhead" flat-face [iter 9].
*   **Anti-Necking vs. Bulbousness**: Maintain $r_{CP3/5}$ between $0.55$ and $0.75$. Below $0.5$ creates an "hourglass" neck [iter 8]; above $r_{CP2/6}$ creates a rear bulb [iter 9].
*   **Camber Coordination**: $r_{CP2} > r_{CP6}$ provides lift, but the entire profile must remain convex. Local "saddles" (concavity) destroy the pressure gradient [iter 9].

## New Evidence (Iteration 9)
*   **The Molar Tooth Effect**: Setting $r_{CP3} (0.76) > r_{CP2} (0.54)$ creates a rear-heavy bulb. Airfoils must taper toward the TE; increasing radius mid-chord creates a "saddle" that kills lift [iter 9].
*   **Hammerhead Nose**: High LE radius ($r_{CP0}=0.86$) combined with lower transition radii ($r_{CP1/7}=0.66$) creates a blunt, flat face that pushes flow outward, causing immediate separation [iter 9].
*   **Radius Continuity**: An airfoil is a gradient. Abrupt jumps (e.g., $0.8 \rightarrow 0.6 \rightarrow 0.5 \rightarrow 0.7$) create wavy surfaces. Radii should follow a smooth $r_{LE} \rightarrow r_{Max} \rightarrow r_{TE}$ progression [iter 9].
*   **Camber Sabotage**: Even with $r_{CP2} > r_{CP6}$, if the rear sections (CP3/5) are too thick/symmetric, the resulting wake negates any pressure differential generated at the front [iter 9].
