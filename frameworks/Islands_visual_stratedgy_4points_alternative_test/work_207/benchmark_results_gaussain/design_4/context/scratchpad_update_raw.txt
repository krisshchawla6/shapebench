# Airfoil Optimization Reference Card

## General Design Rules
*   **Flow Direction**: Right-to-Left. CP0=LE; CP4=TE [iter 2].
*   **TE Dominance**: CP4 is the most critical feature; sharpening it ($r, e \to 1.0$) is the primary driver for drag reduction [iter 5].
*   **Tadpole Effect**: Avoid extreme radius differences between the nose/tail (high $r$) and the midsection (low $r$); high curvature gradients create difficult pressure recovery [iter 5].
*   **Concavity Risk**: Aggressive "pinching" in the rear (CP3/CP5) creates concave surfaces that risk boundary layer separation [iter 5].
*   **Shoulder Drag**: Excessive negative edginess at CP0 creates a flat face with sharp "shoulders," causing localized flow acceleration/separation [iter 5].
*   **Decoupling Strategy**: Use the delta between CP2 and CP6 radii to tune camber independently of thickness [iter 5].

| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |

| **CP5 (L-Back)** | Transition | Radius > 0.22 | Mirrors CP3; critical for preventing blunt wake and rear concavity [iter 5]. |
| **CP6 (Bottom)** | Pressure Side | Rad: 0.2 - 0.3 | Lower $r$ than CP2 creates positive camber; avoid "chin" protrusions [iter 5]. |
| **CP7 (L-Front)** | Transition | Gradual $e$ transition | Prevents early LE separation; must maintain monotonic curvature [iter 4]. |

## New Evidence (Iteration 5)
*   **Successful Sharp TE**: Confirmed $r=1.0, e=1.0$ at CP4 successfully enforces the Kutta condition, transforming the body from a bluff shape to a lifting airfoil.
*   **Camber Control**: Successfully decoupled thickness and camber by setting CP6 radius lower than CP2.
*   **Curvature Gradients**: The "streamlined_kutta_taper" revealed that while thinning the airfoil is good, doing it too abruptly (pinching CP3/CP5) creates "dead air" concavities.
*   **Nose Geometry**: $e = -0.85$ at CP0 is too aggressive; it creates a flat stagnation face that forces the flow to "turn the corner" too sharply at the shoulders.
