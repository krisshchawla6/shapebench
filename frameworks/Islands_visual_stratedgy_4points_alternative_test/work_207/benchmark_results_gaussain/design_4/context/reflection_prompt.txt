## Designer's Predictions
- **Visual Intuition Strategy**: Looking at the wake in the second image, it is clear the flow "gives up" trying to follow the circle's rear curve. To fix this, I must extend the tail to a sharp point, allowing the flow to leave the surface smoothly. 
- **Chord Elongation**: I will maintain a high radius at CP0 (Nose) and CP4 (Tail) while pulling in the "waist" of the shape (CP2, CP3, CP5, CP6) to transform the circle into a slender blade.
- **Leading Edge Profile**: A circular nose is okay, but a slightly parabolic one is better. I'll use a high negative edginess at CP0 to broaden the stagnation region and prevent early separation.
- **Trailing Edge Sharpness**: Following the Design Rules, CP4 needs "support." I will set CP3 and CP5 to low radii to ensure the shape tapers monotonically toward the sharp TE at CP4.
- **Camber Introduction**: To generate lift, I will make the lower surface (CP6) slightly flatter (lower radius) than the upper surface (CP2), creating a subtle camber.

The provided images show Design 1, which is a perfect circle. 
- **Geometry**: 
    - The shape is a bluff body (a cylinder) with a 1:1 aspect ratio.
    - All control points are at maximum radial distance (1.0), creating a uniform perimeter.
    - There is no trailing edge; the rear of the shape is as blunt as the front.
    - The surface is perfectly smooth (edginess 0) but geometrically unsuitable for flight.
- **Flow Field / Simulation**:
    - There is a massive stagnation point at the leading edge (yellow/green region in pressure).
    - A massive, wide wake (dark purple region) exists behind the circle, indicating total flow separation.
    - High velocity gradients occur at the 12 o'clock and 6 o'clock positions where the flow is forced to accelerate around the "shoulders."
    - The simulation results show high pressure drag and likely zero lift due to symmetry.

Design 1's action vector `[1.0, 0.0, 0.0, ...]` maps directly to a circle. The reward (-0.0036) is near zero because it lacks any airfoil characteristics. 
- **Visible Flaws**: 
    - The excessive thickness (caused by `r2` and `r6` being 1.0) creates high drag.
    - The blunt rear (caused by `r4=1.0` and `e4=0.0`) prevents the Kutta condition, causing the massive wake.
- **Key Changes Needed**:
    1. Dramatically reduce the thickness by shrinking radii for CP2 and CP6.
    2. Sharpen the trailing edge by setting `e4` to 1.0.
    3. Streamline the transition from the nose to the tail to prevent separation.
