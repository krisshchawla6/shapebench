## Objective
Optimize an airfoil shape to maximize aerodynamic performance (reward).

## Designer's Predictions
*   **Exploitation Strategy**: I am leveraging the "Optimal Range" data from the Reference Card to transform the existing circular failure into a high-performance subsonic airfoil. Since the circle has maximum drag, I am focusing on the "Drag Reduction" and "Lift Generation" patterns identified in Iteration 2.
*   **Chord Extension**: I will keep CP4 (Trailing Edge) at maximum radius (1.0) and CP0 (Leading Edge) at a high radius (0.85) to maximize the horizontal axis relative to the vertical axis.
*   **Profile Thinning**: I am setting CP2 (Top) to a radius of ~0.28 and CP6 (Bottom) to ~0.16. This creates a thin, streamlined profile that reduces the massive pressure drag seen in the circular wake.
*   **Kutta Condition**: I will set CP4's edginess to 1.0. This transforms the blunt rear of the circle into a sharp point, forcing the flow to leave the airfoil smoothly and reducing wake turbulence.
*   **Camber and Asymmetry**: By setting CP6 radius significantly lower than CP2, I create a flatter lower surface and a curved upper surface, which will generate a pressure differential (lift).
*   **Thickness Distribution**: I am applying positive angle offsets to CP2 and CP6 to shift the maximum thickness toward the first 30% of the chord, which is essential for maintaining laminar flow and preventing early separation.

## Per-Parameter Predictions

- **CP0 [0.85, 0.0, -0.9]**: I am setting r=0.85 and e=-0.9 because I predict it will create a blunt, rounded nose that is stable against varying angles of attack.
- **CP4 [1.0, 0.0, 1.0]**: I am setting r=1.0 and e=1.0 because I predict it will create a sharp, elongated trailing edge that eliminates the circular wake.
- **CP2 [0.28, 0.15, -0.1]**: I am setting r=0.28 and a=0.15 because I predict it will thin the upper surface and move the crest forward to improve pressure recovery.
- **CP6 [0.16, 0.15, -0.1]**: I am setting r=0.16 and a=0.15 because I predict it will create a nearly flat lower surface, providing the necessary camber for lift.
- **CP1 & CP7**: I am setting intermediate radii (~0.6) and negative edginess to ensure a smooth, monotonic transition from the blunt nose to the thinner mid-section.
- **CP3 & CP5**: I am setting positive edginess (~0.4) to begin the "pinching" process that leads to the sharp trailing edge at CP4.

The provided images show the baseline design (Design 1) which is a perfect circle.
- **Geometry**:
    - The shape is a uniform cylinder/circle with a 1:1 thickness-to-chord ratio.
    - It lacks any streamlining or a trailing edge to control flow separation.
    - The control points are distributed at maximum radial distance (radius_param = 1.0) with no angular or sharpness variation.
- **Flow Field**:
    - There is a massive, symmetric stagnation zone at the front (leading edge).
    - A very large, turbulent wake is visible behind the shape, indicated by the dark purple region in the velocity/pressure plots.
    - The flow separates almost immediately after the vertical mid-points, leading to high pressure drag.
    - There is no lift generated, as the flow is perfectly symmetric above and below the shape.

Reference to Design 1: The action vector `[1.0, 0.0, 0.0, ...]` creates this circular profile. The reward of -0.0036 is the baseline for a non-aerodynamic body.
To improve this, we must:
1. Elongate the shape by increasing the chord length (CP0 and CP4).
2. Drastically reduce thickness by lowering the radii of CP2 (top) and CP6 (bottom).
3. Sharpen the trailing edge (CP4) to satisfy the Kutta condition and minimize the wake.
4. Introduce camber by making the bottom surface flatter than the top.
