The provided images show the baseline design (Design 1) which is a perfect circle.
- **Geometry**:
    - The shape is a uniform cylinder/circle with a 1:1 thickness-to-chord ratio.
    - It lacks any streamlining or a trailing edge to control flow separation.
    - The control points are distributed at maximum radial distance (radius_param = 1.0) with no angular or sharpness variation.
- **Flow Field**:
    - There is a massive, symmetric stagnation zone at the front (leading edge).
    - A very large, turbulent wake is visible behind the shape, indicated by the dark purple region in the velocity/pressure plots.
    - The flow separates almost immediately after the vertical mid-points, leading to high pressure drag.
    - There is no lift generated, as the flow is perfectly symmetric above and below the shape.

Reference to Design 1: The action vector `[1.0, 0.0, 0.0, ...]` creates this circular profile. The reward of -0.0036 is the baseline for a non-aerodynamic body.
To improve this, we must:
1. Elongate the shape by increasing the chord length (CP0 and CP4).
2. Drastically reduce thickness by lowering the radii of CP2 (top) and CP6 (bottom).
3. Sharpen the trailing edge (CP4) to satisfy the Kutta condition and minimize the wake.
4. Introduce camber by making the bottom surface flatter than the top.
