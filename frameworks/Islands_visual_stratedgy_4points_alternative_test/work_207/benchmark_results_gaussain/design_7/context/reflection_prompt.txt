- **Geometry**:

    - It is extremely thick (diameter equals chord length) and lacks a trailing edge.
    - All control points (the black dots) are equidistant from the center with zero edginess.
    - There are no visible geometric flaws like self-intersections, but it is a bluff body, not an aerodynamic airfoil.
- **Flow Field**:
    - There is a massive stagnation point at the front (leading edge) and a large, wide wake at the rear.
    - The velocity field shows significant flow separation starting immediately after the vertical mid-point.
    - The pressure distribution is symmetric, indicating high pressure at the nose and very low pressure in the separated wake region, leading to high pressure drag.
    - There is no lift generated, as the flow is perfectly symmetric.

Design 1's action vector consists of all 1.0 radii and 0.0 for angle/edgy parameters, resulting in the cylinder. Its reward of -0.0036 is "good" only compared to failed simulations (-5.0), but poor for actual airfoil performance. The failed designs (3, 4, 5) likely failed due to "pinching" (too low radii in the midsection) or self-intersecting angles. To improve, we must maintain the geometric stability of the circle while introducing the sharp trailing edge and streamlined tapering seen in conventional airfoils.
