## Objective
Optimize an airfoil shape to maximize aerodynamic performance (reward).

## Designer's Predictions
- **Exploitation Strategy**: I am taking the baseline circular shape (the only "successful" geometry in the current population that didn't fail with a -5.0 reward) and applying the established Aerodynamic Design Rules to transform it into a high-performance lifting body.
- **Kutta Condition Enforcement**: By setting CP4 to maximum radius and maximum edginess, I predict the flow will be forced to leave the trailing edge smoothly, eliminating the bluff-body wake seen in the images.
- **Chord Elongation**: Reducing the thickness-to-chord ratio is essential. By pulling the mid-section control points (CP2, CP3, CP5, CP6) toward the center (lower radius), I create a streamlined shape that reduces pressure drag.
- **Camber Introduction**: Differentiating the radii of CP2 (top) and CP6 (bottom) will create a pressure differential. A slightly smaller radius on the bottom (CP6) creates a flatter pressure side, inducing lift.
- **Transition Smoothing**: I will use CP1 and CP7 as transition points with moderate radii and slightly negative edginess to ensure the flow accelerates smoothly from the nose to the suction side without "shoulder" separation.
- **Separation Prevention**: Following "Iter 5" evidence, I will avoid "pinching" the rear too much (keeping CP3/CP5 radius > 0.25) to prevent concave surfaces that cause boundary layer separation.

## Per-Parameter Predictions
- **CP0 [0.95, 0.0, -0.5]**: I am setting radius to 0.95 and edginess to -0.5 to create a blunt, circular leading edge that avoids the "flat-face" drag seen in aggressive designs.
- **CP1 [0.55, 0.0, -0.2]**: I am setting radius to 0.55 to provide a smooth ramp from the nose to the thickest part of the wing.
- **CP2 [0.32, 0.05, 0.0]**: I am setting radius to 0.32 and angle to 0.05 to define the max thickness/suction peak slightly forward of center.
- **CP3 [0.38, 0.0, 0.3]**: I am setting radius to 0.38 and edginess to 0.3 to begin a gradual, convex taper towards the tail.
- **CP4 [1.0, 0.0, 1.0]**: I am setting radius and edginess to 1.0 to create a perfectly sharp trailing edge at the maximum possible chord length.
- **CP5 [0.38, 0.0, 0.3]**: I am mirroring CP3's radius to ensure a symmetric closure toward the trailing edge, preventing a blunt base.
- **CP6 [0.24, 0.05, 0.0]**: I am setting a lower radius (0.24) than CP2 to create positive camber and a flatter lower surface.
- **CP7 [0.55, 0.0, -0.2]**: I am setting radius to 0.55 to mirror the upper transition for a clean flow path to the lower surface.

- **Geometry**: 
    - The observed shape is a perfect circle, representing a classic bluff body.
    - The eight control points are equidistantly distributed around the perimeter at a uniform radius.
    - The surface is perfectly smooth with no curvature discontinuities or sharp features.
- **Flow Field**:
    - There is a massive wake region extending far downstream, indicated by the large dark purple area in the velocity magnitude plot.
    - Flow separation occurs prematurely at approximately 90 and 270 degrees (the "shoulders" of the circle), leading to high pressure drag.
    - A clear stagnation point is visible at the leading edge (the leftmost point) with a corresponding high-pressure zone.
    - The flow is entirely symmetric, meaning the lift is zero and the drag is maximized for this volume.

- **Mapping & Improvements**:
    - Design 1's action vector `[1.0, 0.0, 0.0, ...]` maps directly to this circle: all radii are 1.0 (maximum), all angles are 0.0 (no offset), and all edginess values are 0.0 (perfectly smooth).
    - **Change 1**: Sharpen CP4 (the trailing edge) to $r=1.0, e=1.0$ to enforce the Kutta condition and collapse the wake.
    - **Change 2**: Significantly reduce the radii of CP2, CP3, CP5, and CP6 (the mid-section) to transform the circle into a slender, streamlined airfoil.
    - **Change 3**: Introduce camber by making the upper radius (CP2) larger than the lower radius (CP6).
    - **Change 4**: Adjust the nose (CP0) edginess to approximately -0.5 to maintain a robust, rounded leading edge that can handle variations in angle of attack without separation.
