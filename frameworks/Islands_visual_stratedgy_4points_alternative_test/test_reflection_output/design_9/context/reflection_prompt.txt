## Designer's Predictions
*   **Exploitation Strategy:** I am exploiting the established polar coordinate logic (LE at $a=0$, TE at $a \approx \pm1$) but applying the "Goldilocks Radius" rule derived from the failure of Design 1. By collapsing the mid-body radii while keeping the poles extended, I will transform the "bean" into a "blade."
*   **Camber Generation:** I will set CP1 and CP2 radii to $\approx 0.40$ and CP4, CP5 to $\approx 0.25$. This $0.15$ radial delta creates a curved mean camber line, which is essential for generating lift at low angles of attack.
*   **Smoothing (The "Healer"):** I will set all mid-body and LE `edgy_param` values to $-1.0$. This ensures the transition from the thin mid-body to the extended LE and TE is organic and differentiable, preventing the "starfish" spikes seen in earlier iterations.
*   **Thickness Control:** By keeping the maximum thickness (the delta between upper and lower mid-body points) small, I aim to minimize the pressure drag that neutralized Design 1.
*   **Aft Loading:** I will position the angular parameters to ensure a gradual closure toward the Trailing Edge, encouraging the Kutta condition to settle at the sharp tip.

*   **CP1, CP2 (Upper Mid) [r $\approx$ 0.4]:** I am setting these to lower values than Design 1 to reduce thickness while maintaining a convex upper loft.
*   **CP4, CP5 (Lower Mid) [r $\approx$ 0.25]:** I am setting these lower than the upper points to create a "scooped" lower surface, inducing positive camber.
*   **CP0, CP7 (TE) [1.0, $\pm$1.0, 1.0]:** I am setting these to maximum radius and maximum edginess to create a sharp, elongated trailing edge for clean flow separation.

**Geometry Observations:**
*   The shape is a "bluff body" resembling a thick bean or pebble rather than a streamlined wing.
*   The thickness-to-chord ratio is extremely high (estimated at 60-70%), which is far outside the efficient subsonic range.
*   The Trailing Edge (TE) features a sharp "stinger" due to the high `edgy_param`, but it is attached to a massive, rounded base, preventing efficient flow closure.
*   The Leading Edge (LE) is smooth and rounded, but its transition into the mid-body is too abrupt because of the high mid-body radii.

**Flow Field / Simulation Observations:**
*   **Pressure Field:** There is a massive high-pressure stagnation zone at the nose. Because the body is so thick, the pressure gradient becomes adverse very early on the upper and lower surfaces.
*   **Velocity Field:** The velocity plot shows massive flow separation starting near the mid-chord. A large, turbulent wake is visible behind the airfoil, indicating high pressure drag (form drag).
*   **Lift/Drag:** The symmetry and thickness suggest the lift-to-drag ratio is likely near zero or negative, explaining the 0.0000 reward. The flow is completely stalled.

**Parameter Mapping & Improvements:**
*   Design 1's failure is primarily caused by `radius_params` for CP1, CP2, CP4, CP5, and CP6 being in the "Bloat Zone" ($r \in [0.58, 0.83]$).
*   The `edgy_param` values are mostly correct (smooth mid-body, sharp TE), but they cannot compensate for the poor radial distribution.
*   **Key Changes Needed:** 
    1. Dramatically reduce mid-body radii ($r$) to the range of $0.20-0.45$ to achieve a fineness ratio of 10-15%.
    2. Maintain the $r \approx 1.0$ at CP3 (LE) and CP0/7 (TE) to maximize chord length.
    3. Introduce camber by setting the upper radii (CP1, CP2) significantly higher than the lower radii (CP4, CP5).
