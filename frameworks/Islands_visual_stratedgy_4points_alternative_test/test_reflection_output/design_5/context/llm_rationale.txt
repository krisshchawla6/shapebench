* **Exploration Strategy (Extreme Thinning):** The population is currently producing "slugs" because high radii are being rewarded by default volume. I will explore the "Needle" boundary—setting mid-body radii to near-minimum values (0.15-0.25) to see if a hyper-thin profile can trigger better flow attachment.
* **Asymmetric Camber Trial:** To move away from the symmetric block, I will set the upper surface radii (CP4, CP5) slightly higher than the lower surface (CP1, CP2). This explores the "Camber" region of the design space.
* **Angular Clustering for Chord length:** I will cluster the angles of the leading points very close to the centerline (a=0) and the trailing points close to the poles (a=±1) to maximize the chord length relative to the thickness.
* **Kutta Condition Reinforcement:** I will set the Trailing Edge points (CP0, CP7) to maximum radius (1.0) and maximum edginess (1.0) to create a definitive, sharp separation point.
* **Radial Gradient:** I am testing a "bone-like" radial distribution: high at the poles (LE/TE) and very low in the middle. This is an underexplored transition compared to the uniform-high radii of Design 1.
