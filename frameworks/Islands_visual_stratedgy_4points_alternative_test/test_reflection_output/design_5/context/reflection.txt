This analysis examines the transition from the "slug" (Design 1) to the "asymmetric needle" (Design 2) by evaluating the impact of aggressive radial thinning and angular clustering.

### Per-Parameter Analysis

| Parameter | Prediction | Outcome | Correspondence |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **CP0 [r=1.0, e=1.0]** | Sharp, max-extent Trailing Edge (TE). | The rightmost point of the airfoil is a perfectly sharp, singular vertex. | **High** |
| **CP1 [r=0.25, e=-1.0]** | Aggressively thin the lower-rear to prevent "slug" shape. | The lower-rear boundary is pulled significantly inward, creating a concave-style taper toward the TE. | **High** |

### Key Learnings

#### 1. The "Shard" Effect (Radial Discontinuity)
The "Needle" strategy was partially successful in increasing the aspect ratio, but it revealed a new flaw: **Radial Gradient Extremity.** By setting the poles (CP3, CP0/7) to ~1.0 and the immediate neighbors to ~0.2, the designer created a "shard" or "wedge" rather than a streamlined airfoil. The flow will likely still see the front as a blunt obstruction because the transition from the tip (CP3) to the body (CP2/CP4) is too steep.

#### 2. Camber vs. Thickness
The attempt at asymmetry (CP4 > CP2) successfully broke the "block" symmetry of Design 1. The resulting shape has a flat-bottomed, sloped-top profile resembling a high-speed wedge. While this is better than a circle, the "waist" of the airfoil is now perhaps *too* thin, which may lead to structural instability or flow separation immediately behind the leading edge.

#### 3. Angular Clustering is the "Chord Multiplier"
The clustering of CP3 at $a=0$ and CP0/7 at $a=1.0$ effectively stretched the design. However, the "flatness" of the leading edge is determined by the angular distance to the next points (CP2 and CP4). Because $a=0.28$ and $a=-0.22$ are relatively far from the pole at $a=0$, the "nose" is not a point but a broad triangle. To get a "needle," the neighboring angles need to be much closer to 0 (e.g., $\pm 0.05$).

#### 4. Edgy Param Logic
Setting `edgy_param` to -1.0 for the body successfully eliminated the "corners" seen in Design 1. The resulting silhouette is composed of clean, mathematical curves (or lines, given the high tension), proving that the "blockiness" of the previous iteration was a result of neutral or positive `edgy_param` values.

### Summary for Next Iteration
The design has moved from a **Volume-Maximized Block** to a **Surface-Area-Minimized Shard**. The next step is to "smooth the transition" by raising the mid-body radii slightly (0.4-0.6) and tightening the angular spread around the nose to create a parabolic entry rather than a triangular one.
