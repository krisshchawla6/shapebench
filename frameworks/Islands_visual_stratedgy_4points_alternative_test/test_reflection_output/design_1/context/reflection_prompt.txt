## Objective
Optimize an airfoil shape to maximize aerodynamic performance (reward).

## Designer's Predictions
- **Exploitation Focus**: I am exploiting the negative lesson learned from Design 1: "Angular Clustering = Failure." I will move from a "blob" to a "foil" by maximizing the angular spread of control points.
- **Chord Establishment**: I will designate Control Point 4 as the Leading Edge (LE) and Control Point 0 as the Trailing Edge (TE). By setting their `radius_param` high and their `angle_param` at opposite extremes, I will pull the geometry into a high-aspect-ratio shape.
- **Camber and Thickness**: I will use asymmetric `radius_param` values for the upper surface (points 1-3) versus the lower surface (points 5-7). By making the lower radii smaller, I will create a cambered profile to generate lift.
- **Flow Control (TE)**: To solve the 0.0 reward issue, the trailing edge (Point 0) must be sharp. I will use a high `edgy_param` value here to force a geometric termination point for the flow.
- **Strategic Smoothing**: While the TE needs to be sharp, the LE needs to be rounded to handle varying angles of attack. I will keep Point 4's `edgy_param` moderately negative.

## Per-Parameter Predictions
- **Point 0 [0.9850, 0.9200, 0.8500]**: High radius and high angle to stretch the trailing edge as far back as possible; high edgy value to create a sharp point for flow reattachment.
- **Point 4 [0.9200, -0.9500, -0.4500]**: High radius and extreme negative angle to stretch the leading edge forward; moderately negative edgy value to maintain a smooth, rounded nose.
- **Point 2 [0.4500, 0.2500, -0.8500]**: Moderate radius on the upper surface to create the "hump" of the airfoil; very negative edgy to ensure a smooth transition over the suction side.
- **Point 6 [0.2200, -0.2500, -0.9000]**: Low radius on the bottom surface to pull the lower belly inward, creating the thickness gradient and camber necessary for lift.

- **Geometry Description**:
    - The observed shape is a small, compact "pebble" or "river stone" with a low aspect ratio (nearly 1:1).
    - It is extremely smooth and rounded, lacking any recognizable leading edge (LE) or trailing edge (TE).
    - The control points appear to be "bunched" or clustered together, creating a thick, inefficient volume rather than a streamlined profile.
    - There are no geometric discontinuities, but the proportions are entirely non-aerodynamic.
- **Flow Field Description**:
    - Based on the shape, the flow likely encounters a massive stagnation point at the front of the "pebble."
    - There is likely immediate flow separation on both the top and bottom surfaces due to the extreme curvature and lack of a tapered TE.
    - A very large, turbulent wake is expected behind the object, leading to high pressure drag.
    - Pressure distribution is likely symmetric and chaotic, resulting in the observed 0.0000 reward (zero net lift and high drag).
- **Parameter Mapping & Key Changes**:
    - **Visible Flaw**: Angular Clustering. This maps directly to the `angle_param` values in Design 1 being confined to a narrow range (-0.25 to 0.11).
    - **Visible Flaw**: Lack of Chord. This is caused by `radius_param` being relatively uniform (0.33 to 0.93) without sufficient angular separation to stretch the geometry.
    - **Visible Flaw**: Bluntness. The extremely negative `edgy_param` values (-0.9) forced the geometry into a circle-like smoothing mode.
    - **Key Changes**: 1) Force a wide spread of `angle_param` across the full [-1, 1] range. 2) Increase `radius_param` at the extremities (indices 0 and 4) to define the chord. 3) Set a high `edgy_param` for the trailing edge to prevent separation.
