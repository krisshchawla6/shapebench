## Reference Card: Airfoil Degree of Freedom (DoF) Mapping (Updated Iter 4)

### Global Design Rules
*   **Aspect Ratio & Chord**: Maximized by placing the LE at $a \approx 0.0$ and the TE at the polar limits $a \approx \pm 1.0$. Spanning the origin is necessary to create a 2D object with volume [iter 3, 4].
*   **Structural Priority**: `angle_param` (chord/placement) > `radius_param` (thickness/body) > `edgy_param` (flow regulation) [iter 1, 2, 4].
*   **The "Waist" Trap**: Intermediate radii must be comparable to (or larger than) LE/TE radii. Dropping intermediate radii to $r < 0.4$ while LE/TE are at $r \approx 0.9$ causes **radial collapse**, resulting in a "wasp-waist" or "bone" shape that yields zero reward [iter 4].
*   **Kutta Condition Necessity**: A geometric singularity (high `edgy_param` $\approx 1.0$) at the Trailing Edge is mandatory to define flow reattachment [iter 2, 3, 4].

---

### DoF 1: `radius_param` (Distance from Centroid)
*   **Chord Stretching**: High values (~0.9+) are required at the LE and TE poles to establish the primary dimension of the wing [iter 1, 2, 3, 4].
*   **Body Establishment**: To avoid a "waisted slug," intermediate points (CP1-2, CP4-5) must maintain high radii. The "thickness" of the airfoil is the delta between upper and lower radii at similar angles, not the distance from the origin [iter 4].
*   **Camber Manifestation**: Asymmetric radii between upper and lower surfaces create camber, but this only generates lift if a continuous "body" (sufficient radius) exists to support the pressure differential [iter 4].

### DoF 2: `angle_param` (Angular Position)
*   **Polar Wrap-around**: $a=-1.0$ and $a=1.0$ are the same point. The LE must be at $a \approx 0.0$ if the TE is at the polar limits to maximize chord length [iter 3, 4].
*   **Chord Establishment**: The most critical parameter for unfolding the geometry. Clustering angles near one pole creates "shards"; spanning the poles creates a functional profile [iter 1, 3, 4].
*   **Monotonic Sequencing**: Angles must progress smoothly (TE $\to$ LE $\to$ TE). Non-monotonic jumps create "hooks" or self-intersecting loops [iter 2].

### DoF 3: `edgy_param` (Local Curvature/Sharpness)
*   **TE Flow Regulation**: High positive values ($e \approx 0.85$ to $1.0$) at the Trailing Edge create the "stinger" necessary for flow separation [iter 2, 3, 4].
*   **LE Nose Rounding**: Extreme negative values ($e \approx -0.85$ to $-1.0$) at the Leading Edge (CP3) ensure smooth flow acceleration and prevent "lumpy" pressure distributions [iter 1, 2, 3, 4].
*   **Geometry Healing**: Extreme negative values force the smoothest possible transition between points, effectively "healing" polygons into continuous curves [iter 1, 4].
