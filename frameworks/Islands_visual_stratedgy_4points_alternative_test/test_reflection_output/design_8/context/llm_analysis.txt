* **Geometry Observations**:
    * The airfoil in Design 1 exhibits a severe "radial collapse" or "starfish" effect where the mid-body points are pulled tightly toward the centroid.
    * The resulting shape is extremely thin, appearing as a nearly one-dimensional line or a very sharp, concave arrowhead rather than a convex lifting body.
    * The trailing edge and leading edge are stretched to the polar limits, but the lack of internal volume (thickness) makes the geometry aerodynamically non-viable.
* **Flow Field Observations**:
    * The pressure and velocity plots show almost no interaction with the surrounding fluid; the body is too thin to displace air or generate a pressure differential between surfaces.
    * There is no visible stagnation point at the nose or a smooth acceleration over the upper surface, leading to the 0.000 reward.
    * The velocity field lacks a distinct wake structure, suggesting the flow simply bypasses the collapsed geometry.
* **Parameter Mapping & Design Flaws**:
    * **Low Mid-Body Radii**: The `radius_param` values for CP1, 2, 4, 5, 6 in Design 1 range from 0.19 to 0.31. This is the direct cause of the collapse. To create a functional wing, these must be increased to the "Goldilocks Zone" ($0.6 - 0.85$).
    * **Angular Clustering**: While the LE is at $a \approx 0.0$, the neighboring points are relatively close, which, combined with low radii, created the "pinched" look.
    * **Key Changes Needed**: 
        1. Increase mid-body radii significantly to create thickness and convexity.
        2. Implement asymmetry (camber) by setting upper surface radii (CP1, 2) higher than lower surface radii (CP4, 5, 6).
        3. Use extreme negative `edgy_param` values (-1.0) on all points except the TE to ensure a smooth, organic subsonic surface.
