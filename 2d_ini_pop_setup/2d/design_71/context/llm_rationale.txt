*   **Exploitation Strategy**: This design refines the successful topology of Design 1 by performing a localized search in the parameter space, specifically targeting the thickness and trailing-edge parameters that define the L/D ratio.
*   **Trailing Edge Optimization**: I have increased $e3$ from 0.837 to 0.941 and maximized $a3$. A sharper, more precisely positioned trailing edge reduces the wake size and stabilizes the stagnation point, directly improving aerodynamic efficiency.
*   **Refining Thickness Distribution**: By reducing $r1$ (from 0.212 to 0.182) and increasing $a1$ (from 0.664 to 0.691), I am creating a thinner profile with the maximum thickness located further aft, which is a classic strategy for reducing drag in subsonic flows.
*   **Dorsal Surface Consistency**: All top-performing designs (Rank 0, 1, and 2) utilize the maximum radial boundary for CP2 ($r=1.0$). I am maintaining this constraint as it appears to be a global optimum for maximizing the lift-producing surface area.
*   **Stagnation Point Management**: A slight adjustment to $a0$ and $a2$ aims to optimize the transition between the upper and lower surfaces, smoothing the pressure gradient near the leading edge to prevent premature transition to turbulence.
