*   **Exploitation Strategy**: This design builds directly on the successful topology of Designs 1 and 3, which both utilize maximum radial expansion for the leading and trailing anchors (r0=1.0, r3=1.0) and a retracted interior point (r1 ≈ 0.4).
*   **Trailing Edge Sharpness**: To improve upon Design 1, I am significantly increasing the sharpness of the trailing edge (e3) to 0.8850. A sharper TE reduces base drag and improves the Kutta condition, which should increase lift and reduce the wake seen in the sol/1_u.png image.
*   **Curvature Distribution**: I am shifting CP1's angular position (a1) to 0.5420. By moving this point, we aim to smooth the transition from the leading edge to the upper suction surface, attempting to "flatten" the suction peak and extend it further downstream.
*   **Lower Surface Smoothing**: CP2 is adjusted to r2=0.8320 and a2=-0.1650 with extreme smoothness (e2=-0.9950). This aims to minimize the thickness of the lower boundary layer and improve the pressure profile seen in the p-field.
*   **Anchor Stability**: Maintaining r0=1.0 and a0=-0.0750 ensures the nose remains robust. Design 3 had a more negative a0 than Design 1; I am splitting the difference to find the optimal leading-edge incidence.
