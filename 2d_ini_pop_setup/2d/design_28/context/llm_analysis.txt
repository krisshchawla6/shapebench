Design 1 exhibits a relatively conventional airfoil shape with a blunt leading edge and a sharply tapering trailing edge. The pressure distribution shows a strong suction peak on the upper leading edge, which is typical for generating lift but often leads to early flow separation if the pressure recovery gradient is too steep. The velocity field (U) reveals a noticeable wake region trailing from the posterior, suggesting that the "edgy" parameter (1.0) at the final control point (CP3) combined with the angular offset (0.80) might be creating a trailing edge that is too abrupt or improperly aligned with the freestream, increasing pressure drag. The lower surface is relatively flat (CP2 radius 0.79, angle -0.21), while the upper curvature is largely dictated by CP1 (radius 0.37, angle 0.69). Key changes needed: 1) Reduce the frontal area by lowering the radius_param of CP0 to streamline the entry. 2) Soften the trailing edge by reducing the edgy_param of CP3 to see if a slightly more rounded exit reduces the wake. 3) Explore the negative angular space for CP1 and positive for CP2 to test inverted or reflexed camber profiles that are currently absent from the top performers.
