Design 1 exhibits a high-camber, "pinched-waist" geometry characterized by a very low radius parameter at the second control point (r1=0.212588). While this achieves a reward of 5.4897, the visualization reveals significant aerodynamic compromises. The flow fields (`1_u.png`, `1_v.png`) show a concentrated high-velocity region on the upper surface followed by a potentially unstable pressure recovery due to the extreme concavity of the lower surface. This "thin-plate" behavior is aerodynamically fragile. The trailing edge (CP3) is sharp (e3=0.837), which is effective, but the mid-section's lack of thickness limits the potential for a high-lift, low-drag pressure distribution. Key flaws include: 1) Excessive concavity on the pressure side causing localized drag; 2) A "necking" effect in the thickness distribution; and 3) An overly aggressive curvature transition that risks early stall. To improve, we must: 1) Increase the radius of internal points to create a conventional thickness profile; 2) Distribute curvature more evenly to manage the adverse pressure gradient; and 3) Maintain the sharp trailing edge while rounding the leading edge for better angle-of-attack robustness.
