*   **Apply Novel Design Strategy**: I am intentionally ignoring the "pinched" mid-section trend (low r values) found in the top-ranked designs. *   **Pressure Recovery Optimization**: By using high smoothness (negative edgy_param) for the first three control points and a perfectly sharp trailing edge (e=1.0), the design aims to maintain attached flow over the suction surface for as long as possible before the final pressure recovery at the tail.
*   **Angular Distribution**: The angular parameters are adjusted to distribute the control points more effectively around the chord, specifically shifting the upper surface point (CP1) to broaden the shoulder of the airfoil.
*   **Aerodynamic First Principles**: This design utilizes a "classical" approach—a well-rounded leading edge for flow robustness and a sharp trailing edge to satisfy the Kutta condition, diverging from the more experimental, thin shapes of the current population.
