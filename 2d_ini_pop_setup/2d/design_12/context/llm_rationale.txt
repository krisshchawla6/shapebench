*   **Strategy Focus (Exploration)**: Current successful designs (1, 2, 4, 5) are clustered with `angle_param` for the trailing edge (CP3) near 1.0 and smooth leading edges. This design intentionally pushes `a3` to the opposite boundary (-1.0) to explore a completely different orientation and camber orientation.
*   **Boundary Exploration**: I am pushing `radius_param` and `angle_param` towards their limits (0.9-1.0 and -1.0/1.0 respectively) to test the edges of the stable design envelope.
*   **Sharpness Variation**: While previous designs use very smooth mid-sections (`e` < -0.8), I am introducing a semi-sharp leading edge (`e0` = 0.2234) to investigate how a less rounded nose affects pressure distribution and boundary layer transition.
*   **Camber Innovation**: By setting `a0` (nose) and `a3` (tail) to negative extremes while keeping `a1` (upper) positive, I am creating a high-camber, S-shaped mean line, a geometry currently absent from the top-performing designs.
*   **Geometric Diversity**: This design moves away from the "teardrop" archetype of Design 1 by utilizing more aggressive radial distances for all control points, attempting to maximize the enclosed area and investigate different thickness-to-chord ratios.
