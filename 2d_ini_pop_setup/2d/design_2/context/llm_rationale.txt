- **Establishing a High-Lift Baseline**: Following the Novel Design Strategy, I am generating a "cambered-wing" archetype from first principles. By setting a positive `radius_param` on the upper surface and a near-zero or negative one on the lower surface, I create the pressure differential necessary for lift.
- **Kutta Condition Implementation**: CP2 is designated as the trailing edge. I will maximize its `edgy_param` and extend its `radius_param` to ensure a sharp termination of the airfoil, which is the most critical factor for reducing drag in initial populations.
- **Leading Edge Radius Management**: CP0 is treated as the leading edge. A negative `edgy_param` is used to ensure a smooth, circular nose, which prevents premature flow separation at the stagnation point.
- **Chordwise Thickness Distribution**: I will use the `angle_param` on CP1 (upper) and CP3 (lower) to shift the maximum thickness forward (approx. 30% chord), a strategy derived from aerodynamic principles to maintain a favorable pressure gradient over the fore-section.
- **Evolutionary Step-Function**: Rather than incremental changes, this design uses high-contrast parameter values (e.g., very smooth LE vs. very sharp TE) to define the boundaries of the design space for subsequent generations to refine.
