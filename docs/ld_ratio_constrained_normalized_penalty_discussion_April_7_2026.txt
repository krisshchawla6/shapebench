Normalized Penalty Reward: ld_ratio_constrained_m02_re1e7_normalized
Discussion of Design, Motivation, and Challenges
April 7, 2026
================================================================================

OVERVIEW (updated April 10, 2026)
----------------------------------

PROBLEM STATEMENT:
Optimize a 2D airfoil shape to maximize lift-to-drag ratio (L/D) subject to a
pitching moment constraint (C_M >= -0.133) and geometric feasibility constraints.
The pitching moment constraint matters physically: a highly cambered, high-L/D
airfoil tends to have a strongly nose-down pitching moment, which requires a
proportionally larger horizontal tail to trim the aircraft --- so bounding C_M is
equivalent to bounding the induced trim drag. The airfoil is parameterized with
18 Kulfan/CST coefficients at alpha=5 deg, Re=1e7, Mach=0.2. Geometric
constraints include minimum local thickness, spar thickness at 33% chord,
trailing-edge thickness, trailing-edge angle, leading-edge camber shape, and
shape smoothness (wiggliness).

BENCHMARK: Four optimization methods are compared:
  GA/PSO       120 particles x 500 iterations, 20+ attempts
  L-BFGS-B     gradient-based with finite-difference gradients, 26 seeds
  BO_torch     Bayesian optimization with exact GP, 4 seeds (cancelled early)
  ShapeEvolve  LLM-guided optimizer (v3, Gemini 2.5 Flash), 20 attempts

GROUND TRUTH: XFOIL (panel solver). All final results are XFOIL-validated.

WHY A SURROGATE IS NEEDED:
Direct XFOIL optimization at 60,000 evaluations (GA: 120 particles x 500 iters)
would take weeks. NeuralFoil --- a neural network trained on XFOIL data ---
evaluates in microseconds, making population-based search tractable. All Stage 1
reward functions run against NeuralFoil; XFOIL is used only for final validation.
The trade-off: NeuralFoil overpredicts L/D by ~9% relative to XFOIL, and its
predictions become unreliable for shapes outside its training distribution. It
provides an analysis_confidence score to quantify this reliability.

THE TWO-STAGE PIPELINE (key design decision):
Optimizing against a surrogate introduces two systematic problems that prevent
naive hand-off of raw optimizer outputs to XFOIL:

  1. Surrogate exploitation (confidence constraint always active):
     NeuralFoil reports analysis_confidence, measuring proximity to its training
     distribution. Every optimizer drives designs to exactly the confidence lower
     bound --- because higher L/D predictions correlate with designs at the edge
     of NeuralFoil's reliable range. This is unavoidable: it is a monotonic
     relationship in this design space. Removing the constraint produces fictitious
     L/D gains; keeping it means the bound is always active at optimum.

  2. NeuralFoil -> XFOIL CM bias (design-dependent, 0.003-0.005):
     NeuralFoil systematically underpredicts CM magnitude for high-performance
     designs. At M=0.2, compressibility corrections contribute ~0.003 of this.
     A design optimized to NF CM = -0.133 (the formal limit) has XFOIL CM ~
     -0.136 to -0.138 --- a constraint violation. Raw optimizer outputs mostly
     fail XFOIL CM validation for this reason.

To address both problems, a two-stage pipeline was adopted:

  Stage 1 (exploration): Run each optimizer against NeuralFoil with a penalized
  reward function:
    reward = NF L/D - 500 * sum(normalized_violations)
  where both C_M >= -0.133 and conf >= 0.90 are explicit penalty terms, each
  violation expressed as a dimensionless fraction of its bound (lambda=500 chosen
  so a single complete constraint failure costs more reward than any infeasible
  L/D gain). The four methods reach different Stage 1 NF L/D plateaus:
    GA/PSO ~354,  ShapeEvolve ~344,  L-BFGS-B ~325,  BO_torch ~264 (cancelled)
  These differences reflect how each method interacts with the conf and CM
  constraint boundaries, and are not predictive of final XFOIL performance.

  Stage 2 (refinement): Post-process the best design(s) from each method with
  IPOPT adjoint (CasADi autodiff through NeuralFoil, ~30 iterations) using
  tightened constraints:
    - CM >= -0.125  (tightened from -0.133; 0.008 buffer covers worst-case
                     NF->XF CM bias of ~0.003-0.005)
    - conf >= 0.85  (relaxed from 0.90 but still in-distribution)
  Regardless of Stage 1 differences, IPOPT converges all four methods to NF L/D
  ~357-362. The design space has one dominant optimum in this CM/conf-constrained
  region, and IPOPT finds it from any reasonable starting point.

  Stage 2 sensitivity study (CM threshold): for L-BFGS-B and ShapeEvolve only,
  a second IPOPT sweep was run with the looser bound CM >= -0.130. This yielded
  slightly higher NF L/D (~364-371), but XFOIL validation showed true C_M ~
  -0.133 to -0.135 for most of these designs --- violating even the relaxed bound.
  NeuralFoil's CM model has ~3-5 count of error at the boundary, confirming that
  the 0.008 buffer in CM >= -0.125 is necessary.

  Stage 3 (XFOIL validation): each Stage 2 design evaluated once with XFOIL.
  The ~9% NF->XF L/D gap is consistent across all methods for CM >= -0.125
  designs (all converge to the same design region). CM >= -0.130 designs show
  highly variable XFOIL results, confirming CM >= -0.125 as the reliable
  operating point.

This is analogous to surrogate-based optimization with constraint back-off, standard
in robust/reliability-based design but rarely demonstrated explicitly in benchmarks.

  - GA/PSO is the most expensive by NF eval count (~1.2M). Each attempt runs

  - L-BFGS-B also accumulates ~1M NF calls, spread over 26 seeds × 3 restarts.
    The high count comes from 3-point finite differences: each gradient step

    even when approximated numerically.

    of wall time before cancellation due to stagnation.

  - Stage 2 (IPOPT post-processing) adds negligible compute: each IPOPT run is
    ~30 CasADi iterations, taking ~1-5 s on CPU. The IPOPT step is cheap relative
    to any Stage 1 method.

  - "IPOPT runs (×2)" for L-BFGS-B: four seed candidates (s7, s20, s25, s26)
    each ran through IPOPT twice — once with CM≥-0.130 threshold and once with
    CM≥-0.125 — to find the tighter-constraint optimum. Seed 25 ("2-step" in the
    results table) required two sequential IPOPT warm-starts to converge.

BACKGROUND
----------
The reward function ld_ratio_constrained_m02_re1e7 was developed to match the
operating conditions of the IPOPT/AeroSandbox adjoint optimizer (alpha=5 deg,
Re=1e7, Mach=0.2, N_crit=9.0) and enforce the same geometric and aerodynamic
constraints via soft penalties. The constraints are:

  - Local thickness > 0 everywhere (no self-intersection)
  - t(x/c=0.33) >= 0.128 (minimum spar thickness)
  - t(x/c=0.90) >= 0.014 (minimum trailing-edge thickness)
  - Trailing-edge angle >= 6.03 deg
  - upper_weights[0] > 0.05 (leading-edge camber shape)
  - lower_weights[0] < -0.05 (leading-edge camber shape)
  - Wiggliness <= 2 x NACA0012 wiggliness
  - CM >= -0.133 (pitching moment)
  - analysis_confidence > 0.90 (NeuralFoil reliability)

The adjoint result at these conditions is L/D = 191.85 (marked as infeasible by
its own feasibility check, likely due to a boundary-touching constraint).


PROBLEM WITH THE ORIGINAL PER-CONSTRAINT LAMBDA FORMULATION
------------------------------------------------------------
The original reward used per-constraint penalty weights (lambda_thick_all,
lambda_t033, lambda_t090, lambda_te_angle, lambda_uw0, lambda_lw0,
lambda_wiggliness, lambda_cm, lambda_conf), each defaulting to 1.0. The reward
was:

  reward = L/D - sum_i(lambda_i * violation_i)

where each violation_i is already normalized by its constraint scale (e.g.,
CM violation = max(0, -0.133 - CM) / 0.133), so violations are dimensionless
fractions.

With all lambdas = 1.0, the penalties were far too weak. Infeasible designs
routinely achieved L/D = 400-720 in NeuralFoil's surrogate landscape, making
even a total fractional violation of 10 (penalty = 10) trivial compared to the
200-500 unit L/D gain from entering infeasible space. All four optimization
methods (GA/PSO, L-BFGS-B, BO_torch, v3 flash-2.5) returned infeasible best
designs.

An experiment with lambda_cm = lambda_conf = 50 (lambda_lam50) improved things
marginally: L-BFGS-B seeds 2-4 found designs with CM near -0.133 and conf
near 0.79-0.91, but still infeasible. The rogue seed 1 found L/D = 783 with
conf = 0.000 --- the lambda_conf = 50 penalty of 50 units was no match for a
590-unit L/D gain. GA attempts remained at conf = 0.000 throughout.

The core problem: there are 9 independent lambda parameters, each requiring
manual tuning, and the appropriate values differ by orders of magnitude across
constraints (geometric constraints are easier to satisfy than CM and conf).


NORMALIZED PENALTY DESIGN
--------------------------
The new reward ld_ratio_constrained_m02_re1e7_normalized replaces all 9
per-constraint lambdas with a single scalar lambda_penalty (default = 500):

  reward = L/D - lambda_penalty * sum_i(violation_i)

where all violation_i are already normalized fractional values in [0, inf).
A single tunable parameter now controls constraint enforcement for all
constraints simultaneously.

Lambda = 500 was chosen based on the following reasoning:
  - The adjoint feasible optimum is ~192 L/D
  - The worst infeasible L/D gain observed is ~500 above the feasible baseline
    (some designs reached L/D ~ 700 with NeuralFoil)
  - A single complete constraint failure (violation_i = 1.0) costs 500 reward
    units, sufficient to cancel even extreme infeasible L/D gains
  - Multiple simultaneous violations cost proportionally more, making badly
    infeasible designs decisively unprofitable
  - Mild violations (e.g., violation_i = 0.01) cost only 5 units, preserving
    gradient signal near the constraint boundary without creating a cliff

  seed1: L/D=289.7, CM=-0.1316, conf=0.910, viol={wiggliness: 3e-4}

  seed4: L/D=247.2, CM=-0.1328, conf=0.978, viol={}

All designs have negligible or zero violations --- a qualitative improvement
over the lam50 results where CM was typically off by ~37% of its limit and
conf was typically 0.79-0.91. The designs are now sitting at or within
numerical precision of the constraint boundary.

Note: seeds 2, 3, 4 and GA att1, att2 show feasible=False despite viol={}
(empty violation dict). This is a floating-point boundary issue: the penalty
code uses `if v > 0.0` before adding to the violations dict, while the
feasibility check uses strict inequalities (e.g., `uw0 > 0.05`). When a
design lands exactly on the boundary, the violation is below Python's floating
point threshold for `v > 0.0` but the strict feasibility check still fails.
These designs are effectively feasible.


REMAINING CHALLENGES
--------------------
1. Surrogate vs. high-fidelity discrepancy
   NeuralFoil predicts L/D ~ 250-352 for the best designs, while the adjoint
   (AeroSandbox IPOPT, XFOIL-based) gives 191.85. If the best NeuralFoil design
   is re-evaluated with the adjoint solver, the true L/D will very likely be
   substantially lower. The conf > 0.90 constraint guards against unreliable
   NeuralFoil predictions, but even at conf = 0.90, the surrogate may
   disagree with higher-fidelity analysis by 50-100% on L/D.

   The fundamental tension: NeuralFoil's confidence score measures internal
   model uncertainty, not agreement with XFOIL for a specific airfoil geometry.
   Extreme high-L/D designs may be in sparse regions of the NeuralFoil training
   distribution even at conf = 0.90.

2. Surrogate exploitation at the confidence boundary
   Designs consistently converge to conf ~ 0.90, the exact lower bound of the
   confidence constraint. This is a classic sign of surrogate exploitation: the
   optimizer has learned that the most "rewarding" region of NeuralFoil's
   landscape is near the edge of its reliable training domain. Increasing the
   confidence threshold to 0.95 would shrink the exploitable region and push
   designs toward geometries NeuralFoil is more confident about, reducing the
   surrogate-vs-XFOIL discrepancy.

3. Adjoint is a local optimum
   The adjoint L/D = 191.85 is a gradient-based local optimum from a single
   starting point (likely near NACA0012). The true global optimum under hard
   constraints at these conditions may be higher. L-BFGS-B with random restarts
   explores the design space more broadly and may be identifying better basins,
   but this cannot be confirmed without re-evaluation with the high-fidelity
   solver. Using the best NeuralFoil design as a warm-start for the adjoint is
   a practical way to probe whether better local optima exist.

4. Lambda sensitivity
   While lambda_penalty = 500 is a principled default, it still requires
   verification across methods. GA/PSO and v3 (LLM-guided) may behave
   differently from L-BFGS-B because their search strategies interact differently
   with the penalty landscape. The GA uses population-based stochastic search
   that may need even stronger penalties to guide the swarm away from infeasible
   attractors, especially early in the run when the swarm is initialized randomly.

5. Feasibility flag bug
   The `feasible` flag uses strict inequalities in the feasibility check
   (e.g., `uw0 > 0.05`) while the penalty code uses `if v > 0.0` before
   recording a violation. Designs that land exactly on the constraint boundary
   can have viol={} (no recorded violations) but still be marked feasible=False.
   A tolerance of ~1e-8 should be added to the boundary checks, or the
   feasibility definition should use `>= 0.05 - tol` to be consistent with the
   penalty formulation.

6. Barrier/log penalty alternative
   A log-barrier approach (-log(-g(x)) for g(x) < 0) would provide automatic
   infinite resistance at the constraint boundary, eliminating lambda entirely.
   However, log-barriers require the optimizer to start inside the feasible
   region, which conflicts with random initialization. An augmented Lagrangian
   method (self-updating multipliers) is the theoretically preferred approach
   for constrained optimization with infeasible starting points, but would
   require more significant framework changes.

ARE THE DESIGNS FEASIBLE?
--------------------------
This question has a layered answer:

1. Geometrically: YES. All thickness, TE angle, wiggliness, and leading-edge
   weight constraints are satisfied. Violation dicts are empty or negligible.

2. By the code's feasible flag: NO --- but this is a code artifact. The designs
   land at exactly conf = 0.900 and CM = -0.1330. The strict inequality
   `conf > 0.90` evaluates to False at the floating-point boundary even though
   the value rounds to exactly 0.900. This is the feasibility flag bug described
   in challenge 5 above.

3. Aerodynamically (NeuralFoil): BORDERLINE. The designs are at the conf = 0.90
   boundary by design --- the optimizer drove them there because that is where
   NeuralFoil predicts the highest L/D. At conf = 0.90, NeuralFoil is operating
   at the edge of its stated reliability range. The L/D = 352 prediction is not
   necessarily wrong, but it carries significant uncertainty for unusual
   high-camber geometries.

4. Under higher-fidelity analysis (XFOIL directly): CONFIRMED BETTER, but CM
   constraint violated. See XFOIL Validation section below.

Bottom line: the designs satisfy all hard geometric constraints and are
aerodynamically feasible to within NeuralFoil's stated confidence threshold.
The CM constraint is satisfied per NeuralFoil but violated per XFOIL due to a
systematic bias at the CM boundary.

FEASIBILITY CONCLUSION (updated April 8, 2026)
------------------------------------------------
The normalized-penalty designs are:
  - Geometrically feasible: YES
  - CM feasible per NeuralFoil: YES (at boundary, ~0.003 slack from true limit)
  - CM feasible per XFOIL: NO (CM ≈ -0.136 vs limit -0.133, violated by 0.003)
  - conf feasible: BORDERLINE (at exactly 0.900, floating-point boundary issue)
  - Aerodynamically meaningful: YES — XFOIL L/D ≈ 285-315 is well above the
    NACA0012-initialized adjoint (XFOIL L/D = 149)

The designs represent a substantial improvement in the NeuralFoil landscape and
confirm that the normalized-penalty approach successfully steered optimization
away from infeasible high-L/D attractors. However, true XFOIL feasibility
requires tightening the CM constraint by ~0.003 (to CM >= -0.130).

RELATIONSHIP TO THE FORMAL PROBLEM STATEMENT
--------------------------------------------
The formal optimization problem is:

  maximize  CL(p) / CD(p)
  subject to:
    t(x/c) > 0  for all x/c in [0,1]
    t(0.33) >= 0.128
    t(0.90) >= 0.014
    TE wedge angle >= 6.03 deg
    Wiggliness <= 2 * W_NACA0012
    CM >= -0.133
    sigma_conf > 0.90

The ld_ratio_constrained_m02_re1e7_normalized reward (conf90, CM>=-0.133)
matches this formulation exactly.

The conf95 variant and the CM>=-0.130 adjoint both solve a DIFFERENT, more
constrained problem. Strictly speaking, they are not solving the problem as
stated above: conf95 uses sigma_conf > 0.95 and CM >= -0.130, excluding
designs feasible under the original formulation but with CM in (-0.133, -0.130)
or conf in (0.90, 0.95).

These tighter bounds are surrogate-compensation heuristics, not part of the
problem definition. The correct framing for a paper is:

  - The PROBLEM is as stated above (CM >= -0.133, sigma_conf > 0.90).
  - The REWARD used during optimization applies tightened internal bounds
    (CM >= -0.130, sigma_conf > 0.95) to compensate for known systematic
    biases in the NeuralFoil surrogate:
      * NeuralFoil predicts CM ~0.003 higher than XFOIL at the boundary,
        so CM >= -0.130 in NeuralFoil corresponds to CM >= -0.133 in XFOIL.
      * NeuralFoil overpredicts L/D by 10-15% at conf=0.90; raising to 0.95
        keeps designs in NeuralFoil's well-trained region.
  - FEASIBILITY of a final design is always evaluated against the original
    constraints (CM >= -0.133, sigma_conf > 0.90), using XFOIL as the
    ground-truth evaluator.

ROOT CAUSE: CONF CONSTRAINT IS ALWAYS ACTIVE
---------------------------------------------
Regardless of what confidence threshold is set (0.90, 0.85, or any value), IPOPT
always drives conf to exactly that threshold. This is because there is a monotonic
relationship between design extremity and confidence: the highest-L/D airfoils
(highly cambered, thin) lie at the edge of NeuralFoil's training distribution.
The optimizer finds it profitable to push L/D up by moving into lower-confidence
regions until it hits the constraint wall.

This means the conf threshold is always active at convergence, giving:
  - feasible=False (Python strict inequality fails at floating-point boundary)
  - But solver_ok=True ("Optimal Solution Found") — the NLP is solved correctly
  - The designs ARE the best achievable within the NeuralFoil landscape; the
    feasible=False label is a numerical artifact, not a true infeasibility

Corollary: tightening/relaxing the conf threshold shifts the constraint boundary
but does not eliminate the exploitation — it merely changes which design point
IPOPT converges to.

DISCUSSION: WOULD MACH=0.03 BYPASS THE CM BIAS ISSUE?
------------------------------------------------------
The reward_exact_notebook / multipoint_hpa formulation uses Mach=0.03 (HPA conditions).
The question was whether using M=0.03 in the single-point L/D problem would reduce
the NeuralFoil→XFOIL CM bias observed at M=0.2.

At M=0.2, Prandtl-Glauert compressibility corrections scale all pressure coefficients
by 1/√(1-M²) ≈ 1.02 (~2%), contributing ~0.003 to CM in absolute terms for a typical
CM of -0.133. NeuralFoil and XFOIL may implement these corrections differently, which
can explain part of the observed CM bias. At M=0.03, both codes are essentially
incompressible (correction factor ≈ 1.0005), so this source of bias would nearly vanish.

However, two caveats:
  1. The conf exploitation problem is invariant to Mach number — the optimizer still
     drives designs to the conf boundary regardless of operating conditions.
  2. M=0.03 is a different optimization problem (HPA conditions) with different optimal
     designs. If the actual goal is single-point L/D at M=0.2, switching to M=0.03 is
     not a bypass — it produces different airfoils optimized for different conditions.

The reward_exact_notebook results are separately noted to have much weaker surrogate
exploitation issues (conf stays at ~0.96-0.98, see REWARD_EXACT_NOTEBOOK FEASIBILITY
CHECK section above), consistent with the HPA operating conditions being well within
NeuralFoil's training domain.

WHY XFOIL WAS NOT USED IN STAGE 2 INSTEAD OF NEURALFOIL+IPOPT
==============================================================

Question: given that XFOIL is the ground-truth evaluator used for Stage 3
validation, why was NeuralFoil (with IPOPT adjoint) used for Stage 2
refinement instead of XFOIL directly?

SHORT ANSWER
------------
XFOIL is not differentiable and cannot be embedded in a gradient-based
optimization loop. IPOPT requires exact analytical gradients (Jacobians and
Hessians), which are only available because NeuralFoil is expressed through
CasADi's symbolic backend via asb.numpy. XFOIL is a Fortran panel solver with
no gradient flow — there is no way to differentiate through it.

DETAILED REASONING
------------------

1. Differentiability requirement
   IPOPT uses an interior-point method that requires:
     - First-order derivatives (Jacobians) of the objective and constraints
       with respect to all design variables
     - Second-order derivatives (Hessians of the Lagrangian)
   These are computed by CasADi's automatic differentiation, which builds a
   symbolic computation graph of the entire pipeline:
     Kulfan params → CST geometry (closed-form) → NeuralFoil (asb.numpy) → L/D
   Every step in this chain is expressed symbolically, so CasADi can
   differentiate through the entire pipeline in one backward pass.

   XFOIL cannot be placed in this chain. It is a compiled Fortran executable
   called as a subprocess; there is no CasADi-compatible implementation and no
   gradient flow through it. CasADi has no way to compute dL/D / d(Kulfan) via
   XFOIL.

2. Finite-difference XFOIL adjoint is impractical
   An alternative would be to approximate XFOIL gradients numerically, as
   L-BFGS-B does with NeuralFoil (jac='3-point', 2d=36 extra evals per gradient
   step). However:
     - NeuralFoil evaluates in microseconds; XFOIL takes ~0.1-1 s per call.
     - A finite-difference XFOIL gradient step costs ~36 XFOIL calls.
     - IPOPT typically requires O(100) gradient steps to converge.
     - Total: ~3,600 XFOIL calls per IPOPT run, at ~0.1-1 s each = ~6-60 minutes
       per restart, vs ~1-5 s for CasADi-based IPOPT through NeuralFoil.
   This would be 100-1000x slower than the NeuralFoil-based approach, making
   multi-seed, multi-method post-processing impractical.

3. XFOIL is reserved for one-shot validation (Stage 3)
   The minimum use of XFOIL needed to obtain ground-truth aerodynamic
   coefficients is a single evaluation per final design. Running XFOIL only at
   Stage 3 achieves this at negligible cost. The NeuralFoil→XFOIL systematic
   biases (L/D overprediction ~9%, CM underprediction ~0.003-0.005) are handled
   in Stage 2 via constraint tightening (CM >= -0.125 in Stage 2 corresponds to
   CM >= -0.133 in XFOIL), rather than by switching the Stage 2 solver to XFOIL.

4. Why this approach is valid
   The constraint tightening strategy works because the NeuralFoil→XFOIL biases
   are systematic and approximately constant across designs in the same region
   of design space. Once the bias is characterized (from Stage 3 results on a
   subset of designs), the Stage 2 constraints can be tightened by the bias
   amount to ensure XFOIL feasibility. This is standard practice in
   surrogate-based optimization with constraint back-off.

SUMMARY
-------
  Question                                          Answer
  ---------------------------------------------------------------
  Can XFOIL be used in IPOPT?                       NO — not differentiable
  Can XFOIL gradients be finite-differenced?        YES, but 100-1000x slower
  Is one-shot XFOIL validation sufficient?          YES — bias is systematic
  How is NeuralFoil→XFOIL bias handled?            Constraint tightening in Stage 2
