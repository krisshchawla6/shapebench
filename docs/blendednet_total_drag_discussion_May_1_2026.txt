BLENDEDNET TOTAL-DRAG REWARD VARIANTS — DISCUSSION
Started: May 1, 2026
================================================================================

MOTIVATION
----------
All prior BlendedNet benchmark runs (shapebench_5 and shapebench_5_max_LD) used
Cfx_mean — the mean streamwise skin-friction coefficient over the 8192 sampled
surface points — as the drag proxy. Post-hoc drag decomposition on the best GA
design (iter_0189_p015, att4) revealed:

  CD_proxy   (Cfx_mean)   = 0.004246   ← what the reward minimized
  CD_integrated            ≈ 0.027      ← area-weighted pressure + friction drag
  Discrepancy factor       ≈ 6×

The large discrepancy means the optimizer minimized friction drag only; pressure
drag (~93-98% of total drag) was completely ignored. To evaluate whether the
benchmark results change when the correct aerodynamic drag metric is used, two
new reward variants were implemented:

  shapebench_5_total_drag           min  mean_CD_integrated
  shapebench_5_max_LD_total_drag    max  mean(CL / CD_integrated)

Files:
  environments/BlendedNet/rewards/shapebench_5_total_drag.py
  environments/BlendedNet/rewards/shapebench_5_max_LD_total_drag.py


CD_INTEGRATED FORMULA
---------------------
Matching analysis/BlendedNet/compute_drag_decomposition.py exactly:

  CD_pressure  = (1/S_ref) * Σ_cells  Cp_cell  * A_cell * nx_cell
  CD_friction  = (1/S_ref) * Σ_cells  Cfx_cell * A_cell
  CD_integrated = CD_pressure + CD_friction

where:
  S_ref    — planform XY-projected reference area (Σ |cross(v1-v0, v2-v0)_z| / 2)
             computed from mesh triangle vertices; for the GA reference design:
             S_ref = 0.860 m² (mesh is in meters)
  A_cell   — 3D cell area from pyvista compute_cell_sizes (n_cells,)
  nx_cell  — outward face-normal x-component from compute_normals (cell_normals,
             auto_orient_normals=True)
  Cp_cell  — pressure coeff at cell centre, nearest-neighbour mapped from 8192
             Transolver sample points
  Cfx_cell — streamwise skin-friction coeff, same mapping

S_ref uses the XY (chord-span) projection because the BWB mesh is oriented with
x = freestream (chord), y = span, z = height. Total mesh surface area Σ A_cell
≈ 1.11 × S_ref for the reference design (confirmed diagnostically).

Formula verified: running the code on the GA reference design gives
  cd_pressure  = 0.025973
  cd_friction  = 0.001125
  CD_integrated= 0.027098
matching the reference decomposition output.

Note on Transolver Cp sign convention: the surrogate predicts Cp with the
opposite sign to the standard aerodynamic convention (Cp_mean ≈ -CL, not +CL).
Because of this inversion, the formula cd_pressure = Σ Cp_T * A * nx / S_ref
gives the correct POSITIVE pressure drag without an explicit sign flip. This was
verified against the reference decomposition.


IMPLEMENTATION DETAILS
----------------------
File: environments/BlendedNet/environment.py

Integration-weight cache _geom_integ_cache (module-level dict, keyed by geometry
tuple) stores per-geometry quantities constant across all bisection alpha calls:
  (cell_areas, face_nx, s_ref, cell_to_sample_idx)

Computed once per unique geometry via scipy cKDTree nearest-neighbour query (cell
centres → 8192 sample points). Zero marginal cost on calls 2-24 of each design
evaluation (3 unique CL targets × 8 bisection iterations = 24 total calls).

_run_sim appends to its return dict:
  "CD_integrated": cd_integrated   (= cd_pressure + cd_friction)

SURROGATE EXPLOITATION DISCOVERY
---------------------------------
After seeing BO/CMA-ES total_drag best values of 0.003251–0.003416, a sanity
check revealed these are physically implausible.

Expected CD_integrated for a realistic BWB design: ~0.015–0.040 (based on the
reference design at 0.027 and random-start designs at 0.010–0.020).
Optimizer results: 0.003–0.004 — an order of magnitude lower.

Initial suspicion: formula bug. Verification showed the formula IS correct:

  Diagnostic run (May 1, 2026):

  GA att4 reference (alpha=8.979°):
    S_ref = 0.860 m²   Σ_A = 0.956 m²   ratio = 1.112
    Cfx_mean(pts) = 0.004450   Cfx_area_avg = 0.001012
    cd_pressure   = 0.025973
    cd_friction   = 0.001125
    CD_integrated = 0.027098   ← matches reference decomposition ✓

  BO seed6 best (B1=200, B2=50, B3=200, C2=785, C3=180, C4=90, S1=48.1, S2=40,
                 S3=40; alpha=4.928°):
    S_ref = 0.945 m²   Σ_A = 1.079 m²   ratio = 1.141
    Cfx_mean(pts) = 0.009058   Cfx_area_avg = 0.002135
    cd_pressure   = 0.001222
    cd_friction   = 0.002437
    CD_integrated = 0.003659

The formula is correct. The BO design genuinely has cd_pressure = 0.001 per the
surrogate's prediction. Why?

ROOT CAUSE: PARAM_BOUNDARY_COLLAPSE + SURROGATE EXTRAPOLATION

  The BO optimal design has:
    B3=200 (minimum), B1=200 (maximum), B2=50 (minimum), C3=180 (minimum),
    C4=90 (maximum), S2=40 (minimum), S3=40 (minimum)
  Almost all parameters sit at their box bounds — the same PARAM_BOUNDARY_COLLAPSE
  pattern observed for the Cfx_mean reward (different corner, same phenomenon).

  Mechanical explanation:
  - The design achieves CL=0.206 at alpha=4.928° vs reference's 8.979°.
    Lower alpha is possible because B3=200/C2=785 gives a compact, high-lift-
    slope (low-AR) geometry.
  - Lower alpha → flatter pressure distribution → much lower pressure drag.
    cd_pressure drops from 0.026 (reference) to 0.001.

  Physical implausibility:
  - Aspect ratio of BO design: AR = 4×B3/C2 = 4×200/785 = 1.02 (very low).
  - Low AR normally INCREASES induced drag; a 26× reduction in cd_pressure
    for a low-AR design is not physically realistic.
  - This geometry is likely far from the Transolver training distribution,
    causing the surrogate to extrapolate in a way that predicts anomalously
    low pressure drag at boundary-corner geometries.

  Comparison to Cfx_mean collapse:
  - Old reward: optimizers collapsed to S1=60, C2=550, C4=90, B2≈54 corner.
    Cfx_mean was driven toward zero (unphysically smooth surface prediction).
  - New reward: optimizers collapse to B3=200, B1=200, C2=785, C3=180 corner.
    cd_pressure is driven toward zero (unphysically flat pressure distribution).
  - Both are surrogate extrapolation artifacts at parameter bounds.
    Different reward → different exploited corner → same failure mode.

Additional evidence:
  - BO results are tightly clustered: 0.003416–0.003584 (5% spread across 10 seeds).
    If this were the true optimum, seeds shouldn't cluster so tightly.
    Instead, all seeds find the same boundary corner.
  - CMA-ES best (0.003251) slightly better than BO (0.003416) — consistent with
    CMA-ES being more effective at walking along the boundary face.
  - PSO shows larger variance (0.003665–0.004915): only 2/10 seeds reach the
    best corner, while 8 are trapped in interior local optima.
  - v3: att3/att5 both at ~0.003422/0.003393 — also found the boundary corner.

Cfx_area_avg comparison:
  Reference design: 0.001012 (area-weighted friction avg)
  BO best design:   0.002135 (2.1× higher friction per unit area)
  → BO design has higher friction drag per unit area but lower total CD_integrated,
    meaning cd_pressure overcompensates downward. This is physically inconsistent:
    higher friction does not cause lower total drag.


APPROACHES TO ADDRESS SURROGATE EXPLOITATION
--------------------------------------------
Brainstormed May 1, 2026. Status: not yet implemented (future work).

A. Constrain the geometry directly
   1. Aspect ratio floor: AR = 4·B3/C2 ≥ threshold (e.g., 2.5). Blocks the
      low-AR corner while keeping the full interesting space. Easy to add as
      a feasibility check returning FAIL_REWARD for infeasible designs.
   2. Boundary penalty: fraction of params at bounds → additive penalty term.
      Discourages all-bounds-simultaneously corners without hard exclusion.

B. Expose surrogate unreliability
   3. Sample-seed sensitivity: run surrogate with 3-5 seeds (not just 42);
      if CD_integrated varies >15%, flag as unreliable. No retraining needed.
      Cheap — just re-call with different RandomState seeds.
   4. Nearest-neighbor distance in parameter space: penalize designs far from
      any known training point. Requires access to training data manifest.

C. Physical consistency constraints in the reward
   5. Floor on cd_pressure: require cd_pressure ≥ ε (e.g., 0.003). An unpowered
      body at positive alpha cannot generate net pressure thrust. Any surrogate
      prediction giving near-zero cd_pressure is an artifact → FAIL_REWARD.
   6. Require CD_integrated ≥ Cfx_area_weighted × (Σ A / S_ref): by construction,
      friction is always non-negative, so CD_integrated must be at least the
      friction-only component. If this is violated, reject the design.

D. Reward reformulation
   7. Fixed-alpha evaluation instead of bisection: evaluate at alpha ∈ {3,5,7,9}°
      (no free-alpha exploitation). Removes the optimizer's ability to find
      high-CL-slope geometries that hit CL target at very low alpha.

E. Paper reframing (no code changes)
   8. Document boundary collapse as a primary finding across all reward variants:
      Cfx proxy → S1/C2/C4 corner; CD_integrated → B3/B1/C2 corner; both are
      surrogate extrapolation artifacts at boundary extremes. The finding is that
      gradient-free optimizers reliably exploit boundary regions regardless of
      reward formulation — a fundamental limitation of single-surrogate optimization
      without uncertainty quantification. This motivates UQ-aware surrogate methods
      as future work.

constrained designs reveals residual surrogate exploitation even with AR≥2.5 enforced.

Both designs saturate the same corner: B1→upper, B2→lower, C3→lower, S2→lower.
This is a different corner from the unconstrained exploitation (which drove AR→0
via B3→lower, C2→upper), confirming that the AR constraint blocked one corner
but the surrogate has a second exploitable region.

CD physical plausibility check:
--------------------------------
BO best: CD_integrated = 0.006739 at CL≈0.206, AR=2.547
  Cfx_mean ≈ 0.0083 → estimated area-weighted CD_friction ≈ 0.002 (Cfx_mean/~4×,
  per the Cfx_mean vs Cfx_area_avg ratio documented for the GA reference design)
  → implied CD_pressure ≈ 0.0047

  Theoretical induced drag floor at CL=0.206, AR=2.5, e≈0.8:
    CD_i = CL² / (π × AR × e) ≈ 0.04 / 6.28 ≈ 0.0064

  CD_integrated (0.0067) is at or below the induced drag floor alone, leaving
  essentially zero budget for profile drag. This is physically implausible —
  a well-designed BWB at these conditions should have CD ≈ 0.011–0.015.

Conclusion:
-----------
The AR≥2.5 constraint successfully blocked the flat-wing degenerate corner
(unconstrained best CD ≈ 0.003 → constrained best CD ≈ 0.006), confirming that
the unconstrained exploitation was real. However, the constrained designs still
show 4–5/10 parameters at bounds and CD values at or below the theoretical
induced drag floor. The surrogate exploitation is not fully resolved by the AR
constraint alone — it has migrated to a new boundary corner.

This is consistent with the DrivAer structural exploitation finding: the Transolver
and BlendedNet surrogates both have landscapes that reward boundary-saturated
designs across multiple distinct corners of the parameter space.

Note: no formal LLM diagnostic suite exists for BlendedNet (unlike DrivAer_Star);
this assessment is based on manual parameter inspection and physical drag estimates.
