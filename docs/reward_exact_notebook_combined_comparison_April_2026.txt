KEY OBSERVATIONS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1. BO compute dominates: ~44,800 CPU-h for 4 seeds vs. ~127 CPU-h for 25 PSO
   cases and ~242 CPU-h for 35 v3 cases. The disparity is due to: (a) exclusive
   128-core node allocation, (b) O(n³) GP refit per iteration scaling badly to
   5000 evals, (c) long wall times (up to 135h for seed0).

2. L-BFGS-B is the cheapest per-case at ~1.5h average, but high eval counts
   (~17k mean) reflect many restarts. The nr10 seeds took ~2.2h vs. ~44 min
   for nr3-success seeds — 10 restarts vs. 3 produces ~3× more evals and wall
   time. Total campaign (40 seeds) reaches the highest total eval count of any
   single-core method: ~626k evals across 40 cases.

   NR10 RETRY CONCLUSION: 17/22 nr3-failed seeds recovered successfully with
   nr10, bringing the overall success rate from 45% (18/40 at nr3) to 87.5%
   (35/40 at nr10). The 3 remaining failures (seeds 8, 14, 31) all show
   best_reward = −1.000 after 10 independent starts — the clearest possible
   infeasibility signal. Best recovered designs (seed21: −0.08256, seed7:
   −0.08764) are not competitive with the best nr3 result (seed23: −0.09370),
   so running nr > 10 for the 3 stubborn failures is not worth the cost.
   Full analysis: docs/lbfgsb_failure_analysis_April2026.txt

3. PSO/GA: uniform 60k evals and ~5h wall per attempt. 25 attempts × ~5.1h =
   ~127 CPU-h is modest but the trajectory reaches 60k evals per case, far
   more than BO (5k) or v3 (5-6k).

4. v3 Pro vs. Flash: Pro is ~50% more expensive per case (8h vs. 5.3h) and
   runs fewer evals per case (~5,250 vs. ~5,850). Flash runs more evals in
   less time, likely because Flash generates proposals faster (lower API
   latency) and the optimizer spends less idle time waiting for LLM calls.

5. Total comparison budget (all methods combined): ~45,230 CPU-h, with BO
   accounting for 99% of that.

FIGURE 1 — NeuralFoil_multipoint_objective_vs_iterations
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

What it shows:
  Convergence bands (median + min/max shading) for all four methods vs. model
  evaluations (log scale). Y-axis = penalized objective (-reward, lower is
  better). Dashed horizontal = Adjoint (IPOPT) reference at -0.0785.

Adjoint baseline:
  IPOPT with CasADi autodiff through NeuralFoil, warm-started from NACA 0012.
  It represents a gradient-based local optimum — the best achievable from a
  single starting point using exact surrogate gradients. ~47 NeuralFoil
  evaluations, ~31 seconds wall time. It is a local optimizer: if the landscape
  has multiple basins, it only finds the one nearest NACA 0012. The dashed
  reference line therefore represents the strongest possible single-start
  gradient-informed baseline, not a global optimum.

Key observations:
  - ShapeEvolve (green): fastest convergence — reaches near-adjoint performance
    within ~1,000 evaluations, tightest min-max band.
  - PSO (blue): converges steadily, reaches competitive objective by ~10k evals.
  - L-BFGS-B (pink): ultimately competitive but widest band and longest tail
    (~35k evals); high run-to-run variance driven by the high nr3 failure rate
    (55% of seeds required nr10 retries; 3 seeds permanently failed — see
    FAILURE NOTE below).
  - BO (orange): starts slow due to GP overhead; reaches its plateau at ~5k
    evals but at a higher (worse) objective than the other methods.

L-BFGS-B failure note:
  "Failure" means zero feasible designs found across all restarts — not a
  timeout. L-BFGS-B follows the gradient from a random start to a local
  minimum. If that minimum is infeasible (one or more CL targets unreachable),
  best_reward = -1.000/-2.000/-3.000 (pure penalty, round values, no gradient
  signal pointing toward feasibility). Running longer does nothing — the outcome
  is fixed by the starting point. The only remedy is a new restart.
    nr3 (3 restarts): 22/40 seeds failed (55% failure rate)
    nr10 (10 restarts): 3/40 seeds permanently failed (7.5%): seeds 8, 14, 31
  The wide L-BFGS-B band and long tail in Figure 1 directly reflect this
  initialisation sensitivity.

Key message:
  ShapeEvolve achieves near-adjoint objective earliest and with lowest variance.
  Despite large differences in Stage 1 behavior, the dashed reference lines
  confirm that methods converging to the dominant basin (Adjoint, ShapeEvolve,
  PSO) all reach similar final objectives. L-BFGS-B is competitive when it
  converges but requires many more evaluations and restarts. BO's plateau is
  notably higher — it finds a different basin (see Figure 2).

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

What it shows:
  For each method's best design: side-by-side bars of NeuralFoil weighted CD
  (solid) vs XFOIL weighted CD (hatched), with red dashed line showing the
  NF->XFOIL % error = (XFOIL - NF) / NF per method.

Key numbers:
  Method              NF wCD    XFOIL wCD   NF->XFOIL error
  Adjoint (IPOPT)     0.07851   0.07891     +0.50%
  ShapeEvolve         0.07909   0.07926     +0.21%
  PSO (120p x 500i)   0.07973   0.08009     +0.45%
  L-BFGS-B            0.08263   0.08264     +0.01%
  Bayesian Opt.       0.09421   0.09672     +2.67%

  XFOIL ranking: Adjoint (best) > ShapeEvolve > PSO > L-BFGS-B >> BO

Key observations:
  1. NF->XFOIL bias negligible for top 4 methods (0.01–0.50%) — orders of
     magnitude smaller than the ~9% seen in LAM500. HPA conditions (Ma=0.03)
     sit well within NeuralFoil's training distribution; no constraint back-off
     was required. The surrogate is an essentially faithful proxy for XFOIL here.

  2. XFOIL ranking exactly preserves the NeuralFoil ranking for all 5 methods —
     the surrogate correctly identifies the best optimizer. Ordinal validity
     is confirmed at ground-truth fidelity.

  3. BO's outlier basin confirmed worse by XFOIL: XFOIL wCD = 0.09672, ~22%
     worse than Adjoint (0.07891). Its larger NF->XFOIL error (+2.67%) is
     consistent with the over-cambered design sitting further from NeuralFoil's
     training distribution.

  4. ShapeEvolve matches Adjoint to within 0.44% in XFOIL (0.07926 vs 0.07891).
     ShapeEvolve's NF->XFOIL error (+0.21%) is actually smaller than the
     Adjoint's (+0.50%), meaning its design is slightly more faithfully
     represented by NeuralFoil.

  5. L-BFGS-B achieves near-perfect NF->XFOIL fidelity (+0.01%) but its best
     design (seed21, an nr10 recovery) did not reach the dominant optimum basin
     — confirmed genuinely worse at ground truth.

Key message:
  XFOIL validation confirms NeuralFoil is a highly accurate proxy at HPA
  conditions (0.01–0.50% error), in sharp contrast to LAM500 (~9% bias). The
  surrogate ranking is preserved exactly. ShapeEvolve matches the gradient-based
  adjoint to within 0.44% in XFOIL-validated drag. BO's alternative basin is
  confirmed suboptimal (~22% worse), with a larger surrogate error consistent
  with designs further from the training distribution. No constraint back-off
  was needed for this benchmark.
