To advance the population and explore the design space creatively, I will move away from the high-sweep, symmetric airfoil strategy of Design 1. My goal is to investigate a design that leverages camber and a more moderate sweep, aiming for a different, potentially more efficient, lift generation mechanism.

*   **Focus on Moderate Sweep:** Design 1 used a very high sweep (70.43 deg). I will drastically reduce the sweep to 55.0 degrees. This shifts the design towards a broader delta, potentially reducing reliance on extreme vortex lift and exploring a regime where conventional lift generation combined with moderate vortex lift might be more efficient.
*   **Introduce Camber for Efficiency:** Design 1 used a NACA 00xx (symmetric) airfoil. I will introduce camber by selecting `naca_m=2` and `naca_p=4` (NACA 24xx series). This should allow for more efficient lift generation at design conditions, improving the CL/CDi ratio compared to a purely symmetric section.
*   **Optimize Twist Distribution:** Instead of positive root twist, I will apply a slight negative twist at the root (`twist_root=-2.0`) to potentially offload the inboard section and ensure a smoother lift distribution. I'll maintain a stronger washout at the tip (`twist_tip=-6.0`) to further improve tip flow and potentially delay separation, working in conjunction with the lower sweep and camber.
*   **Adjust Thickness:** Design 1 used 16% thickness. I will specify a slightly thinner airfoil (`naca_t=12`). This aims to reduce profile drag, especially given the introduction of camber which might inherently provide more robust flow.
*   **Moderate Root Chord and Neutral Dihedral:** I'll keep the `root_chord_in` at a moderate value (35.0 inches) similar to Design 1 to maintain a reasonable wing area. `dihedral` will be set to 0.0 to focus on the primary aerodynamic parameters (sweep, camber, twist, thickness) without introducing additional stability complexities at this stage of exploration.
*   **Diversity and Exploration:** This design represents a significant departure from Design 1 across multiple key parameters (sweep, camber, twist, thickness), ensuring bold exploration of the design space rather than incremental tweaking.
