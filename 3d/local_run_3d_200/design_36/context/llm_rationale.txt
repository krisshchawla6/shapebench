*   **Bold Exploration of Higher Sweep:** Designs with higher sweep (e.g., >70 deg) have not achieved the top reward so far, but D4 (74.26 deg) still ranked 2nd. I aim to explore this region by selecting a sweep of 72.0 degrees, a significant increase from D1 (63.12 deg), to investigate if enhanced vortex lift from higher sweep can be synergized with other optimal parameters to unlock new performance peaks.
*   **Reinforce Proven Twist Strategy:** The combination of extreme root washout (`twist_root=-10.0`) and moderate tip washout (`twist_tip=-4.0`) was a key feature of the best-performing designs (D1 and D4). This distribution is critical for managing spanwise load, promoting vortex lift stability, and minimizing induced drag. I will replicate this successful twist profile.
*   **Maximize Effective Camber:** Design 1's NACA 2411 (m=2, p=4) strongly indicates that max camber at 40% chord (`naca_p=4`) is highly beneficial. Designs with `naca_p=0` consistently underperformed. I will test a more aggressive camber (`naca_m=4`) while maintaining the proven `naca_p=4` (NACA 44xx), to see if additional positive camber can further improve lift/drag without detrimental effects, a combination not optimally explored yet.
*   **Moderate Root Chord and Thickness:** To support the potential for increased lift from higher sweep and camber, I will use a moderately larger root chord of 42.0 inches (larger than D1's 33.2 inches, but not the extreme of D5). A moderate thickness of 13% (`naca_t=13`) will be chosen to provide structural integrity and potentially favorable pressure gradients, being slightly thicker than D1's 11% but not excessively so.
*   **Slight Anhedral for Stability/Efficiency:** Design 1 benefited from a slight anhedral (-3.44 deg). To maintain this beneficial characteristic, I will specify a slight anhedral of -2.0 degrees, which typically contributes to lateral stability in highly swept wings and can influence induced drag positively.
