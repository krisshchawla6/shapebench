*   The current population is heavily concentrated in the mid-to-high sweep range (63-75 degrees) and exclusively uses negative twist (washout) at the root. This new design will aggressively explore the **lower sweep range (near 50 degrees)**, which is an underexplored region, to investigate configurations less reliant on strong vortex lift.
*   All previous designs utilize washout (`twist_root` is negative). This design will explore a **positive `twist_root` (washin)**, a completely unexplored configuration. This radical change could potentially increase lift near the fuselage, shifting the load distribution and impacting overall efficiency. `twist_tip` will remain negative for stability.
*   The `root_chord_in` parameter has not been pushed to its upper limit (50 inches). We will utilize the **maximum `root_chord_in` (50.0 inches)** to understand the scaling effects on aerodynamic performance, providing data for a different size class.
*   NACA thickness (`naca_t`) has only been explored up to 18. This design will test the **maximum thickness `naca_t=24`** with a symmetric `NACA 0024` airfoil. This explores the impact of a very thick wing on the CL/CDi, especially in combination with the low sweep and unique twist.
*   The combination of low sweep, high chord, positive root twist, and maximum thickness NACA airfoil creates a design highly distinct from any previously tested. This **bold exploration** will provide crucial data points for sparsely populated regions of the design space, potentially uncovering new design principles or limitations.
