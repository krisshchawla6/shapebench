*   **Hybridization Strategy**: The new design aims to blend the high-performance characteristics of Design 1 (NACA 4412, effective washout) with the higher sweep of Design 6 and the strong root washout of Design 2, along with exploring positive dihedral from Design 6, to create a novel configuration.
*   **Increased Sweep for Enhanced Vorticity**: A `le_sweep` of 69.5 degrees is chosen, slightly higher than Design 1 (65.5) but less extreme than Design 6 (72.9). This aims to strengthen the leading-edge vortices, potentially increasing lift, while attempting to manage induced drag through optimized twist.
*   **Aggressive and Balanced Washout**: The `twist_root` is set to -9.5 degrees (closer to Design 2's max washout), and `twist_tip` to -5.5 degrees (between Design 1 and Design 6). This pronounced washout at the root, tapering towards the tip, is intended to optimize spanwise load distribution, delay root stall, and improve overall lift efficiency.
*   **Cambered Airfoil with Moderate Thickness**: Building on the success of Design 1, a `NACA 4414` profile is selected. This maintains the 4% camber at 40% chord (m=4, p=4) but increases `naca_t` to 14% (from 12% in Design 1). This explores the impact of a slightly thicker, but still cambered, airfoil on lift and drag.
*   **Exploration of Positive Dihedral**: A `dihedral` of 3.0 degrees is chosen. This is a distinct departure from Design 1's negative dihedral and an intermediate value compared to Design 6 (5.8). It explores the effect of positive dihedral on lateral stability and its interaction with the vortex flow, aiming to identify potentially beneficial aerodynamic responses.
*   **Consistent Root Chord**: The `root_chord_in` is set to 35.5 inches, similar to Design 1 (34.8). This maintains a proven scale, allowing the focus to remain on the aerodynamic shaping parameters without introducing significant size effects that might confound the results.
