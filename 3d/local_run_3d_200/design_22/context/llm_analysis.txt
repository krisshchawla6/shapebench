The highest-rewarding design (Design 1) featured a moderate leading edge sweep (63.8 deg) and significant washout at the root (-8.7 deg), with slightly less washout at the tip (-7.0 deg). It used a NACA 4413 airfoil, indicating moderate camber and thickness. In contrast, Design 3, the second-highest performer, had a higher sweep (73.4 deg), even more aggressive root washout (-9.9 deg), and much less tip washout (-4.5 deg), utilizing a thinner, symmetric NACA 0009 airfoil.

From these top performers, several trends emerge:
*   **Sweep Angle**: Both moderate (~64 deg) and high (~73 deg) sweep angles show promise. Lower sweep (D5, ~58 deg) led to lower rewards. This suggests an optimum sweep angle, potentially not at the extreme high end.
*   **Twist Distribution**: Aggressive negative twist (washout) at the root appears critical for high performance. The relative magnitude of tip washout compared to root washout also seems important, influencing load distribution along the span.
*   **Airfoil Type**: Both cambered (NACA 44xx) and symmetric (NACA 00xx) airfoils can yield high rewards, though the cambered Design 1 achieved the highest. A thinner airfoil (D3's 0009) performed well.
*   **Dihedral**: Low dihedral magnitudes (positive or negative) are observed in high-performing designs, while high positive dihedral (D4's 9.4 deg) resulted in very poor performance.

Key design changes needed to improve designs in the population:
1.  **Explore moderate sweep angles more thoroughly**: Investigate the range between Design 1's 63.8 deg and the lower bounds.
2.  **Optimize twist distribution**: Experiment with distinct washout differentials between root and tip, building on the success of D1 and D3's strategies.
3.  **Investigate other camber profiles**: Explore the NACA 24xx series, which offers moderate camber, to see if it provides a good balance for delta wings.
4.  **Balance thickness**: Test thinner-to-medium thickness airfoils for cambered profiles to reduce parasitic drag while maintaining structural integrity and desired lift characteristics.
