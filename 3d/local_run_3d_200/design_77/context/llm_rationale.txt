*   **Embrace Exploration Strategy with Low Sweep:** Previous designs have explored sweep from 51 to 73 degrees. We will push towards the lower boundary of the sweep range, setting `le_sweep` to 48.0 degrees, to explore how a lower sweep might combine with other parameters for unique aerodynamic characteristics. This is a significant departure from the best designs.
*   **Explore High Root Chord:** Current designs mostly cluster around 30-37 inches. We will explore the upper end of the `root_chord_in` range by setting it to 48.0 inches. This will test scaling effects and potential benefits of a larger overall wing area in conjunction with other novel parameters.
*   **Introduce Positive Twist (Washout with Less Negative Values):** All previous designs used negative twist values. To explore a completely new region of the twist parameter space while maintaining the beneficial washout effect observed in Design 1 (`twist_tip > twist_root`), we will set `twist_root` to a slightly negative value (-2.0) and `twist_tip` to a positive value (2.0). This still ensures the tip is less twisted down (or more twisted up) than the root.
*   **Test Extreme Dihedral:** The dihedral parameter has been explored in the mid-range. We will push this to an extreme positive boundary, setting `dihedral` to 14.0 degrees, to understand its impact on CL/CDi in a high-washout, low-sweep configuration.
*   **Maintain Camber, Explore Less Aggressive Profile:** Camber proved crucial for high rewards. We will utilize a NACA 24xx profile (`naca_m=2`, `naca_p=4`), which provides camber but is less aggressive than the NACA 44xx profile of the top design, ensuring lift while exploring a different camber shape.
*   **Explore Thin Wing Profile:** The `naca_t` parameter has been in the mid-range (10-18). We will explore the thinner end of the spectrum by setting `naca_t` to 8, which could potentially reduce form drag and is an underexplored region.
