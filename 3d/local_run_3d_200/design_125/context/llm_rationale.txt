*   **Exploration Strategy: High Sweep**: The current high-reward designs cluster around 63-67 degrees of leading-edge sweep. This new design will explore the higher end of the sweep spectrum (e.g., 75 degrees) to investigate the effects of very strong leading-edge vortices. This pushes into an under-explored region to understand the trade-offs of increased vortex lift versus potential increases in induced drag at higher sweep angles.
*   **Exploration Strategy: Less Aggressive Washout (Root) with Neutral Tip**: While extreme negative twist at the root (-10.0 deg) is common in the population, Design 1 had a slightly less extreme root twist (-7.1 deg). This design will explore a more neutral twist at the root (closer to 0 or slightly negative) to see how it interacts with the high sweep. For the tip, we'll try a very slight washout (e.g., -0.5 deg) to push towards a flatter load distribution compared to the significant tip washouts previously explored.
*   **Exploration Strategy: Unique Camber Profile with Increased Thickness**: The NACA 4412 profile performed well. This design will explore a NACA 20xx profile (e.g., `naca_m=2`, `naca_p=0`), which places the maximum camber at the leading edge. This specific combination with higher thickness (`naca_t=22`) is novel and aims to understand the impact of early camber and a thicker section on high-sweep delta wings.
*   **Exploration Strategy: Extreme Positive Dihedral**: The database shows exploration across negative and moderate positive dihedral. This design will push towards the maximum positive dihedral (+15.0 deg) to thoroughly explore its effect on lateral stability and induced drag, especially when combined with a very high sweep and altered twist distribution.
*   **Root Chord Variation**: The previous designs mostly have root chords in the 33-41 inch range. This design will explore a larger root chord (e.g., 48 inches) to investigate the impact of overall wing scale on performance parameters, especially in conjunction with the other parameter explorations.
