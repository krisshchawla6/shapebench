Based on the analysis of previous designs and the objective to maximize CL/CDi through diverse exploration:

*   The top-performing design (Design 2) strongly suggests that a **mid-range sweep** (approx. 63 deg) coupled with **maximum root washout** (-10.0 deg) and a **cambered airfoil** (NACA 2411) is a highly effective strategy. My new design will leverage these insights.
*   I will select a sweep angle of 65.0 degrees, slightly higher than Design 2, to explore this immediate vicinity of the observed optimum while remaining within the "mid-range" of delta wing sweeps.
*   **Maximum root washout (`twist_root=-10.0`)** will be maintained, as it appears to be a critical feature for efficient spanwise load distribution across multiple high-performing designs.
*   For the airfoil, I will opt for a **cambered NACA 4412 profile** (`naca_m=4`, `naca_p=4`, `naca_t=12`). This profile offers higher camber than the 24xx series used by Design 2, potentially increasing lift, and is similar to Design 5 (4413) but with a slightly thinner profile. The `p=4` ensures the max camber is at 40% chord, a common and effective placement.
*   I will set `root_chord_in` to 28.0 inches. This is notably smaller than Design 2's 33.2 inches and Design 1's 42.67 inches. This explores the possibility that an even smaller, more compact wing with efficient aerodynamic design could yield a good reward by reducing overall wetted area and parasite drag.
*   `twist_tip` will be set to -6.5 degrees. While Design 2 had less aggressive tip washout, some other moderate performers (e.g., Design 5) had higher tip washout. This value will investigate a more pronounced tip washout than Design 2, but less extreme than -10.0, seeking to further offload the tips without excessively reducing overall lift.
*   Dihedral will be set to -5.0 degrees, a moderate anhedral. This is slightly more negative than Design 2's -3.44, continuing to explore the benefits of negative dihedral for induced drag and potentially roll stability characteristics, avoiding the high dihedral that led to poor performance in Design 6.
