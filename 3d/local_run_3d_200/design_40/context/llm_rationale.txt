*   **Leverage Top Performers' Sweep and Chord**: Design 1 (65.5 deg, 34.7 in) and Design 4 (63.1 deg, 33.2 in) achieved the highest rewards, indicating an optimal balance for sweep and root chord. My new design will interpolate within this successful range, targeting a sweep of 67.0 degrees and a root chord of 34.0 inches to slightly push the sweep higher than Design 1 while maintaining a robust wing size.
*   **Hybrid Twist Strategy**: Both top designs feature strong root washout and moderate tip washout. Design 1 had (-7.1, -4.6) and Design 4 had (-10.0, -2.8). I will blend these, aiming for a strong root washout (-9.0 deg) to effectively shed load inboard, and a moderate tip washout (-3.5 deg) to maintain good lift distribution without excessive drag.
*   **Consistent Camber, Varied Thickness**: Design 1's NACA 4412 was highly successful. I will retain NACA 44 (m=4, p=4) as it clearly contributes to high CL. To introduce novelty and explore the design space for thickness, I will slightly increase the thickness percentage to 14% (from Design 1's 12%), testing if a marginally thicker, potentially stiffer wing can maintain performance.
*   **Explore Dihedral near Neutral/Slightly Positive**: Design 1 and 4 both used slight anhedral (-1.7 and -3.4 deg). To explore a different aspect of stability and potentially fine-tune induced drag, I will specify a slight positive dihedral (1.0 deg). This deviates from the top performers but is well within the acceptable range and explores a previously less-used, near-neutral region.
