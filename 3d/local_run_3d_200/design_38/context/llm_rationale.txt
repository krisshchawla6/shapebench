*   The current top designs, while effective, cluster around moderately high sweep angles (63-65 deg). To advance the population through **exploration**, we should push the `le_sweep` towards the upper boundary (e.g., 75 deg). This will aggressively explore the potential for stronger vortex lift, even if it might increase induced drag.
*   All successful designs exhibit washout. To explore a truly novel aerodynamic strategy, this design will feature a slight *positive* `twist_root` (wash-in) combined with a strong `twist_tip` washout. This aims to shift lift further inboard, potentially reducing tip-related drag, delaying tip stall, and exploring an unvisited twist distribution.
*   The `root_chord_in` values for high-performing designs are typically in the mid-range. To broaden the search space and explore the implications of overall wing scale, this design will utilize a significantly smaller `root_chord_in` (e.g., 15 inches), pushing towards the lower bound of the allowed range.
*   Existing high-reward designs used `naca_t` between 11-12. To reduce profile drag and explore performance at a different aspect, this design will incorporate a much *thinner* airfoil, using `naca_t=8`. This pushes towards the lower boundary of the thickness parameter.
*   Camber has proven beneficial, with NACA 24XX and 44XX performing well. This design will use `naca_m=2` and `naca_p=4` to provide moderate camber, which has shown promise without being as aggressive as `naca_m=4`.
*   Dihedral values have been close to zero or slightly negative for high rewards. Extreme positive dihedral resulted in very low reward. This design will explore a moderate positive dihedral (e.g., 7.5 deg) to understand its interaction with the other novel parameters, aiming for a balance between stability and performance, without resorting to the extreme values that performed poorly.
