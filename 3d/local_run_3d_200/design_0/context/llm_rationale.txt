This is the first design iteration, so there is no database or images to draw from for a "Hybrid Strategy." Instead, I will initiate a diverse exploration with a balanced, moderately aggressive design to establish a baseline for future iterations. The reasoning will focus on creating a robust initial design that allows for future blending and optimization.

*   **Initialize diverse exploration:** As this is the first design, select a combination of parameters that are neither at the extreme ends nor dead-center. This ensures the initial design space exploration is broad and provides a meaningful starting point for subsequent evolutionary steps.
*   **Balance lift generation with drag considerations:** Choose a moderately high sweep (68.0 deg) to encourage strong leading-edge vortex lift, characteristic of efficient delta wings. This is combined with moderate camber (naca_m=2, naca_p=4) for additional lift, while keeping thickness (naca_t=12) at a reasonable level to control profile drag.
*   **Employ effective load distribution:** Incorporate a slight positive root twist (1.0 deg) to enhance lift at the wing root, and a moderate tip washout (-3.0 deg) to improve spanwise lift distribution, delay tip stall, and potentially improve CL/CDi. This aims for a more efficient lift profile across the wing.
*   **Maintain general stability:** A slight anhedral (-2.0 deg) is chosen for exploration. While positive dihedral increases stability, a slight anhedral is sometimes used in delta wings for specific stability characteristics or to potentially influence induced drag in certain flight regimes.
*   **Provide a reference scale:** Set a mid-range root chord (32.0 inches) to establish a reasonable and representative scale for the wing, allowing for meaningful aerodynamic evaluation without being too small or excessively large.
