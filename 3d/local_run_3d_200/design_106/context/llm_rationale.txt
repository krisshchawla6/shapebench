My strategy is to build upon the evident successes of the top-performing designs, specifically leveraging aggressive washout and moderate camber, while also exploring slight variations in sweep and thickness to push for further improvements in CL/CDi and increase diversity in the population.

*   **Maximize Washout for Induced Drag Reduction:** The leading designs universally feature strong negative twist (washout). I will push `twist_root` to its maximum negative limit (-10.0 deg) and set `twist_tip` to a substantial negative value (-8.0 deg). This aggressive washout configuration aims to significantly reduce induced drag by shifting the lift distribution inboard, delaying tip stall, and optimizing spanwise loading, which has been a strong indicator of high reward.
*   **Explore Slightly Higher, but Proven, Sweep:** Design 1 succeeded with ~65.5 degrees of sweep. I will test a `le_sweep` of 69.0 degrees. This is within the successful range identified by the top designs (63-67 degrees) but slightly higher, allowing for stronger leading-edge vortex generation while avoiding the performance pitfalls seen with very high sweeps (~79 degrees) where induced drag likely dominates.
*   **Retain Effective Camber, Reduce Thickness for Drag:** The NACA 44xx series has proven very effective. I will stick with `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4` to maintain good lift characteristics. However, to explore drag reduction, I will select a `naca_t` of 10. This is thinner than Design 1's 12%, aiming to decrease profile drag without significantly compromising lift, potentially improving the CL/CDi ratio.
*   **Vary Root Chord for Geometric Exploration:** Design 1 has a root chord of ~34.8 inches. To promote diversity and explore the scaling effect, I will set `root_chord_in` to a value of 40.0 inches. This larger chord, combined with the new sweep and twist, will create a geometrically distinct wing configuration, allowing the optimizer to assess the impact of overall wing size and aspect ratio on performance.
*   **Maintain Small Negative Dihedral:** The top-performing design 1 utilized a small negative dihedral. I will continue this trend with a `dihedral` of -2.5 degrees, as it seems benign or potentially beneficial for lateral stability in highly swept wings and does not appear to hinder CL/CDi performance.
