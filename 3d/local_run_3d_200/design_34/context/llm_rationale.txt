*   **Apply Novel Design Strategy & Explore Uncharted Territory:** Instead of incrementally adjusting existing high-reward designs, I will generate a fresh design with a unique combination of parameters, specifically focusing on an uncambered airfoil with a distinct twist and dihedral profile. This aims to find new high-performing regions in the design space.
*   **Isolate Vortex Lift with Symmetric Airfoil:** The top-ranked design uses a cambered NACA 2411. I will explore using a **NACA 00XX** (no camber, `naca_m=0, naca_p=0`) to isolate and quantify the efficiency of pure vortex lift generated by the delta wing geometry, decoupled from airfoil camber. This addresses the underperformance of Design 2 (NACA 0011) by pairing it with potentially better global parameters.
*   **Explore Moderate-High Sweep (60 deg):** Design 1's sweep (63.12 deg) is high and successful. Designs with very high sweep (74-75 deg) tend to perform poorly. I will select a moderately high sweep (60 deg) to ensure strong vortex formation, but avoid the potential induced drag penalties of extremely high sweep, exploring a slightly different sweet spot.
*   **Investigate a Distributed/Tip-Emphasized Washout:** Design 1 uses maximum negative twist at the root. I will explore a twist distribution with **moderate negative twist at the root (`twist_root=-5.0`) and strong negative twist at the tip (`twist_tip=-8.0`)**. This strategy aims to ensure good load distribution, prevent tip stall, and potentially improve efficiency compared to extremely high root washout.
*   **Test Positive Dihedral:** All top designs have negative or near-zero dihedral. I will introduce a **moderate positive dihedral (`dihedral=5.0`)** to understand its impact on CL/CDi, potentially finding designs with enhanced lateral stability without significant aerodynamic penalties, thereby diversifying the population's characteristics.
*   **Moderate Root Chord & Thickness:** I will keep the `root_chord_in` in a mid-range (35 inches) and `naca_t` relatively thick (18), providing a robust baseline size and structural depth, allowing the other geometric parameters to primarily influence aerodynamic performance.
