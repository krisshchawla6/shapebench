The top-performing design (Design 1) achieved a reward of 4016.0090 with `le_sweep=65.5`, `root_chord_in=34.7`, significant washout (`twist_root=-7.1`, `twist_tip=-4.6`), slight anhedral (`dihedral=-1.7`), and a cambered NACA 4412 airfoil. This indicates that a moderate sweep combined with specific washout and a moderately cambered airfoil can be highly effective in maximizing CL/CDi. The washout likely helps distribute lift more efficiently and delays tip stall, which is crucial for delta wings. The anhedral might contribute to stability characteristics.

Key observations from the population:
- Most high-performing designs utilize significant washout (negative twist at root and tip).
- Cambered airfoils (NACA 24XX or 44XX) are prevalent among the better-performing designs. Design 6 with NACA 0007 had a very low reward, suggesting uncambered thin airfoils might be challenging to optimize for this objective.
- Sweep angles are generally in the mid-60s to low-70s.
- Root chords are mostly in the 30-40 inch range.
- NACA thickness is varied but generally 11-18.

Key design changes needed to improve designs in the population:
1.  Explore more extreme sweep angles, especially towards the upper boundary (closer to 80 degrees) to investigate enhanced vortex lift.
2.  Investigate designs with significantly less washout, or even wash-in (positive twist), to understand its effect on load distribution with different sweep and airfoil types.
3.  Re-explore uncambered airfoils (NACA 00XX) in combination with other aggressive parameters to see if performance can be improved over Design 6.
4.  Test the interaction of very thin airfoils (NACA X.X06-08) with high sweep and different twist distributions, which has only been partially explored (Design 5 used NACA 2406).
