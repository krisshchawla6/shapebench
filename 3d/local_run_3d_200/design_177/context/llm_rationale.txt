*   **Hybrid Strategy Application**: This design blends successful traits from Design 1 (high reward, inverted washout, cambered airfoil) with explorative elements from Design 3 (higher sweep, positive dihedral, thinner airfoil). The aim is to build upon proven success while introducing new characteristics.
*   **Inverted Washout Exploration**: Designs 1 and 2, which yielded the highest rewards, both demonstrate an "inverted washout" pattern where `twist_root` is more negative than `twist_tip`. This design continues to explore this successful load distribution strategy, setting `twist_root` to -9.0 and `twist_tip` to -3.0, providing strong root washout.
*   **Enhanced Vortex Lift Potential**: Drawing inspiration from Design 3's higher sweep, this design increases `le_sweep` to 70.0 degrees. This aims to generate stronger leading-edge vortices, potentially boosting lift significantly, while remaining within a range that should prevent excessive induced drag compared to very high sweeps.
*   **Optimized Lift Area and Airfoil**: The `root_chord_in` is set to 38.0 inches, a slight increase from Design 1 and 2, to provide a larger lifting surface. The NACA 4410 airfoil (NACA m=4, p=4, t=10) retains the strong camber for lift (like Design 1) but reduces thickness slightly from Design 1 (NACA 4412) to potentially lower profile drag, similar to the thinner airfoil of Design 3.
*   **Dihedral Exploration**: This design introduces a slightly positive `dihedral` of 3.0 degrees, moving away from the negative dihedrals of Design 1 and 2. This explores the impact of positive dihedral on lateral stability and its interaction with the vortex lift system, a less explored region within the high-performing designs.
