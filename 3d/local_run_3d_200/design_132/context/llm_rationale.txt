*   **Explore Extreme High Sweep**: The current top designs cluster around 65 degrees sweep. To push the boundaries of vortex lift and explore a less-charted region for delta wings, we will set `le_sweep` to 75.0 degrees, closer to the upper limit (80.0). This could lead to stronger, more stable leading-edge vortices, but also potentially higher induced drag.
*   **Investigate Larger Scale**: The highly rewarded designs generally fall into the 30-40 inch root chord range. We will explore a larger wing by setting `root_chord_in` to 45.0 inches. This expands the planform area, increasing lift potential, and allows us to see how scale interacts with very high sweep and potentially reduced drag from a thinner airfoil.
*   **Challenge Conventional Twist**: Almost all previous designs feature aggressive negative twist (washout). To truly explore, we will drastically reduce washout at the root, setting `twist_root` to 0.0, and implement very mild washout at the tip, `twist_tip` to -2.0. This represents a significant departure from the population trend and will test the impact on load distribution, particularly with high sweep.
*   **Optimize Airfoil Thickness for Drag**: Given the high sweep and larger chord, which might increase induced drag, we will aim to minimize profile drag. We'll utilize the `NACA m=4, p=4` configuration, which has proven effective for lift in Design 1, but select a very thin airfoil (`naca_t=8`). This is close to the minimum allowable thickness, aiming to find an optimal balance between lift and drag components.
*   **Explore Moderate Positive Dihedral**: The best design had slight negative dihedral, while some lower-performing designs explored positive. To diversify, we will set `dihedral` to 10.0. This combination with very high sweep and minimal twist will explore lateral stability characteristics in an unexamined region of the design space.
