The "Novel Design Strategy" encourages a bold exploration of the design space using aerodynamic first principles and judgment, rather than incrementally modifying previous designs. My reasoning focuses on exploring a regime potentially far from a generic baseline to uncover new high-reward regions.

*   **Explore Ultra-High Sweep:** A leading-edge sweep of 78.0 degrees is chosen to push towards the upper limit of the parameter space. This aims to maximize the strength and stability of leading-edge vortices, which are crucial for high-lift generation in delta wings, potentially leading to higher CL values and novel CL/CDi characteristics in this extreme regime.
*   **Aggressive Washout for Load Control:** A strong wash-out is introduced with `twist_root` at 6.0 degrees and `twist_tip` at -9.0 degrees. This creates a significant twist gradient designed to redistribute lift efficiently across the span, reduce induced drag by achieving a more elliptical-like load distribution, and potentially mitigate tip stall, crucial for high-sweep designs.
*   **Significant Camber for Lift Efficiency:** A NACA 44xx airfoil (m=4, p=4) is chosen to introduce substantial camber. This is hypothesized to increase lift at a given angle of attack, thereby potentially improving CL/CDi by generating more lift for the same induced drag, or allowing the wing to fly at a lower angle of attack for the same lift, reducing profile drag.
*   **Investigate Anhedral:** A negative dihedral (anhedral) of -12.0 degrees is selected. For highly swept wings, inherent stability can be strong. Anhedral can reduce the effective dihedral effect, which might be beneficial for agility and maneuvering, exploring a different aspect of performance that could indirectly contribute to CL/CDi through better trim or control.
*   **Moderate Thickness & Large Root Chord:** A `naca_t` of 14 is a reasonable moderate thickness, providing a balance between structural feasibility and minimizing profile drag. A `root_chord_in` of 45.0 inches ensures a substantial wing area, complementing the high sweep and camber to maximize total lift generation potential. This combination aims to provide a large, highly-swept, cambered lifting surface.
