*   **Bold Exploration of High Sweep:** To advance the population beyond the current best, I will explore a significantly higher leading-edge sweep (`le_sweep`) in the upper range (e.g., 78 degrees). This aims to capitalize on enhanced vortex lift phenomena, which are characteristic of high-performing delta wings, and see if the CL/CDi peak shifts further into the high sweep regime.
*   **Maximized Camber for Intrinsic Lift:** The drastic performance difference between NACA 44xx and NACA 00xx/24xx airfoils confirms that camber is critical. I will maintain `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4` to ensure a strong baseline for inherent lift, which complements the vortex lift.
*   **Aggressive Washout Distribution:** To effectively manage the lift distribution and control vortex formation at higher sweep, I will apply the maximum possible negative twist at the root (`twist_root=-10.0`) and a strong negative twist at the tip (`twist_tip=-7.0`). This aims for a more pronounced unloading towards the tip, which can improve vortex stability and prevent premature stall.
*   **Reduced Airfoil Thickness:** To minimize profile drag and explore its impact on the CL/CDi ratio, I will select a thinner airfoil (`naca_t=8`). This is a bold departure from Design 1's `naca_t=12` and aims to find a new optimum where reduced drag outweighs any potential loss in structural stiffness or minor adverse effects.
*   **Moderate Root Chord Adjustment:** I will use a slightly smaller root chord than Design 1 (e.g., 28 inches). This aims to reduce the overall wing area and wetted surface, potentially decreasing total drag, while relying on the aggressive sweep, camber, and twist to maintain a high lift coefficient and CL/CDi.
*   **Neutral Dihedral:** Dihedral primarily affects lateral stability, which is not the direct objective. I will set it close to neutral (e.g., 2.0 degrees) to allow other parameters to dominate the CL/CDi objective without strong influence from lateral stability characteristics.
