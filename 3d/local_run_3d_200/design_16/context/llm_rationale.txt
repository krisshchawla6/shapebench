Following the **Exploitation Strategy**, my goal is to refine and combine the best characteristics observed in the high-reward designs to maximize the reward.

*   **Reinforce Camber (NACA 44xx)**: Design 1 (reward 20.27) uses NACA 4413, and other high-ranking designs use NACA 40xx. The consistent use of `naca_m=4` in top designs is a clear indicator of its benefit. I will use `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4` to leverage the most successful camber profile.
*   **Intensify Washout**: All high-reward designs exhibit significant negative twist (washout). Design 1 has strong washout at both root and tip (-8.77, -7.02), while Design 2 has max tip washout (-10.0). I will aim for robust washout, pushing `twist_root` further negative towards its limit and keeping `twist_tip` strongly negative to ensure an efficient spanwise lift distribution.
*   **Moderate-High Sweep & Moderate Chord**: Design 1 has a sweep of 63.8, while Designs 2 and 5 are closer to 74. I will choose a sweep in the upper-moderate range (e.g., ~69-70 degrees) to capitalize on vortex lift without significantly increasing induced drag. I will pair this with a moderate `root_chord_in` (e.g., ~35 inches), slightly larger than Design 1, to provide ample wing area.
*   **Optimized Thickness**: Design 1 has `naca_t=13`. Designs 2 and 4 use 9 and 15 respectively. To slightly reduce profile drag while maintaining a robust airfoil, I will opt for `naca_t=11`, which is within the range of high performers and slightly thinner than the top design.
*   **Slight Positive Dihedral**: Design 1 shows a slightly positive dihedral (1.06). While other good designs have negative dihedral, maintaining a slightly positive dihedral in line with the top performer might contribute to a favorable stability characteristic without penalizing CL/CDi. I will select a small positive dihedral.
