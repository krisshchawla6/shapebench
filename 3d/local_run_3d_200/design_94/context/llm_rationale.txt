*   **Novel Design Strategy - Pushing Sweep**: The current top design (Design 1) has a sweep of 65.5. To explore novelty and potentially higher performance, I will push the `le_sweep` significantly higher to 70.0 degrees. This aims to maximize the strength and stability of the leading-edge vortices, a primary source of lift for delta wings, testing a region not as extensively explored by the higher-reward designs.
*   **Balanced Washout for High Sweep**: With increased sweep, careful management of twist is critical to maintain efficient spanwise loading and prevent premature vortex breakdown or excessive induced drag. I will implement a strong washout, `twist_root` at -9.0 and `twist_tip` at -7.0, slightly differing from Design 1's values but ensuring significant negative twist to distribute load effectively and improve tip behavior for the higher sweep.
*   **Moderate Camber and Reduced Thickness**: Design 1 utilized NACA 4412. To maintain a strong lift component while exploring alternatives, I'll specify NACA 2410. This maintains a moderate camber (`naca_m=2`) which is beneficial for lift but potentially reduces the pitching moment compared to m=4, and combines it with a slightly thinner airfoil (`naca_t=10`) than Design 1 to reduce profile drag for improved CL/CDi.
*   **Varied Root Chord for Scale Exploration**: Design 1 has a root chord of 34.7 inches. To ensure diversity and explore scaling effects, I will set `root_chord_in` to 30.0 inches. This slightly smaller wing might benefit from the reduced wetted area for drag while still being large enough to generate substantial lift with the high sweep and camber.
*   **Consistent Dihedral**: Most previous designs, including the best one, have a small negative dihedral. I will maintain this trend by setting `dihedral` to -2.5 degrees, as this parameter primarily influences stability and seems to have a less direct, but still relevant, aerodynamic impact.
