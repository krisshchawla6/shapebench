*   **Exploration Strategy - First Design:** Since no previous designs exist, the entire parameter space is "unexplored." This design will aim for a deliberate combination of parameters, pushing some towards the boundaries to gain initial insights into their impact on reward.
*   **High Sweep for Vortex Lift:** A high leading-edge sweep (e.g., 75.0 degrees) will be chosen to investigate the strong vortex lift characteristics inherent to highly swept delta wings. This will help establish a baseline for performance in this regime.
*   **Significant Washout for Load Distribution:** Implementing a notable washout (negative `twist_tip`, e.g., -5.0 degrees) while keeping `twist_root` slightly positive (e.g., 2.0 degrees) aims to achieve a more favorable spanwise lift distribution, potentially delaying tip stall and reducing induced drag compared to a untwisted wing.
*   **Non-Zero Camber:** Incorporating a NACA 4-digit airfoil with camber (`naca_m=2`, `naca_p=4`) will explore the effect of inherent lift generation from the airfoil shape, complementing the vortex lift and twist effects.
*   **Moderate Root Chord and Thickness:** A substantial `root_chord_in` (e.g., 40.0 inches) and moderate `naca_t` (e.g., 12) will ensure a reasonably sized and robust wing, avoiding extreme values that might lead to structural issues or excessive profile drag in this initial exploration.
*   **Positive Dihedral:** A slight positive `dihedral` (e.g., 5.0 degrees) will be included to provide a basic level of lateral stability, which is a common design consideration for aircraft.
