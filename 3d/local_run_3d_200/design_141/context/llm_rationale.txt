*   **Boldly explore higher sweep**: The most successful design (Design 1) has a sweep of ~65.5 degrees. To drive diversity and explore the upper bounds of vortex lift, I will increase the `le_sweep` significantly towards the upper end of the range, aiming for stronger, more stable leading-edge vortices.
*   **Investigate positive dihedral**: All previous top designs exhibit negative or near-zero dihedral. To explore a different region of the stability and aerodynamic performance landscape, I will set a notable positive `dihedral`. This is a bold departure that could reveal novel interactions with high sweep.
*   **Moderate twist gradient**: Instead of the aggressive washout seen in many previous designs (often with `twist_root` at -10.0), I will apply a more moderate washout, with both `twist_root` and `twist_tip` having smaller negative values. This aims to shift the lift distribution and see how it interacts with the higher sweep and positive dihedral.
*   **Varied Camber and Thickness**: Building on the success of cambered airfoils (NACA 4412, 2411), I will use a moderately cambered NACA 2410 airfoil (2% camber at 40% chord, 10% thickness). This slightly reduces camber and thickness compared to Design 1, aiming to balance lift generation with potential drag reduction, especially at higher sweep.
*   **Adjusted Root Chord**: To explore scaling effects and ensure the design isn't simply a re-scaling of previous successful wings, I will select a `root_chord_in` slightly smaller than the average of the previous designs.
