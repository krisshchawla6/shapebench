*   **Hybrid Twist Profile**: Designs 2 and 6, while having lower overall rewards than Design 1, both feature maximum washout at the root (-10.0 degrees). This aggressive root twist might be beneficial for load distribution or stall characteristics. The new design will adopt this maximum root washout, but pair it with a moderate tip washout, higher than Design 1 but less aggressive than Design 6, aiming to optimize lift distribution across the span.
*   **Moderate Increase in Sweep**: The top-performing designs have sweeps around 63-66 degrees. To explore stronger vortex formation and potentially higher lift without venturing into excessively high induced drag, the new design will incorporate a slightly higher sweep (e.g., 68.5 degrees) than Design 1, but still well within the beneficial range.
*   **Larger Root Chord**: Design 6 had a larger root chord (40.87 inches) and still achieved a good reward. A larger chord generally means a larger wing area, potentially increasing total lift. The new design will use a root chord slightly larger than Design 1 but less than Design 6, aiming for increased lift without significant penalties.
*   **Revisiting NACA 4414**: Design 3 used NACA 4414 but had a low reward, likely due to other parameter choices (very high sweep, aggressive twist, very negative dihedral). NACA 4414 offers 4% camber and 14% thickness. By combining this airfoil with the optimized sweep, twist, and chord derived from the top-performing designs, we aim to leverage its camber for lift in a more aerodynamically efficient configuration.
*   **Consistent Dihedral**: Most high-reward designs feature slightly negative or moderate anhedral. This suggests that a small amount of anhedral is either beneficial or at least not detrimental for performance (CL/CDi). A moderately negative dihedral will be chosen for consistency.
