*   **Aggressive Sweep Exploration:** The top design utilizes a sweep of ~65.5 degrees. To explore the design space and push the limits of vortex lift, I will significantly increase the leading-edge sweep to a much higher value (e.g., 72 degrees). This aims to create a stronger, more stable leading-edge vortex system, which could potentially yield even higher CL/CDi.
*   **Consistent Washout Application:** All high-performing designs (especially Design 1) exhibit significant negative twist (washout). This indicates its critical role in managing spanwise lift distribution and mitigating induced drag. I will maintain a strong washout, ensuring the tip is twisted down relative to the root, but with slightly different values than Design 1 to explore that subspace.
*   **Bold Symmetric Airfoil Test:** Design 1 uses a cambered airfoil (NACA 4412). To promote diversity and understand the pure contribution of geometry (sweep, twist) to vortex lift, I will select a symmetric airfoil (NACA 00XX). If a symmetric airfoil can achieve high performance with optimized geometry, it offers a simpler and potentially more robust solution.
*   **Reduced Thickness for Drag Reduction:** With a symmetric airfoil, minimizing form drag becomes even more crucial. I will choose a thinner airfoil (e.g., 10% thickness) compared to Design 1 (12%) to potentially reduce profile drag.
*   **Explore Positive Dihedral:** Design 1 has a slight anhedral. To diversify and investigate its effect on lateral stability and potential interplay with induced drag in a high-sweep, symmetric airfoil context, I will introduce a moderate positive dihedral.
*   **Moderate Root Chord:** The root chord will be kept within a similar range to the top designs (around 35-40 inches) to maintain a comparable scale for aerodynamic assessment.
