*   **Bold Exploration of High Sweep and Zero Camber:** The current top design utilizes moderate-high sweep with significant camber (NACA 4412). To diversify and explore a different aerodynamic strategy, this design will push towards very high sweep (76.5 degrees), aiming to maximize vortex lift. Combined with a zero-camber airfoil (NACA 00XX), this configuration tests the hypothesis that relying solely on vortex lift for high sweep delta wings can yield superior CL/CDi by minimizing geometric camber-induced drag.
*   **Aggressive Tip Washout for Load Distribution:** Design 1 used root-dominant washout. This design will implement an aggressive maximum tip washout (`twist_tip` = -10.0 deg) with a more moderate root washout (`twist_root` = -3.0 deg). This configuration aims to strongly unload the wing tips, which can significantly reduce induced drag by creating a more elliptical-like spanwise lift distribution under vortex flow conditions, and delay tip stall.
*   **Thin Profile for Drag Reduction:** By selecting a NACA 0010 airfoil, we are pairing zero camber with a relatively thin profile. This choice is intended to minimize profile drag, which becomes a more dominant factor once vortex lift is optimized, especially at higher speeds. This complements the high sweep and zero camber strategy.
*   **Moderate Root Chord:** A `root_chord_in` of 30.0 inches provides a reasonably sized wing, slightly smaller than the top performers. This aims to explore the scaling effects in this high-sweep configuration without being excessively large or small.
*   **Moderate Positive Dihedral Exploration:** Unlike the top performing designs that have negative or near-zero dihedral, this design incorporates a moderate positive dihedral (5.0 degrees). This explores a less common configuration in the high-reward space to see its impact on CL/CDi, potentially revealing new design synergies or stability advantages without a significant drag penalty.
