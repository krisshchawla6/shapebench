*   **Exploration Strategy: Target High Sweep Boundary**: Previous designs (D2, D5, D6) ventured into the 73-74 degree sweep range but with significantly lower rewards. This indicates that this region is not inherently bad, but rather poorly optimized. We will push further to the upper boundary of `le_sweep` (e.g., 78-80 deg) to thoroughly explore its potential for strong vortex lift when combined with novel parameters.
*   **Exploration Strategy: Novel Twist Distribution**: The highest-performing designs heavily rely on a `twist_root` value of -10.0 degrees. This region is over-explored. To diversify, we will try a positive `twist_root` (e.g., 5.0 degrees) combined with a highly negative `twist_tip` (-10.0 degrees). This configuration will provide significant washout (`twist_tip < twist_root`), a conventional aerodynamic approach to improve spanwise load distribution and reduce induced drag, which is a significant departure from the current successful designs.
*   **Exploration Strategy: High Thickness**: The `naca_t` parameter has mostly been explored in the 6-15% range. We will explore the upper bound (e.g., 22%) to understand how increased thickness affects performance, particularly in conjunction with very high sweep and the new twist strategy. This also has implications for potential structural benefits.
*   **Exploration Strategy: Large Root Chord**: While `root_chord_in` has been varied, going towards the upper bound (e.g., 48 inches) with the other extreme parameters will explore the scaling effects of a larger wing area on CL/CDi, potentially leveraging the strong vortex lift from high sweep more effectively.
*   **Camber Selection for High Sweep/Novel Twist**: Given the high sweep and the goal of high CL/CDi, providing a strong camber (`naca_m=4, naca_p=4`) should help generate more lift, potentially complementing the novel twist distribution and high sweep in maintaining efficient flow.
*   **Dihedral for Stability**: A neutral or slightly positive dihedral (e.g., 2.0 degrees) will be chosen to avoid the extreme anhedral/dihedral observed in some lower-performing designs, providing a stable baseline for assessing the impact of the other bold parameter choices.
