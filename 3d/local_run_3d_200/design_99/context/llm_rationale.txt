*   **Explore Low Sweep:** The optimal sweep range appears to be 63-66 degrees. However, the entire range from 45-60 degrees is largely unexplored, with no designs testing this region. I will target a low-to-moderate sweep angle (e.g., ~50 degrees) to understand its impact on vortex formation and lift for delta wings. This is a significant departure from the high-performing designs.
*   **Push High Root Chord:** High root chords (up to 40.9 inches in D5) have shown good performance. To compensate for potentially lower vortex lift at lower sweep, a very high root chord (e.g., near the 50-inch maximum) will maximize wing area and thus potential for lift.
*   **Test Symmetric Airfoil (`naca_m=0`, `naca_p=0`):** All high-performing designs use cambered airfoils. It's crucial to explore if a symmetric airfoil can achieve high CL/CDi when combined with other beneficial geometric parameters. This represents a significant exploration of the airfoil shape parameter space.
*   **Explore Positive Dihedral:** The top designs consistently use anhedral. To explore the full design space for lateral stability and induced drag, I will set a moderate positive dihedral. This combination (low sweep, high chord, symmetric airfoil, positive dihedral) is radically different from anything seen in the high-reward population.
*   **Moderate Washout:** While aggressive washout is favored, I will use a moderate washout (e.g., `twist_root=-5`, `twist_tip=-8`) to allow for a more conventional load distribution while still shedding tip vortices effectively, avoiding the most extreme values seen in the top designs.
*   **Moderate Thickness:** Maintain a moderate thickness (`naca_t=14`), as extreme values (low or high) have not been strongly correlated with high rewards. This provides a balanced airfoil without excessive drag from thickness.
