The goal is to generate a diverse, novel design by exploring under-explored regions of the parameter space, with an emphasis on bold exploration over incremental tweaking.

*   **Exploration of Minimum Thickness with High Camber:** The top-performing design (Design 2, NACA 4412) utilized high camber (m=4) and relatively low thickness (t=12). Designs with minimal thickness have been poorly explored. This new design will push the boundary by combining the highest camber (m=4) with the *absolute minimum allowed thickness* (naca_t=6). This extreme combination will test the aerodynamic limits of a very thin, highly cambered delta wing, expecting significant drag reduction and efficient lift.
*   **Investigation of Moderate Sweep for Efficiency:** While high sweep generates strong vortices, it also increases induced drag. The current population has designs spanning 51.2 to 72.9 degrees. Design 2 performed best at 65.5. We will explore a more moderate sweep, specifically `le_sweep=57.0`, a region that has yielded low reward for uncambered designs (Design 4, NACA 0011) but has not been tested with aggressive camber and minimal thickness. This aims to find an optimal balance for CL/CDi by reducing induced drag.
*   **Novel Twist Distribution: Positive Root Twist:** All existing successful designs employ washout (negative twist at root and tip). This design will boldly explore the effect of `twist_root=2.0` (a positive twist or washin at the root) combined with a more typical `twist_tip=-5.0` (washout at the tip). This configuration is largely untried and could significantly alter the spanwise lift distribution, potentially leading to novel vortex interactions or improved load distribution over the inboard sections.
*   **Extreme Positive Dihedral Exploration:** The current population primarily uses near-zero or slightly negative dihedral. This design will explore a significant positive dihedral, `dihedral=12.0`, to test its impact on lateral stability and induced drag characteristics in combination with a high-camber, thin delta wing. This pushes a boundary condition that has not been explored in the context of successful cambered designs.
*   **Increased Root Chord:** To allow for a larger overall wing area while exploring other parameters, and to diversify from the clustered root chord values (mostly 29-37 inches), `root_chord_in=40.0` will be selected. This is towards the upper end of the allowed range and provides a larger scale to observe the effects of the other aggressive parameter choices.
