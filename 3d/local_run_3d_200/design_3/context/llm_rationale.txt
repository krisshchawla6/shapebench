*   **Initial Exploration Strategy:** As this is the first design, the goal is to establish a diverse baseline. I will select a combination of moderate and somewhat aggressive parameters to quickly populate the design space and provide a good starting point for the optimizer.
*   **High Sweep for Vortex Lift:** A `le_sweep` of 70.0 degrees is chosen to explore the benefits of strong vortex lift characteristic of delta wings, providing a potentially high-lift configuration.
*   **Moderate Root Chord:** A `root_chord_in` of 30.0 inches places the wing size in the middle of the range, providing a reasonably sized reference for performance evaluation without being overly large or small.
*   **Washout for Load Distribution:** A `twist_root` of 2.0 degrees and `twist_tip` of -4.0 degrees creates a net washout, which is expected to improve spanwise load distribution, delay tip stall, and potentially reduce induced drag compared to a uniformly twisted or untwisted wing.
*   **Slight Positive Dihedral:** A `dihedral` of 5.0 degrees is introduced to provide some positive lateral stability, a common design feature to improve handling qualities.
*   **Moderate Camber and Thickness:** `naca_m = 2` and `naca_p = 4` introduce a moderate amount of camber, which can enhance lift coefficient at lower angles of attack. `naca_t = 12` provides a reasonable thickness for structural integrity while keeping drag in check.
