All high-performing designs feature camber (`naca_m=2` or `4`). This new design will strategically test a NACA 00xx profile to assess if strong vortex lift from a delta wing can compensate for the lack of camber, potentially reducing drag and improving CL/CDi.

This pushes beyond the prevalent 63-67 degree range observed in the top designs, aiming for a different vortex lift regime to maximize its benefits with a symmetric profile.

This provides a clear washout while using a less aggressively negative `twist_root` than observed in many previous designs, offering a distinct twist characteristic.

This provides a balance, not being excessively thin (which might compromise structural integrity) or thick (which could increase profile drag), and is within a reasonable range but slightly higher than past top designs.
