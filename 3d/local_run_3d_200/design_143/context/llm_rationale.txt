*   **Exploit Optimal Sweep Range:** Designs 1, 2, and 3, which are among the better performers, share similar lead-edge sweep values around 63-65 degrees. Design 6 with a much higher sweep (72.9 deg) performed poorly. This indicates an optimal sweep range exists within the mid-60s. I will slightly increase the sweep from Design 1's 65.5 to 66.5 degrees to explore the upper end of this successful range, potentially enhancing vortex lift benefits.
*   **Refine Washout Distribution:** Design 1's moderate washout (`twist_root=-7.1`, `twist_tip=-4.6`) was highly effective. Designs 2 and 3 employed more aggressive root washout (e.g., -10.0 deg) but yielded significantly lower rewards. This suggests that while washout is beneficial, its magnitude and distribution are critical. I will apply a slightly more aggressive but still balanced washout than Design 1, setting `twist_root` to -8.0 deg and `twist_tip` to -3.5 deg, ensuring the root still has a stronger negative twist.
*   **Maintain Beneficial Anhedral:** All top-performing designs (D1, D2, D3) utilized negative dihedral (anhedral). Design 1's slight anhedral (-1.7 deg) was part of the best-performing configuration. I will slightly increase the anhedral to -2.5 degrees to explore if a marginally greater anhedral provides further benefits in stability or induced drag reduction without adverse effects.
*   **Optimize Cambered Airfoil and Thickness:** The NACA 4412 airfoil (m=4, p=4, t=12) of Design 1 proved superior. Symmetric airfoils (D6: NACA 0018) performed poorly. Therefore, I will retain the `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4` configuration to leverage cambered lift. To explore drag reduction, I will slightly decrease the thickness from 12 to 10 percent, potentially lowering profile drag while aiming to maintain the high CL/CDi ratio.
*   **Adjust Root Chord for Scale:** Design 1's root chord of 34.7 inches performed very well. While larger root chords were seen in some poorer performing designs, a slight reduction might be beneficial. I will set the root chord to 32.0 inches, slightly smaller than Design 1, to investigate if a slightly smaller overall wing scale can improve the CL/CDi by reducing wetted area and associated parasitic drag, while still benefiting from the refined aerodynamic shaping.
