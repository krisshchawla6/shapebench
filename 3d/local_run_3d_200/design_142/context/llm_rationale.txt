This synthesis will explore a potentially superior parameter space.

*   **Optimize Aggressive Washout Distribution**: Implement a strong negative twist at the root (`twist_root` = -9.0 deg), drawing from the aggressive washout of Designs 2 and 3. This will be paired with a significant, but slightly less aggressive, negative twist at the tip (`twist_tip` = -6.0 deg) to maintain the beneficial washout pattern observed in Design 1 (more washout at root than tip) for optimal spanwise lift distribution and vortex stability.
*   **Explore NACA 40xx Profile for Camber Benefits**: Utilize a NACA 4010 airfoil (`naca_m=4, naca_p=0, naca_t=10`). This configuration places maximum camber very close to the leading edge, potentially enhancing initial lift generation and aiding in the development of stable leading-edge vortices, while the 10% thickness aims for a good balance between structural integrity and reduced profile drag compared to D1's 12% or D3's 16%.
*   **Adjust Root Chord for Scale Effects**: Increase the `root_chord_in` to 38.5 inches, an intermediate value between Design 1 (34.78) and Design 3 (40.86). This explores potential Reynolds number benefits and optimal scaling effects on the overall wing performance, aiming for a sweet spot in size.
*   **Maintain Near-Neutral Dihedral**: Set dihedral to -2.0 degrees, a slightly anhedral configuration close to Design 1. This parameter has shown minimal influence on CL/CDi in the top-performing designs, suggesting that its impact on the objective is secondary.
