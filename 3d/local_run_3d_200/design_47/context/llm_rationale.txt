*   **High Sweep Exploration:** Design 1 has a sweep of 65.5 degrees. I will push towards the higher end of the sweep range (e.g., 75-78 degrees). This is a bold move to see if an even stronger leading-edge vortex can be generated, potentially leading to higher lift coefficients that could outweigh an increase in induced drag, or if it leads to a less efficient design that still provides valuable data.
*   **Zero Camber with Increased Thickness:** The top designs utilize cambered airfoils (NACA 4412, 2411). To explore a novel region of the design space, I will specify a NACA 00XX airfoil (no camber). This simplifies the lift mechanism, making it primarily dependent on sweep, angle of attack, and twist. To compensate for the lack of camber lift and explore structural considerations, I will pair this with a relatively high thickness (e.g., 18-20%). (Note: `naca_m=0` requires `naca_p=0`).
*   **Aggressive Washout Distribution:** Design 1 uses a moderate washout. I will try a more aggressive washout at the root, pushing `twist_root` closer to its negative limit, and maintain a significant washout at the tip. This is intended to ensure stable flow and efficient lift distribution, especially with a high sweep and uncambered airfoil, preventing tip stall and potentially improving CL/CDi.
*   **Moderate Root Chord:** I will keep the root chord in the mid-range (30-40 inches), similar to the best-performing designs. This range seems effective for the simulation context, providing a reasonable scale for aerodynamic interactions.
*   **Moderate Positive Dihedral:** All top designs have very low magnitude dihedral. I will explore a moderate positive dihedral (tips up, e.g., +5 to +10 degrees). While its primary effect is on lateral stability, it might have subtle interactions with the vortex system of a highly swept, uncambered wing, providing valuable data on this less explored parameter combination.
