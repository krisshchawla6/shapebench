The representative design (Design 1, Reward: 4016.0090) features a moderate leading edge sweep (65.5 deg), a root chord of 34.8 inches, and a NACA 4412 airfoil. It has a mild negative root twist (-7.1 deg) and tip twist (-4.6 deg), resulting in a small washout. Dihedral is slightly negative (-1.7 deg). This configuration achieved the highest reward, suggesting that a moderate sweep, some camber (NACA 44xx), and a subtle washout distribution are effective.

Examining the population:
*   **Sweep**: Most successful designs are in the mid-60s range. Design 5 at 75.6 degrees had a low reward, suggesting extreme sweep might be challenging.
*   **Twist**: The highest reward design did not use the maximum negative root twist (-10.0), unlike many lower-performing designs. A moderate washout seems beneficial. The parameter space for positive twist is largely unexplored.
*   **Dihedral**: The top design has a slightly negative dihedral. Design 4 with positive dihedral had a very low reward. The extremes of positive and negative dihedral are not well understood in combination with other parameters.
*   **Airfoil**: NACA 44xx series seems promising. NACA 24xx and 40xx yielded lower rewards. The `naca_m=0` (symmetric) option, which also implies `naca_p=0`, is completely unexplored.
*   **Root Chord**: Values around 30-40 inches are common. The lower and upper bounds are largely unexplored.
*   **Thickness**: `naca_t` values are mostly in the mid-range (7-16). The thinner (6) and thicker (17-24) ends are less explored.

Key design changes needed to improve designs in the population, focusing on exploration:
1.  **Explore symmetric airfoils (`naca_m=0, naca_p=0`)**: This tests a fundamentally different lift generation mechanism, relying less on camber and more on incidence and vortex lift, especially at high sweep.
2.  **Investigate extreme sweep angles**: The highest reward is at moderate sweep. Pushing to the upper boundary (80 deg) could reveal new performance plateaus or limitations.
3.  **Test positive twist distributions (wash-in or zero twist)**: All previous designs have negative twist, primarily washout. Positive twist or neutral twist could redistribute load differently, especially with a symmetric airfoil.
4.  **Explore extreme positive dihedral**: Most designs have negative or near-zero dihedral. Max positive dihedral could impact stability and induced drag in novel ways when combined with other extreme parameters.
