Based on the exploration strategy and the analysis of the current population:

*   **Push `le_sweep` to the upper boundary (78.0 deg):** While mid-60s sweep performed best, this design will aggressively explore the very high sweep regime, which has only been sparsely explored with other suboptimal parameters. This tests if very high sweep can be viable with different twists/airfoils.
*   **Select a low `root_chord_in` (15.0 inches):** Combined with high sweep, this creates a very slender, high aspect ratio-like delta wing. Most previous designs had mid-range chords; exploring the lower boundary will test if a smaller, lighter wing can achieve efficiency.
*   **Explore `positive twist` at the root (5.0 deg `twist_root`) and `zero twist` at the tip (0.0 deg `twist_tip`):** This is a significant deviation from successful designs (which all had washout) and is a critical unexplored region. Positive twist at the root might redistribute lift inboard, and zero tip twist avoids negative loading, pushing towards a very different lift distribution.
*   **Introduce slight `positive dihedral` (2.0 deg):** Most successful designs had slightly negative dihedral. A small positive value explores this region without going to the extreme seen in Design 4 (15.0 deg) which performed poorly.
*   **Utilize NACA `2018` airfoil (m=2, p=0, t=18):** This explores the `naca_m=2` option (only seen once in D5) with `naca_p=0`, a less common `p` value when `m > 0`. A much thicker airfoil (`naca_t=18`) also explores the higher end of the thickness range, which is mostly untested. This could offer structural benefits or different aerodynamic characteristics at this extreme geometry.
