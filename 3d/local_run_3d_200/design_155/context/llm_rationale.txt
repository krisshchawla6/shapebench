*   **Novel Sweep Exploration**: The top design has a sweep of 65.5 degrees. Lower sweeps generally reduce induced drag but might reduce vortex lift strength. I will explore a slightly lower sweep of 60.0 degrees to investigate this trade-off, aiming for a different balance of vortex lift and induced drag than the best-performing design.
*   **Increased Root Chord for Scale Effects**: Most previous designs cluster around a root chord of 30-40 inches. I will explore a larger root chord of 45.0 inches to assess the impact of increased Reynolds number and overall scale on CL/CDi, potentially reducing viscous drag components relative to lift.
*   **Reduced Camber with Tailored Twist**: Design 1's NACA 4412 showed success. I'll explore NACA 2409, which has less camber (2% vs 4%) and is thinner (9% vs 12%). This tests if a more subtle airfoil design, when combined with an optimized twist, can still achieve high efficiency or potentially reduce form drag.
*   **Alternative Washout Strategy**: Design 1 featured `twist_root < twist_tip` (root more negative). I will explore a "traditional" washout strategy where the tip is more negative than the root: `twist_root = -3.0` and `twist_tip = -6.0`. This will assess if distributing the washout towards the tip can more effectively manage tip vortices and improve overall lift distribution.
*   **Slight Positive Dihedral**: Extreme dihedral values were detrimental. Design 1 had slight negative dihedral. To broaden exploration, I'll use a very slight positive dihedral of 1.0 degrees. This explores the effect on lateral stability in a region largely unexplored by high-reward designs, away from the detrimental extremes.
