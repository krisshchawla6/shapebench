*   **Explore Higher Sweep for Enhanced Vortex Lift**: The best designs have sweep around 65 degrees. To push the envelope for novel designs, I will explore a higher sweep angle (e.g., 72.0 degrees). This aims to generate stronger and more stable leading-edge vortices, potentially increasing CL at higher angles of attack, while acknowledging the potential for increased induced drag.
*   **Maximize Root Washout for Efficient Lift Distribution**: Consistent with top designs, significant washout is crucial. I will set `twist_root` to its maximum negative value (-10.0 degrees) to ensure the root carries its load effectively and contribute to a more efficient spanwise lift distribution, delaying separation.
*   **Maintain Moderate Tip Washout for Vortex Control**: While the root is aggressively twisted, the `twist_tip` will be set to a less extreme negative value (-6.0 degrees). This provides a substantial positive washout relative to the root, which is effective for managing the vortex system and maintaining control near the wingtips, potentially reducing induced drag.
*   **Introduce Stronger Anhedral for Stability/Performance Balance**: Anhedral is a recurring feature in successful designs. I will push `dihedral` to a more pronounced negative value (-12.0 degrees). This choice aims to further explore its impact on lateral stability and its potential influence on induced drag for highly swept planforms.
*   **Utilize a Cambered Airfoil with Moderate Thickness**: Cambered airfoils with `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4` have shown strong performance. I will select `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4` for effective lift generation. For `naca_t`, I will use a slightly thinner value (10) compared to Design 1 (12) to reduce profile drag while maintaining structural viability and aerodynamic benefit.
*   **Moderate Root Chord for Balanced Area**: A root chord of 40.0 inches provides a substantial wing area, balancing the increased lift potential from higher sweep and camber without making the wing excessively large, which would increase wetted area and overall drag.
