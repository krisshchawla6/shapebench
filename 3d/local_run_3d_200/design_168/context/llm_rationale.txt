*   **Explore higher sweep with aggressive tip washout**: Design 1 has a moderate sweep and its twist distribution is somewhat unique (less tip washout than root). We will push to a higher sweep (e.g., 72 degrees) to enhance vortex lift potential, but pair it with a strong tip washout (maximum negative twist_tip) to explore if this combination can efficiently manage induced drag and improve lift distribution by unloading the tip. This diverges from Design 1's twist pattern.
*   **Introduce moderate camber with 2% max camber (NACA 24xx)**: Design 1 used NACA 4412. To diversify, we will use a NACA 24xx profile (e.g., NACA 2410). This provides some camber for lift at lower angles of attack while potentially having lower drag penalties than a 4% camber profile. The goal is to see how this interacts with higher sweep and aggressive washout.
*   **Evaluate impact of maximum positive dihedral**: All previous high-reward designs feature negative or near-zero dihedral. To explore diversity and understand the aerodynamic trade-offs, we will introduce the maximum positive dihedral (15 degrees). While potentially detrimental to CL/CDi due to increased induced drag or stability issues, it provides valuable information on the boundaries of the design space.
*   **Increase root chord to a higher value**: Most prior designs are in the 30-40 inch root chord range. We will explore a larger root chord (e.g., 45 inches) to see if increased wing area can contribute to higher overall lift, assuming the other parameters effectively manage the drag. This is a bold exploration to test scalability.
*   **Mid-range thickness**: Maintaining a mid-range thickness (e.g., 10%) with the chosen NACA 24xx profile should offer a balance between structural feasibility and aerodynamic performance, without pushing to extremes of very thin or very thick airfoils, allowing us to isolate the effects of sweep, twist, and camber more clearly.
