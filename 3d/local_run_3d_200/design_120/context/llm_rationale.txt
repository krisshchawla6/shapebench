*   **Explore lower sweep boundary:** The current high-reward designs are clustered in the mid-60s for sweep. We will push towards the lower end of the `le_sweep` range (45-80 deg) by setting it to 50.0 degrees. This will significantly alter the vortex formation characteristics and allow us to assess performance in a less explored region, aiming for a different lift generation mechanism potentially less reliant on strong vortex lift for a high CL/CDi ratio.
*   **Investigate novel twist distribution:** Previous high-reward designs utilized negative twist at both root and tip, with the root more twisted. To explore a distinct aerodynamic strategy, we will try a moderate negative `twist_root` (-3.0 deg) combined with a slightly positive `twist_tip` (2.0 deg). This "wash-in" at the tip relative to the root could redistribute lift more towards the wing root, potentially reducing tip loading and induced drag at higher angles of attack. This is a very underexplored combination.
*   **Test symmetric airfoil with new geometry:** No high-performing designs have used a symmetric airfoil (`naca_m=0, naca_p=0`). We will explore this configuration in conjunction with the lower sweep and novel twist. This will help determine if camber is a critical component for high CL/CDi, or if a well-optimized planform and twist can achieve competitive results without it.
*   **Explore higher thickness:** The most successful designs had moderate thickness (11-12%). We will push towards the higher end of the `naca_t` range (20) to see if increased thickness, which often correlates with structural benefits, can be maintained without excessive drag penalties when combined with our exploratory sweep and twist parameters.
*   **Maintain adequate wing area:** To compensate for the lower sweep and ensure sufficient lift, we will use a moderately large `root_chord_in` of 45.0 inches, pushing towards the upper end of the explored range, but not the absolute maximum. This helps ensure the wing has enough area to generate lift effectively at the chosen sweep.
*   **Neutral dihedral:** We will select a small, slightly positive dihedral (2.0 deg). This stays close to the neutral position, avoiding the extreme positive and negative values that previously led to lower rewards, while slightly differentiating from the best design's negative dihedral.
