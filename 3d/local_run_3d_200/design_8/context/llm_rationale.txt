*   **Explore Camber:** The most critical unexplored region in the parameter space is the use of cambered airfoils. All prior designs were NACA 00xx. This design will introduce a moderate positive camber by setting `naca_m=2` and `naca_p=4`, aiming to significantly increase lift and improve overall CL/CDi by shifting the lift curve.
*   **Moderate-High Sweep:** To complement the new cambered airfoil, we will use a `le_sweep=68.0` degrees. This is a deliberate choice to explore a sweep higher than the best performing Design 1 (58.36 deg) but not as extreme as the poorly performing Designs 2 and 3, seeking an optimal balance between vortex lift and the benefits of the new camber.
*   **Novel Twist Distribution with Camber:** With camber introduced, the twist strategy needs re-evaluation. We will use a slight positive `twist_root=2.0` to engage the camber effectively at the wing root, and a moderate `twist_tip=-4.0` (washout) to control tip loading and potentially improve spanwise lift distribution, without being as extreme as Design 1's washout.
*   **High Positive Dihedral Exploration:** Previous designs kept dihedral near zero. This design will push towards the upper boundary with `dihedral=10.0` to thoroughly explore its impact on lateral stability and induced drag in conjunction with the new cambered and swept configuration.
*   **Increased Root Chord:** To provide a larger wing area for the cambered profile and maximize overall lift potential, `root_chord_in=45.0` will be used, exploring the higher end of the permissible range.
*   **Thinner Cambered Airfoil:** To minimize profile drag and enhance efficiency, a `naca_t=12` airfoil will be used. This is thinner than most previous designs, testing if a slimmer profile benefits the CL/CDi ratio for a cambered delta wing.
