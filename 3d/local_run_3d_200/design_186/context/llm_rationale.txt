*   **Bold Exploration of High Sweep:** Design 1 performed well with moderate-high sweep (65.5 deg). To explore a new, potentially higher-reward region, I will push to a significantly higher sweep angle (e.g., ~72-75 deg). This aims to maximize the strength of leading-edge vortices, which are a primary source of lift for delta wings, testing if the CL/CDi ratio can be improved further with stronger vortex flow.
*   **Novel Twist Distribution (Positive Root, Moderate Tip Washout):** Design 1 employs substantial washout. I will try a contrasting approach: a slight *positive* twist at the root and a moderate *washout* at the tip. This seeks to increase lift generation at the wing root while still controlling tip stall and ensuring a more efficient spanwise load distribution different from pure washout.
*   **Symmetric Airfoil (NACA 00XX):** The top designs utilize camber. To diversify the population and isolate the pure aerodynamic effects of sweep and twist on vortex lift, this design will use a symmetric airfoil (NACA 00XX). This allows evaluation of the intrinsic vortex lift capability without the confounding effects of camber.
*   **Moderate Thickness for Balance:** Paired with the symmetric airfoil, a moderate thickness (e.g., 12%) provides a balance between structural feasibility and minimizing profile drag, which is crucial for maximizing CL/CDi, especially at higher speeds potentially associated with stronger vortex lift.
*   **Explore Positive Dihedral:** All top existing designs exhibit negative dihedral. This new design will explore a moderate *positive* dihedral (e.g., 7-8 deg). This is a distinct departure that will test its effect on lateral stability and potential interactions with the high sweep and new twist distribution, diversifying the population's stability characteristics.
*   **Moderate Root Chord:** The root chord will be set at a moderate value (e.g., 28 inches), slightly smaller than Design 1, to explore a different overall scale and potentially influence effective aspect ratio (given taper=0), aiming for efficient lift generation at this new scale.
