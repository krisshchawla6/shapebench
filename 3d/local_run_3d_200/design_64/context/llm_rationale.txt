*   **Exploit Strong Camber**: The most prominent pattern in the high-reward designs (Design 1 & 2) is the use of cambered airfoils (NACA 4412, 2411). Designs with NACA 00xx airfoils consistently perform poorly. To maximize reward, this new design will explicitly use `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4` to provide significant positive camber, directly building on the success of Design 1.
*   **Optimize Washout Twist**: The top two designs both feature substantial washout (negative twist), with the root twist being more pronounced than the tip. I will set a strong negative `twist_root` (e.g., -9.0) and a less negative `twist_tip` (e.g., -4.0) to ensure effective lift distribution across the span, mitigating induced drag and preventing premature tip stall, which has proven effective.
*   **Refine Leading Edge Sweep**: The highest-performing designs cluster around `le_sweep` values of 63-65 degrees. I will slightly increase the `le_sweep` to 66.0 degrees, exploring the immediate upper bound of the current successful range, aiming to potentially enhance vortex lift without incurring excessive drag penalties.
*   **Maintain Medium-High Root Chord**: The root chord of 33-35 inches has yielded the best results. I will set `root_chord_in` to 36.5 inches, slightly above the current optimum, to evaluate if a marginally larger scale, while keeping the proportional geometry, can further improve performance by increasing wing area for lift.
*   **Moderate Thickness and Dihedral**: The top designs have `naca_t` around 11-12% and slightly negative dihedral. I will use `naca_t=12` for a balanced thickness and `dihedral=-2.0` degrees, maintaining these successful characteristics which likely contribute to structural integrity, acceptable drag, and lateral stability.
