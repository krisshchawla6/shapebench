*   **Explore High Sweep with Washout:** The top two designs (D1 and D4) both demonstrate the effectiveness of strong washout. Design 4 also highlights that very high sweep (77 deg) can be effective, even with a symmetric airfoil, likely due to dominant vortex lift. This new design will combine a moderately high sweep (67.5 deg) with significant washout to enhance vortex lift while attempting to maintain good lift distribution.
*   **Apply Strong Washout:** Following the pattern of D1 and D4, a strong washout from `twist_root` (slightly negative) to `twist_tip` (close to -10 deg) is chosen to optimize spanwise lift distribution and reduce induced drag. Specifically, `twist_root=-1.5` and `twist_tip=-9.5`.
*   **Introduce Moderate Camber:** While D4 performed well with a symmetric airfoil, D1 shows the benefit of camber (NACA 4007). This design will use a more moderate camber (NACA 2410) to provide some inherent lift without being overly aggressive, aiming for a balance between vortex lift and airfoil lift.
*   **Investigate Moderate Anhedral:** Design 1 featured significant anhedral (-8.79 deg). This new design will explore a moderate anhedral (-5.0 deg) to see its interaction with higher sweep and camber, potentially improving stability characteristics without excessively penalizing roll performance.
*   **Vary Root Chord and Thickness:** To promote diversity, a moderate `root_chord_in` (28.0 inches) is selected, different from the very small and very large chords in top designs. A moderate `naca_t` (10) is chosen for a reasonable balance between structural thickness and profile drag.
