*   **Bold Exploration of Lower Sweep:** All top-performing designs cluster at very high sweep angles (70-80 degrees). To ensure diversity and avoid local optima, this design will explore a significantly lower sweep angle, specifically 58.0 degrees. This challenges the assumption that only extreme sweep is optimal and investigates a different aerodynamic regime where conventional lift mechanisms and explicit camber might play a larger role.
*   **Enhanced Camber for Lift:** Given the lower sweep, the contribution from leading-edge vortices might be less dominant. To compensate and generate sufficient lift, we will employ a more aggressive cambered airfoil: NACA 4410. This increases the maximum camber (4%) compared to previously explored NACA 24xx profiles, aiming for higher lift at the design point.
*   **Positive Dihedral Investigation:** All high-reward designs in the population utilize maximum negative dihedral. This design will boldly explore the opposite extreme with a positive dihedral of 8.0 degrees. This will test the impact of positive dihedral on lateral stability and induced drag, aiming to broaden the understanding of this parameter's role.
*   **Targeted Washout Distribution:** With a lower sweep and cambered airfoil, a refined twist distribution is needed. We will use a moderate root washout (`twist_root = -5.0`) and a more aggressive tip washout (`twist_tip = -10.0`). This aims to reduce tip loading more effectively, mitigate tip stall tendencies, and optimize the spanwise lift distribution for lower induced drag in this new aerodynamic configuration.
*   **Increased Root Chord:** To maintain a substantial wing area and aspect ratio for the lower sweep configuration, the `root_chord_in` will be set to 38.0 inches. This is larger than the root chords of the current top designs, potentially improving overall lift potential and providing a larger platform for the chosen airfoil and twist.
*   **Moderate Thickness:** The chosen airfoil, NACA 4410, provides a moderate thickness percentage (10%). This is thin enough to avoid excessive form drag but thick enough to potentially support the camber and provide some structural depth, contrasting with the very thin 0006 airfoils of the top designs and the poorly performing 0010 at max sweep.
