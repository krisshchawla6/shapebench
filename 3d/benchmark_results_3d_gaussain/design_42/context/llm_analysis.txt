*   **Significant Washout (Negative Twist):** The extreme negative twist (washout) at both root and tip likely serves to shed the leading-edge vortices efficiently, distribute lift more favorably across the span, reduce induced drag, and potentially delay tip stall, especially with high sweep.
*   **Anhedral (Negative Dihedral):** The maximum anhedral (dihedral=-15.0) is a notable feature. For highly swept delta wings, anhedral can sometimes counteract excessive roll stability, or interact favorably with the vortex flow field to improve performance.
*   **Cambered, Thin Airfoil:** The NACA 24xx series provides camber which increases CL at zero angle of attack, while the 7% thickness minimizes profile drag.
