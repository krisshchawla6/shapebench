*   The top-performing designs, especially Design 1, consistently utilize high leading-edge sweep (around 70-71 degrees), significant washout (negative twist), and negative dihedral (anhedral). This combination strongly suggests a highly effective strategy for generating vortex lift and managing induced drag.
*   To achieve diversity and novel exploration rather than incremental tweaking, this new design will deliberately challenge two key extreme parameters from Design 1: dihedral and twist. By moving these to less extreme or even opposite values, we can test the robustness and necessity of the current optimal settings.
*   Specifically, we will transition from maximum anhedral (`dihedral=-15.0`) to a moderate positive dihedral (`dihedral=10.0`, tips up). This is a significant shift that directly explores the lateral stability and vortex interaction in a completely different configuration.
*   We will also moderate the extreme washout found in Design 1 (`twist_root=-10.0`, `twist_tip=-10.0`) to a milder, but still negative, twist distribution (`twist_root=-5.0`, `twist_tip=-5.0`). This tests if the same performance can be achieved with less aggressive load redistribution.
*   The high `le_sweep` and `root_chord_in` values that proved successful in top designs (around 70 degrees and 32 inches, respectively) will be retained to provide a foundation of known good performance while we vary other parameters.
*   The NACA 24xx airfoil series, with `naca_t=7`, has shown consistent positive results. We will maintain `naca_m=2`, `naca_p=4`, and `naca_t=7` to isolate the impact of the changes to twist and dihedral.
