Design 1, the top performer with a reward of 424.56, features a moderately high sweep (71.2 deg), a significant root chord (32.9 inches), maximum negative twist (washout) at both root and tip (-10.0 deg), maximum negative dihedral (anhedral) (-15.0 deg), and a NACA 2407 airfoil. The success of this design, along with Designs 2 and 4, suggests that strong washout and anhedral are key contributors to high CL/CDi. The NACA 24xx series (cambered) also appears to perform well, as does the very thin NACA 0006 (symmetric).

Issues/Observations from the top designs:
*   High sweep (~70-72 deg) coupled with strong washout seems effective for vortex lift and load distribution.
*   Maximum anhedral (-15 deg) consistently appears in higher-reward designs. Designs with less anhedral (e.g., Design 6, -6.6 deg dihedral) performed significantly worse, suggesting this parameter is critical.
*   Extreme sweep (Design 5, 80 deg) leads to poor performance, indicating an optimum exists within the sweep range.
*   All high-performing designs utilize significant washout (-10 deg at root and/or tip).

Key design changes needed to improve designs in the population by exploring new regions:
1.  Explore lower sweep angles to understand the trade-off with vortex lift and induced drag.
2.  Investigate positive twist (washin) or milder washout to understand its effect on load distribution.
3.  Test positive dihedral (tips up) to understand the impact on stability and induced drag, as previous high rewards are all anhedral.
4.  Explore the `naca_m=4` camber profile and thicker airfoils (`naca_t > 10`) to see if they offer different benefits.
