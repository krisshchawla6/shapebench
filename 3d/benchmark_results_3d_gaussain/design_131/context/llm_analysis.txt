The representative design (Design 1) with `le_sweep=71.2`, `root_chord_in=32.8`, `twist_root=-10.0`, `twist_tip=-10.0`, `dihedral=-15.0`, and `NACA 2407` achieved the highest reward of 424.56. Its Cp distribution shows strong negative pressure on the upper surface, particularly along the leading edge and over a significant portion of the wing, which is characteristic of robust vortex lift. The contours indicate a well-formed and stable leading-edge vortex that contributes significantly to lift generation. The extreme washout (both root and tip at -10 degrees) combined with significant anhedral (-15 degrees) seems to effectively manage the lift distribution and possibly delay vortex breakdown, contributing to a high CL/CDi. The NACA 2407 airfoil provides 2% camber, adding to the inherent lift without excessive thickness.

Key observations and potential areas for improvement for the population:
*   **Optimal Sweep**: The top designs range from ~70 to 76 degrees, with the highest reward at 71.2 degrees. Exploring slightly higher sweeps while maintaining strong washout could test the limits of vortex lift benefits.
*   **Twist Distribution**: Consistent strong washout (near -10 degrees) at either root or tip, or both, is a strong indicator of good performance. This is crucial for controlling spanwise load distribution and preventing premature tip stall.
*   **Dihedral**: Extreme anhedral (-15 degrees) consistently appears in top designs. This suggests it might be beneficial for highly swept wings, possibly by enhancing vortex stability or managing effective dihedral effect.
*   **Airfoil Camber/Thickness**: NACA 24xx (2% camber, 40% position) performs very well. Symmetric airfoils (NACA 00xx) also perform reasonably but generally yield lower rewards. NACA 44xx (4% camber, 40% position) had a very low reward, suggesting that too much camber might be detrimental, perhaps due to increased form or induced drag at the optimal angle of attack. Thicknesses from 6% to 8% are observed in good designs.
