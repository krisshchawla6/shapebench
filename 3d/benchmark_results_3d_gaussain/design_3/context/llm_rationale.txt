*   **Exploration of High Sweep:** To investigate the effects of strong vortex lift, a high leading-edge sweep of 75.0 degrees is chosen. This is towards the upper end of the parameter space, but not the absolute maximum, allowing for further exploration if this proves promising or too extreme.
*   **Moderate Camber for Baseline Lift:** A NACA 2412 airfoil is selected (naca_m=2, naca_p=4, naca_t=12). This introduces camber to generate inherent lift, providing a non-symmetric baseline, and the chosen thickness is moderate to minimize form drag while allowing for structural integrity. This combination explores the use of camber.
*   **Washout for Load Distribution:** A twist distribution with a neutral root (twist_root=0.0) and significant washout at the tip (twist_tip=-5.0) is applied. This aims to achieve a more elliptical lift distribution, reduce induced drag, and mitigate tip stall tendencies, which are common issues in highly swept wings.
*   **Moderate Root Chord and Dihedral:** A root chord of 35.0 inches is chosen to represent a reasonably sized wing, providing a good scale for initial aerodynamic evaluation. A slight positive dihedral of 5.0 degrees is included to provide some lateral stability, a common design feature, without being an extreme value.
*   **Setting an Exploratory Baseline:** This design combines a high-sweep, moderately cambered wing with washout, which is a common strategy for performance. It aims to generate good initial performance data for a specific, yet well-reasoned, region of the design space, providing a strong starting point for the evolutionary process.
