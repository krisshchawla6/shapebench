*   **Novel Sweep Exploration**: Instead of staying at the very high sweep of Design 1 (71.24 deg), I will explore a slightly lower, but still aggressive, sweep angle (e.g., 67.5 deg). This attempts to find a potential sweet spot where vortex lift is still significant, but induced drag might be slightly reduced compared to more extreme sweeps, diversifying the population's sweep range.
*   **Balanced Twist Distribution**: The -10.0 deg twist across the entire wing in Design 1 is extreme. I will adopt a more balanced approach: zero twist at the root (0.0 deg) to maintain good lift generation centrally, combined with a moderate washout at the tip (-7.0 deg) to effectively unload the tips, delay stall, and reduce induced drag without excessively reducing overall lift. This is a novel twist distribution compared to the current top designs.
*   **Investigate Positive Dihedral**: All high-reward designs show extreme anhedral. I will make a bold move to explore the opposite by introducing a moderate positive dihedral (8.0 deg). This is a significant departure and will help understand its impact on spanwise load distribution, effective aspect ratio, and the CL/CDi metric, potentially discovering a new performance regime.
*   **Higher Camber and Moderate Thickness Airfoil**: Design 1 used NACA 2407. I will explore a more aggressive camber profile, NACA 4412 (4% max camber, 40% chord position, 12% thickness). The increased camber aims for higher inherent lift, and the slightly increased thickness provides structural depth while remaining aerodynamically reasonable. This tests the hypothesis that higher camber might improve CL without an excessive CDi penalty.
*   **Moderate Root Chord**: I will set the root chord at 38.0 inches, slightly larger than the previous top designs. This provides a larger overall wing area to generate lift, which, when combined with the other parameters, might contribute to a higher CL while the other optimizations (sweep, twist, dihedral, airfoil) work to manage induced drag.
