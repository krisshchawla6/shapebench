The top-performing designs (Design 1, 2, 4) consistently feature a high leading-edge sweep (around 70-71 degrees), a significant negative twist (washout, often reaching -10 degrees at the tip), and a large negative dihedral (anhedral, typically -11 to -15 degrees). The root chord for these designs hovers around 31-33 inches.

The high sweep is crucial for generating stable leading-edge vortices, which contribute significantly to lift at higher angles of attack, a characteristic of delta wings. The aggressive washout helps to distribute the lift more evenly along the span, preventing premature tip stall and potentially improving the efficiency of the vortex lift by better controlling vortex attachment and reattachment. The anhedral configuration, while affecting lateral stability, might be complementary to the highly swept planform's aerodynamic characteristics.
