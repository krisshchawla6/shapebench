*   The current leading designs exhibit a strong clustering around high sweep, aggressive washout, and significant anhedral with a NACA 24XX airfoil. To foster diversity and avoid local optima, a novel design strategy is required.
*   I will explore a **moderate-high sweep** (e.g., ~62 degrees), significantly lower than the top designs' 70+ degrees, but still within the effective range for vortex lift. This aims to find a new optimum that might balance vortex lift with potentially lower induced drag at this sweep angle.
*   I will implement a **symmetric airfoil (NACA 00XX)**. This is a bold departure from the successful cambered NACA 24XX designs. It will test the hypothesis of whether camber is truly essential or if the delta wing's planform geometry (sweep, twist) is the dominant factor for CL/CDi performance, allowing for potentially simpler manufacturing.
*   I will apply **significant washout** at the tip (negative twist) to maintain good spanwise lift distribution and vortex control, as this is a consistent feature of well-performing delta wings. However, the root twist will be less extreme than the current top designs to explore a different load distribution.
*   The **dihedral will be negative but less extreme** than the current leading designs, aiming for an anhedral of around -7.5 degrees. This explores the parameter space for lateral stability and its interaction with CL/CDi without resorting to the maximum allowable value.
*   The **root chord will be slightly larger** than the current top designs, at 38 inches. This changes the overall scale and effective aspect ratio slightly, providing another variation to explore.
*   A **moderate thickness** for the airfoil (NACA 0012) will be used, providing adequate structural depth while being thicker than the thinnest top-performing designs, allowing for robustness.
