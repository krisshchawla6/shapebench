*   **Explore lower sweep with increased camber:** The current top designs are clustered at very high sweep (70-78 degrees). To promote diversity and discover alternative optimal regions, I will significantly reduce the `le_sweep` to a mid-range value (e.g., 62.0 degrees). To compensate for the potential reduction in vortex lift from lower sweep, I will couple this with a higher camber airfoil (NACA 44XX) to ensure sufficient lift generation.
*   **Maintain strong washout:** The consistent presence of maximum negative `twist_root` and `twist_tip` (-10.0 degrees) in high-reward designs strongly suggests its importance in achieving an efficient spanwise lift distribution and minimizing induced drag. This parameter will be kept at its extreme to support the new, lower-sweep, higher-camber configuration.
*   **Investigate moderate negative dihedral:** The current population heavily favors maximum negative `dihedral` (-15.0). To explore its sensitivity and effect with a different wing geometry, I will choose a less extreme, but still negative, dihedral value (e.g., -7.5 degrees). This will test if the extreme dihedral is universally optimal or specific to the current high-sweep, thin-airfoil designs.
*   **Explore a larger root chord:** Most top designs use root chords in the 27-35 inch range. I will explore a significantly larger `root_chord_in` (e.g., 42.0 inches) to see if scaling up the wing area can be beneficial when combined with the lower sweep and higher camber, potentially leading to higher lift coefficients.
*   **Select a moderately thick, high-camber airfoil:** I will use `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4` for maximum camber, pairing it with a moderate `naca_t=10`. This moves away from the very thin airfoils (06-08%) prevalent in the top designs, aiming for a different aerodynamic characteristic to complement the lower sweep and test a new part of the airfoil design space.
