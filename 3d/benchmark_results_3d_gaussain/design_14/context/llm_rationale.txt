*   **Exploration Strategy: Unexplored Airfoil Thickness**: The `naca_t` parameter has only been explored up to a value of 11. To truly understand its impact and potentially discover novel high-performing designs, we must push into the significantly thicker range. This design will test a `naca_t` of 18, a substantial departure from the existing population.
*   **Boundary Exploration: Extreme High Sweep**: High sweep angles (around 70 degrees) have shown promise (Design 1). To fully map this aspect of the design space, we will push `le_sweep` to the upper boundary (78.0 degrees), aiming to maximize vortex lift potential, especially when combined with a thicker airfoil.
*   **Novel Twist Distribution**: Previous successful designs often feature negative `twist_root` and strong `twist_tip` washout. This design will explore a combination of strong positive `twist_root` (8.0 degrees) with a significant `twist_tip` washout (-9.0 degrees). This creates a large twist differential, aiming to explore a new lift distribution strategy that might synergize with the high sweep and thicker airfoil.
*   **Boundary Exploration: Strong Positive Dihedral**: The `dihedral` parameter has mostly been in the negative to moderate positive range. We will explore a strong positive `dihedral` (12.0 degrees) to understand its effect on stability and potentially lift distribution and induced drag at this extreme.
*   **Maintain Camber for Lift**: To compensate for potentially higher drag associated with a thicker airfoil and high sweep, we will retain a cambered NACA profile (`naca_m=2, naca_p=4`) to ensure adequate lift generation.
*   **Moderate Root Chord**: We will select a `root_chord_in` of 35.0 inches, which is within the intermediate range of the explored designs, allowing us to isolate the effects of the more extreme parameter choices without introducing another highly novel variable.
