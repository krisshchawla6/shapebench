*   **Hybrid Strategy**: We will blend the highest-performing cambered configuration (Design 1: NACA 2407, sweep 71.2) with a successful higher-sweep, uncambered configuration (Design 3: NACA 0008, sweep 76.6). This aims to combine the lift benefits of camber with the enhanced vortex lift from higher sweep.
*   **Increased Sweep**: To leverage Design 3's higher sweep success, we will increase the `le_sweep` to 74.0 degrees. This is a step up from Design 1's 71.2 degrees, aiming for stronger vortex lift, but not as extreme as Design 3, allowing for a more nuanced exploration.
*   **Maximized Washout and Negative Dihedral**: The consistent high rewards for designs with `twist_root = -10.0`, `twist_tip = -10.0`, and `dihedral = -15.0` (seen in Design 1 and 3) strongly suggest these extreme values are critical for optimal lift distribution and stability. We will retain these values.
*   **Optimized Thin Cambered Airfoil**: Design 1 and 2 utilize NACA 24xx, showing camber is effective. Design 5, despite being uncambered, showed decent performance with NACA 0006, suggesting extreme thinness can be beneficial. We will combine these by using NACA 2406 (`naca_m=2`, `naca_p=4`, `naca_t=6`) to investigate if the thinnest possible cambered airfoil can further reduce induced drag and improve CL/CDi.
*   **Mid-Range Root Chord**: The best performing designs have `root_chord_in` around 30-33 inches. We will select `31.5` inches, which is within this sweet spot, providing a well-scaled wing that is slightly smaller than Design 1/2 but larger than Design 3.
*   **Novelty and Exploration**: This design combines a higher sweep than Design 1 with a thinner cambered airfoil, keeping the most successful twist and dihedral settings. This explores a new region of the design space, seeking to maximize CL/CDi by synergizing increased vortex lift with reduced drag from a thinner profile and optimized lift distribution.
