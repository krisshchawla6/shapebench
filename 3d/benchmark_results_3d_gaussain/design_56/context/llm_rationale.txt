*   **Exploiting High Sweep**: The two highest-reward designs show a strong preference for leading-edge sweep angles around 70-71 degrees. We will exploit this by selecting a sweep of 72.0 degrees, slightly above the current best, to explore if increased vortex strength can yield higher CL/CDi without significant induced drag penalties.
*   **Maximized Washout**: Design 1, the top performer, implemented maximum negative twist at both the root and tip (-10.0 degrees). This aggressive washout is a key characteristic of the highest-reward designs, strongly indicating its importance for effective lift distribution and delaying vortex breakdown. We will maintain this maximal washout to preserve this proven aerodynamic strategy.
*   **Extreme Negative Dihedral**: The consistent presence of significant negative dihedral (tips down) in the top designs, with Design 1 at the limit of -15.0 degrees, suggests this parameter positively influences performance, potentially by stabilizing vortices or optimizing the effective wing shape at high angles of attack. We will keep this parameter at the -15.0 degree limit.
*   **Refined Cambered Airfoil**: The NACA 2407 and 2408 airfoils were successful. To build upon this, we will continue with a cambered airfoil (NACA 24xx, specifically NACA 2409). This slightly increased thickness from 7% or 8% to 9% aims to investigate if a slightly more robust airfoil, while still thin, can improve aerodynamic efficiency under the high-lift conditions without incurring excessive drag.
*   **Consistent Root Chord**: The optimal designs consistently feature a root chord around 32-33 inches. We will choose 32.5 inches to remain within this proven range, which appears to provide an effective scale for the wing given the other high-performing geometric parameters.
*   **Exploitation through Refinement**: This design aims to exploit the successful parameter combinations observed in the best-performing designs by making small, informed adjustments to potentially enhance their already high reward. The focus is on refining existing high-performance characteristics rather than exploring radically new design territories.
