*   **Apply Novel Design Strategy:** The current high-reward designs cluster around very high sweep, maximum washout, and maximum negative dihedral. To generate a truly novel design, I will deliberately deviate from these extreme values to explore different aerodynamic strategies.
*   **Moderate Sweep for Drag Reduction:** Instead of the extreme 70+ degree sweep, a more moderate sweep around 60-65 degrees will be explored (`le_sweep=62.0`). This aims to reduce induced drag slightly while still benefiting from vortex lift, potentially finding a different optimal balance.
*   **Varied Twist for Load Distribution:** Rather than maximum washout, I will try a slight positive twist at the root (`twist_root=2.0`) combined with a moderate negative twist at the tip (`twist_tip=-5.0`). This aims to shift some lift towards the wing root while maintaining controlled vortex generation at the tip, exploring a novel lift distribution.
*   **Positive Dihedral Exploration:** All top designs feature strong negative dihedral. To explore the design space effectively, I will invert this trend and use a slightly positive dihedral (`dihedral=5.0`). This will test its impact on stability and performance in combination with the new sweep and twist.
*   **Higher Camber (NACA 44xx):** To potentially compensate for the slightly reduced sweep and less aggressive washout, I will opt for a higher camber profile (NACA 44xx, i.e., `naca_m=4`, `naca_p=4`). This aims to generate more lift at lower angles of attack.
*   **Moderate Thickness:** While still prioritizing low drag, I will select a slightly thicker NACA profile (`naca_t=10`) compared to the very thin ones (06-08%) in top designs. This offers a bit more structural depth while remaining aerodynamically efficient for this type of wing.
