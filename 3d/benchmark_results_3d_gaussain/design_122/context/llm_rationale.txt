The current strategy focuses on **Exploration**: identifying under-explored regions of the parameter space and probing combinations distinct from the existing population.

*   The existing high-reward designs are consistently concentrated in regions of extreme washout (negative twist) and extreme negative dihedral. To truly understand the full design landscape and uncover potential novel optima, we must **explore the opposite extremes: positive twist (wash-in) and positive dihedral (tips up)**. This new design will test if a completely different aerodynamic philosophy can achieve performance or at least map out regions of poor performance to better guide future searches.
*   While high sweep is prevalent in top designs, we will push the `le_sweep` even further, close to its **maximum boundary (77.5 degrees)**, to see if the benefits continue or if diminishing returns/penalties arise when combined with a radically different twist and dihedral setup.
*   The `root_chord_in` parameter has been explored in a relatively narrow mid-range. To investigate scale effects and the interaction with other boundary-pushing parameters, we will specify a **much larger root chord (45.0 inches)**, approaching the upper limit of the range.
*   The `naca_m=4` (maximum camber) has not been tested in any of the previous designs. To provide a comprehensive exploration, we will use **NACA 4420**: maximum camber (`naca_m=4`), camber positioned at 40% chord (`naca_p=4`), and a **thicker profile (`naca_t=20`)**. This aggressively cambered and thick airfoil, combined with the extreme geometric features, represents a highly novel point in the design space.
