*   To encourage diversity and explore new aerodynamic regimes, this design will deviate significantly from the high-performing designs' common strategy of uniform negative twist and negative dihedral.
*   We will maintain a high sweep angle of `75.0 deg`, pushing it slightly higher than the top performers but below the absolute maximum, as high sweep has consistently proven beneficial for vortex lift.
*   A novel twist distribution will be explored: `twist_root = 2.0` (slight wash-in) combined with `twist_tip = -10.0` (maximum washout). This aims to concentrate lift inboard to strengthen the primary vortex while still using tip washout to manage induced drag and prevent tip stall. This is a direct contrast to the established all-washout strategy.
*   A very bold step is to introduce a **positive dihedral** (`10.0 deg`). All top designs utilize negative dihedral, making this a high-risk, high-reward exploration to understand how a positive dihedral affects CL/CDi when combined with other high-performance features.
*   We will test a **higher camber NACA profile**, `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4`, with a moderate thickness `naca_t=8`. Previous designs with NACA 44xx profiles performed poorly, but they were not combined with the optimal sweep and twist configurations now being explored. This aims to unlock the potential benefits of increased camber.
*   A moderate root chord (`35.0 inches`) is selected to provide a substantial lifting surface, positioned slightly above the average of the current best performers, to accommodate the new lift distribution strategy.
