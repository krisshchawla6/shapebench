The top-performing design (Design 1) demonstrates excellent CL/CDi with a high sweep (71.2 deg), moderate root chord, maximum negative twist (washout) at both root and tip, maximum negative dihedral, and a cambered NACA 2407 airfoil. The Cp distribution from the image reveals strong, sustained leading-edge suction, characteristic of well-formed and stable vortex lift, which is key to delta wing performance. The pressure recovery appears smooth, indicating efficient flow. While this design is highly successful, the population exhibits a convergence around these parameters (high sweep, washout, negative dihedral, NACA 24xx), suggesting a need for broader exploration. Designs with very low sweep or maximum sweep perform significantly worse, as do those with symmetric airfoils, though Design 4 shows some promise with high sweep and a symmetric NACA 0008. The consistent use of maximum negative twist and dihedral across high-reward designs indicates these parameters are influential but may have unexplored optimal ranges or alternative beneficial combinations.

Key design changes needed to improve designs in the population:
1.  **Explore novel twist distributions**: Rather than uniform maximum washout, investigate asymmetric twist (e.g., wash-in at root, washout at tip) to redistribute lift and influence vortex development differently.
2.  **Test the impact of positive dihedral**: All high-performing designs feature negative dihedral. A bold exploration into positive dihedral is needed to understand its full effect on CL/CDi in combination with other parameters.
3.  **Re-evaluate higher camber profiles**: NACA 24xx performs well. Test NACA 44xx (higher camber) with optimized sweep and twist to see if its potential was previously masked by suboptimal overall geometry.
4.  **Refine sweep within promising range**: While 70-76 degrees is good, slight variations with significantly altered other parameters can help pinpoint precise optima or discover synergistic effects.
