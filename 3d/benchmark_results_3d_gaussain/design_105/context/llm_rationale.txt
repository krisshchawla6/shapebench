*   **Hybridizing Top Performers**: I will blend successful characteristics from Design 1 (highest reward, high sweep, aggressive twist, max negative dihedral), Design 3 (second highest, high sweep, differential twist, strong negative dihedral), and Design 6 (symmetric airfoil, high sweep, aggressive twist, max negative dihedral).
*   **Pushing High Sweep**: All top designs exhibit high sweep (69.8-71.2 deg). I will increase the leading-edge sweep further to 75.0 degrees, a value not yet explored by the top designs, to potentially unlock even stronger and more stable vortex lift characteristics.
*   **Refined Differential Washout**: Design 1 had uniform -10/-10, Design 3 had -6.1/-10, and Design 6 had -10/-7.55. This indicates that while strong washout is important, a gradient might be optimal. I will use a moderate root washout (`twist_root` = -7.0) combined with a more aggressive tip washout (`twist_tip` = -9.0). This aims to reduce root loading while ensuring the tips maintain effective negative angle of attack for favorable vortex formation, improving spanwise efficiency.
*   **Strong, but Moderated, Negative Dihedral**: Designs 1 and 6 used the maximum negative dihedral of -15.0 deg, while Design 3 used -11.7 deg. I will set the dihedral to -14.0 degrees. This maintains a strong negative dihedral, which seems beneficial for the population's best performers, but pulls back slightly from the absolute extreme to explore its robustness.
*   **Symmetric Airfoil for Vortex Dominance**: Design 6 achieved a good reward with a symmetric NACA 0006 airfoil, suggesting symmetric profiles are viable. Given the very high sweep of 75.0 degrees in this new design, which will rely heavily on vortex lift, a symmetric airfoil is preferred over a cambered one to allow for clean vortex formation. I will use a NACA 0008, which is slightly thicker than Design 6's 0006 but still very thin, aiming for good structural properties while minimizing profile drag and complementing vortex lift.
*   **Balancing Root Chord**: Design 1 and 3 had a root chord of ~32.9 inches, while Design 6 had 27.8 inches. I will choose a root chord of 30.0 inches. This is an intermediate value that may reduce overall wetted area compared to the largest wings, potentially lowering skin friction drag, while still providing sufficient surface area for lift when combined with the optimized sweep and twist.
