*   **High Sweep for Vortex Lift:** The highest performing designs consistently utilize high leading-edge sweep angles (around 70-76 degrees). My new design will push this further to explore the very upper end of the sweep range, hypothesizing that even greater sweep will enhance vortex strength and lift, contributing to a higher CL/CDi ratio.
*   **Aggressive Washout for Induced Drag Reduction:** Extreme negative twist (washout) at the wingtip is a recurring feature in top designs, indicating its importance in reducing induced drag by managing spanwise lift distribution. I will maintain maximum washout at the tip and a strong negative twist at the root to ensure effective vortex shedding and an elliptical-like lift distribution for a delta wing.
*   **Max Anhedral for Stability/Performance:** The consistent appearance of maximum negative dihedral (-15.0 degrees) in high-reward designs suggests a beneficial interaction with other parameters or desirable stability characteristics. I will retain this to build upon the observed performance trend.
*   **Thinner Cambered Airfoil Exploration:** While Design 1 used a NACA 2407, Design 3 and 5 used NACA 0006/0008. To explore novel parameter interactions, I will opt for a thinner, yet still cambered, airfoil (NACA 2406). This aims to reduce profile drag compared to thicker airfoils, while retaining the lift benefits of camber, for a potentially improved CL/CDi.
*   **Moderate Root Chord:** The high-reward designs feature root chords around 30-33 inches. I will choose a value in this range that hasn't been exactly replicated, like 28.5 inches, ensuring a sufficiently large wing area for lift while not incurring excessive wetted area for drag.
