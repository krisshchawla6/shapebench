*   **Apply Exploration Strategy**: The current population's top performers are tightly clustered around high sweep, maximum washout, strong anhedral, and very thin airfoils. My new design will deliberately target underexplored regions of the parameter space to uncover novel optima and test the boundaries of these successful trends.
*   **Investigate Mid-Range Sweep & High Camber**: Most high-reward designs feature sweep angles above 70 degrees. To explore a different aerodynamic regime, I will select a mid-range sweep (62.5 degrees). To potentially compensate for reduced vortex lift associated with lower sweep, I will couple this with a highly cambered airfoil (NACA 44xx), a combination less prevalent in high-reward designs.
*   **Push Towards Significantly Thicker Airfoils**: All top designs utilize very thin airfoils (NACA 0006, 2407, 0008). The NACA thickness parameter (naca_t) ranges up to 24%. I will explore a substantially thicker airfoil (NACA 4418) to understand its impact on CL/CDi, challenging the current bias towards thin sections and exploring a vast, untouched region of the design space.
*   **Explore Positive Dihedral**: The entire top-performing population shows strong anhedral (-11.66 to -15.0 degrees). I will boldly explore positive dihedral (+10.0 degrees). This represents a completely different approach to lateral stability and its interaction with induced drag, which is currently unrepresented in the high-reward designs.
*   **Moderate Washout at Root**: While maximal washout is common in top designs, I will use a moderate washout at the root (-5.0 deg) and full washout at the tip (-10.0 deg). This specific twist distribution allows for exploring a different chordwise and spanwise load distribution, potentially optimizing for the chosen lower sweep and cambered, thick profile.
*   **Slightly Shorter Root Chord**: I will set the root chord slightly lower than the average of top designs (25.0 inches). This, in combination with the lower sweep and thicker airfoil, aims to explore the scaling effects in this novel parameter combination.
