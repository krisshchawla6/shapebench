*   **Push the sweep limits:** While current high performers are around 70-76 degrees, I will explore a higher sweep (`le_sweep = 78.0`) to investigate if greater vortex lift can be achieved without excessive induced drag penalties, extending the observed trend.
*   **Depart from extreme negative dihedral:** The current top designs exclusively use maximum negative dihedral. To explore novelty and potentially different stability or induced drag characteristics, I will set `dihedral = 0.0`, moving away from this heavily exploited extreme.
*   **Introduce positive root twist:** Instead of the universal maximum washout at both root and tip, I will introduce a slight positive twist at the root (`twist_root = 2.0`) to encourage loading near the center while maintaining significant washout at the tip (`twist_tip = -8.0`) to manage tip vortices and prevent early stall. This is a significant deviation to explore alternative lift distributions.
*   **Investigate a stronger camber profile:** Current designs favor NACA 24xx or 00xx. I will select a NACA 44xx profile (`naca_m = 4`, `naca_p = 4`) which has higher camber located further back. This aims to generate more lift, paired with a moderate thickness (`naca_t = 10`) to provide structural depth without excessive drag for this experimental camber.
*   **Modest root chord for overall slenderness:** I will choose a slightly smaller `root_chord_in = 25.0` compared to previous top designs. This, combined with the higher sweep, will result in a more slender planform, potentially reducing overall wetted area and contributing to a better CL/CDi ratio if the lift generation strategies are effective.
