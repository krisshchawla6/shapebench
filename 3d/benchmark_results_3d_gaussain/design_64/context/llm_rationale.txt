*   The current top designs are heavily biased towards negative dihedral and strong washout. To truly understand the design space, we must **aggressively explore the opposite extreme for dihedral**, setting it to its maximum positive value of 15.0. This is a bold step to challenge existing assumptions.
*   All high-reward designs have `le_sweep` in a relatively narrow range (69-72 degrees). I will **push `le_sweep` to a much higher value (e.g., 78.0 degrees)** to investigate the performance implications of very strong leading-edge vortices, especially in combination with the novel positive dihedral.
*   While washout (negative tip twist) has been beneficial, I want to **explore a positive `twist_root` (e.g., 2.0 degrees)** in combination with a still negative, but less extreme, `twist_tip` (e.g., -5.0 degrees). This aims to shift the lift distribution more inboard and test a non-uniform twist strategy.
*   The `NACA m=4` option for max camber has been entirely overlooked by the best designs. I will **incorporate `naca_m=4` with `naca_p=4`**, providing significant inherent lift, and pair it with a **higher `naca_t` value (e.g., 18)** to see if this combination can generate high CL/CDi, particularly for a highly swept wing with positive dihedral.
*   To support potentially higher lift generation from the new airfoil and sweep, I'll select a **larger `root_chord_in` (e.g., 45.0 inches)** to ensure sufficient wing area and Reynolds number, which is on the higher end of the existing high-reward designs but not at the absolute maximum.
