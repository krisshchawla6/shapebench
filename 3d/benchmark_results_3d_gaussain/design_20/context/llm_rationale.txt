*   **Leverage high sweep for vortex lift:** The top-performing designs (Design 1, 2, 4) consistently feature high leading-edge sweep angles (around 70-71 degrees). This indicates that maximizing vortex lift is a key strategy for high CL/CDi in this context. Our new design will aim for a similar high sweep, specifically 70.5 degrees, to stay within this successful regime while slightly exploring.
*   **Adopt aggressive washout for load distribution:** Design 1 and Design 4 both utilized the maximum possible tip washout (-10.0 degrees) and achieved high ranks, suggesting this extreme washout is beneficial for controlling spanwise lift distribution and potentially delaying tip stall. We will maintain this aggressive tip washout for the new design, paired with an intermediate negative root twist of -5.5 degrees, blending insights from Designs 1 and 2.
*   **Consolidate successful NACA profile:** Designs 1, 2, and 4, all high-performers, utilized the NACA 24xx airfoil (m=2, p=4). This specific camber profile appears robustly effective. The new design will adopt NACA 24 for its camber characteristics.
*   **Explore intermediate thickness:** Design 1 used NACA 2408 and Design 2 used NACA 2411. To explore this successful range, the new design will use NACA 2410, offering a slightly higher thickness than Design 1 but less than Design 2, potentially finding an optimum between structural benefits and drag.
*   **Interpolate dihedral for stability:** The best designs exhibit anhedral (negative dihedral), with Design 1 at -11.66 and Design 2 at -3.99. To blend these successful traits, our design will use an intermediate anhedral of -8.0 degrees, expecting it to contribute positively to stability characteristics without excessive drag.
*   **Maintain optimal root chord:** The highest-reward designs (1, 2) have root chords around 32-33 inches. Keeping the root chord within this effective range (33.5 inches) helps maintain the overall scale that has proven successful, avoiding the poor performance associated with significantly smaller or larger chords seen in lower-ranked designs.
