*   **Push sweep to the extreme upper boundary (e.g., 78.5 deg):** The top designs show an optimum around 70-71 degrees. I want to test the very high end of the sweep range to see if new, highly efficient vortex lift phenomena can be achieved when combined with other highly optimized parameters, especially given the "Exploration Strategy" directive.
*   **Maintain extreme negative dihedral (-15.0 deg) and aggressive root washout (-10.0 deg):** The consistently high rewards for designs with maximum negative dihedral and significant washout (especially at the root) indicate these are critical for achieving high CL/CDi by controlling spanwise load and vortex behavior. I will keep these at their boundary-pushing values.
*   **Introduce `NACA 44xx` with a thicker profile (naca_t=14):** Designs 1, 2, 4, 5 primarily use `NACA 24xx`, and Design 3 uses `NACA 00xx`. `NACA 44xx` (more camber) is less explored among high performers. A thicker profile offers structural advantages and allows for a more pronounced camber effect, potentially generating more lift at high angles of attack typical of vortex lift operation.
*   **Utilize a larger `root_chord_in` (e.g., 48.0 inches):** Most high-reward designs have root chords in the 30-37 inch range. Scaling the wing up significantly could enhance overall lift production, and with the extreme sweep and twist, the large chord might allow for more pronounced vortex structures without excessive induced drag. This explores a different scale.
*   **Slightly less aggressive tip washout (-7.5 deg) than root washout:** While Design 1 had max washout at both, with a very high sweep and large root chord, a slightly less extreme tip washout might allow the outer wing to carry a bit more load, potentially increasing overall lift without significantly compromising vortex stability or increasing induced drag, thus exploring a nuanced twist distribution.
