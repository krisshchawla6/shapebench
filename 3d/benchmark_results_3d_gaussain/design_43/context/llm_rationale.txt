*   **Boldly explore lower sweep:** The current high-reward designs are clustered around 70-71 degrees. To diversify and potentially find new optimal regimes, I will significantly decrease the leading-edge sweep to 58.5 degrees. This moves away from the high vortex-lift configurations and explores a region where induced drag might be lower.
*   **Introduce positive dihedral:** All successful previous designs used strong anhedral. This new design will use a positive dihedral of 7.5 degrees. This is a stark contrast and will help determine the impact of positive dihedral on CL/CDi, expanding our understanding of stability-performance trade-offs.
*   **Explore a different camber profile:** The best designs used NACA 24xx. Designs with NACA 00xx performed poorly. I will use NACA 44xx to investigate how a different camber distribution (specifically, max camber at 40% chord with a different mean line shape implied by `m=4`) interacts with the lower sweep and positive dihedral. This maintains camber but shifts its characteristics.
*   **Maintain strong tip washout with moderate root twist:** The `twist_tip=-10.0` was a commonality in high-performing designs, indicating its importance for efficient load distribution. I will retain this. However, I will set `twist_root` to -4.5 degrees, creating a larger twist differential across the span compared to the all-out washout of Design 1, which might better suit the lower sweep and positive dihedral.
*   **Adjust root chord and thickness for the new regime:** Given the lower sweep, a slightly smaller root chord (28.0 inches) seems appropriate to maintain reasonable wing area and aspect ratio in this new design space. I will pair this with a thin NACA profile (naca_t=7) to minimize drag, especially with the altered sweep.
