*   **Apply Exploration Strategy**: The current population strongly converges on high sweep (~70 deg), maximal anhedral (-15 deg), and aggressive washout (twist_tip=-10 deg). To discover new, potentially superior optima, I will deliberately explore significantly different regions of the design space.
*   **Lower Sweep Angle**: The top designs cluster around 70-72 degrees of sweep. Design 4, with max sweep (80 deg), performed poorly. This suggests an optimum exists and isn't at the boundary. I will explore a much lower, yet still effective, sweep angle (e.g., ~60 degrees). This will likely reduce vortex strength but could also reduce induced drag, potentially opening a new performance regime.
*   **Positive Dihedral**: Dihedral has been almost exclusively negative (anhedral) in the top designs. This is a significant unexplored region. Introducing a positive dihedral (tips up) will dramatically alter the wing's lateral stability characteristics and may interact differently with vortex flow. I will specify a moderate positive dihedral to test this extreme.
*   **Milder Twist Distribution**: All high-reward designs utilize aggressive washout (often -10 deg at tip and/or root). I will explore a much milder twist strategy: a neutral or slightly positive twist at the root combined with a very mild washout at the tip. This will create a flatter lift distribution, which might be beneficial at lower sweep angles.
*   **Smaller Root Chord**: Most high-performing designs have a root chord in the 30-35 inch range. I will explore a smaller root chord (~20-25 inches) to investigate the impact of overall wing scale and effective aspect ratio when combined with lower sweep.
*   **NACA 00XX and Moderate Thickness**: Design 5 (NACA 0006) achieved a good reward, indicating uncambered airfoils can be effective. I will use NACA 00XX for simplicity, and explore a moderate thickness (e.g., 12%) which is thicker than most top performers (6-8%) but not maximal (24%). This explores the robustness of the new geometry to a thicker, perhaps more structurally robust, airfoil.
