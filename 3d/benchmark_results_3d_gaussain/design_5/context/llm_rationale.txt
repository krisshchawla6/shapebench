*   **Initial Broad Exploration**: As this is the very first design iteration, the strategy is to broadly explore the parameter space rather than focusing on incremental improvements. This design aims to establish an initial benchmark and probe the effects of key aerodynamic features.
*   **High Sweep for Vortex Lift**: A `le_sweep` of 70.0 degrees is chosen to explore the regime where delta wings generate significant vortex lift, a primary mechanism for high-alpha performance, while not pushing to the absolute extreme.
*   **Aggressive Washout for Spanwise Load**: A significant washout is incorporated with `twist_root = 2.0` and `twist_tip = -4.0`. This aims to distribute the lift more evenly across the span, reducing tip loading, delaying tip stall, and potentially improving the CL/CDi by reducing induced drag.
*   **Moderate Camber and Thickness**: `naca_m = 2` with `naca_p = 4` introduces a moderate level of camber for enhanced lift, exploring the benefits of an asymmetric airfoil. `naca_t = 12` offers a balance between structural feasibility and aerodynamic drag, avoiding extreme thinness or thickness.
*   **Slight Positive Dihedral**: A `dihedral` of 5.0 degrees is selected to introduce a moderate level of roll stability. This is a common starting point for initial stability evaluation.
*   **Mid-Range Root Chord**: `root_chord_in = 30.0` provides a medium-sized wing, allowing for a representative scale for initial aerodynamic evaluation without being excessively large or small.
