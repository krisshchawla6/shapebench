*   **Diversity & Exploration (Strategy Focus)**: Instead of directly mimicking Design 1's high sweep, I will explore a moderate-to-high sweep (65.0 deg) to test performance in an intermediate range. This diversifies the population while staying in a generally successful sweep region.
*   **Optimized Twist Distribution**: Learning from the failures of positive root twist and the success of strong washout, I will implement a consistently negative twist. I'll aim for a strong negative `twist_root` (-7.0 deg) and an even more aggressive `twist_tip` (-9.5 deg) to ensure effective load distribution and delayed tip stall, pushing the limits of washout observed in Design 1.
*   **Explore Extreme Negative Dihedral**: Design 3, despite a lower sweep, achieved a good reward with maximum negative dihedral (-15.0 deg). I will explore a dihedral value close to this extreme (-14.0 deg) to see if this characteristic can combine effectively with a higher sweep and strong washout.
*   **Validated Airfoil with Slight Variation**: The NACA 2411 airfoil has proven very effective (Design 1). To explore slight variations, I will use NACA 2414. This maintains the 2% camber at 40% chord (`naca_m=2`, `naca_p=4`) but increases the thickness from 11% to 14% (`naca_t=14`), probing if a slightly thicker but still cambered profile can offer benefits.
*   **Maintain Moderate Root Chord**: A moderate to large root chord (35.0 inches) provides sufficient lifting area, similar to the successful designs, ensuring the overall scale is appropriate for good aerodynamic performance.
