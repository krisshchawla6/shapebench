This being the first design, the strategy is to establish a well-performing baseline while exploring common delta wing configurations known to generate lift efficiently and manage induced drag. I will aim for a balanced design that provides a good starting point for future evolutionary steps.

*   **Moderate-to-High Leading Edge Sweep**: I will choose a `le_sweep` of 68 degrees. This is a common and effective sweep angle for delta wings, providing strong vortex lift at relevant angles of attack while managing the induced drag penalties associated with extremely high sweeps.
*   **Washout for Improved Lift Distribution**: To reduce induced drag and improve efficiency, I will incorporate washout. A `twist_root` of 1 degree and a `twist_tip` of -5 degrees will create a significant washout, promoting a more even spanwise lift distribution and mitigating tip stall.
*   **Moderate Camber for Enhanced Lift**: I will introduce a moderate positive camber with `naca_m=2` and `naca_p=4`. This should provide additional lift at lower angles of attack, boosting CL without introducing excessive drag and exploring the effect of a non-zero cambered airfoil.
*   **Optimized Thickness**: A `naca_t` of 10% is chosen to represent a relatively thin airfoil. This aims to minimize profile drag, which contributes directly to CDi, while still allowing for structural integrity (though not directly modeled).
*   **Mid-Range Root Chord**: A `root_chord_in` of 35 inches offers a substantial but not extreme size, providing a good balance for initial lift generation and allowing scaling in future designs.
*   **Neutral Dihedral**: I will set `dihedral` to 0 degrees to avoid introducing lateral stability effects that might obscure the direct CL/CDi performance of the chosen planform and airfoil configuration in this initial evaluation.
