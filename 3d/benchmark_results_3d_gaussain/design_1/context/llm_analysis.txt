As no previous designs or images are provided for specific analysis, I will outline general considerations for maximizing CL/CDi in delta wing design and identify key areas for exploration in this initial generation.

**General Aerodynamic Issues in Delta Wings:**
*   **High Induced Drag**: Inherent to low aspect ratio wings, particularly at higher angles of attack. This is the primary challenge for maximizing CL/CDi.
*   **Suboptimal Lift Distribution**: Can lead to premature tip stall, reducing effective lift and increasing drag.
*   **Vortex Breakdown**: Can occur at very high angles of attack, causing a sudden loss of lift and pitching moment changes. While powerful, vortex lift needs to be managed.

**How Parameters Generally Affect Performance:**
*   **`le_sweep`**: Higher sweep enhances vortex lift but increases effective aspect ratio reduction, leading to higher induced drag. Finding a balance is key.
*   **`twist_root`, `twist_tip`**: Critical for spanwise load distribution. Washout (negative `twist_tip` or `twist_tip` < `twist_root`) can improve efficiency by reducing tip loading.
*   **`naca_m`, `naca_p` (Camber)**: Adds lift and affects pitching moment. Can be used to increase CL at a given AoA, but must be balanced against potential drag increases.
*   **`naca_t` (Thickness)**: Affects profile drag and structural volume. Thinner airfoils generally have lower profile drag.
*   **`root_chord_in`**: Scales the wing, affecting Reynolds number and overall lift/drag magnitude.
*   **`dihedral`**: Primarily impacts lateral stability, less so CL/CDi directly.

**Key Design Changes Needed to Improve Designs (General Strategies for Delta Wings):**
1.  **Optimize Twist Distribution**: Systematically explore washout (`twist_tip` < `twist_root` or `twist_tip` negative) to achieve a more elliptical or efficient spanwise lift distribution, directly targeting induced drag reduction.
2.  **Balance Sweep for Vortex Lift vs. Induced Drag**: Investigate a range of `le_sweep` values, starting with moderate-to-high sweeps to leverage vortex lift while ensuring induced drag is kept in check.
3.  **Explore Camber Benefits**: Introduce moderate camber (`naca_m > 0`) to potentially increase lift at design conditions, assessing its impact on the CL/CDi ratio.
4.  **Manage Thickness for Profile Drag**: Keep `naca_t` in a reasonable range (e.g., 10-15%) to minimize profile drag without overly penalizing structural depth.
