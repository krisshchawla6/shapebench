*   **Hybrid Sweep Strategy**: We will blend the highly effective sweep from Design 1 (71.2 deg) with the even higher sweep of Design 3 (76.6 deg). By choosing an intermediate value of 73.5 degrees, we aim to further enhance vortex lift generation, a primary driver of performance for high-reward delta wings, without pushing to an unproven extreme.
*   **Optimized Differential Washout**: Drawing from Design 1's max root washout (-10.0 deg) and Design 4's effective strong tip washout (-10.0 deg) but also considering Design 3's less extreme tip (-6.1 deg), we will use a full -10.0 deg at the root and a strong but not maximal -8.0 deg at the tip. This strategy aims to optimize spanwise lift distribution to reduce induced drag, allowing the inner wing to generate significant lift while preventing premature tip stall or excessive negative lift at the tips.
*   **Cambered Airfoil with Minimized Thickness**: We will adopt the NACA 24xx (2% camber at 40% chord) profile from the top-performing Designs 1 and 4, which has proven beneficial for CL. To further enhance the CL/CDi ratio, we will pair this with the lowest practical thickness (NACA 2406, 6% thickness). This is a direct attempt to reduce profile drag while maintaining the lift benefits of camber.
*   **Consistent Extreme Anhedral**: The strong anhedral of -15.0 degrees is a common trait among the top designs (1 and 3). Its consistent presence suggests it plays a significant role in the overall aerodynamic efficiency or stability characteristics that contribute to high reward, and therefore, we will retain this extreme value to explore its impact within this new hybrid configuration.
*   **Moderate Root Chord for Scale**: The root chord will be maintained around 32.5 inches, consistent with the successful designs (1 and 4). This ensures the wing maintains an appropriate scale, allowing the other geometric parameters to effectively influence the aerodynamic performance without introducing significant scaling effects.
