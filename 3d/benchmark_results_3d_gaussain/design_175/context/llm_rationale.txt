*   **Maximized Leading Edge Sweep**: The top-performing designs heavily favor high sweep angles (70-78 degrees). To further exploit the benefits of strong leading-edge vortices for efficient lift generation, I will push the `le_sweep` to the upper limit of the parameter space (79.0 degrees). This is a bold exploration to see if even higher sweep yields better CL/CDi.
*   **Maximized Washout**: The top designs consistently show strong negative twist, often at or near the extreme of -10.0 degrees for both `twist_root` and `twist_tip`. This aggressive washout is likely critical for optimizing spanwise load distribution, reducing induced drag, and preventing premature tip stall, thereby enhancing CL/CDi. I will maintain this strategy by setting both to -10.0.
*   **Extreme Anhedral Consistency**: The -15.0 degree `dihedral` is a striking commonality among the highest-reward designs, suggesting it plays a significant role in delta wing performance. I will keep this extreme negative dihedral value, exploring its full impact on vortex behavior and overall efficiency.
*   **Optimized Root Chord**: While the top design has a moderate root chord, slightly smaller root chords can reduce wetted area and thus friction drag. I will set the `root_chord_in` to 28.0 inches, slightly higher than the top design (27.28) but still moderate, to balance lift production with drag reduction.
*   **Very Thin Symmetric Airfoil**: Design 1's success with NACA 0006 strongly indicates that minimizing profile drag with a thin, symmetric airfoil is highly effective when vortex lift is dominant. I will choose `naca_m=0`, `naca_p=0`, and a very thin `naca_t=7` to maintain low profile drag while exploring a thickness slightly different from the top design's 6%.
*   **Novel Design Strategy Application**: This design combines several near-extreme parameter values (sweep, washout, dihedral, thickness) based on the clear trends observed in the high-reward designs, rather than tweaking existing ones. It pushes the boundaries of these identified "success parameters" simultaneously to explore a novel, potentially more optimal region of the design space.
