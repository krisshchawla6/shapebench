The top-ranked design (Design 1) achieved a reward of 424.5627 with a high leading edge sweep (71.24 deg), maximum negative twist at both root and tip (-10.0 deg), maximum negative dihedral (-15.0 deg), and a NACA 2407 airfoil. Designs 3 and 6, also high-ranking, share similar characteristics: high sweep (76.64, 69.77 deg), aggressive negative twist, and significant negative dihedral (-15.0, -11.66 deg). The high sweep, combined with aggressive negative twist (washout), is indicative of a strategy that heavily relies on strong leading-edge vortex lift and precise spanwise load distribution to achieve high CL/CDi. The extreme negative dihedral might be contributing to this vortex system or simply isn't heavily penalized for this specific objective. The NACA 2407 airfoil (2% camber, 40% position, 7% thickness) provides some inherent camber lift. Design 3 achieved a high reward with a symmetric NACA 0008 airfoil, suggesting that camber is not strictly necessary if the vortex lift mechanism is dominant.

Key changes needed to improve designs:
1.  **Push the limits of high sweep and aggressive washout:** The correlation between high sweep, aggressive negative twist (especially at the tip), and high reward is strong. Further exploration near the maximum sweep limit and full washout seems promising.
2.  **Investigate extreme negative dihedral:** This parameter is consistently at or near its maximum negative limit in the best designs. Understanding its role and pushing its boundary is important.
3.  **Explore more aggressive camber profiles:** While NACA 24xx and 00xx have been explored, a more aggressive camber (e.g., NACA 44xx) might offer further lift advantages, or reveal detrimental interactions with the strong vortex flow.
4.  **Vary thickness and root chord more broadly:** The current high-reward designs cluster around certain chord lengths and thicknesses. Exploring different scales and structural robustness might uncover new optimal points.
