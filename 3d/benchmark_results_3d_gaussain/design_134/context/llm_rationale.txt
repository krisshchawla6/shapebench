*   The highest reward designs consistently feature very high leading edge sweep (70+ degrees), aggressive negative twist (full washout at root and tip), and maximum negative dihedral (-10 to -15 degrees). This points to a successful strategy centered on maximizing and controlling leading-edge vortex lift. We will push this trend further by setting sweep near its maximum and utilizing full washout and negative dihedral.
*   To explore a new region of the airfoil design space, we will introduce a NACA 44xx airfoil. Designs so far have mainly used NACA 24xx or NACA 00xx. A NACA 44xx profile offers more aggressive camber (4% max camber at 40% chord) which could provide additional lift, or reveal complex interactions with the strong vortex flow expected from the high sweep. This is a bold exploration.
*   We will set the airfoil thickness to a moderate value (e.g., NACA 4412). This is thicker than the 6-8% used by top performers, exploring a slightly more robust configuration without going to the extreme end of the thickness range. This allows us to see how a thicker, more cambered wing performs under high sweep conditions.
*   The root chord will be set at a slightly larger value (e.g., 40.0 inches) compared to previous top designs (which were around 30-37 inches). This explores the effect of scaling up the overall wing area in conjunction with the highly optimized aerodynamic parameters, potentially increasing total lift while maintaining a good L/D ratio.
