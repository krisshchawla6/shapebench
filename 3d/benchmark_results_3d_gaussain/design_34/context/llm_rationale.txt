The "Novel Design Strategy" requires me to generate a fresh design based on aerodynamic first principles and judgment, using the database only for general trends. My goal is to maximize reward while ensuring diversity and bold exploration. The top-performing designs show a clear trend towards high sweep, maximum washout, and maximum negative dihedral with cambered NACA 24xx airfoils. To be novel, I will leverage some of these strong trends but introduce significant deviations to explore new, potentially high-reward regions of the design space.

*   **Maintain high sweep for strong vortex lift**: The correlation between high sweep (around 70-72 degrees) and high reward is strong. I will choose a high sweep value (e.g., 70 degrees) to ensure the design benefits from significant vortex lift, a fundamental principle for efficient delta wing performance.
*   **Implement strong washout for favorable lift distribution**: Maximum negative twist (washout) has proven highly effective. I will ensure significant washout, especially at the tip, to control load distribution, delay tip stall, and enhance vortex shedding, as indicated by the best designs. I'll use a strong but not necessarily maxed out root twist, paired with max tip twist.
*   **Explore positive dihedral as a bold deviation**: The maximum negative dihedral in top designs is a strong but unusual trend. To introduce novelty and test a different hypothesis, I will specify a moderate positive dihedral (e.g., +5 degrees). This explores a completely different lateral stability characteristic, allowing us to see if high performance can be achieved without extreme negative dihedral, thereby diversifying the search.
*   **Investigate higher camber and moderate thickness NACA profile**: The NACA 24xx series worked well. To explore higher lift potential through camber, I will choose a NACA 44xx profile (4% max camber at 40% chord), which is within the allowed `naca_m` and `naca_p` options. I will pair this with a moderate thickness (e.g., 12%) to balance structural considerations with aerodynamic performance, differing from the thinner airfoils of the top designs.
*   **Select a larger root chord**: To ensure the wing has ample size for lift generation, I will select a root chord at the larger end of the spectrum (e.g., 45 inches), which also helps differentiate it from previous designs' exact dimensions. This scaling parameter allows for exploration without altering the core aerodynamic shape defined by the other parameters.
