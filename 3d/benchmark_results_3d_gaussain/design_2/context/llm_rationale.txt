Since no previous designs or images are available to analyze, this initial design aims to establish a balanced and generally performant delta wing configuration. The strategy focuses on selecting parameters known to contribute positively to lift and efficiency in delta wings, while keeping some parameters neutral for initial exploration.

*   **Initial Exploration for Balance**: Without prior data, the goal is to create a robust baseline design that incorporates features generally beneficial for delta wing aerodynamics, serving as a solid starting point for subsequent evolutionary steps.
*   **Moderate-High Sweep for Vortex Lift**: A `le_sweep` of 70.0 degrees is chosen to effectively leverage the vortex lift mechanism inherent to delta wings. This value is high enough to generate strong vortices but avoids the most extreme sweeps which might introduce excessive induced drag or structural challenges.
*   **Controlled Washout for Load Distribution**: Setting `twist_root` to 2.0 degrees and `twist_tip` to -3.0 degrees introduces a mild washout. This aims to shift the lift distribution slightly inboard while still providing some relief at the tip, potentially improving stall characteristics and efficiency by optimizing spanwise load.
*   **Cambered Airfoil for Enhanced Lift**: A NACA 2412 equivalent profile (`naca_m` = 2, `naca_p` = 4, `naca_t` = 12) is selected. The 2% camber and its position at 40% chord should provide good lift characteristics, while the 12% thickness offers a good balance between aerodynamic efficiency and structural feasibility.
*   **Moderate Root Chord for Scalability**: A `root_chord_in` of 30.0 inches is chosen as a moderate size. This ensures the wing is large enough for meaningful aerodynamic effects within the simulation domain, yet not excessively large, allowing for scaling up or down in future iterations if needed.
*   **Neutral Dihedral for Simplicity**: `dihedral` is set to 0.0 degrees. This simplifies the initial design analysis by decoupling lateral stability effects, allowing the focus to remain primarily on the lift and induced drag performance derived from the planform and airfoil choices.
