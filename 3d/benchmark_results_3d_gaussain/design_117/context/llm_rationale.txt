We will apply a **Hybrid Strategy** by blending successful characteristics from the top designs: Design 1 (highest reward, deep uniform washout, high sweep), Design 4 (second highest reward, differential washout with deeper tip, moderate sweep), and Design 3 (high sweep, deep root washout, symmetric airfoil).

*   **Sweep Exploration**: Design 1 succeeded with 71.2 degrees, while Design 3 pushed to 76.6 degrees. We will explore a sweep of `73.5` degrees, an intermediate value that still leverages very high sweep for strong vortex lift, but is less extreme than Design 3, aiming for a sweet spot between vortex strength and induced drag.
*   **Differential Washout**: Design 1 used uniform deep washout (-10/-10), while Design 4 showed success with deeper tip washout (-6.1/-10) and Design 3 with deeper root washout (-10/-6.1). To explore the benefits of tailored lift distribution, we will combine a strong washout at the root (`twist_root=-9.0`) with the maximum allowable washout at the tip (`twist_tip=-10.0`). This should effectively unload the tips, manage vortex separation, and reduce induced drag.
*   **Strong Anhedral**: Designs 1, 2, 3, and 4 all utilized significant anhedral (-15.0 to -11.66 degrees), indicating its positive impact on stability and potentially aerodynamic performance for highly swept delta wings. We will maintain a strong anhedral of `-14.0` degrees to capitalize on this observed trend.
*   **Cambered Airfoil with Moderate Thickness**: Designs 1 (NACA 2407) and 4 (NACA 2408) show the benefit of cambered airfoils. We will use a cambered profile (`naca_m=2`, `naca_p=4`) to boost lift. For thickness, `naca_t=8` is chosen, similar to Design 4, which provides a good balance between low profile drag and potential structural benefits, compared to the slightly thinner 7% of Design 1.
*   **Root Chord Adjustment**: Successful root chords range from 27 to 37 inches. Design 1 had 32.88 inches. We will choose `34.0` inches, a slightly larger chord, to marginally increase the wing area, aiming for higher overall lift capacity while remaining well within the proven range.
