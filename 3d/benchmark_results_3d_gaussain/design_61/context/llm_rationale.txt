*   **Push leading-edge sweep higher**: The consistent success of ~71-degree sweep suggests that high sweep is key for vortex lift. I will push towards 75 degrees to explore if further enhancement of vortex lift at potentially higher angles of attack can yield better CL/CDi, anticipating the need to mitigate increased induced drag with other parameters.
*   **Implement a more nuanced, asymmetrical twist distribution**: Instead of maxing out washout at both root and tip, I will set a less aggressive negative twist at the root (e.g., -5 degrees) and maintain strong washout at the tip (e.g., -9 degrees). This strategy aims to shift some lift distribution towards the root while still ensuring stable vortex formation and tip-stall prevention.
*   **Explore higher camber and moderate thickness**: The top designs use NACA 24xx. I will test a NACA 44xx profile (naca_m=4, naca_p=4) to see if increased camber can significantly boost lift, combined with a moderate increase in thickness (naca_t=12). This explores a different airfoil design space for delta wings.
*   **Reduce the severity of negative dihedral**: Top designs use maximum negative dihedral (-15 degrees). I will select a less extreme negative dihedral (e.g., -7 degrees) to investigate if a less pronounced anhedral can still provide necessary stability characteristics without potentially detrimental effects on effective span or adverse yaw.
*   **Increase root chord for overall scaling**: The top designs cluster around a root chord of ~33 inches. I will experiment with a slightly larger root chord of 38 inches to increase the overall wing area, which could lead to a higher overall lift coefficient for a given sweep and twist, potentially improving the CL/CDi ratio.
*   **Promote diversity and exploration**: This design takes a departure from simply tweaking existing high-reward designs. It combines higher sweep, a higher camber/thicker airfoil, a novel twist distribution, and a less extreme dihedral. This bold combination aims to find an entirely new, potentially superior, region in the design space.
