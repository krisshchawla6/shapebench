As this is the initial design iteration with no prior data, the strategy is to generate a bold, exploratory design that leverages known delta wing characteristics and pushes some parameters towards potentially high-reward regions, rather than starting with a completely neutral design. This will provide valuable initial data points for future optimization.

*   **Exploration of High Sweep:** A high leading-edge sweep (e.g., 72 degrees) is chosen to maximize the potential for strong leading-edge vortex lift, which is characteristic of high-performance delta wings. This explores a key feature from the outset.
*   **Initial Washout Twist:** Applying a moderate negative twist at the tip (`twist_tip` = -3.0 deg) while keeping the root untwisted (`twist_root` = 0.0 deg) explores the effect of washout. This is often used to promote a more uniform spanwise lift distribution and delay tip stall, which could improve CL/CDi.
*   **Introduction of Camber:** Instead of a flat plate (NACA m=0), a mild camber (NACA m=2, p=4) is introduced. This allows us to immediately assess the impact of camber on lift and drag for a delta wing configuration, exploring a more complex airfoil profile.
*   **Moderate Scale and Thickness:** A moderate root chord (38.0 inches) provides a sufficiently large wing for simulation without being at the extreme ends. A moderate thickness (NACA t=14) balances structural considerations with aerodynamic performance, avoiding extreme thinness or thickness for this initial exploration.
*   **Neutral Dihedral:** Starting with zero dihedral (0.0 deg) simplifies the initial stability characteristics, allowing its effect to be isolated and explored in subsequent designs.
