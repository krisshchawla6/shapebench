*   The current successful designs (Rank 0, 1, 2) overwhelmingly cluster around high sweep angles, extreme negative dihedral, and significant washout (negative twist). To effectively explore the design space and discover novel optima, it's crucial to **venture into fundamentally different aerodynamic configurations** rather than incrementally tweaking existing successful ones.
*   All high-reward designs utilize negative dihedral, often at its maximum. I will explore **positive dihedral (+10.0 deg)** to investigate its effects on lateral stability and how it might interact with vortex lift in a new way, potentially unlocking a different performance regime. This is a significant departure from the current successful population.
*   Similarly, all top designs feature strong washout (negative twist). I will explore **positive twist (wash-in)** by setting both `twist_root` and `twist_tip` to positive values (7.0 and 3.0 respectively). This will fundamentally alter the load distribution and interaction with the leading-edge vortices, testing a vastly different aerodynamic strategy.
*   Most successful designs have high sweep. I will select a **moderate sweep angle (58.0 deg)**, significantly lower than the ~70-78 deg seen in the best designs. This shift will likely reduce the dominance of leading-edge vortex lift, making the interplay of camber, twist, and dihedral even more critical for performance.
*   The NACA m=4 camber option is largely unexplored by the existing population's best designs. To compensate for potentially reduced vortex lift at lower sweep and explore new lift generation mechanisms, I will employ **maximum camber (NACA m=4, p=4)**. This allows testing if a higher-lift airfoil can maintain good CL/CDi under these new geometric constraints, especially with a moderately thicker profile (NACA t=14).
