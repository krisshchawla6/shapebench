*   **Explore lower sweep regime:** The current population is heavily biased towards high sweep (~70-71 degrees). To encourage diversity and explore alternative aerodynamic strategies, this design will significantly reduce the sweep angle to 58.0 degrees. This will shift reliance away from pure strong vortex lift towards potentially more distributed lift, while still being a delta wing.
*   **Maintain aggressive washout:** High washout, especially at the tip (-10.0 degrees), has been a consistent feature of high-performing designs. We will retain a strong negative twist at the tip to manage lift distribution and prevent tip stall, and a moderately aggressive root twist (-8.0) to balance the lower sweep.
*   **Introduce higher camber:** To potentially compensate for reduced vortex lift at a lower sweep and to explore a different lift-generating mechanism, we will use a more aggressive camber profile, NACA 4407 (4% max camber at 40% chord). This directly tests if higher inherent lift can substitute or complement vortex lift effectively.
*   **Moderate anhedral:** Instead of the extreme anhedral (-15.0) seen in most top designs, we will test a moderate anhedral of -7.5 degrees. This investigates the sensitivity of performance to dihedral when other parameters are significantly altered, rather than simply replicating previous successful settings.
*   **Slightly larger root chord:** To ensure sufficient wing area and Reynolds number given the potentially different lift characteristics of a lower-sweep, high-camber wing, we will use a slightly larger root chord of 40.0 inches, expanding beyond the typical 30-33 inch range of top designs.
*   **Retain low thickness:** Low thickness (7% in this case) has been consistently associated with higher rewards, presumably due to reduced drag. We will maintain this successful characteristic with the new airfoil.
