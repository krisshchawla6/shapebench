*   **Maximize leading-edge sweep to its absolute limit (80.0 deg):** The highest-performing design had very high sweep (77.84 deg). Pushing this to the maximum boundary will test if even more aggressive sweep yields further increases in vortex lift, potentially leading to higher CL/CDi despite increased induced drag from the longer leading edge. This is a bold exploration of an extremum.
*   **Set both root and tip twist to maximum washout (-10.0 deg):** All top designs feature strong negative twist. Maximizing washout across the entire span ensures an optimal spanwise lift distribution, reduces induced drag, and prevents premature tip stall, which is critical for high CL/CDi performance in delta wings.
*   **Maximize negative dihedral (-15.0 deg):** The consistent presence of maximum negative dihedral in the highest-reward designs suggests it plays a critical role, possibly in enhancing lateral stability at high angles of attack or optimizing the effective angle of attack for the wing's unique flow phenomena. This pattern warrants further exploitation.
*   **Explore mild camber (NACA 24xx) with optimal geometric parameters:** Design 1 used a symmetric airfoil. Design 2, despite lower sweep, used NACA 2407 and achieved a decent reward. This new design will combine the aggressive geometric parameters (max sweep, washout, dihedral) with a mild camber (NACA 24xx) to test if camber can further enhance lift while maintaining low drag in this configuration. This directly addresses the fourth key change from the analysis.
*   **Choose a thin, but slightly thicker airfoil (NACA 2409):** Designs 1 and 2 had 6% and 7% thickness. Let's try 9% thickness to explore the sensitivity of performance to a slightly thicker (but still thin) airfoil, while maintaining the mild camber of NACA 24xx. This contributes to diversity in the `naca_t` parameter.
*   **Vary root chord to a moderate-high value (35.0 inches):** Design 1 had a root chord of 27.28 inches. By selecting a moderately larger root chord, we explore the scale effect while maintaining highly optimized aerodynamic shaping, ensuring diversity in overall wing size.
