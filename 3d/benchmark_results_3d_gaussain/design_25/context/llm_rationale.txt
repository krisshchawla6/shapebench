*   The top-performing designs consistently utilize high leading-edge sweep (around 70 degrees), maximum negative twist at the tip, and significant negative dihedral. This indicates these parameters are crucial for efficient vortex lift generation and load management.
*   Design 1, the best performer, pushed both `twist_root` and `twist_tip` to their maximum negative limits, as well as `dihedral`. This suggests that aggressive washout and negative dihedral are highly beneficial.
*   To explore the design space creatively and understand the sensitivity of these parameters, this new design will maintain high sweep but slightly reduce it to diversify from the absolute peak. It will also explore a strong washout configuration, keeping the tip twist maximized as seen in top designs, but vary the root twist from the absolute maximum.
*   A key exploration will be to test a NACA 00xx airfoil (`naca_m=0, naca_p=0`). All high-reward designs used NACA 24xx. Introducing a zero-camber airfoil provides a bold test of whether camber is essential or if optimal geometry alone can achieve high performance, thus contributing to diverse design generation.
*   Concurrently, we will pair the zero-camber airfoil with the minimum allowed thickness (`naca_t=6`) to minimize profile drag, compensating for any potential loss of lift from the lack of camber through the optimized geometric parameters.
*   The `root_chord_in` will be set to a value smaller than previous top designs to see if scale significantly impacts the CL/CDi ratio, aiming for further diversity.
