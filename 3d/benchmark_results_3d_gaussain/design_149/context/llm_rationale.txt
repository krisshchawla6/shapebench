*   **Boldly explore maximum leading-edge sweep:** The highest-reward designs already leverage significant sweep. Pushing `le_sweep` to its absolute maximum (80.0 deg) will test the limits of vortex lift generation and its interaction with other parameters, aligning with a strategy of bold exploration.
*   **Reinforce extreme washout and negative dihedral:** The consistent high performance of designs with maximum negative `twist_root`, `twist_tip`, and `dihedral` strongly indicates these are critical for maximizing `CL/CDi` in this context, likely by optimizing spanwise load distribution and enhancing vortex generation/stability. We will maintain these extreme values.
*   **Combine successful camber with minimal thickness:** Design 1 uses NACA 2407, a cambered, thin airfoil. Design 5 uses 0006, the thinnest symmetric option. This new design will use NACA 2406, aiming for the benefits of cambered lift with reduced profile drag from a very thin airfoil (`naca_t=6`).
*   **Explore a larger root chord:** The current successful designs cluster around a root chord of 30-34 inches. Increasing the `root_chord_in` to 40.0 inches will explore a larger wing area, potentially increasing lift while we observe its interaction with the very high sweep and aggressive twist. This provides diversity in wing scale.
