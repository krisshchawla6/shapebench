*   **Exploit High Sweep**: The highest-performing designs (Design 1 and Design 4) feature leading-edge sweep angles around 70-71 degrees. To exploit this, I will slightly increase the sweep to 72.0 degrees, attempting to further enhance vortex lift without incurring excessive drag penalties that might be seen at even higher sweeps (like Design 6 at 75.6 degrees).
*   **Reinforce Extreme Washout**: Design 1's success with `twist_root=-10.0` and `twist_tip=-10.0` strongly suggests maximum washout is highly beneficial for lift distribution and induced drag reduction. This will be a core feature of the new design to maximize CL/CDi.
*   **Max Anhedral**: Design 1 also leveraged maximum anhedral (`dihedral=-15.0`) to achieve its high reward. This suggests strong anhedral is critical. I will replicate this extreme anhedral to maintain or improve upon the observed benefits, possibly related to vortex interaction or stability at high angles of attack.
*   **Optimize Airfoil Thinness**: The NACA 2407 and 2408 airfoils were successful for top designs. To refine this, I will keep the `naca_m=2` and `naca_p=4` (cambered profile) but select a `naca_t=6`. This is the thinnest possible airfoil within the allowed range and aims to further reduce form/wave drag while maintaining camber benefits, pushing the limits of aerodynamic efficiency.
*   **Consistent Root Chord**: The root chord for top performers is consistently around 31-33 inches. I will choose a value in the upper end of this successful range (e.g., 33.5 inches) to provide a slightly larger wing area for lift while adhering to the proportions that have proven effective.
