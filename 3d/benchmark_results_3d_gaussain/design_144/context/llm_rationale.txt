*   **Boldly explore higher `le_sweep`**: The top-ranked designs show a strong correlation with high sweep angles. We will push `le_sweep` near its upper limit (e.g., 78.0 degrees) to maximize the potential for stable leading-edge vortices and vortex lift, which is characteristic of high-performance delta wings.
*   **Maintain maximal washout at the tip and root**: Consistent with the best designs (Design 2), setting both `twist_root` and `twist_tip` to -10.0 degrees promotes a highly unloaded tip and a favorable spanwise lift distribution, crucial for minimizing induced drag and delaying stall across the wing.
*   **Apply maximal negative `dihedral`**: The trend among top performers strongly favors `dihedral=-15.0`. This will be applied to maintain consistency with high-reward designs, likely enhancing lateral stability and possibly induced drag reduction.
*   **Explore `NACA 44XX` with minimal thickness**: Previous attempts with NACA 44XX were not highly successful (Design 3). However, this might have been due to other parameters. We will pair the aggressive `NACA 44XX` camber (`naca_m=4`, `naca_p=4`) with a very thin profile (`naca_t=7`) to see if the increased camber can contribute to higher lift without a significant drag penalty when combined with the optimized sweep, twist, and dihedral. This is a creative exploration of a less successful but potentially powerful airfoil type.
*   **Mid-range `root_chord_in`**: Maintaining the `root_chord_in` in the successful mid-range (around 30-35 inches) allows us to isolate the effects of the other more aggressively altered parameters, ensuring the wing's overall scale remains practical within the observed successful window.
