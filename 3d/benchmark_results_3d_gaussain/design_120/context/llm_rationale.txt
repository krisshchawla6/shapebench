*   **Exploiting the top performers:** The most successful designs (Design 1 and Design 2) demonstrate a clear advantage for specific parameter choices. My new design aims to build directly on these patterns, refining them to potentially exceed previous best results.
*   **Refining sweep within the optimal window:** Design 1 had a sweep of 71.24 degrees. I will slightly increase the `le_sweep` to 71.8 degrees. This explores the upper end of the established optimal range (70-72 degrees) that consistently yielded high rewards, aiming to potentially find an even higher peak.
*   **Reinforcing extreme washout and anhedral:** Both `twist_root` and `twist_tip` will be set to their maximum negative value of -10.0 degrees, and `dihedral` to its maximum negative value of -15.0 degrees. This directly replicates the configuration of the best-performing design (Design 1), which suggests these extreme settings are crucial for optimal lift distribution and stability in this high-sweep delta wing.
*   **Fine-tuning root chord:** Design 1 and 2 had root chords around 32.9 inches. I will slightly increase `root_chord_in` to 33.2 inches. This small adjustment aims to marginally increase the wing area, which could lead to a higher lift component without drastically increasing induced drag, based on the successful performance of similar chord lengths.
*   **Exploring minimum thickness with proven camber:** The NACA 24xx airfoils are proven winners. Design 1 used NACA 2407. I will utilize `naca_m=2` and `naca_p=4` to retain the beneficial camber. To further optimize, I will set `naca_t=6`, the minimum allowable thickness. This represents an exploitation step to see if a thinner cambered airfoil can reduce profile drag and thus improve CL/CDi further without compromising lift.
