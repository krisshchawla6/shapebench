This population has converged on a specific high-sweep, washout, anhedral design space. To truly advance the search and discover potentially superior or equally performant, yet structurally or aerodynamically distinct designs, a bold exploratory step is needed. My reasoning for the new design focuses on pushing the boundaries in multiple underexplored dimensions simultaneously:

*   **Explore lower sweep angles:** Current top designs are all >69 degrees. I will design a wing with a significantly lower `le_sweep` (e.g., 55 degrees). This will move away from the high-vortex-lift regime and investigate how a moderately swept delta performs, potentially yielding better L/D at lower angles of attack or reducing induced drag.
*   **Introduce positive dihedral:** All high-reward designs use strong anhedral (-11 to -15 degrees). I will try a moderately positive `dihedral` (e.g., 8 degrees) to understand its interaction with a lower sweep angle and its impact on lateral stability, providing a stark contrast to previous findings.
*   **Experiment with washin at root and moderate washout at tip:** The population heavily favors strong washout. I will introduce a positive `twist_root` (e.g., 5 degrees) combined with a moderate `twist_tip` (e.g., -5 degrees). This creates a unique twist distribution (washin at root, mild washout at tip) that is entirely novel to the current high-performing designs, aiming to explore different spanwise load distributions.
*   **Utilize a highly cambered and thicker airfoil:** The `NACA 40xx` series has been virtually untouched, and `naca_t` has primarily been 6-8%. I will select `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4` (4% max camber at 40% chord) with a substantially thicker `naca_t=16`. This combination explores the effects of higher inherent lift due to camber and increased structural volume from thickness, which might be beneficial for a lower-sweep wing.
*   **Moderate root chord for scaling:** The `root_chord_in` has mostly been in the 27-33 inch range. I will select a slightly smaller, yet still significant, `root_chord_in` of 25.0 inches to ensure the wing maintains a reasonable scale while being distinct from the average population size.
