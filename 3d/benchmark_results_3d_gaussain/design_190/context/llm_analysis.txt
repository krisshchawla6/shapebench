*   **Strong Washout (Twist Root/Tip negative):** This reduces the angle of attack towards the wingtips, which can delay tip stall and improve spanwise load distribution, potentially reducing induced drag by tailoring the lift profile.
*   **Strong Anhedral (-15.0° Dihedral):** Enhances roll stability for highly swept wings, often used to counteract excessive dihedral effect that can arise from sweep.
*   **NACA 0006:** A thin, symmetric airfoil suitable for high-speed flight or where minimal profile drag is desired, relying on the wing geometry for lift.

**Key Issues/Observations from current population:**
1.  **Limited Exploration of Lower Sweep:** The highest rewards are concentrated at very high sweep angles (>70°). The lower end of the sweep spectrum (45-60°) is largely unexplored.
2.  **Dominance of Washout and Anhedral:** Almost all successful designs feature strong negative twist and negative dihedral. The effects of positive twist (washin), zero twist, or positive dihedral are unknown.
3.  **Thin, Symmetric Airfoils:** NACA 00xx is common. While NACA 24xx has been tried, the full range of camber (NACA 40xx) and airfoil thickness (up to 24%) has not been fully explored, especially in combination with different sweep and twist characteristics.
