*   **Strategy Focus: Diversity and Bold Exploration:** The current top designs (1, 2, 4, 5) all feature high sweep (70+ deg), significant negative dihedral, and substantial washout. To foster diversity, the new design will directly challenge these established patterns in twist and dihedral.
*   **High Sweep Retention:** High leading edge sweep consistently appears in high-reward designs, indicating its importance for generating beneficial vortex lift. I will retain a high sweep angle, slightly above Design 1, to further explore this region.
*   **Inverted Twist Strategy:** Design 1 uses maximum washout at both root and tip. I will explore a novel twist distribution by introducing *positive* twist at the root to encourage early lift generation, combined with moderate *negative* twist at the tip to maintain some induced drag control and prevent tip stall. This is a bold departure from current successful designs.
*   **Positive Dihedral Exploration:** All current top designs utilize negative dihedral. To explore a new stability and lift distribution regime, I will apply a moderate *positive* dihedral. This will significantly diversify the population's geometric characteristics.
*   **Enhanced Camber for Lift:** Design 1 used NACA 2407. I will use a NACA 4408 airfoil, providing more camber than Design 1, potentially increasing lift at a given angle of attack, balanced with a reasonable thickness.
*   **Root Chord for Scale:** A moderate to high root chord will be maintained to ensure sufficient wing area for lift generation, complementing the other design choices.
