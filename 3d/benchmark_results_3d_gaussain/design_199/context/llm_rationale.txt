*   **Exploit high sweep:** Design 1's exceptional performance with a very high sweep (77.84) strongly suggests this parameter is critical for maximizing vortex lift and CL/CDi. I will push the sweep angle even higher, closer to the upper limit of 80 degrees, to explore the benefits of even stronger leading-edge vortices.
*   **Maximize uniform washout:** The top designs consistently show large negative twist values. Design 1 had nearly uniform washout. Setting both `twist_root` and `twist_tip` to their maximum negative value (-10.0 degrees) should ensure an aggressive and uniform load distribution towards the root, effectively reducing induced drag by minimizing tip loading.
*   **Retain maximum anhedral:** The consistent use of maximum negative dihedral (-15.0 degrees) in high-reward designs, especially Design 1, indicates it's a beneficial characteristic for these delta wings. I will keep this parameter at its maximum negative value.
*   **Optimize root chord for area and aspect ratio:** Design 1 had a moderate root chord (27.28). While D2 and D3 had larger chords with lower rewards, this was likely due to other parameter choices. With extremely high sweep and washout, a slightly larger root chord than D1, but not excessively large, could increase overall wing area and potentially lift, without unduly increasing induced drag if the other parameters are optimized. I'll select a value slightly above Design 1's.
*   **Reaffirm symmetric, thin airfoil:** Design 1's symmetric NACA 0006 airfoil yielded the highest reward, outperforming cambered options. This suggests that for high-sweep delta wings relying heavily on vortex lift, a simple, thin symmetric profile may be optimal. I will use a symmetric airfoil (NACA 00) and choose a thickness (naca_t) that is slightly varied from Design 1's 6, to explore its sensitivity while maintaining overall thinness, e.g., 7 or 8.
