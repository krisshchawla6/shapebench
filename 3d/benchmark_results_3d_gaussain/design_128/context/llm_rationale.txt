*   **Explore Low Sweep:** The current population's high-performing designs consistently feature high sweep angles (70+ degrees). To promote diversity and discover potentially different optimal configurations, this design will aggressively explore the lower boundary of `le_sweep` at 50.0 degrees. This will fundamentally alter the vortex formation and overall aerodynamic characteristics compared to previous successful designs.
*   **Test Positive Dihedral:** All top designs exhibit extreme anhedral (-15.0 deg). This design will explore the opposite extreme by setting `dihedral` to +15.0 degrees. This significant shift will allow the optimizer to understand how positive dihedral interacts with a low-sweep delta wing and its effect on stability and induced drag, which is currently an unexplored region of the design space for high-reward configurations.
*   **Moderate Twist Distribution:** While aggressive washout (-10.0 deg) is prevalent in high-reward designs, this design will experiment with a neutral `twist_root` (0.0 deg) to maximize initial lift generation and a moderate `twist_tip` (-5.0 deg) to still provide some tip-stall mitigation. This less aggressive twist profile is a deliberate departure, designed to test its viability with the lower sweep and positive dihedral.
*   **Higher Camber and Moderate Thickness:** Cambered airfoils have shown promise. We will use `NACA 44xx` for higher camber, which is known but less common than `24xx` in top designs. This will be combined with a `naca_t` of 18, which is significantly thicker than most explored designs (6-12%). This explores a robust, potentially structurally sound airfoil with high lift potential, in conjunction with the novel planform.
*   **Mid-Range Root Chord:** A `root_chord_in` of 35.0 inches is chosen to maintain a comparable scale to successful previous designs, ensuring that the effects of the highly exploratory sweep, twist, dihedral, and NACA parameters are clearly isolated without being confounded by extreme wing size.
