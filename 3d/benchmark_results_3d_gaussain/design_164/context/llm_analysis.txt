*   **Washout (negative twist at root and tip):** Both `twist_root` (-9.55) and `twist_tip` (-9.13) are significantly negative, indicating a strong washout. This helps to distribute lift more evenly across the span, delaying tip stall and potentially reducing induced drag, especially crucial for highly swept wings where tips tend to generate high lift. The almost uniform washout (root and tip twists are very close) suggests a somewhat consistent load distribution.
*   **Anhedral (dihedral=-15.0):** Maximum anhedral contributes to roll stability and can also influence the vortex flow field. For highly swept wings, anhedral can sometimes enhance the stability characteristics.

For high sweep, the contribution of vortex lift often overshadows the benefits of geometric camber, making a symmetric airfoil a good choice for minimizing drag.
