*   The most successful designs (Rank 0, 1, 2) all feature high sweep (70-76 deg), extreme negative twist at both root and tip (-10 deg), and extreme negative dihedral (-15 deg or close). This strongly indicates that these aggressive geometric parameters are key drivers for high CL/CDi. We will continue to explore this successful region.
*   Design 1 achieved the highest reward with `le_sweep=71.2`, `twist_root=-10.0`, `twist_tip=-10.0`, and `dihedral=-15.0`. This new design will maintain these extreme values for twist and dihedral, and push sweep slightly higher to explore a new edge of this successful region.
*   Previous high-reward designs utilized NACA 24xx or 00xx airfoils. Design 6 explored NACA 44xx but with less aggressive twist/dihedral and lower sweep, leading to a moderate reward. This design will explore the potential of higher camber (`naca_m=4`, `naca_p=4`) *in combination* with the proven extreme sweep, twist, and dihedral. This is a novel combination not yet seen in the top ranks.
*   A higher `naca_t` value (e.g., 9-10) will be chosen to provide a slightly thicker section than some top designs (7-8), potentially offering more structural volume and possibly better performance with higher camber, while still remaining within a reasonable range.
*   The `root_chord_in` will be set around the middle of the successful range (30-37 inches) to ensure a sufficiently large lifting surface, without increasing overall size unnecessarily.
