*   **Maximize Camber for Lift Potential:** Designs using symmetric airfoils (NACA 00xx) consistently performed poorly. Design 1 and 2, which are top performers, used NACA 24xx. To explore a bolder design, I will utilize the maximum available camber, `naca_m=4` (with `naca_p=4`), which has only been explored once in Design 5 with a suboptimal sweep and twist combination. This aims to significantly increase inherent lift and potentially interact favorably with vortex lift.
*   **Retain Optimal Washout and Dihedral:** The top-performing Design 1 featured `twist_root=-10.0`, `twist_tip=-10.0`, and `dihedral=-15.0`. This aggressive configuration for washout and dihedral appears critical for optimizing lift distribution, reducing induced drag, and enhancing stability for high CL/CDi. I will carry these values forward.
*   **Explore a Unique High Sweep Region:** The 70-72 degree sweep range yielded the top results. However, to ensure diversity and explore the design space creatively, I will select a high sweep of `le_sweep=68.5`. This is still within the generally successful high-sweep domain but differs from the previously identified optimal range, allowing us to test the robustness of the high camber and aggressive twist/dihedral in an adjacent, but distinct, region.
*   **Moderate Increase in Root Chord:** The top designs had root chords around 32-33 inches. Design 5, despite its low reward, had a larger root chord of 36.1 inches. Combining this slightly larger chord with the aggressive aerodynamic parameters (max camber, max washout, max negative dihedral) and a well-chosen sweep could potentially lead to higher overall lift, warranting this exploration.
*   **Maintain Thin Airfoil:** To minimize profile drag, a thin airfoil section is desirable. `naca_t=6` was used in a few designs, and `naca_t=7` in the top design. I will choose `naca_t=6` to prioritize drag reduction, especially with the higher camber and potentially higher lift.
