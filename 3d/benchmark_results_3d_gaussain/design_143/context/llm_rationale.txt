The current population is heavily biased towards high sweep, strong washout, and anhedral. To truly advance the population and explore the design space effectively, a design with fundamentally different characteristics is needed. This next design will target an underexplored region to identify new performance optima and understand the sensitivity of the reward to these parameters.

*   **Explore lower sweep regime:** The current top designs are clustered at high sweep angles (70-76 degrees). A significantly lower sweep, around 55 degrees, will shift the aerodynamic characteristics, potentially reducing induced drag at lower angles of attack and exploring a new region of vortex lift formation.
*   **Investigate positive dihedral:** All high-performing designs exhibit strong anhedral (-15 to -11 degrees). Introducing a strong positive dihedral (+12 degrees) represents a completely orthogonal design choice, drastically altering lateral stability and effective angle of attack distribution across the span, which is crucial for discovering novel CL/CDi optima.
*   **Moderate, differential twist:** Instead of maximum washout at both root and tip, a moderate washout (-5 degrees) at the root and stronger washout (-10 degrees) at the tip will be explored. This aims to balance lift distribution, prevent tip stall, and minimize induced drag without the extreme penalties that might arise from maximum washout in a lower sweep, positive dihedral configuration.
*   **Larger root chord:** The current successful designs are in the 27-37 inch root chord range. To explore scaling effects and potentially increase the effective wing area, a root chord towards the upper end of the range (45 inches) will be utilized. This might interact with the lower sweep to influence overall lift and drag characteristics.
*   **Symmetric airfoil (NACA 00XX):** While cambered airfoils (NACA 24XX) have shown high rewards, NACA 00XX also yielded respectable results (Designs 3, 4) in high sweep, anhedral configurations. Combining a symmetric airfoil (NACA 0012) with the novel low sweep, positive dihedral, and moderate twist aims to isolate the impact of the wing geometry changes without the added complexity of camber-induced lift, allowing for clearer exploration of this distinct region.
