**Key observations and issues (based on parameters and reward):**
*   **Successful Strategy:** The combination of high sweep, significant washout, anhedral, and a cambered airfoil is highly effective. This configuration likely promotes stable vortex formation, distributes lift efficiently across the span, and delays stall.
*   **Twist Extremes:** The maximum washout at both root and tip (-10.0 deg) in Design 1, and strong negative twists in other high-performing designs, indicate that washout is crucial for managing induced drag and potentially enhancing vortex lift characteristics.
*   **Dihedral Impact:** The consistently negative dihedral in top designs suggests that anhedral contributes to stability or favorable lift distribution for delta wings in this regime, potentially influencing vortex behavior.
*   **NACA Choice:** NACA 24xx series appears beneficial for lift generation, although NACA 00xx also produced respectable results, indicating that the wing geometry itself is a primary driver.

**3-4 key design changes needed to improve designs in the population:**
1.  **Explore lower sweep angles:** All top designs are clustered at high sweep (69-76 deg). Significantly lower sweep angles (e.g., 45-60 deg) are largely unexplored but could yield different aerodynamic benefits (e.g., less induced drag at certain AoAs, different vortex lift characteristics).
2.  **Investigate positive dihedral:** The current population heavily favors negative dihedral. Testing strong positive dihedral would be a radical departure and could uncover novel interactions with sweep and twist.
3.  **Vary twist distribution creatively:** While max washout seems effective, exploring different combinations of root and tip twist (e.g., less extreme overall washout, or more differential washout) could fine-tune lift distribution for new sweep/dihedral combinations.
4.  **Systematically test NACA profiles with new geometries:** Revisit symmetric airfoils (NACA 00xx) or slightly different cambered profiles (e.g., NACA 20xx, NACA 40xx) in conjunction with these novel geometric explorations to understand their isolated and combined effects.
