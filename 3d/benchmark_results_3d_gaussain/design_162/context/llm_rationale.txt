The top design demonstrates that high sweep, strong washout, and aggressive anhedral with a thin symmetric airfoil are very effective. My strategy is to build upon these insights while introducing bolder changes to explore new optimal regions and diversify the population, adhering to the "creative, diverse, and bold exploration" directive.

*   **Maintain High Sweep (but not extreme)**: Design 1 is at 77.84 degrees. I will use a high sweep of 74.5 degrees. This maintains the strong vortex lift benefit but explores a slightly different region than the very highest sweep, potentially finding a better balance for overall drag.
*   **Introduce Aggressive Camber (NACA 44xx)**: The top design uses NACA 0006. To explore a significant departure, I will use NACA 4410. This applies a max camber of 4% at 40% chord, which could enhance lift at lower angles of attack and potentially reduce the reliance on extreme angle of attack for CL, leading to better CL/CDi if induced drag is effectively managed. This directly addresses the need to explore camber.
*   **Strong Washout, with Gradient**: The previous high-reward designs universally feature strong washout. I will implement a strong washout, with a greater emphasis on the tip (`twist_root = -6.0`, `twist_tip = -10.0`). This aims to maintain optimal spanwise load distribution and prevent tip stall, crucial for delta wing efficiency.
*   **Moderate-to-Strong Anhedral**: Extreme anhedral (-15.0) appears in top designs. I will choose `dihedral = -12.5` degrees. This is still a strong anhedral, promoting vortex stability, but explores slightly away from the absolute maximum to see if a slightly different value might offer a more balanced performance or similar benefits.
*   **Moderate Thickness**: Design 1 is NACA 0006. I will use NACA 4410. This is a moderate thickness, thicker than the top design, exploring the trade-off between profile drag and potential benefits related to flow over the thicker section, especially with camber.
*   **Optimized Root Chord**: I will select a root chord of 30.5 inches, a moderate size, within the range observed in successful designs, ensuring the planform generates sufficient lift area.
