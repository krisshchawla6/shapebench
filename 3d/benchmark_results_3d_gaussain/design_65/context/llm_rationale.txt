*   **Apply Exploration Strategy (Positive Dihedral)**: All existing high-reward designs utilize negative dihedral, with the best one at the maximum negative (-15.0). To explore a completely new region of the design space and understand its impact on stability and lift distribution, I will specify a significant **positive dihedral** (+10.0 deg). This is a bold departure from current trends.
*   **Apply Exploration Strategy (Twist Distribution)**: High-reward designs feature aggressive washout (negative twist at root and tip). I will explore a new twist configuration, incorporating **positive twist at the root** (+5.0 deg) to potentially shift lift inboard, combined with a **mild washout at the tip** (-2.0 deg). This aims to understand the effects of a non-conventional twist distribution compared to the uniformly negative twists seen so far.
*   **Explore Higher Sweep**: The best designs cluster around 70-71 degrees sweep. I will push towards a slightly higher sweep of **76.0 degrees**. This attempts to further leverage vortex lift associated with high sweep, combined with the novel twist and dihedral, to see if a new performance peak exists.
*   **Vary NACA Thickness**: High-reward designs mostly used NACA 2407/2408. To explore the effect of structural depth and potentially higher volume, while maintaining a proven camber profile, I will choose a **thicker NACA 2415** (15% thickness) to test its interaction with the exploratory geometric parameters.
*   **Root Chord Adjustment**: High-reward designs have moderate root chords (30-33 inches). To ensure a manageable wing size that complements the higher sweep and potentially higher-lift generating twist/dihedral, I will choose a moderate-to-lower root chord of **25.0 inches**. This might help keep induced drag in check with the higher sweep.
