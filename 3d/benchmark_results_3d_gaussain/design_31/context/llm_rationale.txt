*   **Exploit high sweep for vortex lift**: The highest-performing designs (Design 1 and 3) consistently feature high leading-edge sweep angles around 70-71 degrees. This indicates that strong vortex lift generation is crucial for maximizing the CL/CDi ratio. We will maintain a similar high sweep to continue leveraging this effect.
*   **Maximize washout for optimal load distribution**: Designs with maximum washout at the tip (-10.0 deg, Designs 1, 3, 4, 5) have shown superior performance. Setting both root and tip twist to -10.0 deg will aggressively unload the wingtips, promoting a more elliptical-like spanwise lift distribution ideal for minimizing induced drag.
*   **Replicate extreme negative dihedral**: Design 1, the top performer, utilizes the maximum negative dihedral of -15.0 degrees. This strong correlation suggests that this extreme configuration, while unconventional, is highly beneficial for the objective function and warrants direct exploitation.
*   **Refine NACA profile thickness**: The NACA 24xx series (m=2, p=4) with low thickness (7-8) is present in the top designs. We will continue with this effective camber configuration and slightly increase the thickness to 9. This slight increase might offer a minor boost in overall lift or structural integrity without significantly penalizing induced drag, exploring a minor variation within a proven airfoil type.
*   **Maintain root chord**: The root chord of approximately 32-33 inches has been consistent across the highest-reward designs. We will keep the root chord within this effective range, as it provides a good overall wing scale for the identified optimal aerodynamic characteristics.
