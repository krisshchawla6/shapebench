*   **Explore High Sweep Boundary:** The top-performing designs cluster around a `le_sweep` of ~70-72 degrees. To push exploration, this design will target the higher end of the sweep range, near 80 degrees, to see if an even more acute sweep further enhances vortex lift, despite potential penalties. This tests an under-explored boundary condition.
*   **Invert Dihedral for Exploration:** All high-reward designs in the database exhibit strong anhedral (`dihedral` between -10.0 and -15.0). This design will explore the opposite boundary by using a strong positive `dihedral` (tips up) to assess its impact on lift, drag, and stability, as this region is entirely unexplored by successful designs.
*   **Investigate Alternative Twist Distribution:** While strong negative `twist_tip` is common, the `twist_root` has more variation. This design will experiment with a *positive* `twist_root` (wash-in) combined with a moderate `twist_tip` washout, a combination not seen in top performers, to investigate how this alters the spanwise lift distribution and potentially reduces induced drag.
*   **Integrate Higher Camber with New Sweep:** Designs 2 and 4 (high reward) used `NACA 24xx`. Design 5 used `NACA 4406` but with lower sweep and achieved a lower reward. This design will re-evaluate `naca_m=4` (higher camber) but paired with the new high-sweep configuration and a moderate `naca_t` to determine if increased camber becomes more beneficial at very high sweeps.
*   **Maintain Moderate Root Chord:** The `root_chord_in` values of the top designs are around 32-35 inches. Maintaining a similar value (e.g., 35 inches) will keep the wing at a relevant scale for comparison, allowing other parameter changes to be isolated more effectively.
