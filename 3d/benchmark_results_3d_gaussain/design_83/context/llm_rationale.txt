*   **Exploitation of Optimal Sweep**: The best designs show a sweet spot for `le_sweep` around 71-72 degrees. We will maintain this high sweep, perhaps pushing slightly higher than Design 1's value to explore a local optimum that leverages strong vortex lift, but not so high as to incur excessive induced drag.
*   **Consistent Washout and Anhedral**: `twist_root`, `twist_tip`, and `dihedral` are consistently at or near their extreme negative values (-10.0 and -15.0 degrees, respectively) for the highest-reward designs. This strongly suggests these configurations are beneficial for lift distribution and overall aerodynamic efficiency, so we will set them to their maximum washout/anhedral limits.
*   **Root Chord Stability**: The `root_chord_in` values for the top designs are tightly clustered around 32-33 inches. We will keep the root chord within this range, slightly below the highest in Design 1, as larger chords increase wetted area and potentially friction drag.
*   **Cambered, Very Thin Airfoil**: Designs 1 and 2 (NACA 2407, 2408) performed better than Design 3 (NACA 0006), indicating camber is important. However, Design 3 also used the thinnest airfoil (`naca_t=6`). We will combine the best of both worlds by using a NACA 2406 airfoil, providing camber for lift while maintaining minimal thickness to reduce drag.
