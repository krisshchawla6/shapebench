*   **Emphasize strong washout at the tip:** The consistent high performance of designs with significant negative `twist_tip` (Designs 1 and 3) strongly indicates its importance for efficient lift generation and drag reduction. We will push this parameter towards its negative limit.
*   **Maintain mild negative twist at the root:** Strong wash-in at the root (positive `twist_root`) correlated with poor performance (Design 5). A slightly negative twist at the root helps distribute lift more evenly and avoids premature root stall.
*   **Explore a high sweep angle for strong vortex lift:** Designs with moderate-to-high sweep (e.g., 68-72 degrees) have shown good performance. A higher sweep angle (e.g., ~75 degrees) promotes stronger leading-edge vortices, contributing significantly to lift, while ensuring we're still within a beneficial range.
*   **Implement significant anhedral:** Both top designs (1 and 3) utilize anhedral. A stronger negative dihedral might further enhance vortex stability or reduce effective dihedral effect, contributing to a better CL/CDi, representing a bold exploration in this dimension.
*   **Utilize a highly cambered and moderately thin airfoil:** Cambered airfoils generally provide more lift. A `NACA 44xx` profile offers higher camber than `NACA 24xx`, and combining it with a `p=4` position for max camber will create a distinct profile. A moderate thickness (`naca_t`) strikes a balance between aerodynamic efficiency and structural viability.
*   **Increase root chord for scale diversity:** To explore a larger design space and potentially higher overall lift, a larger `root_chord_in` value will be used, moving towards the upper end of the range.
