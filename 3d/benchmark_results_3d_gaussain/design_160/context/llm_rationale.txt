*   **Hybrid Strategy Application**: This new design will blend the high-efficiency high sweep characteristic from Design 1 with the successful cambered airfoil and strong washout observed in Design 3, aiming to create a novel hybrid that leverages the strengths of both.
*   **Optimized Sweep for Vortex-Camber Synergy**: Design 1's high sweep (77.84 deg) is a key driver for performance. This new design will utilize a slightly reduced but still high sweep (74.5 deg), interpolated between Design 1 (77.84 deg) and Design 3 (71.24 deg). This aims to maintain strong vortex lift while potentially being more amenable to camber benefits.
*   **Max Washout for Lift Distribution**: Both Design 1 and Design 3 demonstrate the efficacy of aggressive washout for optimal spanwise lift distribution. Therefore, the new design will adopt maximum washout at both root and tip (twist_root=-10.0, twist_tip=-10.0), a configuration proven effective by Design 3.
*   **Consistent Dihedral for Stability**: The maximum negative dihedral (-15.0 deg) is consistently present across all top-performing designs, indicating its importance for CL/CDi. This parameter will be kept constant to maintain this favorable characteristic.
*   **Cambered Airfoil Integration**: Unlike Design 1's symmetric airfoil, this design will incorporate a cambered airfoil (NACA 2407), inspired by Design 3's success. This explores whether combining a cambered profile with high sweep can offer synergistic lift benefits without excessive drag, especially given the thinner 7% chord.
*   **Intermediate Root Chord**: To support the combined high sweep and cambered profile, a moderately large root chord (30.0 inches) will be selected, interpolating between Design 1 (27.28 inches) and Design 3 (32.88 inches). This aims to provide sufficient wing area for lift generation while avoiding excessive induced drag.
