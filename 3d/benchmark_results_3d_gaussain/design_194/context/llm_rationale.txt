The new design will adopt a "Hybrid Strategy," blending successful characteristics from the top designs while exploring variations identified in the analysis.

*   **Leverage high sweep but moderate extremism:** Designs 1, 2, and 3 all have high sweep angles (77.84°, 71.24°, 69.77°). I will select a high sweep of 74.5° to remain in the effective high-sweep regime for vortex lift, but slightly less extreme than Design 1, aiming to find an optimal balance between vortex lift generation and induced drag reduction.
*   **Incorporate differential washout:** Designs 2 and 3 demonstrate good performance with cambered airfoils and varying washout. Design 3 specifically showed differential washout (`twist_root=-6.11`, `twist_tip=-10.0`). I will combine the strong tip washout from Design 2/3 (`twist_tip=-10.0`) with a less negative root twist (`twist_root=-7.5`), creating a pronounced washout differential to further optimize the spanwise lift distribution and mitigate tip stall.
*   **Introduce moderate camber:** While Design 1 excelled with a symmetric airfoil, Designs 2, 3, and 4 show decent performance with camber (`NACA 24xx`, `44xx`). I will introduce a `NACA 2408` airfoil (`naca_m=2`, `naca_p=4`, `naca_t=8`). This allows for exploration of higher lift coefficients provided by camber, paired with a slightly thicker profile for structural robustness without excessive drag, attempting to outperform the symmetric airfoil where possible.
*   **Explore moderate anhedral:** The top performers utilize maximum anhedral (-15.0°). Design 3, with a still good reward, used -11.66°. I will set dihedral to -12.5°, a less extreme anhedral value, to explore if similar or improved performance can be achieved without resorting to the maximum setting.
*   **Intermediate root chord:** Design 1 has a smaller chord, while 2 and 3 have larger ones. An intermediate `root_chord_in` of 30.0 will be used to explore a different scale.
