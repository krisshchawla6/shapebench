The leading design (Design 1) achieved its high reward of 626.7341 with an extremely high leading-edge sweep (77.84°), a relatively small root chord (27.28 in), maximum anhedral (-15.0°), and a very thin, symmetric airfoil (NACA 0006). It also features significant, nearly uniform washout (-9.55° root, -9.13° tip). This suggests that high sweep, likely maximizing vortex lift, combined with a thin airfoil (minimizing profile drag) and strong washout (optimizing spanwise lift distribution to reduce induced drag) are critical factors. The extreme anhedral might contribute to stability or a specific aerodynamic interaction not immediately obvious.

**Aerodynamic Issues/Observations:**
*   The very high sweep angle, while beneficial for vortex lift, can also lead to increased induced drag due to larger planform areas and spanwise flow components.
*   The uniform nature of the washout in Design 1, while effective, might not be the absolute optimum for spanwise lift distribution.
*   The symmetric NACA 0006, while minimizing profile drag, limits the potential for increased lift due to camber.
*   The maximum anhedral is an aggressive setting that could influence stability characteristics beyond just CL/CDi, but it seems to correlate with high performance in the current population.

**Key Design Changes Needed to Improve the Population:**
1.  **Explore a "sweet spot" for sweep:** Investigate high sweep angles slightly less extreme than Design 1 (e.g., 70-75°) to balance vortex lift benefits against potential induced drag penalties.
2.  **Optimize twist distribution:** Introduce more differentiated washout (e.g., more negative twist at the tip than at the root) to further refine the spanwise lift distribution and potentially reduce induced drag more effectively than uniform washout.
3.  **Integrate moderate camber:** Test the effect of a modest amount of camber (e.g., NACA m=2 or 4) on CL/CDi. This could increase maximum lift without excessively penalizing induced drag, potentially surpassing symmetric profiles.
4.  **Vary dihedral:** Investigate moderate anhedral values (less than -15.0°) to understand their impact and potentially find a more robust or equally performing setting without the extreme geometric configuration.
