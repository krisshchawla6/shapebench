*   **Extreme Sweep Exploration:** The top-performing designs cluster around high sweep angles (70-77 degrees). This design pushes `le_sweep` to its absolute maximum of `80.0` degrees to thoroughly explore the upper boundary of the design space and maximize the potential for stable, strong leading-edge vortices.
*   **Minimum Root Chord:** Most previous designs utilize a moderate `root_chord_in`. This design explores the absolute minimum `root_chord_in` of `10.0` inches. This combination with maximum sweep will result in an extremely narrow and high-aspect-ratio-like delta planform, a highly underexplored geometric configuration.
*   **Leveraging Proven Washout and Anhedral:** The consistent high performance of designs with `twist_root=-10.0`, `twist_tip=-10.0`, and `dihedral=-15.0` indicates these values are crucial for optimal lift distribution and stability. These parameters are retained to provide a foundation for evaluating the performance of the more extreme geometric variations.
*   **Boundary Exploration of Airfoil Camber and Thickness:** Previous high-reward designs used NACA 00xx or 24xx airfoils with `naca_t` values typically between 6 and 10. To significantly explore the airfoil parameter space, this design employs `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4` (maximum camber and position used in the population) and pushes `naca_t` to its absolute maximum of `24`. This tests the impact of a very thick, highly cambered airfoil on an extremely swept, small delta wing, representing a bold move into an uncharted region.
