The current population, especially the high-reward designs, is clustered in a specific region of the parameter space: high sweep (~70-72 deg), aggressive washout, strong negative dihedral, and thin to moderately thick NACA 00xx or 24xx airfoils. To advance the population using an **Exploration Strategy**, I need to push into genuinely *unexplored* regions.

1.  **Pushing `le_sweep` to the extreme upper boundary:** While ~71 deg sweep worked well, the range goes up to 80 deg. Design 3 at 74.3 deg was poor, but this doesn't preclude an optimum further out. Exploring 78-79 degrees tests the limits of "very high sweep" and its effect on vortex lift characteristics and induced drag. This is a significant exploration step.
2.  **Exploring strong positive `dihedral`:** All high-reward designs have significant negative dihedral. This new design will use a high positive dihedral (+14.5 deg), which represents a completely orthogonal exploration of the lateral stability characteristics and its impact on performance. This configuration is entirely absent from the high-performing population.
3.  **Investigating minimal `root_chord_in`:** The existing designs primarily use root chords between 27 and 37 inches. I will explore the lower boundary of `root_chord_in` (12.5 inches). This will result in a much smaller overall wing and will test the scalability of the aerodynamic effects observed with larger delta wings. This is a considerable departure in overall wing size.
4.  **Testing zero `twist`:** The current successful designs heavily rely on aggressive washout. This new design will explore a delta wing with zero twist (`twist_root=0.0`, `twist_tip=0.0`). This will help determine if the benefits of washout are absolute, or if for certain combinations of sweep, dihedral, and chord, a simpler, untwisted wing can still perform well. This is a completely unexplored twist regime.
5.  **Re-evaluating symmetric airfoil with moderate thickness:** Design 4 (NACA 0006) performed respectably, showing symmetric airfoils are viable. However, its thickness was at the lower end. To complement the other extreme parameter choices, I will use a symmetric airfoil (NACA 00xx) but with a moderately increased thickness (`naca_t=12`), which is still well within the practical range but thicker than previous top symmetric designs. This investigates if a thicker, simpler airfoil can still be efficient under these extreme geometric conditions.
