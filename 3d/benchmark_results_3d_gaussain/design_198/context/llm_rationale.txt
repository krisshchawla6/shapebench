The goal is to generate a novel design by blending characteristics of complementary strong designs, specifically Design 3 (Rank 0, very high sweep, symmetric, very thin) and Design 4 (Rank 1, slightly lower sweep, cambered, thin).

*   **Hybrid Sweep Exploration:** Design 3 has a sweep of 77.8 degrees, and Design 4 has 71.2 degrees. I will interpolate these values to 74.5 degrees. This maintains a high sweep to leverage vortex lift, a key feature of the top delta wing designs, while exploring a middle ground that might offer a broader operating envelope or different lift-to-drag characteristics for a cambered airfoil.
*   **Consistent Strong Washout and Anhedral:** Both Design 3 and Design 4 feature maximum anhedral (-15.0 deg) and very strong washout (twist_root and twist_tip close to -10.0 deg). This is a strong pattern among high-reward designs, suggesting these parameters are critical for optimizing lift distribution and stability. Therefore, I will set both `twist_root` and `twist_tip` to -10.0 degrees and `dihedral` to -15.0 degrees.
*   **Interpolated Root Chord:** Design 3 has a smaller root chord (27.28 in) and Design 4 a larger one (32.88 in). To explore a balanced size, I will set `root_chord_in` to 30.0 inches, which is an interpolation between these values, providing a moderate-sized wing.
*   **Cambered and Ultra-Thin Airfoil:** Design 3 uses a NACA 0006 (symmetric, very thin), while Design 4 uses a NACA 2407 (cambered, thin). My hybrid strategy involves combining the *camber* from Design 4 with the *extreme thinness* observed in Design 3 for optimal drag reduction. I will select a NACA 2406 airfoil (naca_m=2, naca_p=4, naca_t=6). This provides inherent camber for improved lift at specific angles of attack, while the minimal thickness (6%) aims to reduce profile drag, a potential improvement avenue over Design 4's slightly thicker NACA 2407.
