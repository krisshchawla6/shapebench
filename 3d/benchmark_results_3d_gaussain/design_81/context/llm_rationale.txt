This new design focuses on the "Exploration Strategy" by deliberately moving away from the clusters of parameters found in the high-reward designs, aiming to discover entirely new optimal regions.

*   **Explore lower sweep angles:** The current high-reward designs cluster around 70-71 degrees. This design will explore a significantly lower sweep angle of 58.0 degrees. This region is largely uncharted by the current population and could reveal designs that prioritize aspect ratio benefits or different flow characteristics over strong vortex lift.
*   **Investigate a different twist distribution:** Instead of the strong, uniform washout (-10/-10) seen in top designs, this design will use a moderate wash-in at the root (twist_root=2.0) and a very slight washout at the tip (twist_tip=-2.0). This unconventional profile aims to redistribute lift towards the root, potentially reducing induced drag at the tips and exploring a new load management strategy.
*   **Test a positive dihedral:** All top designs feature significant anhedral (-15.0 deg). This design will employ a moderate positive dihedral of 8.0 degrees. This is a completely opposite stability characteristic and, when combined with lower sweep and the new twist profile, could lead to novel performance outcomes and understand the trade-offs.
*   **Utilize higher NACA camber and thickness:** The existing population mainly uses NACA 0006, 2407, 2408, and 2411. This design will use a NACA 4420 airfoil (4% camber, max camber at 40% chord, 20% thickness). This significantly higher camber and thickness are largely unexplored and could provide higher lift coefficients or structural advantages, while its impact on induced drag needs to be evaluated in this new geometric context.
*   **Explore a larger root chord:** The successful designs mostly use root chords around 30 inches. This design will use a larger root chord of 45.0 inches. This will increase the overall scale and potentially affect Reynolds number and aspect ratio characteristics, complementing the lower sweep and higher thickness.
