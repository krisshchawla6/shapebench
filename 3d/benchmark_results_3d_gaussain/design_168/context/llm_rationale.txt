*   **Hybrid Strategy Application:** This new design will blend key successful characteristics from Design 1 (highest reward, very high sweep) and Design 3 (second highest reward, maximal washout, cambered airfoil) to generate a novel configuration that integrates their complementary strengths.
*   **Optimized Sweep for Vortex Lift:** We will select a high `le_sweep` of 75.5 degrees. This value is an interpolation between Design 1's extreme sweep (77.84) and Design 3's high sweep (71.24), aiming to harness strong leading-edge vortices while potentially balancing induced drag effects.
*   **Maximal Washout for Load Distribution:** Both `twist_root` and `twist_tip` will be set to their maximum negative value of -10.0 degrees, directly adopting the approach from Design 3 and reinforcing the strong washout observed in Design 1. This is critical for improving spanwise load distribution and delaying tip-related separation.
*   **Extreme Negative Dihedral Consistency:** The `dihedral` will be kept at -15.0 degrees. This parameter consistently appears at its maximum negative limit in the highest-rewarding designs (Design 1, 3, 4, 5), indicating its strong positive correlation with CL/CDi for these delta wing configurations.
*   **Cambered Airfoil for Enhanced Lift:** We will utilize a NACA 2407 airfoil (`naca_m=2`, `naca_p=4`, `naca_t=7`). This choice is inspired by Designs 2 and 3, which successfully employed thin cambered profiles. The camber is expected to provide additional lift efficiency compared to a symmetric profile, complementing the vortex lift.
*   **Root Chord Exploration:** A `root_chord_in` of 31.0 inches is chosen. This value lies between Design 1's (27.28) and Design 3's (32.88), allowing for exploration of wing scale effects within the successful range of the current population.
