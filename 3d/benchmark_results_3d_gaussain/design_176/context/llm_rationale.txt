*   The current top designs exhibit a strong convergence towards very high sweep (70-77 deg), significant washout (twist_root/tip around -10 deg), maximum negative dihedral (-15 deg), and thin symmetric or mildly cambered airfoils (NACA 00xx/24xx with t=6-8). This indicates a successful but narrow region of the design space.
*   To promote diversity and bold exploration, I will design a wing that deviates significantly from these converged parameters, exploring a different aerodynamic strategy rather than incrementally tweaking existing successful designs.
*   Specifically, I will explore a *mid-range sweep* (65 deg) which could balance vortex lift with a potentially higher effective aspect ratio, reducing induced drag compared to extreme sweeps, and possibly synergize with added camber.
*   I will drastically alter the twist distribution, using a *positive root twist* (wash-in) and a *mild washout at the tip*. This challenges the strong washout trend and aims to redistribute lift differently across the span, potentially delaying tip stall or improving overall lift efficiency.
*   I will also challenge the persistent negative dihedral trend by specifying a *positive dihedral* value. This explores a completely different lateral stability characteristic, whose effect on CL/CDi needs to be evaluated.
*   To complement these changes, I will incorporate a *highly cambered airfoil* (NACA 44xx) with a *moderate thickness* (naca_t=12). This aims to generate more inherent lift, which might be particularly effective with the lower sweep and altered twist distribution.
*   Finally, I will use a *larger root chord* (40 inches) to explore the effect of increased overall wing area and scale in this new design configuration.
