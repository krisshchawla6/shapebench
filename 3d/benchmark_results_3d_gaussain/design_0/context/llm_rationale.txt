As this is the initial design generation and no prior data or images are available, the strategy focuses purely on *exploration* by testing a bold combination of parameters to map out the design space. The aim is to generate a distinct design that can provide valuable information about the impact of specific parameter choices on CL/CDi.

*   **Explore High Sweep for Vortex Lift:** A high leading-edge sweep (75.0 deg) will be chosen to investigate the benefits and challenges of strong vortex lift dominance, particularly relevant for delta wings. This pushes towards the upper end of the sweep range.
*   **Investigate Significant Washout:** By setting `twist_root` to a positive value (5.0 deg) and `twist_tip` to a negative value (-5.0 deg), a substantial washout condition is created. This aims to control tip stall, distribute lift more effectively towards the root, and understand its impact on induced drag and overall lift distribution in a high-sweep configuration.
*   **Test Maximum Camber with Thin Airfoil:** Choosing the maximum camber (`naca_m = 4`, `naca_p = 4`) combined with a relatively thin airfoil (`naca_t = 8`) will explore the upper limits of camber's effect on lift augmentation and pitching moment, while attempting to mitigate associated profile drag penalties from thickness.
*   **Probe Anhedral Effects:** A distinct anhedral configuration (`dihedral = -10.0 deg`) is selected. This explores a less common but potentially interesting lateral stability characteristic, contrasting with the more typical positive dihedral or flat wings.
*   **Provide a Substantial Scale:** A relatively large root chord (45.0 inches) ensures the design has a significant planform area, allowing for a clearer assessment of its aerodynamic characteristics without being overly constrained by small-scale effects in initial simulations.
