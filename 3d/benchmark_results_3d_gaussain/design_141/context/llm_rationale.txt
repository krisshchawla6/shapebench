*   **Pushing Sweep Boundaries**: The top designs (e.g., Design 1, 2) have sweep angles around 70-71 degrees. To explore the limits of vortex lift and identify potential optima, I will choose a significantly higher sweep angle (e.g., 78.5 degrees), pushing towards the upper end of the allowed range. This aims to generate even more powerful leading-edge vortices.
*   **Targeted Washout Distribution**: Design 1 featured uniform maximum washout. I will explore a differentiated washout profile, setting maximum washout at the root (`twist_root=-10.0`) but a more moderate negative twist at the tip (`twist_tip=-5.0`). This could help manage the load distribution more efficiently, potentially reducing induced drag by shedding load towards the root while still benefiting from tip washout.
*   **Exploring No Camber**: The current top designs use NACA 2407/2408. To understand the isolated effect of extreme sweep, aggressive twist, and anhedral on performance, I will select a NACA 00xx profile. This removes the inherent lift and pitching moment from camber, allowing us to see how well vortex lift alone can perform under these conditions.
*   **Maintaining Strong Anhedral**: The consistent presence of strong anhedral (e.g., -15.0 degrees) in the highest-reward designs suggests it is a beneficial feature for highly swept delta wings, likely related to vortex dynamics or lateral stability. I will maintain this extreme anhedral to leverage its observed advantages.
*   **Moderate Thickness**: With a NACA 00xx profile, a moderate thickness (e.g., 10%) provides a balance between structural integrity and aerodynamic efficiency, avoiding excessive drag from a very thick section while not being overly thin.
*   **Root Chord Consistency**: The root chord values of top designs are in the 30-34 inch range. I will select a value within this range (30 inches) to keep the wing size comparable and focus the exploration on other geometric parameters.
