The primary strategy is **Exploration**, focusing on pushing parameters to their boundaries or investigating underexplored regions to uncover novel high-performing designs or expand our understanding of the design space.

*   **Push `le_sweep` to the absolute maximum (80.0 deg):** Design 1's high reward strongly correlates with its high sweep. By setting `le_sweep` to its absolute maximum, we test the extreme boundary of this parameter, aiming to maximize vortex lift generation, a characteristic clearly beneficial in previous high-reward designs.
*   **Explore the opposite extreme of `dihedral` (+15.0 deg):** All top-performing designs used the maximum negative dihedral (-15.0 deg). To truly explore the design space and understand the impact of this parameter, we must test the opposite boundary (+15.0 deg, positive dihedral/tips up). This is a significant deviation and represents a highly underexplored region relative to the successful designs.
*   **Investigate maximum `naca_t` (24):** The current high-reward designs rely on very thin airfoils (NACA XX06, XX08). The parameter space for thickness up to NACA XX24 is virtually unexplored. While thicker airfoils generally increase drag, their interaction with extreme sweep, high camber, and different dihedral could yield unexpected results or highlight design limitations. This represents a bold exploration into a completely new region.
*   **Utilize maximum `root_chord_in` (50.0 inches):** Most previous designs have a moderate root chord (27-36 inches). Exploring the maximum root chord will significantly increase the wing's overall scale and surface area. This could increase total lift, but also induced drag. It tests the scalability of the high-sweep, high-thickness configuration.
*   **Combine high camber (`naca_m=4`, `naca_p=4`) with extreme sweep and thickness:** NACA 44xx has only been explored once (Design 2) with low sweep and resulted in low reward. Pairing it with max sweep (80.0) and max thickness (24) is a novel combination that explores whether higher camber can contribute positively in an extremely swept and thick configuration, or if it further penalizes performance.
*   **Maintain strong washout at the tip, but vary the root twist slightly (`twist_root=-7.0`, `twist_tip=-10.0`):** Strong washout at the tip (`twist_tip=-10.0`) has been consistent in high-reward designs, likely aiding lift distribution. Varying the root twist slightly (`-7.0`) allows for a nuanced exploration of the twist distribution in conjunction with the other extreme parameters, rather than simply replicating previous best settings.
