*   **Explore positive dihedral:** All top designs feature maximum anhedral (-15.0 deg). This new design will aggressively explore the opposite boundary by setting `dihedral` to its maximum positive value of `15.0` degrees. This is a significantly underexplored region and could reveal a completely different stable aerodynamic configuration.
*   **Investigate moderate sweep:** The current population heavily favors high sweep angles (70-78 deg). To diversify the search and test different aerodynamic strategies, I will set `le_sweep` to a moderate value of `58.0` degrees. This explores the lower-middle range, which is less explored and might lead to designs with different drag components or lift generation mechanisms.
*   **Test a significantly thicker airfoil:** The parameter `naca_t` (thickness) is concentrated at the lower end (6-12%) across the population. This design will explore the upper-middle range with `naca_t = 18`. A thicker airfoil may offer different structural benefits and could impact profile drag and lift generation in unforeseen ways when combined with other novel parameters.
*   **Uniform moderate washout:** While high washout is common, I will use a moderate, uniform washout (`twist_root = -5.0`, `twist_tip = -5.0`). This explores a simpler twist distribution than some highly differential twists, in combination with the radical changes in dihedral and thickness.
*   **Larger root chord:** To complement the thicker airfoil and moderate sweep, and explore a different scale from the current top designs, I will set `root_chord_in` to a larger value of `45.0` inches, exploring the upper end of its range.
*   **Modest camber:** To provide some inherent lift without relying solely on geometric angle of attack or vortex lift, I will include modest camber with `naca_m = 2` and `naca_p = 4`, which has shown some success in the existing population, but will be tested in a very different geometric context.
