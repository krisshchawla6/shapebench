2.  **Significant Washout (Negative Twist):** All top designs feature substantial negative `twist_root` and `twist_tip` values, often at or near the maximum -10.0 degrees. This strongly implies that tailoring the load distribution to reduce induced drag is crucial. Design 1 (highest reward) had slightly less negative twist at the tip than the root (`-9.13` vs `-9.55`), while Design 2 had both at `-10.0`.
3.  **Maximum Negative Dihedral:** The three highest-ranked designs all have `dihedral` at the maximum negative value of -15.0 degrees. This suggests a strong benefit for CL/CDi, potentially by influencing vortex formation or stability.

In contrast, designs using cambered airfoils (`NACA 24xx`, `NACA 44xx`) or thicker airfoils generally yield lower rewards (e.g., Design 4, 5). This indicates that camber might increase drag disproportionately for this objective, and thin airfoils minimize profile drag.
