*   **Aggressively explore lower sweep angles**: The current top designs (Rank 0, 1, 2) are all within the 69-78 degree sweep range. To effectively explore the design space and discover novel solutions, this design will target a significantly lower sweep angle, e.g., around 52.5 degrees. This challenges the assumption that only high sweep generates high CL/CDi and may uncover different aerodynamic mechanisms at play.
*   **Investigate extreme positive dihedral (+15.0°)**: All the high-ranking designs exhibit strong anhedral (negative dihedral), with the best design at the maximum of -15.0 degrees. This design will explore the complete opposite extreme: maximum positive dihedral (+15.0 degrees). This is a highly underexplored region that will test the sensitivity of performance to lateral stability and how it interacts with lift-generating vortices at lower sweep.
*   **Maintain strong tip washout with moderate root twist**: The consistent presence of strong negative twist at the tip (near -10.0 degrees) in high-reward designs indicates its importance for efficient load distribution. This design will retain the maximum tip washout (-10.0 degrees) but explore a moderate negative twist at the root (-6.0 degrees) to observe the effect of a differential twist distribution in this novel parameter combination.
*   **Test a very thin cambered airfoil (NACA 2407)**: While the best design uses a NACA 0006, designs with NACA 24xx profiles also performed well, albeit slightly lower. To combine camber's potential lift benefits with minimal drag, we will use a NACA 2407 profile. This combines a moderate camber (m=2) and position (p=4) with very low thickness (t=7), hoping to optimize for lift while keeping drag in check, especially with the lower sweep where camber might play a different role.
*   **Explore a smaller root chord (16.5 inches)**: The current successful designs have root chords in the mid-range (27-33 inches). To diversify the population, this design will utilize a significantly smaller root chord (16.5 inches). This will explore how overall wing scale and aspect ratio interact with the selected lower sweep, extreme positive dihedral, and cambered airfoil, potentially leading to different Reynolds number effects.
