*   **Hybridizing Top Performers (Design 1 & 2) with High Sweep Exploration (Design 3):** The goal is to blend the high reward characteristics of Design 1 (aggressive washout, anhedral, cambered airfoil) and Design 2 (slightly less root washout, good performance) with the high sweep potential observed in Design 3.
*   **Maximize Vortex Lift with High Sweep**: Design 3 has the highest sweep (76.6 degrees). We will push `le_sweep` higher than Design 1 (71.2 deg) to 75.0 degrees, closer to Design 3, to potentially enhance leading-edge vortex strength and lift, a key characteristic of efficient delta wings.
*   **Optimizing Twist for Load Distribution**: We will blend the aggressive `twist_tip` (-10.0) from Design 1 and 2 with a slightly less extreme `twist_root` (-8.0) compared to Design 1 (-10.0), similar to the idea in Design 2 (-6.1). This aims for effective spanwise load distribution, ensuring efficient lift generation without excessive root unloading.
*   **Strong, but Moderated, Anhedral**: Both Design 1 and 3 feature max anhedral (-15.0). Design 2 uses -11.6. We will retain a strong anhedral value, -14.0 degrees, to benefit from its positive impact on stability and potential vortex interaction, but slightly less extreme than the absolute maximum, allowing for subtle exploration.
*   **Exploring Higher Camber with Moderate Thickness**: The top designs utilize NACA 24xx (2% camber). We will introduce a significantly higher camber `naca_m=4`, `naca_p=4` (NACA 44xx profile) with a moderate thickness `naca_t=10`. This is a novel exploration into more aggressively cambered airfoils for delta wings, aiming to provide greater lift for a given sweep and potentially improve the CL/CDi ratio by shifting the lift curve.
*   **Increased Root Chord for Scale**: To complement the increased sweep and maintain a healthy wing area, we will use a `root_chord_in` of 35.0 inches, slightly larger than the previous top designs. This helps ensure sufficient overall lift capability with the higher sweep.
