*   **Apply Exploration Strategy: Target lower sweep for a different lift paradigm.** Most top designs are in the 70-75 degree sweep range. To explore the design space effectively and find novel solutions, I will significantly reduce `le_sweep` to the lower end (around 50-55 degrees). This will transition from predominantly vortex-lift-dependent designs to those where conventional lift plays a more significant role, potentially revealing new high CL/CDi regions.
*   **Explore completely untried dihedral direction: Positive dihedral.** All high-reward designs feature strong negative dihedral (anhedral). I will introduce a substantial positive `dihedral` (e.g., +12.5 degrees). This represents a bold exploration into an entirely opposite stability configuration, which might offer unexpected aerodynamic benefits despite being counter-intuitive for swept wings in some contexts.
*   **Novel NACA camber distribution: `naca_m` with `naca_p=0`.** The parameter `naca_p=0` has only been used with `naca_m=0` (symmetric airfoils). I will test `naca_m=2` with `naca_p=0` (NACA 20xx). This implies the maximum camber is located at the leading edge, which is a highly unconventional and unexplored airfoil shape for this context, potentially offering unique flow control or leading-edge suction effects for a lower-swept wing.
*   **Experiment with positive root twist:** While strong washout is prevalent, the `twist_root` range allows for positive values (washin). To further differentiate this design and explore the load distribution capabilities for a lower-sweep, positive-dihedral wing, I will set `twist_root` to a moderately positive value (e.g., +5 degrees) while maintaining moderate `twist_tip` washout (-5 degrees). This creates a unique spanwise load distribution.
*   **Maintain moderate root chord and thickness:** The `root_chord_in` of ~30-37 inches appears robust across various designs. I'll maintain it in this range (e.g., 30 inches). For `naca_t`, I will choose a medium thickness (e.g., 14), which is thicker than the top designs but not at the extreme upper limit, providing a balance for the lower sweep and exploratory airfoil.
