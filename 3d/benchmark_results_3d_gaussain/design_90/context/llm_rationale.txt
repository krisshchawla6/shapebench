*   **Explore lower sweep regime**: The current high-reward designs predominantly feature sweep angles around 70-71 degrees. I will significantly lower the sweep to 55.0 degrees to explore a new aerodynamic regime where vortex lift might be less dominant, allowing for different parameter interactions to become effective and promote population diversity.
*   **Investigate positive dihedral**: All the highest-reward designs (1, 2, 5) have significantly negative dihedral. To challenge this pattern and explore different stability and lift distribution characteristics, I will set a positive dihedral of 10.0 degrees.
*   **Introduce positive root twist with tip washout**: Instead of uniform heavy washout, I will apply a positive twist at the root (5.0 degrees) transitioning to a negative twist at the tip (-5.0 degrees). This aims to redistribute lift more towards the root while still providing some tip stall mitigation, a departure from the current maximum washout trend.
*   **Utilize higher camber airfoil**: The best designs used NACA 24xx or 00xx. I will incorporate a NACA 4410 airfoil to assess the impact of significantly higher camber (4% camber) on lift generation and CL/CDi ratio, especially in conjunction with a lower sweep and different twist/dihedral.
*   **Moderate root chord**: A root chord of 35.0 inches is chosen to be within the effective range observed in the previous designs, providing a well-proportioned wing for evaluating the new combination of parameters without scaling issues.
