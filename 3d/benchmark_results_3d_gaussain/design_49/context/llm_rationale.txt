*   **Exploiting Maximum Washout**: The top-performing design (Design 1) shows maximum negative twist at both root and tip. To fully exploit this, the new design will also set both `twist_root` and `twist_tip` to their maximum negative value of -10.0 degrees, aiming to optimize the spanwise lift distribution and vortex stability for maximum CL/CDi.
*   **Refining High Sweep**: The current high-reward designs cluster around 70-71 degrees sweep. I will push `le_sweep` slightly higher to 72.5 degrees. This aims to further enhance vortex lift while staying within a proven range that avoids excessive induced drag penalties.
*   **Leveraging Extreme Anhedral**: Design 1, the best performer, utilizes the maximum negative dihedral of -15.0 degrees. This strong anhedral appears to be a key characteristic for high performance. The new design will adopt this maximum negative dihedral to maintain any associated benefits to stability or drag.
*   **Optimal Airfoil Profile**: The NACA 2407 airfoil (2% camber at 40% chord, 7% thickness) was present in the top-reward design. This airfoil appears to offer an excellent balance of lift and low drag for this wing configuration. The new design will retain these NACA parameters (`naca_m=2`, `naca_p=4`, `naca_t=7`).
*   **Consistent Root Chord**: The most successful designs have `root_chord_in` around 32-33 inches. A value of 33.0 inches will be maintained, as it falls within this range and seems to provide an appropriate scale for the wing's overall geometry.
