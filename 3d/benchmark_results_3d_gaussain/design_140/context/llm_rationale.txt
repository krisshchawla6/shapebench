*   **Moderate High Sweep for Vortex Lift and Reduced Drag**: The top designs benefit significantly from vortex lift, enabled by high sweep. I will maintain a high sweep, but slightly less aggressive than Design 1 (e.g., 71.24 deg), choosing a value around 69.0 degrees. This aims to retain strong vortex generation while potentially reducing overall wetted area and form drag associated with extremely high sweep, thereby improving CL/CDi.
*   **Differential Washout Strategy**: Design 1's uniform extreme washout (-10 deg root and tip) is a strong signal. However, I hypothesize that a *differential* washout, with more washout at the tip than the root, could lead to a more aerodynamically efficient lift distribution, delaying flow separation at the tips and reducing induced drag more effectively. I will set the root twist to a moderate washout (e.g., -4.0 deg) and the tip twist to a more aggressive washout (e.g., -9.0 deg).
*   **Symmetric Airfoil for Reduced Profile Drag**: Since NACA 00xx designs also perform well (Designs 4, 6), I will explore a symmetric airfoil (NACA 00xx). This eliminates the inherent lift and pitching moment of camber, reducing potential parasitic drag associated with camber, and allowing the primary lift generation to come from the leading-edge vortices and angle of attack, in conjunction with the optimized twist.
*   **Optimized Thickness**: Given the range of thickness (6-24) and the prevalence of thinner airfoils in high-reward designs (NACA 0006, 0008, 2407), I will select a moderate thickness for the symmetric airfoil (e.g., 10%). This aims for a balance between low form drag and robust flow characteristics.
*   **Significant Anhedral for Lateral Stability/Vortex Control**: The consistent presence of strong anhedral (-11 to -15 deg) in top designs is a clear trend. I will maintain a significant, but not maximum, anhedral (e.g., -12.0 deg) to capitalize on its benefits, whether for lateral stability or favorable interaction with the leading-edge vortices.
*   **Root Chord in Established Range**: The successful designs have root chords between 30 and 37 inches. I will select a root chord within this effective range, specifically 33.5 inches, to ensure a reasonable scale for the wing without excessive wetted area.
