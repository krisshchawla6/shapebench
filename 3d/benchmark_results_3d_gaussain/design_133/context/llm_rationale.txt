The current population shows a strong convergence towards high sweep, aggressive washout, negative dihedral, and thin airfoils for high reward. My strategy will be to **explore under-represented regions and boundary conditions** to diversify the population and potentially uncover alternative high-performing designs, even if they challenge the current patterns.

*   **Explore Extreme Sweep (Upper Boundary):** The current best designs are around 70-76 degrees. I will push the leading-edge sweep to its maximum of 80.0 degrees. This aims to maximize the potential for stable vortex lift, though it could also increase induced drag, providing data on this trade-off at the extreme.
*   **Introduce Positive Twist (Washin at Root):** All top designs use heavy washout. I will introduce positive twist (washin) at the root (e.g., `twist_root=5.0`) while maintaining moderate washout at the tip (`twist_tip=-5.0`). This explores a completely different load distribution strategy, which might improve performance by better utilizing the inner wing or managing stall differently.
*   **Explore Positive Dihedral:** All top designs feature negative dihedral. I will set dihedral to a positive value (e.g., `dihedral=10.0`). While primarily a stability parameter, its interaction with high sweep and the new twist distribution is unknown and needs exploration.
*   **Test Higher Camber and Thickness:** The current successful NACA airfoils are 00xx or 24xx (lower camber) and are relatively thin (06-08). I will explore the NACA 44xx series (`naca_m=4, naca_p=4`) which features more camber, and a thicker airfoil (`naca_t=15`) to see if increased lift from camber and structural depth can be beneficial, despite potential drag penalties.
*   **Vary Root Chord (Moderate-Low):** The previous designs are in the 27-33 inch range. I will choose a slightly smaller, but still moderate, root chord of 25.0 inches to see how the overall scale interacts with these aggressive geometric changes.
