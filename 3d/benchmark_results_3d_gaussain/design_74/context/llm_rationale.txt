*   **Leverage Proven Traits:** The highest-reward designs consistently feature maximum negative twist at both root and tip (washout) and maximum negative dihedral (anhedral). This combination strongly suggests these parameters are critical for optimal lift distribution and stability in this simulation environment. I will retain these settings for their demonstrated efficacy.
*   **Explore Sweep for Vortex Optimization:** The best sweep values were around 70-71 degrees. I will slightly increase the sweep to 73 degrees. This maintains a high sweep angle to encourage strong leading-edge vortices while exploring if a slightly higher sweep in this advantageous range can further optimize the balance between vortex lift and induced drag without overshooting the optimum observed in lower-ranked designs (e.g., 80 degrees).
*   **Investigate Stronger Camber:** Previous high-reward designs utilized NACA 24xx airfoils. To creatively explore new performance peaks, I will introduce a more aggressive camber profile with `naca_m=4` (4% max camber). This is a significant departure from previous top designs and aims to generate higher inherent lift.
*   **Test Moderate Thickness with Stronger Camber:** The previous successful designs used relatively thin airfoils (NACA 0006, 2407, 2408). With the introduction of stronger camber (`naca_m=4`), I will also increase the thickness slightly to `naca_t=12`. This novel combination will test whether a thicker, more cambered wing can sustain robust vortex structures and generate more lift, or if drag penalties will dominate.
*   **Maintain Representative Chord:** The top designs generally had root chords between 28 and 33 inches. I will choose a root chord of 35.0 inches, slightly larger than previous top performers, to explore if a larger scale might enhance the overall lift capabilities with the new airfoil profile. This also pushes the boundary of the 'optimal' range slightly.
*   **Novelty and Exploration:** This design strategy combines established high-performing parameters (twist, dihedral) with a deliberate exploration of new, more aggressive profiles for camber, thickness, and slightly higher sweep/chord. This aims for significant novelty while being informed by existing trends, rather than simply perturbing an existing solution.
