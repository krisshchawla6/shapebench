The top-ranked design (Design 1, Reward: 424.5627) exhibits a set of extreme parameters that yielded superior performance. Key characteristics include:
- **High `le_sweep` (~71.2 deg):** Essential for generating strong leading-edge vortices, a primary lift mechanism for delta wings.
- **Max negative `twist_root` and `twist_tip` (-10.0 deg each):** This implies a uniform, strong negative incidence across the entire wing. This is an unusual configuration but appears to be highly effective, potentially by pitching the entire wing down significantly, or by creating a highly stable vortex structure at a higher angle of attack (relative to the wing's mean chord). The high reward suggests this unique twist distribution manages to maintain lift while drastically reducing induced drag.
- **Max negative `dihedral` (-15.0 deg, strong anhedral):** Consistently present in top-performing designs. Anhedral can enhance lateral stability, particularly at high angles of attack, and might influence the vortex system favorably.
- **Cambered and thin airfoil (NACA 2407):** The NACA 24xx series (cambered, max camber at 40% chord) is prevalent in high-reward designs, and thinner airfoils (like 07 or 08) generally perform better than thicker ones. Symmetric airfoils (NACA 00xx) appear to yield much lower rewards (e.g., Design 5).

Aerodynamic issues observed in lower-performing designs, when interpreted against the top designs, suggest:
- **Insufficient vortex lift:** Likely due to lower sweep angles or poor twist distribution.
- **Suboptimal load distribution:** Designs with less aggressive twist or anhedral appear to suffer.
- **Higher induced drag:** Possibly from less stable or less effective vortex structures.

**Key design changes needed to improve designs in the population:**

1.  **Maintain high leading-edge sweep:** Critical for robust vortex generation.
2.  **Optimize twist distribution:** The uniform aggressive negative twist of D1 is a breakthrough. Further exploration around this concept, or combining it with strong tip washout gradients, is needed.
3.  **Prioritize strong anhedral:** Max anhedral (-15.0 deg) seems consistently beneficial.
4.  **Confirm cambered, thin airfoils:** NACA 24xx with low thickness is a strong candidate.
