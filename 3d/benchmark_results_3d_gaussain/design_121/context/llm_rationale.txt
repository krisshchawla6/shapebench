*   The top-ranked designs consistently utilize aggressive washout (twist_root and twist_tip at or near -10.0 deg) and maximum negative dihedral (-15.0 deg). This combination strongly suggests these parameters are critical for achieving high CL/CDi in delta wings, likely by optimizing lift distribution and stability. I will retain these settings.
*   Cambered airfoils (`NACA 24xx`) generally outperformed symmetric ones. Design 5, using `NACA 4412` (higher camber, `naca_m=4`), performed poorly, but its twist and dihedral were less aggressive. This presents an opportunity for bold exploration: combine the higher camber (`naca_m=4`) with the proven aggressive twist and dihedral to see if its potential benefits can be unlocked.
*   While `NACA 2407` was optimal, I want to explore the higher `naca_m=4` (max camber) with `naca_p=4` (camber at 40% chord). To counteract potential drag increases from higher camber, I will experiment with a slightly thinner airfoil (`naca_t=9`), which is still thicker than the thinnest (6) but thinner than the previously used `NACA 4412`.
*   Leading-edge sweep in the range of 70-76 degrees has shown good performance. I will choose a value towards the higher end of this range (75.0 degrees) to maximize the potential for strong vortex lift, pairing it with the aggressive washout.
*   Root chord values between 27-35 inches have been common. I will choose a value slightly on the lower end of this range (28.0 inches) to maintain a relatively compact design, which could contribute to lower overall drag while allowing the optimized airfoil and twist to generate sufficient lift.
*   This design strategy focuses on a "high-camber, aggressive washout, thin profile" combination, which is a novel exploration given previous designs have not successfully combined high camber with the top-performing twist/dihedral.
