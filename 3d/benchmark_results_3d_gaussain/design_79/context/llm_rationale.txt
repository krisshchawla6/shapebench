1.  **Boldly explore lower sweep**: The current high-reward designs are tightly clustered around 70-71 degrees sweep. To promote diversity and discover potentially new optima, this design will target a significantly lower sweep angle (e.g., 55 degrees). This will shift the primary lift generation mechanisms and likely lead to a different optimal parameter set for other features.
2.  **Utilize high camber with lower sweep**: To compensate for the reduced vortex lift typically associated with lower sweep, this design will employ a NACA 44xx airfoil (NACA 4414). The increased camber (4%) aims to generate more lift at lower angles of attack, which could be beneficial for CL/CDi.
3.  **Implement strong twist gradient (zero root, max tip washout)**: Instead of uniform aggressive washout like Design 1, this design explores a strong twist gradient with zero twist at the root and maximum washout (-10.0 deg) at the tip. This configuration aims to maintain root lift while aggressively reducing tip loading, potentially mitigating induced drag for the lower sweep planform.
4.  **Explore moderate positive dihedral**: Breaking the strong trend of negative dihedral among top designs, this wing will feature a moderate positive dihedral (e.g., 5.0 degrees). This explores a less-trodden path in the design space and could reveal interesting stability or vortex interaction characteristics for a lower-sweep, cambered wing.
5.  **Increase root chord for scale**: With lower sweep, the effective aspect ratio might be higher, but overall wing area might decrease for a fixed chord. A slightly larger root chord (e.g., 40.0 inches) compared to the top designs will provide more surface area and volume, which could be advantageous for this configuration.
