**Critical assessment of Design 1 (le_sweep=71.2, twist_root=-10.0, twist_tip=-10.0, dihedral=-15.0, NACA 2407):**
This design leverages high sweep for strong vortex lift, which is characteristic of delta wings operating at higher angles of attack. The aggressive washout at both root and tip suggests an attempt to flatten the spanwise lift distribution, reduce induced drag, and delay separation, optimizing for the CL/CDi ratio. The maximum anhedral (-15.0 degrees) is likely employed to enhance stability and perhaps optimize vortex shedding characteristics for highly swept configurations. The NACA 2407 airfoil provides moderate camber for lift enhancement and a relatively thin profile, minimizing form drag. The high reward indicates that this combination of parameters effectively balances lift generation with induced drag reduction for this specific flight regime.

**Aerodynamic Issues/Observations from the current population trends:**
1.  **Clustering in high sweep**: Almost all top designs are concentrated around 70-71 degrees of sweep. This suggests that the optimizer has converged on a local optimum in this region.
2.  **Extreme washout**: Many high-reward designs employ maximum or near-maximum washout (-10 degrees). While effective, it's unclear if this is universally optimal or a consequence of the high sweep.
3.  **Predominant anhedral**: Negative dihedral (anhedral) is a strong trend among top performers. The effects of zero or positive dihedral in conjunction with other parameters are less explored in high-reward regions.
4.  **Camber exploration**: While NACA 24xx is common, NACA 0006 also performs well, indicating that camber might be context-dependent. Thicker airfoils (above 11%) are not well-represented in high-reward designs.

**Key design changes needed to improve designs in the population:**
1.  **Explore significantly lower sweep angles**: Investigate if a different aerodynamic strategy, less reliant on extreme vortex lift from very high sweep, can achieve high CL/CDi.
2.  **Systematically vary twist distribution**: Instead of uniform max washout, explore configurations with different root/tip twist combinations (e.g., zero root twist with max tip washout).
3.  **Investigate different camber and thickness combinations for new sweep regimes**: Try more aggressive camber or thicker airfoils in conjunction with lower sweep to see if they can compensate for reduced vortex lift.
4.  **Explore positive or neutral dihedral**: Break the trend of maximum anhedral to understand the dihedral's role outside the very high sweep regime.
