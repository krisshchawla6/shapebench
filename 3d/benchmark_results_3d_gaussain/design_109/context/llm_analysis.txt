Key changes needed to improve designs in the population by diversifying the search:
1.  **Explore the lower end of the `le_sweep` range (45-60 deg):** This region is largely unrepresented, especially in combination with other parameters.
2.  **Investigate positive `twist_root` and `twist_tip` (washin):** All high-reward designs use washout. Washin could drastically alter load distribution and vortex behavior.
3.  **Test positive `dihedral`:** The population is heavily biased towards anhedral. Positive dihedral represents a significant shift in lateral stability and effective angle of attack across the span.
4.  **Explore thicker `naca_t` airfoils:** Most designs are thin (6-11%). Testing thicker sections (e.g., 15-24%) could provide structural benefits and different aerodynamic characteristics, especially at lower sweeps.
