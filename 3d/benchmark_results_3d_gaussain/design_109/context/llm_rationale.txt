*   **Drastically reduce `le_sweep`:** The current population is heavily biased towards high sweep (70+ degrees). To explore a truly novel design space, we will set `le_sweep` to a much lower value (e.g., 48 degrees), approaching the minimum range. This will investigate a regime where vortex lift generation might be less dominant, and the interaction with other parameters could be fundamentally different.
*   **Introduce significant washin (positive twist):** All high-performing designs have used washout (negative twist). This design will actively explore washin by setting both `twist_root` and `twist_tip` to positive values (e.g., 8 degrees and 5 degrees, respectively). This is a completely unexplored strategy within the current population and could lead to unique load distributions.
*   **Apply positive dihedral:** High-performing designs exclusively utilize anhedral. To further diversify the search and explore new stability and aerodynamic interactions, `dihedral` will be set to a positive value (e.g., 10 degrees), which is also an under-explored region of the parameter space.
*   **Utilize a moderately thick, cambered airfoil:** While thin airfoils are common in the high-reward designs, we'll choose a moderately thicker airfoil (`naca_t=15`) than previously successful designs, paired with a standard camber profile (`naca_m=2, naca_p=4`). This aims to ensure sufficient lift generation while assessing the impact of increased thickness in this novel low-sweep, washin configuration.
*   **Increase `root_chord_in`:** Given the low sweep and the exploration of washin, a larger `root_chord_in` (e.g., 45 inches) will be used to ensure sufficient overall wing area and volume for potential lift generation, complementing the other radical parameter changes. This pushes the root chord towards the upper end of its range, another less explored region.
