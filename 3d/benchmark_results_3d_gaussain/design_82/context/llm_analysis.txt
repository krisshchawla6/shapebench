1.  **High Sweep Dominance**: The current population heavily favors high sweep angles (around 70-71 degrees), likely leveraging strong vortex lift. Design 5 at 80 degrees performed poorly, suggesting an upper limit, but the lower sweep range (45-60 degrees) is virtually unexplored.
2.  **Aggressive Washout and Anhedral**: Maximum negative twist and dihedral appear consistently in top designs. This indicates these parameters are crucial for optimizing load distribution and potentially enhancing vortex lift or stability in a way that minimizes induced drag for these high-sweep configurations.
3.  **Camber Importance**: NACA 24xx profiles (cambered) generally outperform NACA 00xx (symmetric) profiles (compare Design 1 vs. Design 3), highlighting the importance of camber for lift generation. However, higher camber values (NACA 4xxx) and different camber positions are less explored.
4.  **Limited Thickness and Chord Exploration**: Airfoil thickness is concentrated in the lower range (6-11%), and root chord has a relatively narrow range around 30-33 inches. The effects of significantly thicker airfoils or larger/smaller chords are not well understood within the existing population.
