*   The current population is heavily biased towards high sweep (~70 deg), maximum negative twist (washout), and maximum negative dihedral. This suggests an optimum exists in that region, but also means a large portion of the design space is unexplored. An exploration strategy mandates a significant departure.
*   The exploration strategy demands pushing boundaries. I will significantly decrease `le_sweep` to a much lower value (55.0 degrees) to investigate its impact on vortex formation and overall CL/CDi, moving away from the high-sweep vortex lift regime towards potentially more traditional aerodynamic lift.
*   All current designs use negative twist (washout). I will explore a positive `twist_root` (washin, 5.0 degrees) combined with a moderate `twist_tip` (washout, -5.0 degrees) to create a novel load distribution. This explores positive twist values and aims to understand the sensitivity of twist direction with a lower sweep.
*   All top designs feature negative dihedral. I will explore a moderate positive dihedral (10.0 degrees), as this region is entirely unexplored and could yield insights into lateral stability/performance tradeoffs when combined with lower sweep and a novel twist distribution.
*   The NACA `4xxx` series (higher camber) and thicker airfoils (`naca_t > 11`) are underrepresented. I will use `NACA 4420` to explore the impact of higher camber at the root and increased thickness (20% thickness) on overall performance, especially with a lower sweep wing where profile drag might become more dominant.
*   I will also explore a larger `root_chord_in` (45.0 inches) compared to the current population, to investigate scaling effects and the impact of a larger wing area with the chosen novel aerodynamic configuration.
