*   The highest performing designs (Rank 0 and 1) exhibit high leading edge sweep (around 70-72 degrees). To explore the boundaries of this advantageous trend and potentially discover even more efficient vortex lift generation, this new design will feature an **even higher sweep angle**, pushed closer to the upper limit of 80 degrees, specifically at 76.0 degrees.
*   Maximum washout (-10.0 degrees for both root and tip twist) and maximum anhedral (-15.0 degrees dihedral) are consistently observed in the top-ranked designs. This combination is crucial for optimizing lift distribution, preventing premature tip stall, and enhancing overall efficiency by potentially stabilizing vortex flow at high angles of attack. Therefore, these values will be maintained in the new design to leverage this established high-reward pattern.
*   While cambered airfoils (NACA 24xx) achieved the absolute highest reward, symmetric airfoils (NACA 00xx) also showed good performance (Rank 2). To explore a novel combination and minimize profile drag, this design will utilize a **symmetric airfoil (naca_m=0, naca_p=0)**.
*   The thinnest airfoils (NACA xx06, xx07) are associated with higher rewards. Combined with the symmetric profile choice, selecting the **thinnest possible NACA thickness (naca_t=6)** aims to reduce parasitic drag, allowing the extreme sweep and twist to dominate the aerodynamic benefits, potentially leading to a highly efficient design.
*   The optimal root chord in top designs is around 32-34 inches, with a slightly smaller one also performing well (Design 5 at 27.8 inches). For this new design, a **mid-range root chord of 30.0 inches** will be chosen. This value offers a good balance, slightly smaller than the highest performer, to explore if efficiency can be maintained or improved with a reduced wing area, complementing the aggressive aerodynamic shaping.
