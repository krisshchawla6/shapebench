*   **Diversify sweep angle:** The leading designs show high sweep (>70 degrees) is effective. To explore the design space more thoroughly and test different aerodynamic regimes, we will select a moderate sweep angle (e.g., 60.0 degrees). This still promotes vortex lift but allows for a larger span and potentially different load distributions compared to extremely swept wings.
*   **Introduce maximum camber:** The current top performer uses a symmetric NACA 0006 airfoil. To test a fundamentally different lift-generating strategy, this design will incorporate maximum camber (NACA 44xx). This explores if a moderately swept wing can benefit from camber-induced lift in addition to vortex lift, or if it interferes.
*   **Maintain aggressive washout and anhedral:** Given the consistent success of extreme washout (twist_root, twist_tip near -10 degrees) and maximum anhedral (dihedral -15.0 degrees) across the top designs, these parameters will be retained. This leverages a known beneficial characteristic while varying other primary parameters.
*   **Explore moderate airfoil thickness:** The top design uses a very thin airfoil (NACA 0006). To complement the maximum camber, a moderate thickness (e.g., NACA xx12) will be chosen. This explores the interaction between camber, thickness, and a more moderate sweep.
*   **Adjust root chord:** A slightly larger root chord will be chosen (e.g., 35.0 inches) to ensure sufficient wing area for the moderate sweep and to potentially scale up the total lift capability. This is within the range of top performers.
