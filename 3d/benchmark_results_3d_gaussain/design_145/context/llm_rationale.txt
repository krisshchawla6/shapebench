*   **Hybridize high sweep from Design 4 with camber from Design 1**: Design 4 achieved good performance with very high sweep (76.6 deg) and a symmetric airfoil, while Design 1 had the highest reward with slightly lower sweep (71.2 deg) and a cambered airfoil. This new design will target a sweep of 73.5 degrees, an intermediate value that still benefits from strong vortex lift but is slightly less extreme than D4, allowing more room for other aerodynamic benefits, combined with a cambered airfoil.
*   **Maintain maximum anhedral**: The consistent high performance of designs featuring -15.0 degrees dihedral (anhedral) in Designs 1, 3, and 4 indicates its strong positive impact on CL/CDi, likely through stability or favorable vortex interactions. This parameter will be kept at its maximum negative value.
*   **Experiment with a new cambered airfoil profile**: Designs 1 and 2 performed well with NACA 2407/2408. To explore the design space for camber more broadly, we will switch to a NACA 4407 airfoil. This still provides camber and maintains a thin profile (7% thickness) as seen in high-performing designs, but with a different camber distribution.
*   **Refine washout distribution**: Designs 1, 2, and 4 all featured strong washout. Design 1 had -10.0/-10.0, Design 2 had -6.1/-10.0, and Design 4 had -10.0/-6.2. To explore a slightly different load shedding strategy, this design will use a strong, but not maximal, root washout (-9.0 deg) combined with the proven maximal tip washout (-10.0 deg). This aims to achieve an optimized spanwise load distribution for lower induced drag.
*   **Keep root chord in the optimal range**: The top designs generally feature a moderate root chord between 30 and 33 inches. A value of 32.0 inches will be chosen to maintain a good wing size without excessive wetted area or structural demands.
