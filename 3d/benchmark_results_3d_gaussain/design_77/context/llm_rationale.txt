*   The top-performing designs consistently cluster around a leading-edge sweep of 70-71 degrees, a root chord of ~33 inches, strong anhedral, and aggressive tip washout. This suggests a locally optimized region, but also indicates significant unexplored territory.
*   To explore beyond this cluster, I will push the `le_sweep` to a much higher value (e.g., 78.0 degrees), close to the upper boundary. This will test the limits of vortex lift generation and the associated drag penalties at extremely high sweep angles, which is currently unrepresented by high-reward designs.
*   The current designs utilize a narrow range of `root_chord_in`. I will significantly increase the `root_chord_in` to 45.0 inches to explore the impact of larger planform area on CL/CDi, and how it interacts with the increased sweep. This provides a different scaling effect than previously observed.
*   Most high-performing designs feature uniformly negative twist or negative twist at both root and tip. I will implement a distinct twist strategy by setting a positive `twist_root` (e.g., 5.0 deg) while maintaining aggressive `twist_tip` washout (-10.0 deg). This creates a highly differentially twisted wing, aiming to redistribute lift further inboard while still shedding tip vortices effectively to reduce induced drag.
*   High-reward designs predominantly feature strong anhedral (`dihedral` between -11 and -15 deg). To understand the full design space and potentially discover new performance optima, I will explore the opposite extreme by setting a significant positive `dihedral` (e.g., 12.0 deg). This represents a bold departure from current trends.
*   The `NACA_m` values of 0 and 4 are less common among top designs than `m=2`. I will choose `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4` (NACA 44xx series) to investigate the effect of increased camber. Additionally, I will select a higher `naca_t` (e.g., 14), as thicker airfoils are largely unexplored in the current high-reward population, potentially offering different aerodynamic characteristics or structural advantages.
