The goal is to maximize CL/CDi. The current best design (Design 1) heavily relies on high sweep, extreme washout, and anhedral. My strategy is to explore a novel combination of these successful elements, pushing some parameters to new regions while refining others based on first principles and observed trends.

*   **Explore slightly higher sweep:** Design 1 had 71.24 degrees, and Design 6 (80 deg) performed poorly. I want to test the upper-middle range, specifically 75 degrees, to see if an optimal balance of vortex lift and manageable induced drag exists just above the current best. This avoids the pitfalls of extremely high sweep while still leveraging strong vortex generation.
*   **Implement a differentiated twist strategy:** Instead of uniform extreme washout (-10, -10 as in Design 1), I will use a moderate root washout (-5 degrees) and strong tip washout (-10 degrees). This aims to maintain more lift generation near the root while aggressively preventing tip stall and optimizing the spanwise load distribution for minimal induced drag. This is a novel twist profile in the top ranks.
*   **Moderate, yet significant, anhedral:** Design 1 used -15 degrees. I'll use -10 degrees. This maintains the roll stability benefits associated with anhedral seen in the higher-ranking designs but explores if the extreme value is truly necessary or if a slightly less aggressive dihedral offers better overall performance.
*   **Incorporate more aggressive camber:** Design 1 used NACA 2407. I will use NACA 4410 (4% camber, 40% position, 10% thickness). This increases the inherent lift component from the airfoil itself, aiming for higher CL. Combined with optimized sweep and twist, this could lead to a significant CL/CDi improvement. The slightly increased thickness (10% vs 7%) might slightly increase form drag but also offers more structural integrity and potentially better flow characteristics over the cambered profile.
*   **Optimize root chord:** Design 1 had 32.88 inches. I'll use 38.0 inches. This is slightly larger than the previous best, scaling the wing up a bit to potentially increase overall lift area, while staying within the effective range observed across the population (10-50 inches).
