This helps to reduce induced drag, prevent tip stall, and ensure a more stable aerodynamic center. Its presence in all high-reward designs underscores its importance.
*   **Anhedral:** The maximum anhedral (-15.0 degrees) is a strong and consistent feature in high-reward designs. For highly swept delta wings, anhedral can be used to tune lateral stability (reducing the inherent strong dihedral effect of sweep) or to influence the effective dihedral in ground effect or during maneuvers.

This suggests that for this high-sweep configuration, the benefits of vortex lift outweigh the need for airfoil-generated lift through camber, and any additional drag from camber would be detrimental to the CL/CDi ratio.
