*   **Apply Novel Design Strategy:** My goal is to explore a less-saturated part of the design space that still builds on the general principles observed in high-reward designs, rather than just incrementally modifying the best existing design. I aim for a distinct combination of features.
*   **Explore Slightly Lower Sweep for Balance:** While high sweep is dominant, I want to explore a design with a moderate-high sweep (e.g., 68.5 degrees). This is less extreme than Design 1, potentially reducing some induced drag components while still maintaining strong vortex lift characteristics, and offers a departure from the very high-sweep cluster.
*   **Maintain Strong Washout:** The pattern of strong negative twist (washout) is robust across high-reward designs. I will implement significant washout at both root and tip (`twist_root=-8.5`, `twist_tip=-9.8`) to ensure efficient load distribution and minimize induced drag, with a slightly more aggressive tip washout to further delay tip stall.
*   **Re-evaluate Cambered Airfoil Potential:** The top design uses NACA 0006. However, NACA 24xx also appears in good designs. I want to explore a more aggressive cambered airfoil (NACA 4409) with a moderate thickness (`naca_t=9`). This airfoil provides higher camber and positions it further forward, potentially generating more lift at lower angles of attack while still being relatively thin, and offers a direct contrast to the pure symmetric top performer.
*   **Explore Moderate Anhedral:** Instead of the maximum anhedral of -15 degrees, I will choose a value of -11.0 degrees. This continues the trend of anhedral but explores if a slightly less extreme value can provide an optimal balance between lateral stability characteristics and potential drag penalties, especially with a different sweep and airfoil.
*   **Utilize a Larger Root Chord:** I will select a larger root chord (`root_chord_in=38.0`) compared to Design 1 (27.28 in) and other top designs. This increases the overall wing area, which could lead to higher total lift at a given angle of attack, potentially improving CL/CDi if the drag increase is not proportional. This also explores a different scale factor for the wing.
