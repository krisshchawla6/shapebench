The current population exhibits a strong convergence towards high-sweep, washout-dominated, anhedral-heavy, thin-airfoil delta wings. While this combination yields high rewards, it represents a limited exploration of the vast parameter space. To follow the **Exploration Strategy**, I will generate a design that deliberately pushes parameters into underexplored regions, aiming to discover new optima or better understand the design landscape's sensitivity.

*   **Explore Low Sweep**: All top designs cluster in the 70-80 degree sweep range. To break this pattern and explore different aerodynamic regimes where vortex lift might be less dominant, I will set `le_sweep` to a much lower value, around 50 degrees. This tests a more conventional delta wing geometry.
*   **Introduce Positive Twist (Washin)**: All current designs feature strong negative twist (washout). This design will explore the opposite extreme with a moderate positive `twist_root` and a slightly positive `twist_tip`. This will investigate how washin affects the lift distribution and induced drag, especially in a lower-sweep configuration.
*   **Investigate Positive Dihedral**: Every high-performing design uses maximal anhedral (-15.0 deg). I will explore the opposite extreme by setting `dihedral` to a moderate positive value. This will test how positive dihedral (tips up) influences stability and aerodynamic performance, departing from the current pattern.
*   **Utilize Maximum Camber (NACA m=4)**: The `naca_m=4` parameter value has not been explored at all. Combining this with a lower sweep and washin could yield a wing that relies more on airfoil-generated lift rather than pure vortex lift, and explore a higher lift coefficient design. I will use `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4`.
*   **Moderate Thickness**: Given the shift to lower sweep and higher camber, a slightly thicker airfoil might be less penalizing than with high sweep, or even beneficial. I will choose a mid-range `naca_t` value (e.g., 16) to explore this region, as thinner airfoils (6-8) have dominated high rewards so far.
*   **Vary Root Chord**: Current designs are in the 27-33 inch range. I will choose a larger `root_chord_in` value, e.g., 40.0 inches, to explore the upper end of the scale for this novel configuration.
