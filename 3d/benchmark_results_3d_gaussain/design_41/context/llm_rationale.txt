*   **Exploitation of Top Performers:** The current strategy is to exploit the patterns observed in the highest-rewarding designs (Design 1: 424.5627, Design 2: 353.7832). Both exhibit a combination of high sweep, maximal negative twist, and significant negative dihedral.
*   **Optimal Sweep Consistency:** The sweep angle of ~70-71 degrees has proven highly effective. I will select `le_sweep=70.5` to stay within this proven high-performance band, slightly adjusting from Design 1's 71.2 to explore the peak within this narrow range.
*   **Maximized Washout for Efficiency:** The maximal negative twist (`twist_root=-10.0`, `twist_tip=-10.0`) in the top designs indicates a strong benefit for CL/CDi. This aggressive washout optimizes the spanwise lift distribution, reduces induced drag, and likely delays stall, leading to higher efficiency. I will replicate this feature.
*   **Reinforce Negative Dihedral:** The `dihedral=-15.0` in Design 1, and -11.6 in Design 2, suggests that a significant "tips down" configuration is advantageous. I will use `dihedral=-15.0` to maintain this successful characteristic, further reinforcing the established pattern.
*   **NACA Profile Refinement:** The NACA 2407 and 2408 profiles performed exceptionally. This confirms `naca_m=2` (2% max camber) and `naca_p=4` (camber at 40% chord) are effective for generating lift with low drag. I will explore a slightly increased thickness of `naca_t=9` to see if a marginal increase in structural depth can be tolerated or even beneficial without significantly compromising the aerodynamic efficiency, compared to 7% or 8%.
*   **Consistent Root Chord:** The `root_chord_in` values in the high-reward designs were consistently around 32-33 inches. I will keep it in this established range at `root_chord_in=32.5` to maintain the overall scale and aspect ratio characteristics that proved successful.
