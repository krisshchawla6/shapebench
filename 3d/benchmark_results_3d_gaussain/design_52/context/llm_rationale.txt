*   **Explore underexplored high sweep:** Designs 5 and 6, which tested higher sweep (75.67 and 78.69 degrees), yielded very poor rewards. This doesn't mean high sweep is inherently bad, but rather that the *combinations* of other parameters tested with them were not optimal. This new design will push `le_sweep` to a high value (e.g., 76 degrees) to specifically explore if high sweep can be made to work when paired with parameters that were successful at lower sweeps.
*   **Test NACA 00xx (no camber):** None of the previous designs have explored airfoils with zero camber (`naca_m=0`, `naca_p=0`). This is a significant unexplored region. This design will use a NACA 00xx airfoil to determine if camber is essential for high CL/CDi in a delta wing or if optimal vortex lift and twist can compensate.
*   **Reinforce strong tip washout and negative dihedral:** To give the high sweep and no-camber airfoil the best chance, `twist_tip` will be set to its maximum washout (-10.0 deg) and `dihedral` to its maximum negative value (-15.0 deg). These parameters showed strong correlation with high rewards in the successful designs (2 & 3), and will serve as a strong baseline to isolate the effects of sweep and camber.
*   **Explore slightly larger root chord:** The top designs clustered around 32-33 inches. This design will explore a slightly larger `root_chord_in` (e.g., 40 inches) to test if a larger scale, while maintaining aero-optimised proportions, impacts the reward positively or negatively, expanding the search in this dimension.
*   **Select moderate thickness for no-camber:** For the NACA 00xx airfoil, a moderate `naca_t` (e.g., 12) will be chosen. This is thinner than some previous designs (like 4418) but thicker than 2407/2408, providing a balance that aims to reduce drag associated with excessive thickness while still allowing for reasonable structural depth, and exploring a less common thickness value.
