*   **Strategy Focus:** My goal is to generate a diverse and novel design, drawing insights from the general trends observed in the database rather than directly tweaking existing successful designs. I will leverage aerodynamic principles to explore a distinct region of the design space.
*   **Sweep Exploration:** Design 1's moderate sweep (58 deg) was successful. I want to explore if a slightly *lower* sweep (e.g., ~55 deg) can still provide effective vortex lift while potentially reducing some of the induced drag penalties associated with very high sweep, differentiating from the previously explored higher sweep range.
*   **Twist Distribution for Efficiency:** The high washout (`twist_tip` much more negative than `twist_root`) of Design 1 suggests this is a crucial factor for optimizing spanwise load and minimizing induced drag. I will maintain an aggressive washout to promote desirable lift distribution.
*   **Anhedral for Stability/Vortex Control:** Design 1 utilized significant anhedral. I will continue to explore this feature, hypothesizing it may contribute positively to lateral stability characteristics or vortex interactions for highly swept wings, setting it apart from designs with dihedral.
*   **Novel Airfoil Exploration (Symmetric):** All previous designs included camber (`naca_m` > 0). To diversify and isolate the effects of sweep and twist, I will introduce a symmetric airfoil (`naca_m=0, naca_p=0`). This allows assessment of performance solely from angle of attack and vortex lift without explicit camber, providing a novel point in the design space.
*   **Thin Profile:** Consistently, thinner airfoils (`naca_t=7` in Design 1) correlated with higher rewards. I will select the minimum allowable thickness (`naca_t=6`) to further reduce profile drag and maintain a high CL/CDi ratio.
*   **Root Chord Scaling:** A moderate root chord (e.g., between 20-30 inches) seems to be effective, balancing overall wing size and induced drag. I'll choose a value in this range.
