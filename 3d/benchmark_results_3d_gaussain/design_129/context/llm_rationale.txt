*   **Maximize Sweep for Vortex Lift**: Designs with higher `le_sweep` (like Design 5 at 76.6 degrees) showed promising rewards. Pushing `le_sweep` towards its maximum limit (80.0 degrees) will explore the extreme benefits of leading-edge vortex lift, aiming for high lift-to-induced-drag ratios.
*   **Reinforce Extreme Washout**: The top-performing designs consistently utilize maximum washout, with both `twist_root` and `twist_tip` at -10.0 degrees. This configuration likely optimizes the spanwise lift distribution, reduces induced drag, and prevents premature tip stall for delta wings. This trend is crucial to maintain.
*   **Maintain Extreme Anhedral**: Similarly, `dihedral` at -15.0 degrees is a recurring feature in high-reward designs. This strong anhedral might contribute to stability characteristics or induced drag reduction at operating conditions, and its consistent presence suggests it's a beneficial configuration for this objective.
*   **Explore Novel Camber Profile (NACA 20xx)**: Instead of the `24xx` or `00xx` profiles, we will introduce a `NACA 20xx` profile (e.g., `NACA 2007`). This profile has 2% camber with the maximum at the leading edge (p=0), which is a valid configuration (`naca_p` must be 0 if `naca_m`=0, but can be 0 if `naca_m` is not 0). This represents a different lift-generating mechanism and could offer novel aerodynamic characteristics, contributing to diversity.
*   **Adjust Root Chord for High Sweep**: With a very high sweep angle, a slightly reduced `root_chord_in` (e.g., around 28-30 inches) can maintain a reasonable overall wing area and potentially reduce wetted area for drag, complementing the increased vortex lift. This explores the effect of planform scaling at extreme sweep.
