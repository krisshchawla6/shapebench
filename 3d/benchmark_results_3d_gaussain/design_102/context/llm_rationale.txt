*   **Explore slightly reduced but still high sweep:** The dominant trend for high rewards is very high sweep (around 71 degrees). While this maximizes vortex lift, there might be a point where the increase in induced drag or other drag components outweighs further sweep benefits. I will reduce the sweep slightly to 68.5 degrees, maintaining strong vortex lift characteristics but exploring a slightly less extreme configuration to potentially optimize the overall CL/CDi ratio.
*   **Maintain strong, but differentiated, washout:** The top designs feature strong washout at both root and tip. I will implement a strong negative twist at both locations, specifically -9.0 degrees at the root and -10.0 degrees at the tip. This maintains the beneficial unloading of the tips for induced drag reduction and stall control, while allowing a slight variation at the root compared to the uniform -10/-10 of the best design, which might further refine the load distribution.
*   **Retain maximum anhedral:** The consistent presence of maximum anhedral (-15.0 degrees) in the highest-reward designs suggests it is crucial for performance or stability in this highly swept regime. I will maintain this parameter at its extreme value.
*   **Introduce a very thin, moderately cambered airfoil:** Designs with NACA 2407 (cambered, 7% thick) and NACA 0006 (symmetric, 6% thick) performed well. To push for lower profile drag while retaining some camber benefit, I will specify NACA 2406 (2% camber at 40% chord, 6% thickness). This is a thinner, cambered profile, combining aspects of two successful airfoil types.
*   **Adjust root chord for diverse scaling:** The best designs cluster around a root chord of 30-33 inches. To explore the effect of overall wing size while maintaining other high-performing characteristics, I will select a root chord of 31.0 inches. This is within the successful range but distinct from previous top designs.
