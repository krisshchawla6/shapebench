*   **Push `le_sweep` to a higher extreme:** Current top designs are around 70-71 degrees. To explore the boundaries and understand the limits of high sweep, I will set `le_sweep` to 76.0 degrees. This is a significant step further than previous successful designs and helps explore potential benefits of even stronger vortex generation.
*   **Introduce new camber profile (NACA 44xx):** Previous high-reward designs used NACA 24xx. I will explore `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4` to introduce a different camber distribution with a higher maximum camber, potentially enhancing lift at higher angles of attack. This moves away from the previously explored `m=2` and `m=0` values.
*   **Moderate but significant washout and anhedral:** While extreme negative twist and dihedral have proven successful, I will set `twist_root` to -8.0, `twist_tip` to -8.0, and `dihedral` to -10.0. This explores a region where washout and anhedral are still strong but not at their absolute boundary, aiming to find if a slightly less aggressive setup yields similar or better results with the new sweep and camber, or to understand the performance drop-off.
*   **Explore a smaller `root_chord_in`:** The high-reward designs clustered around 30-33 inches. I will choose a smaller `root_chord_in` of 22.0 inches to investigate the effects of overall wing size and aspect ratio scaling on performance, providing diversity in the dimension parameter.
*   **Test a moderately thicker airfoil (`naca_t=12`):** Most successful designs used relatively thin airfoils (7-8% thickness). I will set `naca_t` to 12. This is a moderate increase in thickness, allowing for exploration of its impact on drag, lift, and potentially structural properties without going to the extreme upper limit.
