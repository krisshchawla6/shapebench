The representative design (Design 1) demonstrates the highest reward (8.8696) in the current population, indicating a highly effective combination of parameters. It features a moderately high leading edge sweep (68.796 degrees), a moderate root chord (28.84 inches), and a distinct washout distribution from root (-4.11 degrees) to tip (-6.55 degrees). The wing employs a NACA 2411 cambered airfoil and exhibits slight anhedral (-4.89 degrees).

Critically assessing these characteristics:
*   **High Sweep (68.796 deg):** This contributes significantly to vortex lift, a dominant lift mechanism for delta wings, which is crucial for achieving high lift coefficients at relevant angles of attack. Extremely high sweep (e.g., Design 4, 5) led to lower rewards, suggesting an optimal range exists around 60-70 degrees.
*   **Washout (twist_root -4.11, twist_tip -6.55):** The negative twist towards the tip unloads the outer wing sections. This is critical for optimizing spanwise lift distribution, delaying tip stall, and minimizing induced drag, directly boosting the CL/CDi ratio. The magnitude of washout in Design 1 (approx 2.44 deg) is moderate compared to Design 2 (~6.22 deg), which also performed well.
*   **Cambered Airfoil (NACA 2411):** The 2% camber at 40% chord significantly enhances lift generation at lower angles of attack compared to symmetric airfoils (like NACA 0008, Design 4), improving efficiency. The 11% thickness is moderate, balancing structural needs with profile drag.
*   **Anhedral (dihedral -4.89 deg):** This negative dihedral provides a stabilizing effect for highly swept wings by reducing the effective dihedral, which can otherwise lead to excessive roll coupling and "dutch roll" instability. This helps maintain stable flight for better performance data capture.

**Key design changes needed to improve designs in the population:**
1.  **Refine Washout Gradient:** Explore a slightly more aggressive washout at the tip, building on the success of Design 1 and 2, to further optimize spanwise lift distribution and reduce induced drag.
2.  **Optimize Airfoil Thickness:** Investigate slightly thinner cambered airfoils (e.g., NACA 2409 or 2408) to potentially reduce profile drag without significantly sacrificing lift, especially when paired with an optimized lift distribution.
3.  **Tune Root Chord for Area:** Experiment with slightly increased root chord while maintaining high sweep to potentially increase overall wing area and effective aspect ratio, which could further improve CL/CDi.
4.  **Slight Dihedral Adjustment:** Fine-tune the anhedral angle within the successful negative range to balance roll stability and overall aerodynamic efficiency.
