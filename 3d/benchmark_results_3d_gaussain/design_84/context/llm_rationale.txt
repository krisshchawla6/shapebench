*   **Broaden Sweep Exploration (R1):** The top designs are clustered at high sweep (~71 degrees). To explore novelty and potentially find new optima, I will choose a significantly lower sweep angle, e.g., 55 degrees. This tests a different regime for vortex generation, potentially with lower induced drag, challenging the notion that only very high sweep leads to the best CL/CDi.
*   **Test Moderate Positive Dihedral (R2):** The top design utilized maximum anhedral. To explore how dihedral affects performance differently, I will set a slightly positive dihedral, e.g., +5 degrees. This is a direct contrast to the successful anhedral and will provide data on the effects of tips-up configuration on stability and vortex flow for a lower-sweep delta.
*   **Investigate Non-Uniform Washout with Strong Tip Unloading (R3):** The previous best designs had aggressive uniform washout. I will try a configuration with minimal or zero twist at the root (`twist_root=0.0`) to maximize lift generation in the central part of the wing, combined with a strong negative twist at the tip (`twist_tip=-10.0`) to ensure efficient tip unloading and delay vortex breakdown.
*   **Explore Max Camber with Moderate Thickness (R4):** Current successful designs use NACA 24xx or 00xx. To aggressively pursue higher lift, I will select the maximum available camber (`naca_m=4`, `naca_p=4`). For thickness, I will choose a moderate value like 10% (`naca_t=10`), balancing structural needs with drag considerations.
*   **Adjust Root Chord for Scale Effects (R5):** The high-performing designs have root chords around 33 inches. I will explore a smaller root chord, e.g., 25 inches. This will test the impact of overall wing scale on efficiency in conjunction with the other novel parameter choices.
