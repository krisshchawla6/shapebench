*   **Leverage Proven Patterns**: The strong correlation between high leading-edge sweep, max anhedral, and significant washout (negative twist) with high rewards is undeniable. The new design will incorporate these successful elements.
*   **Push Sweep for Enhanced Vortex Lift**: Design 1 and Design 2 have very high sweep. I will push the leading-edge sweep further towards the upper limit (but not maxed out at 80.0) to explore the effectiveness of even stronger leading-edge vortices for lift generation, which is a primary driver for delta wings.
*   **Aggressive, Uniform Washout**: Designs with max root washout (-10.0) and high tip washout (-9.0 to -10.0) perform well. I will set both root and tip twist to the maximum negative value (-10.0) to investigate if a uniform, aggressive washout across the span provides a more optimal and stable lift distribution with very high sweep.
*   **Maintain Max Anhedral**: The -15.0 deg dihedral (max anhedral) has consistently yielded high rewards. This suggests a strong beneficial interaction with the delta wing's aerodynamics, possibly in stabilizing vortices or improving effective dihedral, and should be maintained.
*   **Explore Larger Root Chord**: While current high-reward designs have root chords around 27-33 inches, exploring a somewhat larger root chord (e.g., 35-40 inches) could potentially increase overall wing area and thus total lift, while maintaining the same aspect ratio properties (due to fixed taper=0, tip_chord=0), potentially leading to higher CL/CDi if induced drag scales favorably.
*   **Test Moderately Thicker Symmetric Airfoil**: Most top designs use NACA 0006 or 0008. To promote diversity and explore the parameter space, I will try a slightly thicker symmetric airfoil (NACA 0012). This tests if the penalty in skin friction drag is offset by other benefits or if it allows for more robust vortex behavior, or to understand the threshold beyond which thickness becomes detrimental for CL/CDi.
