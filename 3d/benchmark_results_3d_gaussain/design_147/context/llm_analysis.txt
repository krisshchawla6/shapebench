The top-performing designs (Design 1 & 2) consistently exhibit a highly swept wing (around 70-72 degrees), a mid-range root chord (32-33 inches), aggressive washout at both root and tip (especially `twist_tip=-10.0`), and significant anhedral (`dihedral=-11.0 to -15.0`). They also favor NACA 24xx airfoils (2% max camber at 40% chord, 7-8% thickness). The representative Design 1 with a NACA 2407 profile achieved the highest reward of 424.56. This suggests that strong vortex lift (due to high sweep) combined with load alleviation via aggressive washout and potentially favorable stability characteristics from anhedral are key contributors to high CL/CDi.

Aerodynamic issues to address generally involve optimizing the lift distribution to minimize induced drag while maintaining sufficient lift and avoiding adverse stall characteristics. The consistent washout suggests that designers are attempting to prevent tip stall and improve spanwise load distribution. The anhedral might be helping with lateral stability at these high sweeps, or interacting beneficially with the vortex flow.

Key design changes needed to improve designs in the population:
1.  **Explore varying twist distributions**: While max washout seems beneficial, other twist strategies, including less aggressive washout or even mild washin at the root, should be explored to understand their interaction with different sweep angles and camber.
2.  **Investigate dihedral beyond extreme anhedral**: The current population heavily favors negative dihedral. Exploring positive or near-zero dihedral is crucial to understand its impact across the design space.
3.  **Test different NACA profiles**: The top designs use NACA 24xx. Exploring other camber levels (NACA 44xx or NACA 00xx with varied thickness) can reveal new performance optima.
4.  **Boundary exploration for sweep and chord**: The majority of designs hover around 70-72 degrees sweep and 30-35 inches root chord. Exploring the extremes (very high/low sweep, very large/small root chord) is necessary for a comprehensive search.
