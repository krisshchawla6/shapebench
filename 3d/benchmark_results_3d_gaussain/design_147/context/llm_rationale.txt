*   **Exploration of High Sweep & Larger Chord**: The current top performers are around 70-72 degrees sweep and ~33 inches root chord. I will push towards a higher sweep of `75.0` degrees and a larger `root_chord_in` of `45.0` inches. This explores the upper end of these ranges, testing if increased wing area and even stronger vortex generation (due to higher sweep) can yield benefits, or if there's a diminishing return or penalty.
*   **Challenging the Dihedral Preference**: All high-reward designs feature significant anhedral. I will explore the opposite extreme by setting `dihedral = 10.0` (positive dihedral). This is a completely underexplored region and will reveal how positive dihedral affects CL/CDi in delta wings, especially when combined with high sweep.
*   **Investigating Milder Washout with Different Camber**: While extreme washout appears beneficial for current designs, I want to explore if this holds true for different wing geometries. I will use a milder washout with `twist_root = -2.0` and `twist_tip = -6.0`. This is still washout, but less aggressive than the -10/-10 seen in top performers. This moderate twist will be combined with a different airfoil.
*   **Exploring a Different Camber Profile**: The high-reward designs use NACA 24xx. I will explore NACA `4410` (4% max camber at 40% chord, 10% thickness). This profile offers higher camber than the prevalent NACA 24xx, combined with a slightly thicker profile. This combination (`naca_m=4`, `naca_p=4`, `naca_t=10`) is distinct from previously successful designs and allows testing the effect of increased camber on performance with a high sweep and moderate washout.
