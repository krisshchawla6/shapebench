The top-performing designs (Designs 1 and 2, with rewards of 424.56 and 353.78 respectively) share several common characteristics:
*   **High Leading Edge Sweep**: Both designs have sweep angles around 70-71 degrees.
*   **Extreme Washout**: Both `twist_root` and `twist_tip` are heavily negative, often at or near the maximum washout of -10.0 degrees. This suggests a strong unloading of the wing, particularly the tips, which is crucial for managing vortex lift and preventing premature tip stall in highly swept delta wings.
*   **Strong Negative Dihedral**: Dihedral is at or near the maximum negative value of -15.0 degrees ("tips down"). This likely contributes to stabilizing the vortex flow and improving lift distribution.
*   **Cambered Airfoil**: Both designs utilize a `NACA 24xx` airfoil, indicating 2% maximum camber at 40% chord. Design 4 with a symmetric `NACA 0006` airfoil, while having a decent reward (218.48), performed significantly worse than the top two, underscoring the benefit of camber for lift.
*   **Moderate Thickness**: `NACA_t` values of 7 or 8 are observed in the top performers.

Design 5, which had a higher sweep of 75.67 degrees but a very low reward (15.80), suggests that simply increasing sweep without appropriate accompanying parameter adjustments (e.g., twist, chord) can be detrimental. Similarly, Design 6 shows that less negative dihedral (e.g., -3.99) significantly reduces reward.

**Key design changes needed to improve designs in the population:**
1.  **Investigate higher sweep with refined twist distribution**: The current high performers are around 71 degrees. We need to cautiously explore the 75-80 degree range, but with carefully tailored twist to maintain vortex stability and lift.
2.  **Explore higher camber profiles**: `NACA 24xx` is good, but `NACA 44xx` offers more camber, potentially yielding more lift. This needs to be tested to see if the drag penalty is acceptable.
3.  **Systematic variation of root chord**: The top designs cluster around 32-33 inches. Exploring slightly smaller or larger chords in conjunction with other parameter changes could reveal new optimal regions.
4.  **Maintain beneficial extreme parameters**: Strong negative washout and dihedral seem critical, and these should be maintained or slightly varied from their current extremes to see if any small deviations can further optimize.
