*   **Exploration Strategy: Pushing Sweep to Upper Boundary**: The highest reward designs are around 70-71 degrees of sweep. To explore novelty and potentially further enhance vortex lift, we will increase `le_sweep` significantly towards the upper bound, specifically to 78.0 degrees. This will test the limits of what very high sweep can achieve.
*   **Exploring Maximum Camber and Minimum Thickness**: The most successful designs use NACA 24xx profiles. We will explore `naca_m=4` (maximum camber) with `naca_p=4` (camber position) to see if increased camber can provide more lift, especially with very high sweep. To counter potential drag increases from camber and high sweep, we will pair this with `naca_t=6` (minimum thickness), exploring the boundary of thin airfoils.
*   **Investigating Positive Dihedral**: All top designs feature strong anhedral (-15 degrees). This design will explore a contrasting `dihedral` of 5.0 degrees (slight positive dihedral). This is a significant departure and tests how a different lateral stability characteristic impacts performance with very high sweep and camber.
*   **Novel Wash-in/Washout Twist Distribution**: The best design uses max washout at both root and tip. This design will explore a less common twist distribution: `twist_root=2.0` (slight wash-in) and `twist_tip=-9.0` (strong washout). This setup aims to tailor the spanwise lift distribution differently, potentially delaying root stall while maintaining beneficial tip washout.
*   **Moderate Root Chord**: While top designs use ~33 inches, we'll select `root_chord_in=28.0`. This is slightly smaller, potentially reducing overall wetted area and induced drag, while still being substantial enough for a high-performance delta wing.
