*   **Database Insight & Novel Strategy:** High sweep and aggressive washout are consistent features of high-reward designs. However, the database predominantly features NACA 24xx airfoils and often negative root twist or anhedral. To adhere to the "Novel Design Strategy," I will *not* mimic Design 1 but instead apply these insights to a fresh design by explicitly exploring alternative aerodynamic strategies.
*   **Sweep Optimization for Robustness:** While Design 1's sweep is nearly 70 degrees, I will slightly reduce it to 65.0 degrees. This still ensures robust vortex generation, a key driver for delta wing performance, but might offer a more stable vortex structure and potentially lower induced drag across a wider range of conditions than extremely high sweeps, addressing the 'Vortex Breakdown Sensitivity' point.
*   **Testing Twist-Dominant Lift Generation:** To explore the hypothesis about camber vs. twist, I will select an uncambered NACA 0012 airfoil (naca_m=0, naca_p=0, naca_t=12). This allows us to isolate the effects of geometric twist on lift distribution and vortex formation, providing a direct comparison to cambered designs in the population. The moderate thickness provides structural depth without excessive profile drag.
*   **Aggressive and Differentiated Twist Distribution:** To compensate for the lack of camber and actively shape the lift distribution, I will implement a distinct twist strategy: a slight positive twist at the root (twist_root=2.0) combined with a significant negative twist at the tip (twist_tip=-8.0). This aims to distribute the lift more evenly across the inner wing while maintaining strong washout at the tips for vortex control and delayed stall, contrasting with Design 1's universally negative twist.
*   **Dihedral Exploration:** Design 1 has strong anhedral. I will explore a slightly positive dihedral (dihedral=5.0) to understand its impact on CL/CDi when combined with an uncambered, highly twisted delta wing. This diversifies the population's exploration of stability configurations.

This provides a larger reference area, potentially allowing for higher overall lift while still being within the established effective scale for delta wings in this context.
