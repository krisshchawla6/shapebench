*   **Explore extreme high sweep (75-80 degrees)**: The current designs haven't pushed this boundary effectively. High sweep accentuates vortex lift, which can be a dominant lift mechanism for delta wings. By exploring this boundary, we aim to understand its full potential when combined with other optimal parameters.
*   **Introduce a symmetric airfoil (NACA 00XX)**: All top-performing designs used cambered airfoils (NACA 24XX or 40XX). Using a symmetric profile (NACA 00XX) would allow us to isolate the effects of geometric twist and planform on lift distribution and induced drag, without the inherent pitching moment and profile drag penalty of camber. This is a significant exploration of the airfoil parameter space.
*   **Utilize a larger root chord (e.g., 40-50 inches)**: Design 1, with the highest reward, had a relatively small root chord (21.7 in). Exploring the upper end of the root chord range (40-50 inches) can significantly change the wing loading and overall scale, providing insight into the impact of larger planform areas on CL/CDi, especially in conjunction with high sweep.
*   **Employ significant washout (negative twist at tip) with a slightly negative root twist**: High-performing designs like D1 and D3 consistently show good washout. Maintaining a strong washout ensures good spanwise lift distribution and reduces the likelihood of tip stall, which is crucial for high sweep and potentially larger wings. A slightly negative root twist can further fine-tune the load distribution.
*   **Investigate moderate positive dihedral**: Previous high-reward designs exhibited anhedral or very slight positive dihedral. Exploring moderate positive dihedral (e.g., 5-10 degrees) could offer insights into its interaction with high sweep and vortex flow, potentially improving stability without significant performance penalties.
*   **Select a thinner airfoil (naca_t towards 6-8)**: To compensate for potential drag increases from high sweep and larger chord, a thin airfoil (e.g., NACA 0008) is chosen to minimize profile drag. This combination has not been thoroughly tested with high sweep and a symmetric profile.
