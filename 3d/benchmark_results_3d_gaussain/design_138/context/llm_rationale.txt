*   **Leverage extremely high sweep for maximum vortex lift**: Building on Design 4's success with very high sweep (76.6 degrees), this new design will explore an even higher sweep angle of 78.0 degrees. This pushes further into the regime known for strong leading-edge vortex generation, aiming to maximize this contribution to lift and potentially improve the CL/CDi ratio by enhancing the efficiency of the vortex system.
*   **Introduce higher camber with aggressive sweep**: Previous top designs primarily used NACA 24xx or 00xx airfoils. This design will introduce a NACA 4410 airfoil (4% max camber at 40% chord) to be combined with the very high sweep. This is a novel combination, testing if the added camber can further augment lift characteristics or improve performance across a wider angle of attack range when paired with the strong vortex lift from high sweep, a strategy not fully explored in the top designs.
*   **Refine twist distribution for balanced lift**: Design 1 and 4 both had significant root washout (-10.0 deg), with Design 1 also having extreme tip washout. This design will implement a milder root washout (-5.0 deg) to ensure robust lift generation across the inboard wing sections, combined with a strong, but not extreme, tip washout (-8.0 deg). This aims to achieve a more balanced lift distribution, reduce induced drag, and delay vortex breakdown at the tip while not sacrificing too much overall lift.
*   **Maintain strong anhedral for consistent performance**: The top-performing designs (1, 2, 4) consistently featured significant negative dihedral (anhedral, ranging from -11.66 to -15.0 degrees). This suggests that anhedral contributes positively to the CL/CDi objective, possibly by influencing the effective aspect ratio or vortex interaction. We will retain the maximum anhedral of -15.0 degrees to maintain this beneficial characteristic in the new high-sweep, high-camber configuration.
*   **Explore larger root chord**: The top designs generally have root chords in the 30-36 inch range. To explore the impact of scale and potentially achieve higher absolute lift while maintaining efficiency, this design will utilize a larger root chord of 40.0 inches. This allows for a proportionally larger wing planform, which might interact differently with the extreme sweep and camber characteristics.
