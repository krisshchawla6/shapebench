*   **Hybrid Strategy:** This new design will blend the exceptionally high reward characteristics of Design 1 (aggressive washout, max anhedral) with the very high sweep of Design 6 and the exploration of symmetric airfoils seen in Designs 4 and 6.
*   **Pushing Sweep Limits:** Design 6 showed decent performance with the highest sweep so far (76.64 deg). I will push `le_sweep` even closer to the maximum allowed (80.0 deg) to aggressively explore the upper bounds of vortex-dominated flow regimes. This might lead to even greater vortex lift generation.
*   **Consistent Aggressive Washout:** Design 1's full -10.0 degree twist at both root and tip was highly successful. To maintain this effective load distribution and vortex control, I will keep both `twist_root` and `twist_tip` at significantly negative values, slightly adjusting from Design 1 to explore a new point in this high-performance region (e.g., -9.0 at root, -10.0 at tip).
*   **Retaining Max Anhedral:** Designs 1, 4, and 6 all feature the maximum anhedral (`dihedral=-15.0`). This strong correlation with higher rewards suggests it's a beneficial characteristic for these delta wing configurations, possibly improving effective aspect ratio or stability. I will keep `dihedral` at -15.0.
*   **Exploring Symmetric Airfoils:** Designs 4 and 6, despite using symmetric NACA 00xx airfoils, achieved respectable rewards. This design will test if a simpler, symmetric airfoil (NACA 00xx) can achieve top-tier performance when combined with extremely high sweep, aggressive washout, and max anhedral, potentially simplifying manufacturing while maintaining aerodynamic efficiency. I'll choose `naca_t=8` as a mid-range thickness from previous good designs.
*   **Adjusted Root Chord:** The top designs have root chords around 30-33 inches. I will choose a `root_chord_in` value of 29.5 inches, slightly below Design 1 but within the successful range, to maintain a reasonable scale while perhaps offering a slight reduction in overall wetted area.
