*   **Exploration of Low Sweep**: The current high-reward designs are highly clustered at `le_sweep` ~70-71 degrees. To explore the design space effectively and avoid local optima, I will venture into a significantly lower sweep regime (e.g., 55.0 degrees). This will test how other parameters (twist, dihedral, airfoil) interact when vortex lift is potentially less dominant.
*   **Symmetric Airfoil Strategy**: All previously observed designs that achieved good rewards employed a NACA 24XX airfoil (cambered). I will explore a symmetric airfoil (`naca_m=0`, `naca_p=0`) to understand if camber is strictly necessary for high CL/CDi, or if purely geometric lift generation combined with optimal twist and sweep can achieve good results. This is an entirely untried configuration.
*   **Positive Dihedral Exploration**: The highest reward designs consistently feature aggressive anhedral (`dihedral` negative). To explore the impact of lateral stability design, I will try a moderate positive `dihedral` (e.g., 7.5 degrees). This starkly contrasts with previous successful designs and will provide insights into its effect on a less-swept, symmetric wing.
*   **Moderate Twist for Lower Sweep**: With a lower sweep, the extreme washout of the current top designs might be suboptimal. I will implement a milder twist configuration: a slight positive twist at the root (`twist_root=2.0`) for some wash-in, and a slight negative twist at the tip (`twist_tip=-2.0`) to provide a small amount of washout. This deviates significantly from the uniform -10/-10 twist observed.
*   **Smaller Scale Investigation**: The high-reward designs have `root_chord_in` values clustered around 32-33 inches. I will explore a significantly smaller `root_chord_in` (e.g., 20.0 inches). This tests how the scaling of the wing impacts performance with an entirely different set of aerodynamic characteristics, potentially revealing optimal designs at smaller physical scales.
*   **Mid-Range Thickness**: To ensure a robust exploration and isolate the effects of the more radical changes, I will select a `naca_t` in the middle of the range (12). This is thicker than the previous high-reward designs and will allow us to see how a less slender airfoil performs under these novel conditions.
