*   **Exploration Strategy**: The current population is heavily concentrated around high sweep, maximum washout, and maximum anhedral. To truly explore, we need to push parameters into largely uncharted territory, especially those that are inversions of the current "optima" (like positive dihedral or wash-in).
*   **Maximize `le_sweep`**: High sweep has consistently shown strong performance. We will push this to the absolute boundary of 80.0 degrees to see if even higher sweep is beneficial when combined with other novel parameters, potentially enhancing vortex lift further.
*   **Inverse `dihedral` to positive**: This is the most drastically underexplored region. All top designs are at -15.0. Setting `dihedral` to a high positive value (e.g., 10.0 or 15.0) will drastically alter the lateral stability characteristics and is a bold exploration to see how the system responds to this geometric change. We'll try 10.0.
*   **Explore `NACA 44xx` and thicker airfoils**: The highest camber (`naca_m=4`, `naca_p=4`) has not been thoroughly explored in combination with other extreme parameters. We will pair this with a thicker airfoil (`naca_t=16`) which is significantly thicker than the successful thin profiles, to investigate if added camber and structural depth can yield benefits.
*   **Experiment with positive `twist_root` and neutral `twist_tip`**: Instead of the ubiquitous washout, we will introduce positive twist at the root (e.g., 5.0 degrees) and neutral twist at the tip (0.0 degrees). This creates wash-in, which is completely opposite to the current successful designs, and will provide crucial data on the impact of positive twist on lift distribution with a highly swept, anhedral wing. This combination is highly novel.
*   **Moderate `root_chord_in`**: While exploring other extremes, we'll keep `root_chord_in` in a moderate-to-high range (e.g., 35.0 inches) to allow the airfoil and twist properties to manifest effectively without being too small or excessively large. This provides a balance, not adding another extreme to an already bold design.
