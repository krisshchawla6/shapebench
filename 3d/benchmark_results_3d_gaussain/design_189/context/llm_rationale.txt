Our exploration strategy is to deliberately move away from the current population's successful high-sweep, high-washout, negative-dihedral paradigm. This will help us map the design space more thoroughly and potentially uncover new high-performing regions or robust designs.

*   **Explore Moderate Sweep:** Most top designs are highly swept (70-78 degrees). We will explore a significantly lower sweep angle, setting `le_sweep` to 60.0 degrees. This moves us towards a more conventional delta wing geometry, which might exhibit different vortex dynamics and potentially lower induced drag characteristics at certain flight conditions compared to very highly swept wings.
*   **Investigate Maximum Camber:** The `naca_m=4` (maximum camber) option has not been explored in the provided designs. We will set `naca_m=4` and `naca_p=4` to introduce significant airfoil camber, aiming to generate more lift at lower angles of attack and shift the lift distribution, potentially reducing trim drag or improving performance at specific lift requirements.
*   **Test Positive Dihedral:** All high-reward designs feature extreme negative dihedral (-15.0 degrees). We will take a bold exploratory step by setting `dihedral` to 10.0 degrees (positive). While this might reduce lateral stability, it's a critical parameter to vary to understand its full impact on CL/CDi in conjunction with other design choices.
*   **Vary Twist Distribution:** The current successful designs all feature significant washout. We will experiment with a flatter root (`twist_root=0.0`) and a moderate tip washout (`twist_tip=-5.0`). This less aggressive washout distribution, combined with the new camber and lower sweep, will provide insight into how twist interacts with other parameters to distribute lift.
*   **Explore Higher Root Chord and Moderate Thickness:** The current root chord values are clustered around 27-33 inches, and thickness around 6-10. We will explore a larger `root_chord_in` of 45.0 inches to test scale effects, and a `naca_t` of 14, representing a moderate increase in thickness, which is still well within structural limits but provides more volume and potentially affects vortex formation and drag differently.
