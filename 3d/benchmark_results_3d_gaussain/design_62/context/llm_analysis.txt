The top-ranked designs consistently exhibit high leading-edge sweep (around 70-72 degrees), maximum or near-maximum negative twist (washout) at the tip and often at the root, and significant anhedral (negative dihedral, often -15 degrees). The most successful designs (1 and 2) also utilize cambered NACA 24xx airfoils with moderate thickness (7-8%). Design 3, with a symmetric NACA 0006 airfoil, achieved a lower but still respectable reward, suggesting camber might be beneficial.

The common characteristics of the high-reward designs point towards an aerodynamic strategy heavily relying on vortex lift generated by highly swept leading edges, with washout to manage load distribution and potentially delay flow separation, and anhedral for stability or vortex enhancement. The Cp distribution (if available for Design 1) would likely show strong suction peaks along the leading edge, indicative of vortex formation.

Key design changes needed to improve exploration of the population:
1.  **Explore higher sweep angles**: The current cluster is around 70-72 degrees. The range extends to 80 degrees, which is largely unexplored by high-performing designs.
2.  **Investigate different twist strategies**: All top designs use heavy washout. Positive twist (washin) or a more neutral twist distribution, or different root/tip combinations, are significantly underexplored.
3.  **Evaluate positive dihedral**: All top designs use negative dihedral (anhedral). Positive dihedral (tips up) needs to be tested to understand its impact, especially with high sweep.
4.  **Test higher camber and different thickness values**: NACA m=4 (more camber) and thicker profiles (naca_t > 11) are underexplored among the higher-reward designs.
