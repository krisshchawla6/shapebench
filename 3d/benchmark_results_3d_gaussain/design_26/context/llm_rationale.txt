*   **Explore a novel airfoil strategy with a symmetric profile:** The current top designs exclusively use NACA 24xx cambered airfoils. To achieve "diversity and bold exploration," this new design will employ a NACA 00xx symmetric airfoil (naca_m=0, naca_p=0). This tests whether high performance can be achieved primarily through vortex lift generated by wing geometry, rather than airfoil camber, representing a distinct aerodynamic approach.
*   **Push leading-edge sweep to a higher extreme:** The current high-reward designs have sweep around 70-72 degrees. To compensate for the lack of camber and potentially enhance vortex strength, `le_sweep` will be set higher, closer to the upper limit (75.0 degrees). This pushes the boundary of the successful high-sweep characteristic.
*   **Maintain aggressive tip washout for stability and load distribution:** Despite the change to a symmetric airfoil, the consistent success of `twist_tip = -10.0` across top designs indicates its importance for managing tip stall and distributing lift effectively. This parameter will be kept at its maximum negative value. `twist_root` will be set negative but slightly less extreme than -10 to allow for some load distribution adjustment with the new airfoil.
*   **Investigate less extreme negative dihedral:** While Design 1 has max negative dihedral, other good designs have less extreme negative values. We will set `dihedral` to a moderately negative value (-5.0) to see if the benefits of anhedral persist without being at the maximum, potentially offering a more balanced stability profile without sacrificing too much CL/CDi.
*   **Utilize minimum airfoil thickness:** The top designs favour thin airfoils (NACA 2407, 2408). To further reduce profile drag, especially with a symmetric airfoil, `naca_t` will be set to the minimum allowed value of 6. This aligns with reducing drag characteristics.
*   **Select a moderate root chord:** The root chord in top designs ranges from 32-35 inches. We will choose a value within this successful range (`33.5 inches`) to provide a reasonably sized platform for the high-sweep, thin, symmetric wing, allowing the other novel parameter combinations to be evaluated effectively without introducing significant scale effects that might overshadow the primary aerodynamic strategy changes.
