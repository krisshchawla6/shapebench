*   **Exploit high sweep, aggressive washout, and strong anhedral:** The top-ranked designs (1 and 2) strongly feature high `le_sweep` (~70-71 degrees), extreme `twist_tip` (-10.0 degrees), and significant `dihedral` (close to -15.0 degrees). This combination is highly effective for maximizing CL/CDi, suggesting this aerodynamic strategy is successful.
*   **Refine sweep and root chord:** Design 1's `le_sweep` of ~71.2 degrees was most successful. We will explore a slightly higher sweep (72.5 degrees) to push the boundary while remaining within the effective range, potentially increasing vortex lift further. The `root_chord_in` will be kept in the successful 30-35 inch range (33.5 inches) to maintain wing area and scale.
*   **Optimize twist distribution:** Design 1 used -10.0 for both root and tip twist. Design 2 used -6.1 for root and -10.0 for tip. The `-10.0` `twist_tip` seems critical. We will retain `-10.0` for `twist_tip` and set `twist_root` to a slightly less aggressive, but still substantial, -9.5 degrees to explore variations in load distribution while maintaining overall aggressive washout.
*   **Confirm anhedral:** The `-15.0` `dihedral` in Design 1 (and Design 5) is consistently associated with higher rewards. We will maintain this strong anhedral angle.
*   **Leverage cambered airfoils:** The NACA 24xx series consistently appears in higher-reward designs. We will use `naca_m=2` and `naca_p=4` to ensure positive camber is present.
*   **Explore thickness:** Design 1 used NACA 2407 and Design 2 used NACA 2408. We will slightly increase thickness to `naca_t=9` to investigate if a slightly thicker profile can maintain or improve performance by potentially offering better pressure recovery or internal volume without excessive drag penalty.
