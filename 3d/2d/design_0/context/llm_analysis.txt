In a 4-control-point system, the primary risks are generating non-physical "blob" shapes with blunt trailing edges or self-intersecting geometries. To maximize the lift-to-drag ratio ($C_L/C_D$), the design must avoid high-pressure stagnation at a blunt rear (which causes massive pressure drag) and ensure enough thickness to prevent leading-edge separation. Specifically, a rounded leading edge is required to handle various angles of attack, while a sharp trailing edge is necessary to satisfy the Kutta condition and minimize the wake. The lack of previous data suggests that the initial "search" should prioritize a high-camber, asymmetric profile to immediately capture significant lift.
