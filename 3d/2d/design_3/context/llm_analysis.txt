As no previous designs or images are provided, I am initiating the optimization process from a baseline aerodynamic perspective. In many initial parameter-to-geometry mappings for N-point control systems, common flaws include:
- **Symmetry Mismatch**: Using identical radius parameters for upper and lower points results in a non-cambered (symmetric) airfoil, which typically yields lower lift-to-drag ratios at low angles of attack.
- **Leading Edge Sharpness**: High `edgy_param` values at the forward-most control point lead to flow separation and premature stalling.
- **Trailing Edge Blunting**: Low `edgy_param` at the rear point creates a rounded trailing edge, resulting in a large pressure-drag wake.
- **Thickness Distribution**: Improper spacing of `angle_param` can cluster points, leading to undulating surfaces rather than smooth curvature.

Key changes needed:
1.  **Differentiate Upper/Lower**: Assign a higher radius to the upper surface point (P1) and a lower/negative radius to the lower surface (P3) to create camber.
2.  **Kutta Condition Enforcement**: Maximize the `edgy_param` at the trailing edge (P2) to ensure a clean flow departure.
3.  **Leading Edge Smoothing**: Minimize `edgy_param` at the leading edge (P0) to maintain attached flow over a wider range of operating conditions.
