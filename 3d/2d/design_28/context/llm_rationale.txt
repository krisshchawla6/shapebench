*   **Strategy Focus (Exploration)**: The current high-reward designs (Designs 1-3) are clustered around specific parameter sets: CP0 radius near 1.0, CP1 angle between 0.4 and 0.8, and edgy_params for CP0-CP2 near -1.0. This design deliberately explores the "moderate sharpness" region by setting edgy_params closer to 0.0 and explores negative angular offsets for CP1.
*   **Leading Edge Variation**: By significantly reducing the CP0 radius (0.45) and pushing its angle to an extreme negative (-0.85), I am testing a "drooped" or "slanted" leading edge that the population has not yet encountered.
*   **Inverted Curvature**: Most designs use a positive angle for CP1 and negative for CP2. I am reversing this (CP1: -0.62, CP2: 0.78) to explore how shifting the bulk of the displacement to the lower-forward and upper-aft regions affects the lift-to-drag ratio.
*   **Mid-Body Edginess**: Existing designs are almost perfectly smooth (e = -1.0). Introducing slight "edginess" (e = -0.15 to -0.35) at CP1 and CP2 may allow for more distinct pressure zones or "rooftop" distributions which can be beneficial for laminar flow.
*   **Trailing Edge Transition**: Design 1 has an edgy_param of 1.0 at the tail. I will use 0.62 to explore a design that maintains a defined point but allows for a more gradual flow recombination.
