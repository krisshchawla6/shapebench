Design 1 is characterized by a conventional, smooth airfoil geometry, evidenced by `edgy_param` values near -1.0 for the first three control points and a `radius_param` distribution that stays within the positive [0.4, 1.0] range. The flow fields show a standard pressure distribution with a well-attached boundary layer, typical of high-lift supercritical profiles. However, the geometric strategy is very "safe," avoiding any sharp transitions or concave features. The trailing edge (CP3) has a slightly higher `edgy_param` (0.4199), which likely contributes to the high reward by defining a clearer stagnation point. Key flaws include a potentially oversized wake and a lack of diversity in thickness distribution. Needed changes: 1) Transition from global smoothness to localized sharp curvature to test non-intuitive pressure recovery; 2) Explore the negative `radius_param` space to create concave or inverted surface features; 3) Shift `angle_param` to extreme boundaries to reposition the leading and trailing edge influence.
