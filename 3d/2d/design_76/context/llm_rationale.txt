*   **Novel Design Strategy**: This design explicitly rejects the "pinched-middle" trend seen in the top three designs (where r1 is approx. 0.2). Instead, it explores a higher-volume, supercritical-inspired topology to test if a more conventional thickness distribution can yield higher efficiency.
*   **Aft-Loading Insight**: Drawing from population patterns where high r2 and r3 values correlate with success, I am pushing CP2 and CP3 to near-maximum radial distances while shifting their angles to concentrate camber in the final 30% of the chord.
*   **TE Definition**: To improve upon the drag characteristics of Design 1, I am increasing the `edgy_param` of the trailing edge to 0.9876, forcing a cleaner flow separation and a more defined Kutta condition.
*   **Stagnation Control**: By setting a negative angular offset for the leading edge (CP0), I am creating a "drooped" entry. This is intended to manage the stagnation point more effectively at higher lift coefficients, potentially reducing the leading-edge suction peak.
*   **Search Advancement**: As an evolutionary optimizer, I am diversifying the population by moving away from the local optimum of Design 1's "scooped" lower surface and exploring the high-lift potential of a rear-cusped, aft-loaded profile.
