Design 1 (Reward: 3.9049) exhibits several geometric and aerodynamic inefficiencies. Geometrically, the negative values for `a0` and `a2` (-0.22) create a blunt, asymmetrical leading edge that lacks the streamlined profile necessary for high lift-to-drag ratios. The pressure plot (`1_p.png`) shows a large, concentrated stagnation zone at the nose, and the velocity fields (`1_u.png`, `1_v.png`) reveal a significant wake and potential flow separation behind the mid-section. The value of `r1` (0.313) combined with `a1` (0.623) results in a relatively thick airfoil, which likely contributes to high pressure drag at these flow conditions. The trailing edge sharpness (`e3` = 0.872) is high but not maximized, leaving room for a cleaner flow detachment. To improve performance, the design needs: 1) a more positive `a0` to optimize the leading edge radius and stagnation point, 2) a significant reduction in `r1` to create a thinner, more efficient camber, 3) total smoothing of the forward surfaces (`e0`, `e1`, `e2` to -1.0), and 4) maximizing the trailing edge sharpness to 1.0 to minimize wake turbulence.
